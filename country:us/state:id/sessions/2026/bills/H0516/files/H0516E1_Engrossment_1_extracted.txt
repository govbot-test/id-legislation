11 SECTION 4. That Chapter 12, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is

14 33-1277. TAXPAYER FUNDING OF TEACHERS UNIONS PROHIBITED. (1) No
15 school district, including a specially chartered district, or its agents or
16 representatives, may use or authorize the use of taxpayer funds to support
17 teachers unions by:
18 (a) Deducting dues, fees, assessments, fines, or contributions from
19 the pay of a professional employee on behalf of a teachers union or its
20 affiliate;
21 (b) Increasing a professional employee's compensation with the in-
22 tent that the additional compensation, or a part of it, be used to pay
23 teachers union or affiliate dues, fees, assessments, fines, or contri-
24 butions;
25 (c) Providing more personal or contact information of a professional
26 employee to a teachers union or its affiliate than the school district
27 is required to disclose pursuant to chapter 1, title 74, Idaho Code, un-
28 less the school district receives written authorization from the pro-
29 fessional employee to disclose additional information;
30 (d) Requiring or coercing a professional employee to meet, communi-
31 cate, listen to, or otherwise interact with a teachers union or its
32 affiliate;
33 (e) Distributing communications or membership solicitations on behalf
34 of a teachers union or its affiliate;
35 (f) Contributing funds to, or expending funds on behalf of, a teachers
36 union or its affiliate; or
37 (g) Except as provided by sections 33-1216 and 33-1279, Idaho Code, and
38 except as otherwise provided by this section, providing any form of com-
39 pensation or paid leave to a public employee, directly or indirectly,
40 for the purpose of engaging in teachers union activities.
41 (2) If provided by a negotiated agreement between a school district,
42 including a specially chartered district, and a teachers union:
43 (a) A professional employee may be granted time off without pay or ben-
44 efits to engage in teachers union activities, and a teachers union may
45 compensate a professional employee for engaging in teachers union ac-
46 tivities.
47 (b) A professional employee may use compensated personal leave,
48 whether the professional employee's own leave or leave voluntarily
