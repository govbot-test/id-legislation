LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 516
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTION 33-6001, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PRO-
3 VISIONS REGARDING PARENTAL RIGHTS IN EDUCATION; AND DECLARING AN EMER-
4 GENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 33-6001, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 33-6001. PARENTAL RIGHTS. (1) A student's parent or legal guardian
9 has the right to reasonable academic accommodation from the child's public
10 school. "Reasonable academic accommodation" means the school shall make its
11 best effort to enable parents and legal guardians to exercise their rights
12 without substantial impact to staff and resources, including employee
13 working conditions, safety and supervision on school premises for school
14 activities, and the efficient allocation of expenditures, while balancing
15 the parental rights of parents and legal guardians, the educational needs
16 of other students, the academic and behavioral impacts to a classroom, a
17 teacher's workload, and the assurance of the safe and efficient operations
18 of the school.
19 (2) School districts and the boards of directors of public charter
20 schools, in consultation with parents, teachers, and administrators, shall
21 develop and adopt a policy to promote the involvement of parents and legal
22 guardians of children enrolled in the schools within the school district or
23 the charter school, including:
24 (a) A plan for parent participation in the schools that is designed to
25 improve parent and teacher cooperation in areas such as homework, at-
26 tendance, and discipline;
27 (b) A process by which parents may learn about the course of study for
28 their children and review learning materials, including the source of
29 any supplemental educational materials;
30 (c) A process by which parents who object to any learning material or
31 activity on the basis that it harms the child or impairs the parents'
32 firmly held beliefs, values, or principles may withdraw their child
33 from the activity, class, or program in which the material is used;
34 (d) The public school's responsibility for notifying a student's par-
35 ent or legal guardian regarding known changes in the student's mental,
36 emotional, or physical health or well-being;
37 (e) The reinforcement of the fundamental rights and responsibilities
38 of parents as primary stakeholders to make decisions regarding the up-
39 bringing and control of the parent's child; and
40 (f) A process for staff to encourage students to discuss issues related
41 to the student's well-being with the student's parent or legal guardian

2
1 and, if necessary, to facilitate discussion of the issues with the par-
2 ent or legal guardian.
3 (3) A policy shall not prohibit parents or legal guardians from ac-
4 cessing any of their children's education and health records created,
5 maintained, or used by the public school unless such documentation relates
6 to physical abuse, abandonment, or neglect by the parent or legal guardian.
7 Unless a timeline is otherwise delineated in a specific applicable state or
8 federal law, records shall be provided to the parent or legal guardian within
9 five (5) school days after a request to access such records is made.
10 (4) A public school shall not adopt procedures, policies, or student
11 support forms that prohibit public school personnel from notifying a parent
12 or legal guardian about a student's mental, emotional, or physical health or
13 well-being or a change in related services or monitoring or that encourage
14 or have the effect of encouraging a student to withhold from a parent or le-
15 gal guardian such information. This subsection does not prohibit a public
16 school from adopting procedures that permit public school personnel to with-
17 hold information from a parent or legal guardian if ordered by a court of com-
18 petent jurisdiction.
19 (5) A public school shall be required to adopt procedures and policies
20 that prohibit classroom instruction by public school personnel on sexual
21 orientation or gender identity from kindergarten through grade 12 or in a
22 manner that is not age-appropriate or developmentally appropriate for stu-
23 dents in accordance with state standards.
24 (6) At the start of the school year, each public school shall notify
25 parents and legal guardians of health services offered or made available
26 through the school or by private organizations, including preventative
27 health and wellness services, screenings, medication administration, first
28 aid and emergency care, and appropriate management of all health conditions
29 with parental consent. Parental consent to any health or wellness service
30 does not waive parents' or legal guardians' right to access their children's
31 educational or associated health records or to be notified about their chil-
32 dren's health status or monitoring as provided in this section.
33 (7) A public school shall notify a student's parent or legal guardian if
34 a student has been or may be questioned by a school resource officer or other
35 law enforcement official, unless the child is a victim or suspected victim
36 of physical child abuse. School districts and public charter schools shall
37 develop and adopt policies to ensure compliance with this subsection.
38 (8) Before any public school employee administers any noncurricu-
39 lar-related student survey, well-being questionnaire, or health screening
40 to any student, the employee must first obtain consent from the school's su-
41 perintendent or designee. If the content of any such survey, questionnaire,
42 or screening regards an individual student's sexuality, sex, religion,
43 personal political beliefs, mental or psychological problems, personal fam-
44 ily information, or individual or family financial information, the public
45 school shall provide the survey, questionnaire, or screening form to the
46 student's parent or legal guardian in advance and obtain the permission of
47 the parent or legal guardian.
48 (9) A parent or legal guardian of a child enrolled in an Idaho public
49 school shall have reasonable access to observe all school activities during
50 school hours in which the child is enrolled, except for those who are oth-

3
1 erwise legally prohibited. Observations of individual classrooms during
2 instructional time shall be permitted with the principal's and teacher's
3 pre-approval. Visits shall not be permitted if their occurrence, duration,
4 frequency, or conduct on campus interferes with the delivery of instruction
5 or disrupts the normal school environment. Nothing in this section shall
6 prevent a reasonable denial of entry due to emergency or safety drills,
7 situations outlined in school safety plans, an emergency lockdown, periods
8 of statewide testing, school officials' enforcement of the provisions of
9 section 33-512, Idaho Code, or other specific situations enumerated by the
10 school.
11 (10) A parent or legal guardian whose rights, as provided by this sec-
12 tion, are violated by a public school may file a complaint with the school.
13 Public schools shall implement policies and procedures to accept, evaluate,
14 and remedy complaints.
15 (11) If a complaint is not satisfactorily remedied by the public school,
16 a parent shall have a private cause of action for injunctive relief, damages,
17 and any other relief available under law against the school.
18 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
19 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
20 July 1, 2026.
