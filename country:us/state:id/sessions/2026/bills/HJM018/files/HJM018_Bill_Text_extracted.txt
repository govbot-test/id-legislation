LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 18
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRE-
3 SENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, AND THE CONGRES-
4 SIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE
5 UNITED STATES.
6 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
7 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
8 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
9 WHEREAS, it is documented that federal agencies, military contractors,
10 nongovernmental organizations, and private entities acting on behalf of or
11 at the request of the federal government are engaging in solar geoengineer-
12 ing experiments, including but not limited to solar radiation modification
13 and stratospheric aerosol injection, all without public knowledge or con-
14 sent; and
15 WHEREAS, the intent of this Memorial is to address only solar geoengi-
16 neering activities, not weather modification generally; and
17 WHEREAS, according to the Environmental Protection Agency, "the main
18 difference between weather modification and solar geoengineering is that
19 weather modification is intended to have local, short-term effects, whereas
20 solar geoengineering is intended to have larger regional or global effects
21 that persist" and "weather modification is the intentional modification of
22 regional or local weather patterns. The most common example is cloud seed-
23 ing, which involves introducing artificial particles into clouds with the
24 objective of increasing rain or snow or modifying regional or local weather
25 patterns."; and
26 WHEREAS, solar geoengineering activities involve substances with un-
27 known or unreported impacts on human health, livestock, crops, ecosystems,
28 and weather stability being emitted into the atmosphere without public
29 knowledge or consent; and
30 WHEREAS, solar geoengineering activities are currently allowed without
31 oversight in Idaho's airspace; and
32 WHEREAS, under the Tenth Amendment to the United States Constitu-
33 tion, it is the constitutional duty of state government to safeguard public
34 health, defend private property, protect agricultural integrity, and assert
35 state sovereignty over activities conducted within its borders and above its
36 skies; and
37 WHEREAS, the Idaho Department of Environmental Quality is charged with
38 protecting the safety and quality of the state's air, water, and soil and
39 must be empowered to investigate and stop unauthorized atmospheric contam-
40 ination within Idaho's jurisdiction; and
41 WHEREAS, multiple states, including Tennessee and Florida, have now
42 enacted legislation prohibiting solar geoengineering modifications, and

2
1 dozens more are considering similar laws to preserve natural climate systems
2 and ensure transparency in all atmospheric activity.
3 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
4 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
5 the Senate concurring therein, that the Idaho Legislature supports and urges
6 the United States Congress passing legislation to: prohibit the intentional
7 injection, dispersion, or release of chemical, biological, or radiologi-
8 cal substances into the atmosphere for the purpose of solar geoengineering
9 without the express approval of the State of Idaho; ban the use of unmarked
10 aircraft, drones, balloons, or space-based platforms to conduct solar geo-
11 engineering modifications over the State of Idaho; make such unauthorized
12 acts a felony offense, with substantial fines and criminal penalties per
13 violation; encourage partnerships between the relevant federal law en-
14 forcement agencies, Idaho Department of Environmental Quality, and relevant
15 state law enforcement entities to enforce such felonies; and formally reject
16 any federal, international, or foreign-sponsored solar geoengineering pro-
17 grams conducted over Idaho airspace without full disclosure, state consent,
18 and public transparency.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Idaho Legislature urges all elected
20 officials, agencies, and candidates to support legislation that preserves
21 Idaho's natural skies, defends individual and agricultural health, and as-
22 serts constitutional control over state airspace.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
24 tives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this
25 Memorial to the President of the United States, the President of the Senate
26 and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of Congress, and the con-
27 gressional delegation representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the
28 United States.
