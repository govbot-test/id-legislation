LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 947
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO IDAHOMES FOR IDAHO CITIZENS NOT CORPORATIONS; AMENDING CHAPTER
3 1, TITLE 55, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 55-116, IDAHO
4 CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS PROHIBITING THE PURCHASE OF SINGLE-FAM-
5 ILY HOMES BY INSTITUTIONAL INVESTORS, TO DEFINE TERMS, TO PROVIDE FOR
6 ENFORCEMENT, AND TO PROVIDE FOR IMMUNITY FROM LIABILITY; AMENDING SEC-
7 TION 55-103, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING EXCEPTIONS TO
8 WHO MAY OWN PROPERTY AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL CORRECTION; AND DECLARING
9 AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
10 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
11 SECTION 1. That Chapter 1, Title 55, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
12 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
13 ignated as Section 55-116, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
14 55-116. IDAHOMES FOR IDAHO CITIZENS NOT CORPORATIONS. (1) On and after
15 July 1, 2026, an institutional investor, as defined in this section, shall
16 not be permitted to purchase or acquire a controlling interest in the owner-
17 ship of a single-family home property in Idaho.
18 (2) For the purpose of this section:
19 (a) "Home builder" means an entity whose primary business is the con-
20 struction of new single-family homes that are offered for sale to indi-
21 vidual buyers and are not for long-term rental purposes.
22 (b) "Institutional investor" means any corporation, limited liabil-
23 ity company, limited partnership, partnership, or other corporate
24 structure with more than one (1) member that purchases or otherwise
25 acquires title or ownership to single-family dwellings for the purpose
26 of profit. "Institutional investor" does not include the following,
27 regardless of how they hold real property: a home builder, remodeler,
28 natural person, marital community, family trust or estate, nonprofit,
29 community land trust, tribal housing, or a foreclosure or deed-in-lieu
30 acquisition by a lender or servicer with a mandatory disposition win-
31 dow.
32 (c) "Single-family home" means a residential structure that is in-
33 tended for occupancy by one (1) family, such as a detached house.
34 (3) An institutional investor that acquires, directly or indirectly,
35 control of a single-family home property on or after July 1, 2026, by de-
36 vise, by descent, through the enforcement of security interests, or through
37 the collection of debts, must sell, transfer, or otherwise divest itself of
38 such property within one hundred eighty (180) days of acquiring control of
39 the property.
40 (4) If an institutional investor does not divest the single-family home
41 property as required by this section, the attorney general shall commence an
42 action in the district court within the jurisdiction of the property.

1 (5) If the property is held in violation of this section, the district
2 court shall order that the property be sold through judicial foreclosure.
3 Proceeds of the sale shall be disbursed to lienholders, in the order of pri-
4 ority, except for liens that, under the terms of the sale, are to remain on
5 the property.
6 (6) The responsibility for determining whether an institutional in-
7 vestor is subject to the provisions of this section rests solely with the
8 institutional investor and the attorney general. An individual or entity
9 who is not an institutional investor shall not be required to determine or
10 inquire whether another person or entity is or may be subject to the provi-
11 sions of this section and shall bear no civil or criminal liability under
12 this section.
13 SECTION 2. That Section 55-103, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
14 amended to read as follows:
15 55-103. WHO MAY OWN PROPERTY. (1) Except as provided in subsection (2)
16 or (3) of this section or as otherwise provided in this chapter, any person,
17 whether citizen or alien, may take, hold, and dispose of property, real or
18 personal.
19 (2) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, on and af-
20 ter the effective date of this subsection, a foreign government or a foreign
21 state-controlled enterprise shall not purchase, acquire, or hold any con-
22 trolling interest in agricultural land, forest land, water rights, mining
23 claims, or mineral rights in the state of Idaho. This subsection does not
24 apply to any interest in agricultural land, forest land, water rights, min-
25 ing claims, or mineral rights acquired by a foreign government or foreign
26 state-controlled enterprise prior to the effective date of this subsection
27 or to any foreign pension fund.
28 (3) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, a for-
29 eign principal from a foreign adversary shall not purchase, acquire, or hold
30 any controlling interest, except a de minimis indirect interest, in agricul-
31 tural land, water rights, mining claims, or mineral rights in the state of
32 Idaho. A foreign principal has a de minimis indirect interest if any owner-
33 ship is the result of the foreign principal's ownership of registered equi-
34 ties in a publicly traded company owning the land and if the foreign princi-
35 pal's ownership in the company is either:
36 (a) Less than five percent (5%) of any class of registered equities or
37 less than five percent (5%) in the aggregate in multiple classes of reg-
38 istered equities; or
39 (b) A noncontrolling interest in an entity controlled by a company that
40 is both registered with the United States securities and exchange com-
41 mission as an investment advisor under the investment advisers act of
42 1940, as amended, and is not a foreign entity.
43 (4) A foreign principal that directly or indirectly owns or otherwise
44 controls agricultural land, water rights, mining claims, or mineral rights
45 in the state of Idaho must sell, transfer, or otherwise divest itself of
46 the agricultural land, mining claims, or mineral rights within one hundred
47 eighty (180) days of the effective date of this act.
48 (5) A foreign principal that directly or indirectly owns or acquires
49 agricultural land, water rights, or any interest in such land in this state

1 must register with the Idaho state department of agriculture within sixty
2 (60) days of the effective date of this act or the date of acquisition,
3 whichever is latest. The department must establish a form for such registra-
4 tion, which, at a minimum, must include all of the following:
5 (a) The name of the owner of the agricultural land, water rights, or the
6 owner of the interest in such land;
7 (b) The address of the agricultural land, the property appraiser's par-

17 include the following:
18 (a) The mineral lease number with lease length and premises by parcel
19 and acres; and
20 (b) The application for use of state land by the individual or legal en-
21 tity.
22 (7) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (3) of this section, a
23 foreign principal from a foreign adversary country may acquire agricultural
24 land, mining claims, or mineral rights on or after July 1, 2025, by devise
25 or descent, through the enforcement of security interests, or through the
26 collection of debts, provided that the foreign principal sells, transfers,
27 or otherwise divests itself of the agricultural land, water rights, mining
28 claims, or mineral rights within one hundred eighty (180) days of acquiring
29 the agricultural land, mining claims, or mineral rights.
30 (8) The provisions of subsections (3) through (7) of this section shall
31 not apply to an entity that has a national security agreement with the com-
32 mittee on foreign investment in the United States as of July 1, 2025, and
33 maintains such national security agreement.
34 (9) The Idaho state department of agriculture and department of lands
35 shall adopt rules, subject to legislative approval, to implement the provi-
36 sions of this section.
37 (10) The responsibility for determining whether an entity is subject to
38 this section rests solely with the foreign entity, the attorney general, or
39 any qualifying whistleblower, and no other individual or entity. An indi-
40 vidual or entity who is not a foreign entity shall not be required to deter-
41 mine or inquire whether another person or entity is or may be subject to the
42 provisions of this section and shall bear no civil or criminal liability un-
43 der this section.
44 (11) If a foreign principal from a foreign adversary country does not
45 divest the public or private lands, mining claims, or mineral rights as re-
46 quired by this section, the attorney general shall commence an action in the
47 district court within the jurisdiction of the public or private land, mining
48 claims, or mineral rights.

26 (17) As used in this section:
27 (a) "Agricultural land" shall have the same meaning as "land actively
28 devoted to agricultural purposes" as provided in section 63-604, Idaho
29 Code.
30 (b) "Controlling interest" means:
31 (i) Possession of more than fifty percent (50%) of the ownership
32 interest in an entity; or
33 (ii) A percentage of ownership interest in an entity that is fifty
34 percent (50%) or less if a foreign government actually directs the
35 business and affairs of the entity without the requirement or con-
36 sent of any other party.
37 (c) "Foreign adversary" means any foreign government or foreign non-
38 government person engaged in a long-term pattern or serious instances
39 of conduct significantly adverse to the national security of the United
40 States or security and safety of United States persons as determined un-
41 der 15 CFR 791.4, as it existed on January 1, 2025.
42 (d) "Foreign government" means a government other than the federal gov-
43 ernment of the United States or the government of any state, political
44 subdivision of a state, territory, federally recognized Indian tribe,
45 or possession of the United States.
46 (e) "Forest land" means privately owned or state-owned land being held
47 and used primarily for the continuous purpose of growing and harvesting
48 trees of a marketable species. Having met the above criteria, forest
49 land includes:

5
1 (i) Forest land is land evidenced by present use and silvicul-
2 tural treatment.
3 (ii) Forest land is land that has a dedicated use that is further
4 evidenced by a forest land management plan that includes eventual
5 harvest of the forest crop.
6 (iii) Forest land is land bearing forest growth or land that has
7 not been converted to another use.
8 (iv) Forest land is land that has had the trees removed by man
9 through harvest, including clear-cuts or by natural disaster,
10 such as but not limited to fire, and which within five (5) years af-
11 ter harvest or initial assessment will be reforested as specified
12 in the forest practices act, chapter 13, title 38, Idaho Code.
13 (f) "Foreign pension fund" means a trust, corporation, or other entity
14 created or organized under the law of a country other than the United
15 States to provide retirement or pension fund benefits. However, the
16 term "foreign pension fund" shall not include any trust, corporation,
17 or other entity that is owned by or subject to a controlling interest of
18 a sovereign wealth fund.
19 (g) "Foreign principal" means:
20 (i) The government or any official of the government of a foreign
21 adversary;
22 (ii) A political party or member of a political party or any subdi-
23 vision of a political party of a foreign adversary;
24 (iii) A partnership, association, corporation, organization, or
25 other combination of persons organized under the laws of or having
26 its principal place of business in a foreign adversary, or a sub-
27 sidiary of such entity, owned or controlled wholly or in part by
28 any person, entity, or collection of persons or entities of a for-
29 eign adversary;
30 (iv) Any person who is domiciled in a foreign adversary and is not
31 a citizen or lawful permanent resident of the United States; and
32 (v) Any person, entity, or collection of persons or entities de-
33 scribed in this paragraph having a controlling interest in a part-
34 nership, association, corporation, organization, trust, or any
35 other legal entity or subsidiary formed for the purpose of owning
36 real property.
37 (h) "Mineral right" shall have the same meaning as provided in section
38 47-701, Idaho Code.
39 (i) "Mining claim" means a portion of land containing minerals that a
40 miner has a right to occupy and possess for the purpose of extracting
41 minerals.
42 (j) "State-controlled enterprise" means a business enterprise, how-
43 ever denominated, sovereign wealth fund, or state-backed investment
44 fund in which a foreign government has a controlling interest.
45 (k) "Water right" shall have the same meaning as provided in section
46 42-230, Idaho Code.
