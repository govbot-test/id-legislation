15 44-301. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
16 (1) "Alien" means any person who is not a citizen or national of the
17 United States as described in 8 U.S.C. 1101 et seq.
18 (2) "Director" means the director of the Idaho department of labor or
19 the director's designated agent.
20 (3) "E-verify" or "e-verify program" means the electronic verifica-
21 tion of federal employment authorization program of the illegal immigration
22 reform and immigrant responsibility act, 8 U.S.C. 1324a, operated by the
23 United States department of homeland security.
24 (4) "Employee" means any person directed, allowed, or permitted to per-
25 form labor or service of any kind by an employer. For the purposes of this
26 chapter, the employees of an independent contractor working for a business
27 entity shall not be regarded as the employees of the business entity.
28 (5) "Employer" means any individual, person, corporation, depart-
29 ment, board, bureau, agency, commission, division, office, company, firm,
30 partnership, council, or committee of the state government, public benefit
31 corporation, public authority, political subdivision of the state, or other
32 business entity that employs or seeks to employ an individual or individuals
33 and that registers with the secretary of state.
34 (6) "Federal work authorization program" means any of the electronic
35 verification of work authorization programs operated by the United States
36 department of homeland security or an equivalent federal work authorization
37 program operated by the United States department of homeland security to
38 verify information of newly hired employees under 8 U.S.C. 1324a.
39 (7) "Knowingly" means, with respect to conduct or to a circumstance de-
40 scribed by a statute defining an offense, that a person is aware by docu-
41 mentation or action that the person's conduct is of that nature or that the

33 44-302. UNAUTHORIZED WORKERS OR ALIENS -- EMPLOYMENT PROHIBITED. (1)
34 It is unlawful for any employer to knowingly employ, hire, recruit, or re-
35 fer, either for the employer itself or on behalf of another, for private or
36 public employment within the state an unauthorized worker who is not duly au-
37 thorized to be employed by law. No business entity, employer, or public em-
38 ployer may knowingly employ, hire for employment, or continue to employ an
39 unauthorized alien to perform work within the state of Idaho. "Knowingly
40 employ, hire for employment, or continue to employ an unauthorized alien"
41 means the actions described in 8 U.S.C. 1324a.
42 (2) Employers are required to verify an employee's legal status and au-
43 thorization to work after employing the individual within the first three
44 (3) days after the employee's first day of work for pay has elapsed or by no
45 later than the first day of work for pay if the employee will work for fewer
46 than three (3) days.
47 (3) For the purpose of this chapter, proof of legal status or authoriza-
48 tion to work includes but is not limited to a valid social security card, a
49 valid immigration or nonimmigration visa with photo identification, a valid

3
1 birth certificate, a valid passport, a valid photo identification card is-
2 sued by a government agency, a valid work permit or supervision permit autho-
3 rized by the Idaho department of labor, a valid permit issued by the United
4 States department of justice, or another valid document providing evidence
5 of legal residence or authorization to work in the United States.
6 (4) For the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this chapter and
7 notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the director may
8 access information maintained by any state agency, including but not limited
9 to the Idaho department of labor and the Idaho state department of trans-
10 portation, for the limited purpose of confirming the validity of a worker's
11 legal status or authorization to work. The director shall promulgate rules,
12 subject to legislative approval, in accordance with chapter 52, title 67,
13 Idaho Code, to safeguard against the release of any confidential or identi-
14 fying information that is not necessary for the limited purpose of enforcing
15 the provisions of this chapter.
16 (5) On and after July 1, 2026, every business entity and employer in
17 this state shall enroll in the e-verify program and shall thereafter, ac-
18 cording to the federal statutes and regulations governing e-verify, verify
19 the employment eligibility of new hires through e-verify. A business en-
20 tity or employer that uses e-verify to verify the work authorization of an
21 employee shall not be considered to have violated this section with respect
22 to the employment of that employee.
23 (6) As a condition for the award of any contract, grant, or incentive
24 by this state, any political subdivision of the state, or any state-funded
25 entity to a business entity or employer that employs one (1) or more employ-
26 ees, the business entity or employer shall provide documentation establish-
27 ing that the business entity or employer is enrolled in the e-verify program.
28 A subcontractor shall enroll in the e-verify program prior to performing any
29 work on the project and shall verify every employee that is required to be
30 verified according to the applicable federal rules and regulations. During
31 the performance of the contract, the business entity or employer shall con-
32 tinue to participate in the e-verify program and shall verify every employee
33 that is required to be verified according to the applicable federal rules
34 and regulations. No subcontractor on a project paid for by contract, grant,
35 or incentive by this state, any political subdivision of the state, or any
36 state-funded entity may knowingly employ, hire for employment, or continue
37 to employ an unauthorized alien.
38 (7) An employer may not avoid liability under this section by contract-
39 ing, subcontracting, or otherwise arranging for labor to be performed if the
40 employer knows or reasonably should know that unauthorized workers are being
41 used to perform work that benefits such employer.
42 (8) Compliance with this section may be verified by state authorities
43 or law enforcement at any time to ensure a contractual agreement as provided
44 for in this section is being met.

16 44-305. DEFENSES. (1) This chapter may not be construed to deny any
17 procedural mechanisms or legal defenses included in the e-verify program or
18 any other federal work authorization program. A business entity, employer,
19 subcontractor, or other person that establishes that it has complied in good
20 faith with section 44-302(5), Idaho Code, establishes an affirmative de-
21 fense that the business entity, employer, or subcontractor did not knowingly
22 hire or employ an unauthorized alien.
23 (2) It is an affirmative defense to a violation of section 44-302(1),
24 Idaho Code, that a business entity or employer was entrapped. To claim en-
25 trapment, the business entity or employer must admit by testimony or other
26 evidence the substantial elements of the violation. A business entity or em-
27 ployer who asserts an entrapment defense has the burden of proving by clear
28 and convincing evidence the following:
29 (a) The idea of committing the violation started with law enforcement
30 officers or their agents rather than with the business entity or em-
31 ployer;
32 (b) The law enforcement officers or their agents urged and induced the
33 business entity or employer to commit the violation; and
34 (c) The business entity or employer was not already predisposed to com-
35 mit the violation before the law enforcement officers or their agents
36 urged and induced the employer to commit the violation.
37 44-306. EXCLUSIONS FROM LIABILITY. Any business entity or employer
38 that terminates the employment of an employee to comply with this chapter
39 shall not be liable for any claims made against the business entity or em-
40 ployer by the terminated employee if such termination is made without regard
41 to the race, ethnicity, or national origin of the employee.
42 44-307. INTERPRETATION. The provisions of this chapter shall be in-
43 terpreted consistently with 8 U.S.C. 1324a and any applicable federal rules
44 and regulations.
