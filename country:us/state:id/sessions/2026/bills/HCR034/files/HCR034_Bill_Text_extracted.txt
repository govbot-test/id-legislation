LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 34
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND REAFFIRMING IDAHO'S RIGHTS PURSUANT
3 TO THE BEAR RIVER COMPACT.
4 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
5 WHEREAS, the Bear River is a vital lifeline for the agricultural, munic-
6 ipal, and recreational economy of southeastern Idaho, specifically sustain-
7 ing Idaho's Bear Lake, Franklin, Caribou, and Oneida counties; and
8 WHEREAS, the distribution of the waters of the Bear River is governed
9 by the Amended Bear River Compact of 1980 (Compact), a binding interstate
10 agreement between the states of Idaho, Utah, and Wyoming that is ratified by
11 the United States Congress; and
12 WHEREAS, the Compact divides the Bear River into three segments, the Up-
13 per Division, Central Division, and Lower Division; and
14 WHEREAS, the Lower Division begins at Stewart Dam, north of Bear Lake in
15 Idaho, and terminates at the Great Salt Lake, in Utah; and
16 WHEREAS, under the Compact's specific provisions governing the Lower
17 Division, the water in the Bear River is allocated as follows (compact allo-
18 cation waters):
19 (1) Idaho holds a statutorily defined "first right" to develop, de-
20 plete, and beneficially use the first 125,000 acre-feet of water;
21 (2) Utah holds a subsequent "second right" to develop up to 275,000
22 acre-feet of water, only after Idaho's specific allocation has been satis-
23 fied; and
24 (3) Both Idaho and Utah hold an equal right to divert up to 75,000 acre-
25 feet of any remaining water in the Bear River's Lower Division; and
26 WHEREAS, based on these allocations, Idaho has a competitive imperative
27 to develop and actively manage its allocation of water to prevent subordina-
28 tion or loss; and
29 WHEREAS, the preservation of Bear Lake is critical to Idaho, not only
30 for its immense recreational value but also as a storage reservoir subject to
31 the irrigation reserve, which acts as a safeguard for Idaho irrigators dur-
32 ing periods of drought; and
33 WHEREAS, growing pressures, including demands from downstream users
34 and potential federal encroachment, threaten to strand Idaho's undeveloped
35 compact allocation waters unless concrete steps to develop infrastructure
36 are taken; and
37 WHEREAS, prior appropriation, combined with the pressures of regional
38 population growth, dictates that Idaho must move from passive entitlement to
39 active development of its compact allocation waters.
40 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
41 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
42 the Senate concurring therein:

2
1 (1) That the Legislature unequivocally reaffirms Idaho's entitlement
2 to the first 125,000 acre-feet of water in the Lower Division as guaranteed
3 by the Amended Bear River Compact and declares it the policy of this state
4 to vigorously oppose any federal or interstate attempt to reallocate, dimin-
5 ish, or encumber this water for downstream purposes in Utah;
6 (2) That the State of Idaho shall invest in state-of-the-art telemetry,
7 stream gauging, and diversion metering in the Lower Division as this data
8 collection is essential to scientifically quantify Idaho's current develop-
9 ment and to substantiate future development;
10 (3) That Idaho's commissioners to the Bear River Commission are di-
11 rected to strictly interpret and enforce the irrigation reserve water
12 elevation statutes for Bear Lake;
13 (4) That while the State of Idaho supports collaborative efforts to
14 control invasive species and improve water quality, such cooperation shall
15 not be construed as a waiver of Idaho's right to develop its full compact
16 allocation;
17 (5) That the Idaho Water Resource Board is directed to prepare two re-
18 ports as follows:
19 (a) The first report, shall be provided to House and Senate leadership
20 no later than June 1, 2026, and shall include the following:
21 (i) A description of the current development of the compact allo-
22 cation waters for Idaho and Utah as of the date of this resolution;
23 (ii) The frequency and timing of water supplies in the Lower Divi-
24 sion that are available for further development; and
25 (iii) Preliminary opportunities for further development of
26 Idaho's compact allocation to address Idaho's statewide water
27 obligations, including estimated costs and timelines for com-
28 pleting projects related to the opportunities identified in the
29 report.
30 (b) The second report shall be completed no later than September 30,
31 2026, presented to the Natural Resources Interim Committee, and include
32 the following:
33 (i) Recommended and prioritized projects for further development
34 of Idaho's compact allocation; and
35 (ii) An outline of the necessary steps for the Idaho Water Re-
36 source Board, the Idaho Department of Water Resources, and the
37 Idaho Legislature to secure future development of Idaho's compact
38 allocation waters.
39 (6) That the Idaho Water Resource Board is hereby directed to prior-
40 itize and expedite feasibility studies on projects identified in the Bear
41 River Basin reports, including but not limited to new surface water storage
42 sites, off-stream storage facilities, and aquifer recharge projects, to
43 protect and develop the state's allocation under the Bear River Compact,
44 without harming the natural hydrology of Bear Lake.
45 (7) That the Legislature directs the Idaho Water Resource Board to al-
46 locate funds from the $30,000,000 fiscal year 2027 transfer to the water man-
47 agement account to support implementation of these reports and any legisla-
48 tion pursuant to the reports' findings.
49 (8) That the Legislature directs the Idaho Water Resource Board to
50 confer with the leadership of the Idaho House of Representatives and Senate

3
1 prior to entering into any agreement regarding the use or management of the
2 Bear River or Bear Lake with any power utility or the states of Wyoming or
3 Utah.
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
5 tives is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this Resolution
6 to the Governor of the State of Idaho, the Director of the Idaho Department of
7 Water Resources, the Idaho Water Resource Board, the Idaho delegation to the
8 Bear River Commission, and the Governor of the State of Utah.
