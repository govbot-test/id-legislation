8 SECTION 1. That Section 74-115, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 74-115. PROCEEDINGS TO ENFORCE RIGHT TO EXAMINE OR TO RECEIVE A COPY OF
11 RECORDS -- RETENTION OF DISPUTED RECORDS. (1) A person aggrieved by the de-
12 nial or partial denial of a request for disclosure, or the charge for dis-
13 closure, may file a complaint in the magistrate court of the county where
14 the records of some part thereof are located. The complaint contesting the
15 public agency's response to the records request shall be filed within ninety
16 (90) calendar days from the date of the response. The complaint shall in-
17 clude the full text of the original request, the full text of the response,
18 all communications between the requester and agency related to the request
19 and response, and the statutory reason the filing party is contesting the re-
20 sponse. The filing party shall pay a one-hundred-dollar ($100) filing fee,
21 which shall be distributed to the county district court fund and may not be
22 waived. The district court clerk shall assign a case number. The filing
23 party shall serve the public agency. Within fourteen (14) days of receiv-
24 ing the complaint, the public agency in question shall file a response with
25 the court that includes the statutory reason for the response to the original
26 records request. Parties may attach affidavits to their respective plead-
27 ings. Neither party may use discovery for the initial complaint, nor shall
28 they summon witnesses. Attorneys are not required to file or respond to the
29 complaint. Oral arguments are not permitted. Upon receipt of the agency's
30 response, the judge shall review the filings and may request an in-camera re-
31 view of the documents in question. The judge shall issue a decision within
32 fourteen (14) days after receiving the agency's response. If the parties
33 reach an agreement before the judge issues an order, the court shall dismiss
34 the complaint. Upon issuance of the decision, either party may initiate an
35 appeal in the district court.
36 (1) (2) The sole remedy for Regardless of whether a complaint, as pro-
37 vided in subsection (1) of this section, has occurred, a person aggrieved
38 by the denial of a request for disclosure is to may institute proceedings in
39 the district court of the county where the records or some part thereof are
40 located, to compel the public agency to make the information available for
41 public inspection in accordance with the provisions of this chapter. The

27 74-114. ACCESS TO AIR QUALITY, WATER QUALITY AND HAZARDOUS WASTE
28 RECORDS -- PROTECTION OF TRADE SECRETS. (1) To the extent required by the
29 federal clean air act, the federal clean water act and the resource conser-
30 vation and recovery act for state primacy over any delegated or authorized
31 programs, even if the record is otherwise exempt from disclosure under this
32 chapter, any person may inspect and copy:
33 (a) Air pollution emission data;
34 (b) The content of any title V operating permit;
35 (c) The name and address of any Idaho pollutant discharge elimination
36 system (IPDES) applicant or permittee;
37 (d) The content of any IPDES permit;
38 (e) IPDES permit applications, and information required to be submit-
39 ted by IPDES application forms, whether the information is submitted on
40 the application forms themselves or in any attachments used to supply
41 information required by the application forms;
42 (f) Effluent data or a standard or limitation, as defined in 40 CFR
43 2.302;
44 (g) The name and address of any applicant or permittee for a hazardous
45 waste treatment, storage, or disposal facility permit pursuant to chap-
46 ter 44, title 39, Idaho Code; and
47 (h) Any other record required to be provided to or obtained by the de-
48 partment of environmental quality pursuant to the federal clean air
49 act, the federal clean water act and the resource conservation and re-

15 (a) To any officer, employee, or authorized representative of the state

19 (b) As determined necessary by the director of the department of en-

27 (5) It shall be the responsibility of any person providing a record to
28 the department of environmental quality to give notice of the existence of a
29 trade secret on each page or other portion of information at the time of sub-
30 mittal, and such person shall have the burden of demonstrating that the in-

32 (6) Notwithstanding the time frames set forth in section 74-103(2),
33 Idaho Code, when a request is made to the department of environmental quality

38 receipt of the request for the disclosure of the information, the department

40 to the person making the confidentiality claim. A response shall be submit-

42 trade secret protection within ten (10) working days after receipt of the
43 request for substantiation, or the information subject to the claim shall be
44 disclosed without further notice. Upon receipt of a timely response to the
45 request for substantiation, the director of the department of environmental
46 quality shall determine whether the information is a trade secret subject to
47 protection.
48 (a) If it is determined that the information, or any portion of the in-

29 (9) As it relates to the department of environmental quality, or to
