LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 571
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO DISTURBING THE PEACE; AMENDING SECTION 18-6409, IDAHO CODE, TO
3 PROVIDE FOR DISTURBING THE PEACE OF RELIGIOUS WORSHIP, MEETINGS, OR
4 EVENTS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 18-6409, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 18-6409. DISTURBING THE PEACE. (1) Every person who maliciously and
9 willfully disturbs the peace or quiet of any neighborhood, family or per-
10 son, by loud or unusual noise, or by tumultuous or offensive conduct, or by
11 threatening, traducing, quarreling, challenging to fight or fighting, or
12 fires any gun or pistol, or uses any vulgar, profane or indecent language
13 within the presence or hearing of children, in a loud and boisterous manner,
14 is guilty of a misdemeanor.
15 (2) Every person who maliciously and willfully disturbs the dignity or
16 reverential nature of any funeral, memorial service, funeral procession,
17 burial ceremony or viewing of a deceased person is guilty of a misdemeanor.
18 (3) Every person who maliciously and willfully disturbs, disquiets, or
19 delays any assemblage of people gathered for religious worship, meetings, or
20 events, whether at a place of worship or an off-site location used for reli-
21 gious worship, meetings, or events, by profane discourse, rude or indecent
22 behavior, any unnecessary noise, or any action that obstructs or interferes
23 with the timely commencement or continuation of the gathering, either within
24 the place where the worship, meeting, or event is held or so near it as to dis-
25 turb the order and solemnity of the gathering, is guilty of a misdemeanor.
26 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
27 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
28 July 1, 2026.
