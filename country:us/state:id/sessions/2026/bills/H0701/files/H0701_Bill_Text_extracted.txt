LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 701
BY BUSINESS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO RENTAL APPLICATIONS; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; AMENDING
3 CHAPTER 3, TITLE 55, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 55-316,
4 IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR CERTAIN LIMITATIONS ON RENTAL APPLICATION
5 FEES; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent of the Legislature to
8 provide certain limitations on the charging of rental application fees to
9 reduce the number of rental applicants who are charged such fees for rental
10 properties for which they are not considered.
11 SECTION 2. That Chapter 3, Title 55, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
12 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
13 ignated as Section 55-316, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
14 55-316. LIMITATION ON FEES FOR RENTAL APPLICATIONS. (1) A property
15 owner or property manager shall not charge rental application fees to more
16 than two (2) rental applicant households per rental unit concurrently and
17 may charge such fees only if the property owner or property manager:
18 (a) Has a rental unit currently available for rent, has a rental unit
19 that will become available by a specified future date, or does not have
20 a rental property available but the rental applicant acknowledges in
21 writing that no rental property is available and consents to being
22 placed on a waiting list for future available rental units;
23 (b) Discloses to the prospective tenant, prior to accepting the rental
24 application, any information regarding the rental applicant screening
25 process that the property owner or property manager will evaluate as a
26 condition of accepting the rental applicant as a tenant in the residen-
27 tial unit; and
28 (c) Completes the screening process as disclosed pursuant to paragraph
29 (b) of this subsection.
30 (2) Rental application fees may only be charged to each rental appli-
31 cant within a rental applicant household who requires a criminal history and
32 background check.
33 (3) If the rental application of the first of the two (2) rental ap-
34 plicant households pursuant to subsection (1) of this section is denied, a
35 property owner or property manager may charge a rental application fee to the
36 next rental applicant household and may charge a rental application fee to
37 each individual rental applicant of such household who requires a criminal
38 history and background check, as long as no more than two (2) rental appli-
39 cant households are being charged concurrently at any given time.

2
1 (4) All rental application fees shall be related to the average costs of
2 the screening as disclosed pursuant to subsection (1)(b) of this section and
3 any other costs incurred from processing the rental application.
4 (5) This section shall not apply where a property owner or property man-
5 ager does not charge or receive rental application fees.
6 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
7 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
8 July 1, 2026.
