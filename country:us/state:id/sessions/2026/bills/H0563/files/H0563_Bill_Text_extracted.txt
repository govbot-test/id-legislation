35 SECTION 6. That Chapter 2, Title 54, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
36 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
37 ignated as Section 54-227, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
38 54-227. PRACTICE PRIVILEGES. (1) An individual whose principal place
39 of business is not in the state of Idaho and who holds a valid active license
40 in good standing as a certified public accountant from any other state shall
41 have all the practice privileges of licensees of this state without the need
42 to obtain a license under this chapter, provided that such individual meets
43 the minimum educational and experience requirements set forth in sections
44 54-207 and 54-209, Idaho Code.
45 (2) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, an individual identi-
46 fied in subsection (1) of this section who offers or renders professional

6
1 services, whether in person, by mail, telephone, or electronic means, or in
2 any other way, by this section is granted practice privileges in this state,
3 and no notice or other submission shall be required of any such individual.
4 (3) An individual licensed in another state who exercises the practice
5 privilege afforded under this section and the firm that employs that li-
6 censee are deemed to simultaneously consent, as a condition of the grant of
7 this privilege:
8 (a) To the personal and subject matter jurisdiction and disciplinary
9 authority of the board;
10 (b) To comply with the provisions of this chapter and applicable board
11 rules;
12 (c) That in the event the license from the state of the individual's
13 principal place of business is no longer valid, the individual will
14 cease offering or rendering professional services in this state indi-
15 vidually and on behalf of a firm; and
16 (d) To the appointment of the state board that issued the individual's
17 license as the agent upon whom process may be served in any action or
18 proceeding by the Idaho board against the licensee.
19 (4) An individual whose principal place of business is not in this state
20 and who, as of December 31, 2024, held and continues to hold a valid active
21 license as a certified public accountant from any state and has practice
22 privileges in this state pursuant to section 54-227, Idaho Code, as that
23 section existed on December 31, 2024, shall continue to have all the privi-
24 leges of a licensee in this state without the need to obtain a license under
25 sections 54-207 and 54-209, Idaho Code.
