23 15-5-101. SHORT TITLE. This chapter shall be known and may be cited as
24 the "Uniform Guardianship, Conservatorship, and Other Protective Arrange-
25 ments Act."
26 15-5-102. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
27 (1) "Adult" means an individual at least eighteen (18) years of age or
28 an emancipated individual under eighteen (18) years of age.
29 (2) "Adult subject to conservatorship" means an adult for whom a con-
30 servator has been appointed under this chapter.
31 (3) "Adult subject to guardianship" means an adult for whom a guardian
32 has been appointed under this chapter.
33 (4) "Advance care planning document," "advance directive," "direc-
34 tive," or "health care directive" means a document as defined in section
35 39-4502(1), Idaho Code.
36 (5) "Artificial life-sustaining procedures" means any medical proce-
37 dure or intervention that utilizes mechanical means to sustain or supplant
38 a vital function. Artificial life-sustaining procedures shall not include
39 the administration of medication, and it shall not include the performance
40 of any medical procedure deemed necessary to alleviate pain or any procedure
41 that could be expected to result in the recovery or long-term survival of the
42 patient and his restoration to consciousness.

6 15-5-104. SUBJECT MATTER JURISDICTION. (1) The district court has
7 jurisdiction over a conservatorship or protective arrangement instead of
8 a conservatorship for a minor domiciled or having property in this state.
9 Except to the extent jurisdiction is precluded by the uniform child custody
10 jurisdiction and enforcement act, chapter 11, title 32, Idaho Code, or the
11 Idaho child protective act, chapter 16, title 16, Idaho Code, the district
12 court has jurisdiction over a guardianship for a minor domiciled or present
13 in this state. Where a minor is within the jurisdiction of a court under the
14 child protective act or where a guardianship proceeding arose in connec-
15 tion with a permanency plan for a minor who was the subject of a proceeding
16 under the child protective act, the court having jurisdiction over the pro-
17 ceeding under the child protective act shall have exclusive jurisdiction
18 and venue over any guardianship proceeding involving such minor unless, in
19 furtherance of the permanency plan, the court declines to exercise such ju-
20 risdiction and venue, notwithstanding the provisions of this subsection.
21 (a) In any action connected to a guardianship pursuant to this subsec-
22 tion, in addition to notice or service upon interested parties, as pro-
23 vided in section 15-5-203, Idaho Code, notice of the following shall
24 be served upon the department of health and welfare in the manner pre-
25 scribed in Idaho supreme court rules:
26 (i) Any petition for the appointment of a guardian of a minor;
27 (ii) Any pleading filed in connection with such guardianship;
28 (iii) Any proceeding of any nature in such guardianship; and
29 (iv) The time and place of any hearing in connection with such
30 guardianship.
31 (b) In any action occurring pursuant to the provisions of this subsec-
32 tion, the department of health and welfare shall have the right to ap-
33 pear and be heard at any hearing and shall have the right to intervene at
34 any stage of the action.
35 (c) A guardian appointed in an action occurring pursuant to this sub-
36 section may not consent to the adoption of the minor without providing
37 prior notice of the action of adoption to the department of health and
38 welfare.
39 (2) The district court has jurisdiction over a guardianship, conserva-
40 torship, or protective arrangement under this chapter for an adult as pro-
41 vided in the uniform adult guardianship and protective proceedings juris-
42 diction act, chapter 13, title 15, Idaho Code.
43 (3) After notice is given in a proceeding for a guardianship, conserva-
44 torship, or protective arrangement under this chapter and until termination
45 of the proceeding, the court in which the petition is filed has:
46 (a) Exclusive jurisdiction to determine the need for the guardianship,
47 conservatorship, or protective arrangement;

1 (b) Exclusive jurisdiction to determine how property of the respondent

5 (c) Nonexclusive jurisdiction to determine the validity of a claim
6 against the respondent or property of the respondent or a question of
7 title concerning the property; and
8 (d) If a guardian or conservator is appointed, exclusive jurisdiction
9 over issues related to administration of the guardianship or conserva-

14 appointment or protective arrangement expires by its terms.

16 15-5-106. VENUE. (1) Venue for a guardianship proceeding for a minor
17 shall be in:
18 (a) The county in which the minor resides or is present at the time the
19 proceeding commences; or
20 (b) The county in which another proceeding concerning the custody or
21 parental rights of the minor is pending.
22 (2) Venue for a guardianship proceeding or protective arrangement in-
23 stead of guardianship for an adult shall be in:
24 (a) The county in which the respondent resides;
25 (b) If the respondent has been admitted to an institution by court or-
26 der, the county in which the court is located; or
27 (c) If the proceeding is for appointment of an emergency guardian for
28 the respondent, the county in which the respondent is present.
29 (3) Venue for a conservatorship proceeding or protective arrangement
30 instead of conservatorship shall be in:
31 (a) The county in which the respondent resides, whether or not a
32 guardian has been appointed in another county or other jurisdiction; or
33 (b) If the respondent does not reside in this state, in any county in
34 which property of the respondent is located.
35 (4) If proceedings under this chapter are brought in more than one (1)
36 county, the court of the county in which the first proceeding is brought has
37 the exclusive right to proceed unless the court determines venue is properly
38 in another court or the interest of justice otherwise requires transfer of
39 the proceeding.

18 15-5-110. CO-GUARDIAN -- CO-CONSERVATOR. (1) The court at any time may
19 appoint a co-guardian or co-conservator.
20 (2) A co-guardian or co-conservator appointed to serve may act when
21 that co-guardian or co-conservator complies with the provisions of section
22 15-5-108, Idaho Code.
23 (3) Unless an order of appointment under subsection (1) of this section
24 or subsequent order states otherwise, co-guardians or co-conservators shall
25 make decisions jointly.
26 15-5-111. JUDICIAL APPOINTMENT OF SUCCESSOR GUARDIAN OR SUCCESSOR
27 CONSERVATOR. (1) The court at any time may appoint a successor guardian or
28 successor conservator.
29 (2) A person entitled under section 15-5-202 or 15-5-302, Idaho Code,
30 to petition the court to appoint a guardian may petition the court to appoint
31 a successor guardian. A person entitled under section 15-5-402, Idaho Code,
32 to petition the court to appoint a conservator may petition the court to ap-
33 point a successor conservator.
34 (3) A successor guardian or successor conservator has the predeces-
35 sor's powers unless otherwise provided by the court.
36 15-5-112. EFFECT OF DEATH, REMOVAL, OR RESIGNATION OF GUARDIAN OR
37 CONSERVATOR. (1) Appointment of a guardian or conservator terminates on
38 the death or removal of the guardian or conservator or when the court under
39 subsection (2) of this section approves a resignation of the guardian or
40 conservator.
41 (2) A guardian or conservator shall petition the court in order to re-
42 sign. The petition may include a request that the court appoint a successor.
43 Resignation of a guardian or conservator is effective on the date the resig-
44 nation is approved by the court.
45 (3) Death, removal, or resignation of a guardian or conservator does
46 not affect liability for a previous act or the obligation to account for:

1 (a) An action taken on behalf of the individual subject to guardianship
2 or conservatorship; or

27 15-5-115. GUARDIAN AD LITEM. The court at any time may appoint a
28 guardian ad litem for a respondent or individual subject to guardianship

33 guardian ad litem may not be the same individual as the attorney representing
34 the respondent or individual subject to guardianship or conservatorship.
35 The court shall state the duties of the guardian ad litem and the reasons for
36 the appointment.

43 lationships and services for the adult subject to guardianship.

49 or support animals of the adult subject to guardianship and bring a

41 15-5-401. BASIS FOR APPOINTMENT OF CONSERVATOR. (1) Upon petition and
42 after notice and hearing, the court may appoint a conservator for the prop-
43 erty or financial affairs of a minor if the court finds by a preponderance of
44 evidence that appointment of a conservator is in the minor's best interest,
45 and:

44
1 (a) If the minor has a parent, the court gives weight to any recommenda-
2 tion of the parent whether an appointment is in the minor's best inter-
3 est; and
4 (b) Either:
5 (i) The minor owns funds or other property requiring management
6 or protection that otherwise cannot be provided;
7 (ii) The minor has or may have financial affairs that may be put at
8 unreasonable risk or hindered because of the minor's age; or
9 (iii) Appointment is necessary or desirable to obtain or provide
10 funds or other property needed for the support, care, education,
11 health, or welfare of the minor.
12 (2) Upon petition and after notice and hearing, the court may appoint
13 a conservator for the property or financial affairs of a respondent if the
14 court finds by clear and convincing evidence that:
15 (a) The respondent is unable to manage property or financial affairs
16 because:
17 (i) The respondent is limited in ability to receive and evaluate
18 information or make or communicate decisions, even with the use
19 of appropriate supportive services, technological assistance, or
20 supported decision-making; or
21 (ii) The respondent is missing, detained, or unable to return to
22 the United States;
23 (b) Appointment is necessary to:
24 (i) Avoid harm to the respondent or significant dissipation of
25 the property of the respondent; or
26 (ii) Obtain or provide funds or other property needed for the sup-
27 port, care, education, health, or welfare of the respondent or of
28 an individual entitled to the respondent's support; and
29 (c) The respondent's identified needs cannot be met by a protective ar-
30 rangement instead of conservatorship or other less restrictive alter-
31 native.
32 (3) The court shall grant a conservator only those powers necessitated
33 by demonstrated limitations and needs of the respondent and issue orders
34 that will encourage development of the respondent's maximum self-determina-
35 tion and independence. The court may not establish a full conservatorship
36 if a limited conservatorship, protective arrangement instead of conserva-
37 torship, or other less restrictive alternative would meet the needs of the
38 respondent.

4 15-5-418. DUTIES OF CONSERVATOR. (1) A conservator is a fiduciary and
5 has duties of prudence and loyalty to the individual subject to conservator-
6 ship.
7 (2) A conservator shall promote the self-determination of the individ-
8 ual subject to conservatorship and, to the extent feasible, encourage the
9 individual subject to conservatorship to participate in decisions, act on
10 the individual subject to conservatorship's own behalf, and develop or re-
11 gain the capacity to manage the individual subject to conservatorship's per-
12 sonal affairs.
13 (3) In making a decision for an individual subject to conservatorship,
14 the conservator shall make the decision the conservator reasonably believes
15 the individual subject to conservatorship would make if able, unless doing
16 so would fail to preserve the resources needed to maintain the individual
17 subject to conservatorship's well-being and lifestyle or otherwise unrea-
18 sonably harm or endanger the welfare or personal or financial interests of
19 the individual subject to conservatorship. To determine the decision the
20 individual subject to conservatorship would make if able, the conservator
21 shall consider the individual subject to conservatorship's prior or current
22 directions, preferences, opinions, values, and actions, to the extent actu-
23 ally known or reasonably ascertainable by the conservator.
24 (4) If a conservator cannot make a decision under subsection (3) of
25 this section because the conservator does not know and cannot reasonably
26 determine the decision the individual subject to conservatorship probably
27 would make if able, or the conservator reasonably believes the decision the

29 resources needed to maintain the individual subject to conservatorship's

33 of the individual subject to conservatorship. In determining the best in-
34 terest of the individual subject to conservatorship, the conservator shall
35 consider:
36 (a) Information received from professionals and persons that demon-
37 strate sufficient interest in the welfare of the individual subject to
38 conservatorship;
39 (b) Other information the conservator believes the individual subject

44 quences for others.
