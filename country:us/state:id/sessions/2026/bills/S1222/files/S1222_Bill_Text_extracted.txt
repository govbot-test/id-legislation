8 31-3805. DELIVERY OF WATER WITHIN SUBDIVISIONS. (1) The provisions of
9 this section shall apply to any proposed subdivision, as defined in chapter
10 13, title 50, Idaho Code, within the state of Idaho or to a subdivision sub-
11 ject to a more restrictive county or city zoning ordinance. A completed ap-
12 plication to develop a subdivision filed pursuant to chapter 65, title 67,
13 Idaho Code, that was filed on or before June 30, 2025, is governed by the law
14 as it existed at the time of filing.
15 (2) For any subdivision that lies within the service area or area of
16 city impact of a municipal provider, the following requirements apply:
17 (a) If a shared well or public water system is installed, it shall be
18 designed to meet requirements of that municipal provider and be planned
19 to integrate with and connect to the municipal provider's system when
20 appropriate. The requirements of this paragraph shall not apply if an
21 agreement is reached with the municipal provider;
22 (b) The municipal provider shall be consulted in the design of the
23 shared well or public water system to ensure proper integration; and
24 (c) Upon connection to the municipal provider's system, the water
25 rights associated with the subdivision's shared well or public water
26 system shall be conveyed to the municipal provider.
27 (3) If surface water for irrigation is reasonably available to all or
28 any part of a subdivision, as provided in section 67-6537, Idaho Code, then
29 no subdivision plat, amendment to a subdivision plat, or any other plat or
30 map recognized by the city or county for the division of land will be ac-
31 cepted, approved, and recorded unless the owner or entity has provided for
32 the irrigation of lots within the subdivision using the available surface
33 water.
34 (4) For subdivisions using water provided by an irrigation entity, as
35 required by section 67-6537, Idaho Code, the subdivision plat or with the
36 plat mat as an exhibit shall include a description of the system used to de-
37 liver irrigation water from the irrigation entity's point of delivery to the
38 individual lots. In such cases:
39 (a) For proposed subdivisions within the incorporated limits of a city,
40 the irrigation system must be approved by the city zoning authority or
41 the city council, as provided by city ordinance, with the advice of the
42 irrigation entity charged with the delivery of water to such lands; and
43 (b) For proposed subdivisions located outside an incorporated city,
44 the irrigation system must be approved by the board of county commis-
45 sioners, with the advice of the irrigation entity charged with the
46 delivery of water to such lands.
47 (5) For subdivisions receiving water from an irrigation entity, the
48 subdivision plat or amendment to a subdivision plat shall contain notes
49 stating the following:
