LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 11
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CON-
3 GRESS ASSEMBLED, TO THE CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE
4 STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES, TO THE PRESIDENT OF
5 THE UNITED STATES, TO THE IDAHO SUPERINTENDENT OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION,
6 AND TO THE UNITED STATES SECRETARY OF EDUCATION.
7 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
8 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
9 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
10 WHEREAS, in 1975, Congress enacted the Education for All Handicapped
11 Children Act, now known as the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act
12 (IDEA), guaranteeing that children with disabilities receive a free, appro-
13 priate public education in the least restrictive environment; and
14 WHEREAS, before the enactment of IDEA, many children with disabilities
15 were placed in restrictive settings and provided only minimal food, cloth-
16 ing, and shelter. The children's families were not afforded the opportunity
17 to take part in planning or placement decisions for their child; and
18 WHEREAS, with IDEA's passage, Congress committed to provide up to 40%
19 of the national average per-pupil expenditure in federal funding toward the
20 "excess cost" of educating children with disabilities. This formula has re-
21 mained a central feature of IDEA funding; and
22 WHEREAS, in the nearly 50 years since IDEA's passage, the federal gov-
23 ernment has never met this 40% funding commitment; and
24 WHEREAS, according to the Congressional Research Service and other
25 analyses, current federal IDEA funding covers only a fraction of the 40%
26 originally committed in the law. Federal IDEA funding in Idaho is approxi-
27 mately 12%; and
28 WHEREAS, the continual shortfall in federal IDEA funding has placed
29 a growing financial burden on taxpayers, school districts, families, and
30 state legislatures, undermining the law's promise and limiting resources
31 available for students with disabilities; and
32 WHEREAS, parents, advocates, and legislators have repeatedly advocated
33 for legislation requiring Congress to meet its long-standing commitment
34 through mandatory, regular increases in IDEA funding; and
35 WHEREAS, students with disabilities become adults within communities,
36 and improved outcomes for students with disabilities strengthen the commu-
37 nity by preparing them to contribute as members of the future workforce, en-
38 gaged citizens, and parents of future generations; and
39 WHEREAS, in recent reintroductions, the IDEA Full Funding Act has gar-
40 nered bipartisan support and broad backing from national organizations, re-
41 inforcing the urgency of fulfilling the original federal commitment; and
42 WHEREAS, fully funding IDEA is not only a moral and legal obligation but
43 also a wise financial investment because stable and adequate federal support

2
1 helps ensure high-quality special education services, improves outcomes for
2 students with disabilities, and relieves pressure on taxpayers and state and
3 local education budgets.
4 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
5 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
6 the Senate concurring therein, that the Idaho Legislature strongly urges the
7 United States Congress to pass and fully fund legislation that fulfills the
8 federal commitment to provide up to 40% of the national average per-pupil ex-
9 penditure for the excess cost of educating children with disabilities under
10 IDEA. The Legislature encourages Idaho's congressional delegation to sup-
11 port increasing mandatory, sustained federal IDEA funding so that the 40%
12 target is reached within a reasonable, defined timeline. The Legislature
13 calls on the President of the United States to endorse fully funding IDEA and
14 to propose in the President's annual budget the mandatory increases neces-
15 sary to meet the 40% commitment.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature directs the Idaho Superin-
17 tendent of Public Instruction to provide an annual overview of how federal
18 IDEA funding supports Idaho's commitment to high-quality, inclusive special
19 education services, including examples of effective practices, innovative
20 supports, and areas where increased federal investment would enhance sus-
21 tainability and long-term program strength.
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature encourages the United
23 States Department of Education to provide clear, accessible information
24 about nationwide and statewide IDEA funding levels that will help Idaho con-
25 tinue to plan proactively, celebrate progress, and advocate for Congress to
26 fulfill the long-standing federal funding commitment.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
28 tives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this
29 Memorial to the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Rep-
30 resentatives of Congress, to the congressional delegation representing the
31 State of Idaho in the Congress of the United States, to the President of the
32 United States, to the Idaho Superintendent of Public Instruction, and to the
33 United States Secretary of Education.
