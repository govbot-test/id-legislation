20 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
21 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
22 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
23 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
24 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
25 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.

25 SECTION 11. That Section 49-406, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
26 amended to read as follows:
27 49-406. IDAHO OLD TIMER -- SPECIAL LICENSE PLATE PROGRAM -- REGISTRA-
28 TION AND STANDARD LICENSE PLATES. (1) Any motor vehicle manufactured prior
29 to January 1, 1943, that is maintained to its original likeness using origi-
30 nal-type parts and materials, without major modifications shall be known as
31 an "Idaho Old Timer." Any motor vehicle which is altered from its original
32 design is not an "Idaho Old Timer" as herein defined.
33 (2) Any motor vehicle which qualifies as an "Idaho Old Timer" shall be
34 used for exhibits, parades, tours, club activities and such occasional use
35 as is necessary for operation and maintenance of the vehicle, and shall not
36 be used for business or commercial purposes or as customary and usual trans-
37 portation.
38 (3) Applicants for a special "Idaho Old Timer" license plate shall pay
39 an initial program fee of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) and the license plate
40 fee required in section 49-450, Idaho Code, for each "Idaho Old Timer" plate
41 which shall be displayed on the rear of the vehicle. The initial program fee
42 shall be deposited in the state highway account, and the plate manufacturing
43 fee shall be deposited in the plate manufacturing account.
44 (4) Once every three (3) years, on a schedule set by the department, an
45 ownership verification form shall be mailed to each plate holder on file with
46 the department. The owner shall provide such information as is requested by
47 the department to verify ownership of the vehicle(s) and that the special li-
48 cense plate(s) is still in use by the owner. A fee of three dollars ($3.00)
49 shall be charged by the department for each vehicle. This fee shall be de-

5
1 posited in the state highway account to defray costs of the license plate
2 program. If the owner no longer has an interest in a vehicle(s) the owner may
3 retain the plates as specified in subsection (7) of this section. If the own-
4 ership verification form is not returned by the date specified by the depart-
5 ment, the registration record will be purged from the files of the depart-
6 ment. Any further use of the plate is lost to the owner and the plate number
7 becomes available for issue to another applicant.
8 (4) The reissue of license plates as specified in section 49-443(2),
9 Idaho Code, shall not be required unless there is a general consensus among
10 the majority of plate holders that a new plate design is needed. Represen-
11 tatives of the plate holders shall make the request known to the department.
12 The cost of manufacturing a new design will be set by the department based
13 upon the cost of manufacturing supplies and administering the reissue. The
14 equivalent cost of each plate will be charged to each plate holder who pur-
15 chases the new plate. If a new plate design is authorized, the design and
16 color shall be approved by representatives of the interest group. The de-
17 sign, color and numbering scheme shall also be subject to approval of the de-
18 partment. The existing plate design will be canceled and all plate holders,
19 present and future shall purchase and display the new plate.
20 (5) An applicant for the special "Idaho Old Timer" plate shall execute
21 an affidavit on a form provided by the department that the vehicle qualifies
22 as an old timer and shall only be used for the purposes allowed.
23 The department shall have the authority to refuse to issue a plate and
24 may demand the return of such plate if the applicant has failed to comply with
25 the provisions of this section.
26 (6) If an "Idaho Old Timer" is to be used as customary and usual trans-
27 portation, or for business or commercial purposes, the owner shall register
28 the vehicle under the provisions of section 49-402, or section 49-434, Idaho
29 Code, as applicable, and shall obtain and display the standard issue of li-
30 cense plates after payment of the plate fee required in section 49-450, Idaho
31 Code. It shall be permissible to display both the standard issue of plates
32 and the special "Idaho Old Timer" plate.
33 (7) Whenever title or interest in an Old Timer vehicle is transferred
34 or assigned, the transferor may retain the plates for use on another vehicle
35 which qualifies by providing the information required in subsection (5) of
36 this section and by paying the required transfer fee. If the vehicle is also
37 registered under the provisions of section 49-402 or section 49-434, Idaho
38 Code, the provisions of section 49-431, Idaho Code, relating to the proce-
39 dure for assignment and transfer of interest, shall apply.
