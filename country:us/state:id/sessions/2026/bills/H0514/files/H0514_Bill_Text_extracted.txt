1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE BARBER AND COSMETOLOGY SERVICES ACT; AMENDING SECTION
3 54-5810, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH A PROVISION REGARDING EXAMINATIONS;
4 AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 54-5810, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 54-5810. QUALIFICATIONS FOR LICENSURE -- INSTRUCTORS -- APPRENTICES
9 -- STUDENTS. (1) To qualify for licensure under this chapter, an applicant
10 for licensure must:
11 (a) Be at least sixteen and one-half (16 1/2) years of age at the time of
12 application;
13 (b) Have completed at least two (2) years of high school or have at-
14 tained an equivalent education as determined by the board;
15 (c) Pass an examination for the occupation in which the applicant is
16 seeking licensure, which examination shall be conducted or approved by
17 the board; and
18 (d) Prove to the satisfaction of the board that the applicant has not
19 engaged in conduct that would constitute grounds for discipline under
20 section 54-5823, Idaho Code.
21 (2) Except as otherwise provided, and in addition to the requirements
22 listed in subsection (1) of this section, an applicant for licensure:
23 (a) As a barber, must have:
24 (i) Completed and graduated from a course of instruction of at
25 least nine hundred (900) hours in a barber school approved by the
26 board; or
27 (ii) Completed at least one thousand eight hundred (1,800) hours
28 as an apprentice in an apprenticeship that covered all aspects of
29 the practice of barbering;
30 (b) As a barber-stylist, must have:
31 (i) Completed and graduated from a course of instruction of at
32 least one thousand five hundred (1,500) hours in a barber school
33 approved by the board; or
34 (ii) Completed at least three thousand (3,000) hours as an appren-
35 tice in an apprenticeship that covered all aspects of the practice
36 of barber-styling;
37 (c) As a cosmetologist, must have:
38 (i) Completed and graduated from a course of instruction of at
39 least one thousand six hundred (1,600) hours in a cosmetology
40 school approved by the board; or

2
1 (ii) Completed at least three thousand two hundred (3,200) hours
2 as an apprentice in an apprenticeship that covered all aspects of
3 the practice of cosmetology;
4 (d) As an electrologist, must have:
5 (i) Completed and graduated from a course of instruction of at
6 least six hundred (600) hours in a school approved by the board; or
7 (ii) Completed at least one thousand two hundred (1,200) hours as
8 an apprentice in a cosmetology establishment under the direct per-
9 sonal supervision of a licensed electrologist instructor;
10 (e) As an esthetician, must have:
11 (i) Completed and graduated from a course of instruction of at
12 least six hundred (600) hours in a school approved by the board; or
13 (ii) Completed at least one thousand two hundred (1,200) hours
14 as an apprentice in a cosmetology establishment under the direct
15 personal supervision of a licensed cosmetology instructor. Such
16 establishment must have at least one (1) licensed esthetician
17 on-site in accordance with board rules; and
18 (f) As a nail technician, must have:
19 (i) Completed and graduated from a course of instruction of at
20 least four hundred (400) hours in a school approved by the board;
21 or
22 (ii) Completed at least eight hundred (800) hours as an apprentice
23 in a cosmetology establishment under the direct personal super-
24 vision of a licensed cosmetology instructor. Such establishment
25 shall have at least one (1) licensed nail technician on-site in ac-
26 cordance with board rules.
27 (3) To qualify as an instructor of barbering, barber-styling, cosme-
28 tology, electrology, esthetics, or nail technology, an applicant must:
29 (a) Hold a current license in the profession or closely related profes-
30 sion, as determined by the board, for which the applicant seeks to in-
31 struct;
32 (b) Have worked for at least five (5) of the last seven (7) years as a li-
33 censed barber, barber-stylist, cosmetologist, electrologist, estheti-
34 cian, or nail technician, or have satisfactorily completed:
35 (i) A minimum six (6) month teacher's course of instruction in one
36 (1) of the specialties described in this subsection; or
37 (ii) A minimum three (3) month teacher's course of instruction in
38 a school of one (1) of the specialties described in this subsec-
39 tion, if the applicant has at least two (2) years of experience as
40 a licensee in one (1) of the specialties described in this subsec-
41 tion; and
42 (c) Have completed twelve (12) college credit hours or equivalent edu-
43 cation, as determined by the board, or pass an examination approved by
44 the board.
45 (4) To be qualified to hold an apprenticeship for purposes of this chap-
46 ter, a person must:
47 (a) Meet the qualifications set forth in paragraphs (a), (b), and (d) of
48 subsection (1) of this section; and
49 (b) Be registered as an apprentice with the board.
