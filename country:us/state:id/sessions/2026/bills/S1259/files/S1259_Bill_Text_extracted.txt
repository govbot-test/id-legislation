Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1259
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO JUVENILE CORRECTIONS; AMENDING SECTION 20-516, IDAHO CODE, TO
3 AUTHORIZE JUVENILE PROBATION OFFICERS TO ARREST A JUVENILE PROBATIONER
4 WITHOUT A WARRANT FOR PROBATION VIOLATIONS IN CERTAIN INSTANCES AND TO
5 MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN
6 EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 20-516, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 20-516. APPREHENSION AND RELEASE OF JUVENILES -- DETENTION. (1) A
11 peace officer may take a juvenile into custody, or a private citizen may
12 detain a juvenile until the juvenile can be delivered forthwith into the
13 custody of a peace officer, without order of the court:
14 (a) When he has reasonable cause to believe that the juvenile has com-
15 mitted an act that would be a misdemeanor or felony if committed by an
16 adult; or
17 (b) When in the presence of a peace officer or private citizen the ju-
18 venile has violated any local, state, or federal law or municipal ordi-
19 nance; or
20 (c) When there are reasonable grounds to believe the juvenile has com-
21 mitted a status offense. Status offenses are truancy, running away from
22 or being beyond the control of parents, guardian, or legal custodian,
23 alcohol age violations under pursuant to section 18-1502(e), Idaho
24 Code, and curfew violations. Status offenders shall not be placed in
25 any jail facility, including juvenile detention centers, but instead
26 may be placed in juvenile shelter care facilities, except in the case
27 of runaways, when there is a specific detention request from a foreign
28 jurisdiction to hold the juvenile pending transportation arrangements,
29 and a peace officer may, in his discretion, notify the parent, guardian,
30 or legal custodian. In the event of an alcohol age infraction under
31 pursuant to section 18-1502(e), Idaho Code, the status offense under
32 this section shall be in addition to the infraction.
33 (2) A peace officer may take a juvenile into custody upon a written or-
34 der or warrant signed by a judge. The judge may issue the order or warrant af-
35 ter finding that there is reasonable cause to believe that the juvenile comes
36 within the purview of this chapter. Such taking into custody shall not be
37 deemed an arrest. Jurisdiction of the court shall attach from the time the
38 juvenile is taken into custody. When an officer takes a juvenile into cus-
39 tody, he shall notify the parent, guardian, or custodian of the juvenile as
40 soon as possible. Unless otherwise ordered by the court, or unless it ap-
41 pears to the officer taking the juvenile into custody that it is contrary to
42 the welfare of society or the juvenile, such juvenile shall be released to
