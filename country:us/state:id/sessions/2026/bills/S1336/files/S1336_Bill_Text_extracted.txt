40 33-118C. CURRICULAR MATERIALS -- HISTORY AND CIVICS. (1) Notwith-
41 standing the provisions of section 33-118, Idaho Code, the legislature finds
42 that to promote the stability of America's republican form of government and
43 to establish and maintain a general, uniform, and thorough system of public,
44 free common schools, as provided in section 1, article IX of the constitution
45 of the state of Idaho, all curricular materials adopted by school districts
46 or public charter schools in the subjects of history, American government
47 and civics, and social studies shall:
48 (a) Align with the provisions of section 33-1602, Idaho Code;

7
1 (b) Align with state content standards established by the state board
2 of education;
3 (c) Be academically rigorous;
4 (d) Be historically and factually accurate;
5 (e) Directly utilize or otherwise promote engagement with primary
6 sources and documents;
7 (f) Encourage pupils to believe what is true, love what is good, and ad-
8 mire what is beautiful;
9 (g) Inspire patriotism;
10 (h) Contribute to the cultivation of good moral character and the
11 virtues necessary for self-government and human flourishing;
12 (i) Cultivate knowledge and appreciation for the nation's history and
13 cultural heritage as derived from the tradition of Western civiliza-
14 tion; and
15 (j) Foster an appreciation for and attachment to America's republican
16 form of government, along with a proper understanding of the pupils'
17 rights and duties as citizens of the state of Idaho and the United
18 States.
19 (2) School districts and public charter schools shall have the discre-
20 tion to utilize other supplementary instructional materials, such as pri-
21 mary sources, documents, maps, or other resources, provided that such ma-
22 terials align with the requirements provided in subsection (1) of this sec-
23 tion.
