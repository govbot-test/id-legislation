7 SECTION 1. That Title 58, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
8 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-

10 CHAPTER 16
11 MANAGED RECREATION OPPORTUNITIES ON ENDOWMENT LAND
12 58-1601. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. (1) The legislature finds
13 that:
14 (a) Upon statehood, Idaho was endowed with certain land to be held and
15 used exclusively for the purposes provided by the admission act, in such
16 a manner as the legislature of the state may provide, thereby creating
17 a "sacred-trust" between the general government, as trustor, the state,
18 as trustee, and the various beneficiaries;
19 (b) Such lands became known as endowment lands and are actively man-
20 aged by the Idaho department of lands, under the direction of the board
21 of land commissioners, in accordance with its constitutional mandate
22 to "secure the maximum long term financial return to the institution to
23 which granted", of which there are nine (9), the most prominent being
24 public schools;
25 (c) To generate revenue, endowment lands are leased for various pur-
26 poses, such as timber harvests, grazing, and commercial uses, and are
27 generally open to the public, free of charge, provided such public ac-
28 cess does not interfere with the execution of a valid, existing lease,
29 or is not otherwise incompatible with the trust purposes of the endow-
30 ment land;
31 (d) Idaho law requires that all state-owned lands classified as chiefly
32 valuable for forestry, reforestation, recreation, and watershed pro-
33 tection be set aside as state forests, thereby ensuring that endowment
34 lands classified as such, while they may be reclassified from time to
35 time, provide future beneficiaries with suitable revenue-generating
36 land while also ensuring ecologically sensitive areas and rural commu-
37 nities with single-source watersheds are protected;
38 (e) The Idaho park and recreation board and the Idaho department of
39 parks and recreation successfully manage state parks, trails, and other
40 attractions throughout every region of Idaho, giving them the ability,
41 expertise, and experience necessary to design, construct, operate,

2
1 maintain, and administer public recreation infrastructure and activi-
2 ties within and across Idaho;
3 (f) In 1989, the Idaho legislature recognized "that in order to obtain
4 the maximum long-term benefits to the people of Idaho, it is necessary
5 for lands of outstanding park and recreation potential to be acquired
6 and incorporated into the state park system in the most economical man-
7 ner possible.";
8 (g) The department of parks and recreation, under the direction of the
9 park board, is statutorily charged with acquiring or leasing areas of
10 scenic beauty, recreational utility, or historic, archaeological, or
11 scientific interest to ensure that the health, happiness, availability
12 of recreational opportunities, and overall wholesome enjoyment of life
13 by the people will be furthered; and
14 (h) Both boards and departments are uniquely positioned to collabora-
15 tively fulfill the land board's constitutional mandate, meet statutory
16 requirements, and promote the park board's duties all while ensuring
17 future generations of beneficiaries and citizens have access to endow-
18 ment lands, the outdoors, and a healthy environment.
19 (2) Therefore, it is the intent of the legislature to:
20 (a) Maximize the long-term benefit to the endowment beneficiaries by
21 providing a variety of long-term, consistent revenue sources;
22 (b) Protect traditional uses of endowment land and valid, existing
23 rights;
24 (c) Conserve existing public access to lands historically open to the
25 public; and
26 (d) Preserve, establish, and promote outdoor recreation and education
27 opportunities.
28 58-1602. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
29 (1) "Endowment land" means any and all land granted or acquired pur-
30 suant to sections 4 through 14 of the Idaho admission act, including in-lieu
31 and indemnity lands and such other lands as may have been acquired by the
32 state through the exchange of such lands or the sale of such lands and pur-
33 chase of additional lands, and such other lands that may have been acquired
34 by the state board of land commissioners through gift, grant, or donation
35 pursuant to section 58-133(1), Idaho Code.
36 (2) "IDL fee" means the fee set by and paid to the Idaho department of
37 lands for each managed recreation opportunity.
38 (3) "IDPR fee" means the fee that may be set by the Idaho department of
39 parks and recreation for a managed recreation opportunity.
40 (4) "Managed recreation opportunity" means a specific, defined site or
41 area of endowment land that is developed and operated for fee-based recre-
42 ation opportunities, including the associated infrastructure. A managed
43 recreation opportunity may include providing, promoting, and expanding
44 outdoor education and research opportunities, similar to the McCall outdoor
45 science school. A managed recreation opportunity may also include but is not
46 limited to:
47 (a) Camp, tent, recreational vehicle, yurt, park-and-ski, and picnic
48 sites;
49 (b) Bike, equestrian, off-highway vehicle, and snowmobile parks; and

3
1 (c) Development of the land for hiking, backpacking, fishing, hunting,
2 and other such recreational uses as may be desired by the departments.
3 (5) "Managed recreation plan" or "plan" means a site-specific develop-
4 ment and management plan containing site-specific terms for a managed recre-
5 ation opportunity.
6 (6) "Recreation advisor" means the designated representative from the
7 department of lands or the designated representative from the department
8 and parks and recreation who serves as that department's primary point of
9 contact for managed recreation opportunities established pursuant to this
10 chapter.
11 58-1603. MANAGED RECREATION OPPORTUNITIES. (1) A managed recreation
12 opportunity shall be approved by the board of land commissioners and the park
13 and recreation board prior to its implementation.
14 (2) A managed recreation opportunity shall not terminate, effectively
15 terminate, or interfere with, harm, or impede any bona fide right or valid,
16 existing right that precedes the use of the land for a managed recreation op-
17 portunity without written consent of the affected right-holder.
18 (3) For the purposes of this chapter only, any lease or other land use
19 agreement executed for a managed recreation opportunity shall not be consid-
20 ered a disposal of endowment land subject to requirements regarding conflict
21 auctions.
22 58-1604. DUTIES OF THE DEPARTMENTS. (1) The director of the department
23 of lands and the director of the department of parks and recreation, and the
24 boards, departments, and recreation advisors associated therewith, shall
25 collaboratively work together to execute the provisions of this chapter.
26 (2) The department of lands and the department of parks and recreation
27 shall:
28 (a) Mutually identify endowment lands that can be developed to offer
29 managed recreation opportunities to generate revenue, protect endow-
30 ment land assets, and promote public recreational values;
31 (b) Appoint a recreation advisor; and
32 (c) To the maximum extent possible, facilitate multiple simultaneous
33 leases or uses on endowment lands to maximize revenue, including work-
34 ing with existing lessees or right-holders, if any, to ensure that a
35 managed recreation opportunity does not substantially interfere with,
36 harm, or impede any bona fide right or valid, existing right.
37 (3) The department of lands shall:
38 (a) Compile, keep, periodically update, and make available to the de-
39 partment of parks and recreation an inventory of endowment lands from
40 which the departments may identify and select mutually agreeable lands
41 for managed recreation opportunities;
42 (b) Provide technical assistance on the development, design, con-
43 struction, implementation, maintenance, and management of the managed
44 recreation opportunity; and
45 (c) Provide guidance for each managed recreation opportunity to
46 assist, coordinate, and consult with the department of parks and recre-
47 ation.
48 (4) The department of parks and recreation shall:

4
1 (a) Design, manage, maintain, operate, and administer all managed
2 recreation opportunities on endowment lands;
3 (b) Prepare, solicit, contract, and procure goods and services nec-
4 essary to construct, develop, maintain, operate, and manage managed
5 recreation opportunities; and
6 (c) Incorporate all managed recreation opportunities into its regular
7 operations and management, including use of its reservation system and
8 concessionaires or vendors.
9 58-1605. MANAGED RECREATION PLAN. (1) The departments shall develop a
10 managed recreation plan after identifying endowment land suitable for a man-
11 aged recreation opportunity.
12 (2) The management plan shall include, at a minimum:
13 (a) An explanation of the recreation opportunity sought to be developed
14 and the need for it in the community;
15 (b) The projected monetary benefit to the beneficiary and impact to ex-
16 isting users of the land, if any; and
17 (c) Site-specific terms for the managed recreation opportunity that
18 may include provisions governing use, construction, maintenance, oper-
19 ation, marketing, funding, revenue distribution, and other such terms
20 as the boards may deem necessary for the respective managed recreation
21 opportunity.
22 (3) Once a managed recreation plan is completed, the departments shall
23 present the plan to their respective board for review and approval. The plan
24 shall be approved by:
25 (a) The board of land commissioners if it finds that:
26 (i) The IDL fee associated with the managed recreation oppor-
27 tunity is reasonably likely to compensate the beneficiary in an
28 amount equal to the fair market value for a similar recreation op-
29 portunity on the specific area of endowment land sought to be used
30 for the managed recreation opportunity; and
31 (ii) The managed recreation opportunity will not actually and
32 substantially interfere with, harm, or impede any bona fide right
33 or valid, existing right that precedes the use of the land for a
34 managed recreation opportunity, unless written consent of the af-
35 fected right-holder is obtained and presented to the board; and
36 (b) The park and recreation board if it finds that:
37 (i) The managed recreation opportunity aligns with its strategic
38 plan and that it will preserve, protect, or promote the overall en-
39 joyment of life for the people through outdoor recreation and re-
40 source stewardship; and
41 (ii) The managed recreation opportunity will not actually and
42 substantially interfere with, harm, or impede any bona fide right
43 or valid, existing right that precedes the use of the land for a
44 managed recreation opportunity, unless written consent of the af-
45 fected right-holder is obtained and presented to the board.
46 (4) Upon the approval of the plan by both boards, the departments shall
47 implement the plan and take the necessary actions to open the managed recre-
48 ation opportunity to the public as soon as is practicable.

5
1 (5) The directors of the departments may use any power and duty granted
2 to them pursuant to Idaho law to develop a managed recreation opportunity or
3 associated plan or to otherwise effectuate the provisions of this chapter.
4 58-1606. REVENUE GENERATION AND DISTRIBUTION. (1) For each managed
5 recreation opportunity, the department of lands shall set an IDL fee.
6 (a) The IDL fee shall be based on the fair market value for a similar
7 recreation opportunity on the specific area of endowment land sought to
8 be used for the managed recreation opportunity.
9 (b) When setting the fee, the department of lands shall consider mul-
10 tiple fair market factors, including local and regional recreation mar-
11 ket conditions and consistency with fiduciary obligations to endowment
12 beneficiaries and with the provisions of this chapter.
13 (c) The IDL fee may be periodically reviewed and adjusted as the board
14 of land commissioners may see fit but no more frequently than on an an-
15 nual basis. Prior to adjusting the IDL fee, the land board shall consult
16 with the park and recreation board and shall attempt to adjust the fee to
17 a mutually agreeable amount but does not need the park board's consent
18 prior to adjusting the IDL fee.
19 (2) The department of parks and recreation shall remit all IDL fees to
20 the department of lands at the end of each fiscal year for deposit into the
21 earnings reserve fund of the respective endowment beneficiary for which the
22 land was used for a managed recreation opportunity.
23 (3) The department of parks and recreation may charge an IDPR fee.
24 (a) The IDPR fee shall be determined according to the fee schedule ap-
25 proved by the park and recreation board.
26 (b) IDPR fees shall be deposited into the park and recreation fund es-
27 tablished in section 67-4225, Idaho Code, and used solely by the depart-
28 ment of parks and recreation for managed recreation opportunities.
29 (4) The department of parks and recreation may utilize funds, as appli-
30 cable, provided pursuant to:
31 (a) Section 67-7126(4), Idaho Code, for off-highway vehicle opportuni-
32 ties and related facilities and management; and
33 (b) Section 67-7106(1)(c), Idaho Code, for snowmobile opportunities
34 and related facilities and management.
35 (5) Any proceeds from the operation of a managed recreation opportunity
36 shall be deposited into the park and recreation fund to be used by the direc-
37 tor of the department of parks and recreation, subject to legislative appro-
38 priation, solely for the purposes provided for by this chapter, along with
39 such other moneys as may be provided by legislative appropriation to further
40 effectuate the purposes of this chapter.
41 (a) IDL fees shall not be considered proceeds.
42 (b) Up to fifteen percent (15%) of the proceeds may be used for admin-
43 istrative costs related to managed recreation opportunities, includ-
44 ing providing compensation to the departments' recreation advisors for
45 their work effectuating the provisions of this chapter.
46 (c) The remaining proceeds may be used by the department of parks and
47 recreation to cover the costs of developing, designing, constructing,
48 implementing, maintaining, managing, and operating managed recreation

6
1 opportunities, acquiring managed recreation opportunities, and other-
2 wise implementing the provisions of this chapter.
3 (d) Such proceeds shall not be used toward:
4 (i) Expenses incurred to manage endowment lands for purposes or
5 uses other than a managed recreation opportunity; or
6 (ii) Expenses incurred to manage lands under the direction or con-
7 trol of the department of parks and recreation for purposes or uses
8 other than a managed recreation opportunity.
9 (6) Any proceeds from managed recreation opportunities over five hun-
10 dred thousand dollars ($500,000) that remain unencumbered in the fund at the
11 end of each fiscal year shall be transferred by the state treasurer to the de-
12 partments as follows:
13 (a) Fifty percent (50%), along with any interest collected on such mon-
14 eys, shall be deposited into the park and recreation fund established in
15 section 67-4225, Idaho Code; and
16 (b) Fifty percent (50%), along with any interest collected on such mon-
17 eys, shall be deposited in the earnings reserve fund of the respective
18 endowment beneficiary for which the land was used for a managed recre-
19 ation opportunity.
20 58-1607. REPORTING BY THE DEPARTMENTS. (1) No later than June 30 of
21 each year, beginning in 2027, the directors of the department of lands and
22 department of parks and recreation shall jointly prepare and submit to the
23 governor and the legislature an annual report concerning endowment lands
24 used for managed recreation opportunities and the departments' performance
25 administering such lands, the managed recreation opportunities associated
26 with such lands, and the provisions of this chapter.
27 (2) The departments' annual report shall, at a minimum:
28 (a) Provide a detailed accounting of all active endowment land leases
29 that are categorized for recreation purposes, including the total
30 acreage under such lease, the lease terms, and the revenue generated
31 thereunder;
32 (b) Provide the inventory of endowment lands required to be kept by this
33 chapter and an overview of potential future managed recreation opportu-
34 nities, including total acreage, recreation opportunity, and potential
35 revenue;
36 (c) Provide a detailed accounting of each active managed recreation op-
37 portunity that includes:
38 (i) The total proceeds generated at that managed recreation op-
39 portunity;
40 (ii) The associated IDL fee amount and the total distribution, or
41 anticipated distribution, to the endowment beneficiaries; and
42 (iii) The total expenditures related to that managed recreation
43 opportunity;
44 (d) Provide an overview of the total proceeds generated by all managed
45 recreation opportunities throughout the state, including the antici-
46 pated distributions to the departments pursuant to section 58-1606(5),
47 Idaho Code;

7
1 (e) Provide comprehensive operational details for both departments for
2 the preceding fiscal year, including budgetary expenditures, staffing,
3 and progress toward mutually planned strategic milestones; and
4 (f) Be made publicly available on the respective departments' official
5 websites.
6 (3) The department of lands may request for its accounting purposes, no
7 more frequently than once per fiscal quarter, and the department of parks and
8 recreation shall provide, within ten (10) business days of receiving such
9 request in writing, an accurate report containing the information required
10 by subsection (2)(c) and (d) of this section.
11 58-1608. APPLICABILITY. Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to
12 require, permit, or approve the transfer of endowment land from the depart-
13 ment of lands to the department of parks and recreation or to otherwise di-
14 vest the state board of land commissioners of title to or management author-
15 ity over endowment lands, regardless of the endowment land's use as a man-
16 aged recreation opportunity. Endowment land may be disposed of or otherwise
17 transferred to the department of parks and recreation only pursuant to a sale
18 or exchange made in accordance with applicable law.
19 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
20 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
21 July 1, 2026.
