LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 125
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 A SENATE RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE SENATE AND PROVIDING THAT ALL TEMPORARY AND PEND-
3 ING RULES OF THE IDAHO DEPARTMENT OF FISH AND GAME, THE IDAHO DEPARTMENT
4 OF LANDS, THE IDAHO DIVISION OF OCCUPATIONAL AND PROFESSIONAL LICENSES,
5 THE IDAHO DEPARTMENT OF PARKS AND RECREATION, THE IDAHO DEPARTMENT OF
6 WATER RESOURCES, AND THE IDAHO DEPARTMENT OF ENVIRONMENTAL QUALITY HAVE
7 BEEN REVIEWED AND APPROVED BY THE SENATE RESOURCES AND ENVIRONMENT COM-
8 MITTEE, WITH EXCEPTIONS.
9 Be It Resolved by the Senate of the State of Idaho:
10 WHEREAS, pursuant to Section 67-5291, Idaho Code, standing committees
11 of the Legislature shall review any temporary or pending rule that is ger-
12 mane to its committee and has been published in the Idaho Administrative Bul-
13 letin; and
14 WHEREAS, the Senate Resources and Environment Committee reviewed tem-
15 porary and pending rules adopted by the Idaho Department of Fish and Game,
16 the Idaho Department of Lands, the Idaho Division of Occupational and Pro-
17 fessional Licenses, the Idaho Department of Parks and Recreation, the Idaho
18 Department of Water Resources, and the Idaho Department of Environmental
19 Quality.
20 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Senate, assembled
21 in the Second Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, that
22 all temporary and pending rules adopted by the Idaho Department of Fish and
23 Game, the Idaho Department of Lands, the Idaho Division of Occupational and
24 Professional Licenses, the Idaho Department of Parks and Recreation, the
25 Idaho Department of Water Resources, and the Idaho Department of Environ-
26 mental Quality, pursuant to the Administrative Procedure Act and submitted
27 through the Office of the Administrative Rules Coordinator to the Legisla-
28 ture for review during the 2026 legislative session have been reviewed and
29 approved by the Senate Resources and Environment Committee with the excep-
30 tion of the following, which were not approved:
31 (1) IDAPA 26.01.20, Department of Parks and Recreation, Rules Govern-
32 ing the Administration of Park and Recreation Areas and Facilities,
33 Docket No. 26-0120-2501, Sections 075. and 250., only; and
34 (2) IDAPA 58.01.11, Department of Environmental Quality, Ground Water
35 Quality Rule, Docket No. 58-0111-2501, Section 003. and Section 200.,
36 Subsection 01.a., only.
37 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate Resources and Environment Com-
38 mittee held that the following pending rule docket become effective upon
39 sine die of the Second Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legisla-
40 ture: IDAPA 58.01.08, Department of Environmental Quality, Idaho Rules for
41 Public Drinking Water Systems, Docket No. 58-0108-2502.
