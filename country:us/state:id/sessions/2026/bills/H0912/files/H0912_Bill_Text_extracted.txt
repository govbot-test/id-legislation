20 56-276. MEDICAID WORK REQUIREMENTS. (1) As used in this section:
21 (a) "Applicable individual" means an individual who is eligible
22 to enroll under the state medicaid program pursuant to 42 U.S.C.
23 1396a(a)(10)(A)(i)(VIII) or who is otherwise eligible to enroll or
24 is enrolled under a waiver of such plan that provides coverage that is
25 equivalent to minimum essential coverage and has attained the age of
26 nineteen (19) and is under sixty-five (65) years of age, is not preg-
27 nant, is not entitled to or enrolled for benefits under part A of title
28 XVIII of the social security act, or enrolled for benefits under part B
29 of title XVIII of the social security act, and is not otherwise eligible
30 to enroll under such plan, and is subject to work requirements.
31 (b) "Department" means the Idaho department of health and welfare.
32 (c) "Work requirements" means the requirements established by 42
33 U.S.C. 1396a(xx).
34 (2) No later than December 31, 2026, the department shall implement
35 work requirements for applicable individuals.
36 (a) No applicable individual shall be enrolled in medicaid unless, at
37 the time of application, the individual demonstrates compliance with
38 the work requirements for the three (3) consecutive months immediately
39 preceding the month during which the individual applies. The depart-
40 ment shall require documentary evidence and shall not accept self-at-
41 testation at the time of application.

2
1 (b) The department shall verify an applicable individual's compliance
2 with work requirements through state wage data, education or training
3 program enrollment, or verified volunteer service documentation. The
4 department shall verify an applicable individual's compliance with
5 work requirements on an ongoing basis, at least quarterly between re-
6 determination periods, and shall not accept self-attestation. Such
7 verification shall occur between periods of formal redetermination for
8 individuals.
9 (c) No applicable individual shall remain enrolled in medicaid in any
10 month unless the individual has demonstrated compliance with the work
11 requirements.
12 (3) An applicable individual seeking an exemption from work require-
13 ments shall provide documentation for the exemption sought. The department
14 shall verify all exemptions and shall not accept self-attestation from indi-
15 viduals seeking exemptions.
16 (a) The department shall not seek or implement any additional optional
17 exemptions under 42 U.S.C. 1396a(xx)(3)(B) or any other optional exemp-
18 tion without obtaining approval from the legislature. Such approval
19 shall be provided in statute.
20 (b) The department shall not accept exemption designations, approvals,
21 or determinations by a managed care organization.
22 (c) The department shall only approve an exemption for an individual
23 who is medically frail or otherwise an individual with special needs
24 if the individual has been medically certified by a statement from a
25 physician, physician's assistant, nurse, nurse practitioner, desig-
26 nated representative of a physician's office, a licensed or certified
27 psychologist, or a social worker as having a disabling mental disorder,
28 having a physical, intellectual, or mental disability that signif-
29 icantly impairs the ability to perform activities of daily living,
30 including eating, dressing, bathing, grooming, getting in and out of
31 beds and chairs, walking, going outdoors, or using the toilet, or is
32 in treatment for chronic substance use disorder. In no case may the
33 department expand the definition of "medically frail or otherwise an
34 individual with special needs" beyond the scope of the definition es-
35 tablished under 42 CFR 440.315(f).
36 (4) The department shall disenroll any applicable individual who fails
37 to comply with the work requirements.
38 (5) The department shall submit quarterly reports to the legislature
39 and the governor on compliance rates, the number and type of exemptions
40 granted, and the impact on medicaid enrollment, consistent with the report-
41 ing requirements of section 71119, P.L. 119-21.
42 (6) The requirements of this section shall be implemented no later than
43 December 31, 2026, or such earlier date as permitted by federal law and ap-
44 proved by the centers for medicare and medicaid services.

7 SECTION 3. That Chapter 2, Title 56, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
8 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
9 ignated as Section 56-278, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 56-278. INELIGIBLE ALIENS. (1) The department of health and welfare
11 shall not provide medical assistance to an individual in Idaho unless the in-
12 dividual is:
13 (a) A resident of the United States; and
14 (b) At least one (1) of the following:
15 (i) A citizen or national of the United States;
16 (ii) An alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence as an im-
17 migrant, as defined in 8 U.S.C. 1101(a)(15) and (20), excluding
18 alien visitors, tourists, diplomats, students, or other individ-
19 uals admitted temporarily without intent to abandon their resi-
20 dence in a foreign country;
21 (iii) An alien who has been granted the status of Cuban or Haitian
22 entrant, as defined in section 501(e), P.L. 96-422; or
23 (iv) An individual lawfully residing in the United States in ac-
24 cordance with a compact of free association pursuant to 8 U.S.C.
25 1612(b)(2)(G).
26 (2) The department of health and welfare shall require that all income
27 of ineligible household members be included when calculating financial eli-
28 gibility for medicaid.
29 (3) The department of health and welfare shall include an immigration
30 status on all presumptive eligibility applications submitted to the depart-
31 ment. The department shall require hospitals, clinics, and other qualified
32 entities conducting presumptive eligibility determinations to collect and
33 transmit such attestations to the department. No presumptive eligibility
34 application shall be approved unless the applicant certifies that the appli-
35 cant is a qualified citizen.
36 (4) The department of health and welfare shall verify citizen-
37 ship or immigration status prior to enrollment and shall conduct regular
38 cross-checks of applicant and recipient information against federal
39 databases, including the systematic alien verification for entitlements
40 program. A match through any electronic or data verification system shall
41 not, by itself, be accepted as proof of identity. Each applicant shall be
42 required to provide documentary proof of United States citizenship, United
43 States national status, or alien status eligible for medical assistance.
44 The department shall promptly refer any applicant identified as an individ-
45 ual who is not a citizen or national of the United States and does not have
46 lawful immigration status as defined under federal law to the United States
47 immigration and customs enforcement or other appropriate federal authori-
48 ties for further investigation and enforcement.

6 SECTION 4. That Chapter 2, Title 56, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
7 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
8 ignated as Section 56-279, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
9 56-279. MULTISTATE ENROLLMENT. (1) The department of health and wel-
10 fare shall receive and review address change information from returned mail
11 by the United States postal service, the national change of address data-
12 base, and medicaid managed care companies operating in Utah. The department
13 shall conduct cross-checks of such address change information against state
14 medicaid enrollment at least monthly to identify enrollees who have moved
15 out of state. The requirements of this subsection shall be implemented no
16 later than January 1, 2027.
17 (2) The department of health and welfare shall receive and review in-
18 formation regarding out-of-state electronic benefit transactions and con-
19 duct cross-checks of such information against state medicaid enrollment at
20 least monthly to identify enrollees who have moved out of state. The re-
21 quirements of this subsection shall be implemented no later than January 1,
22 2027.
23 (3) Upon receiving information concerning a medicaid enrollee that in-
24 dicates a change in circumstances that may affect medicaid eligibility, in-
25 cluding a change in residency, the department of health and welfare shall
26 promptly conduct an eligibility redetermination for the enrollee.
27 (4) The department of health and welfare shall submit enrollment infor-
28 mation to the centers for medicare and medicaid services national medicaid
29 enrollment database every month to identify individuals enrolled in medic-
30 aid in multiple states at the same time. The requirements of this subsection
31 shall be implemented no later than October 1, 2029.
32 (5) No later than August 31 of each year, the department of health and
33 welfare shall submit an annual report to the legislature detailing the im-
34 plementation of the requirements established in this section. The annual
35 report shall be made publicly available on the department of health and wel-
36 fare website and shall include:
37 (a) The number of enrollees flagged through address change information
38 and out-of-state electronic benefit transactions;
39 (b) The number of enrollees removed from medicaid due to enrollment in
40 multiple states; and
41 (c) The estimated fiscal impact to the state due to implementing the re-
42 quirements of this section.

22 SECTION 6. That Chapter 2, Title 56, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
23 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
24 ignated as Section 56-281, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
25 56-281. RETROACTIVE ELIGIBILITY. (1) As used in this section:
26 (a) "Expansion population" means individuals who are eligible for
27 medical assistance under 42 U.S.C. 1396a(a)(10)(A)(i)(VIII), commonly
28 known as the medicaid expansion population under the affordable care
29 act.
30 (b) "Medicaid" means the medical assistance program established under
31 title XIX of the social security act and administered by the department
32 of health and human services.
33 (c) "Non-expansion population" means all individuals eligible for med-
34 icaid who do not qualify under the expansion population definition, in-
35 cluding but not limited to pregnant women, children, elderly individu-
36 als, and persons with disabilities.
37 (d) "Retroactive eligibility" means medicaid coverage for services
38 provided prior to the month of application, as authorized by 42 U.S.C.
39 1396a(a)(34).
40 (2) The department of health and welfare shall limit retroactive eligi-
41 bility for medicaid benefits as follows:
42 (a) For individuals determined to be eligible as part of the expansion
43 population, medicaid coverage may be made retroactive for no more than
44 one (1) month prior to the month in which the individual submits a com-
45 pleted medicaid application.
46 (b) For individuals eligible as part of the non-expansion population,
47 medicaid coverage may be made retroactive for no more than two (2)
48 months prior to the month in which the individual submits a completed
49 medicaid application.

1 (3) The limitations set forth in subsection (2) of this section apply
2 only to initial applications for medicaid and do not affect eligibility for
