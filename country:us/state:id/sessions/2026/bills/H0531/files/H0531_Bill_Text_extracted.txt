LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 531
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EPINEPHRINE DELIVERY SYSTEMS; AMENDING SECTION 33-520, IDAHO
3 CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING EPINEPHRINE DELIVERY SYSTEMS;
4 AMENDING SECTION 33-520A, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING
5 LIFE-THREATENING ALLERGIES IN SCHOOLS AND GUIDELINES REGARDING EPI-
6 NEPHRINE DELIVERY SYSTEMS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN
7 EFFECTIVE DATE.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Section 33-520, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
10 amended to read as follows:
11 33-520. POLICY GOVERNING MEDICAL INHALERS, EPINEPHRINE AUTO-INJEC-
12 TORS DELIVERY SYSTEMS, INSULIN AND BLOOD GLUCOSE MONITORING SUPPLIES. (1)
13 The board of trustees of each school district, including charter districts,
14 shall adopt a policy permitting the self-administration of medication ad-
15 ministered by way of a metered-dose inhaler by a pupil for asthma or other po-
16 tentially life-threatening respiratory illness or by way of an epinephrine
17 auto-injector delivery system for severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis).
18 On or before September 1, 2016, such boards of trustees shall also adopt a
19 policy permitting the self-administration of diabetes medication and blood
20 glucose monitoring by a pupil with diabetes.
21 (2) As used in this section:
22 (a) "Medication" means an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system, a
23 metered-dose inhaler or a dry powder inhaler or insulin prescribed by a
24 physician and having an individual label; and
25 (b) "Self-administration" means a student's use of medication or of
26 blood glucose monitoring supplies pursuant to prescription or written
27 direction from a physician.
28 (3) A student who is permitted to self-administer medication or blood
29 glucose monitoring pursuant to this section shall be permitted to possess
30 and use a prescribed inhaler, an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system,
31 insulin or blood glucose monitoring supplies at all times.
32 (4) Nothing in this section shall be construed to prevent a school dis-
33 trict from requiring pupils to maintain current duplicate prescription med-
34 ications or blood glucose monitoring supplies with the school nurse or, in
35 the absence of such nurse, with the school administrator.

3 (a) "Administer" means the direct application of an epinephrine auto-
4 injector delivery system to the body of an individual.
5 (b) "Designated school personnel" means an employee, agent or vol-

8 auto-injector delivery system to a student.
9 (c) "Epinephrine auto-injector delivery system" means a device that

11 proved by the federal food and drug administration that contains a
12 premeasured dose of epinephrine and that is used to administer epineph-

14 (d) "Provide" means the supply of one (1) or more epinephrine auto-in-
15 jectors delivery systems to an individual.
16 (e) "School" means any public or nonpublic school.
17 (f) "Self-administration" means a student or other person's discre-
18 tionary use of an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system, whether
19 provided by the student or by a school nurse or designated school per-
20 sonnel pursuant to the provisions of this section.
21 (2) Any physician, advanced practice registered nurse licensed to pre-
22 scribe or physician assistant licensed to prescribe pursuant to title 54,
23 Idaho Code, may prescribe epinephrine auto-injectors delivery systems in
24 the name of a school to be maintained for use in accordance with subsection
25 (3) of this section. Licensed pharmacists and physicians may dispense epi-
26 nephrine auto-injectors delivery systems pursuant to a prescription issued
27 in accordance with this subsection. A school may maintain a stock supply of
28 epinephrine auto-injectors delivery systems.
29 (3) The governing authority of a school may authorize school nurses and
30 designated school personnel to do the following:
31 (a) Provide an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system to a student
32 to self-administer the epinephrine auto-injector delivery system in
33 accordance with a prescription specific to the student on file with the
34 school nurse;
35 (b) Administer an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system to a stu-
36 dent in accordance with a prescription specific to the student on file
37 with the school nurse; and
38 (c) Administer an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system to any
39 student or other individual on school premises that the school nurse
40 or designated school personnel in good faith believes is experiencing
41 anaphylaxis regardless of whether the student or other individual has a
42 prescription for an epinephrine auto-injector delivery system.
43 (4) A school may enter into arrangements with manufacturers of epineph-
44 rine auto-injectors delivery systems or third-party suppliers of epineph-
45 rine auto-injectors delivery systems to obtain epinephrine auto-injectors
46 delivery systems at fair market price, reduced price or free.
47 (5) The governing authority of a school that participates in supplying

17 (6) There shall be no civil liability for any damages for a physician,
18 advanced practice registered nurse, physician's assistant or pharma-
19 cist providing a prescription or standing protocol for school epinephrine
20 auto-injectors delivery systems consistent with the standard of care for
21 the provider. Further, there shall be no civil liability for damages for
22 a school or its employees or agents for any injuries that result from the
23 administration or self-administration of an epinephrine auto-injector
24 delivery system regardless of whether authorization for use was given by the
25 student's parents, guardian or medical provider provided the actions taken
26 in administering or providing the injector epinephrine delivery system
27 were reasonable under the circumstances. The liability protections in this
28 section do not apply to acts or omissions constituting gross negligence,
29 those that are reckless or that constitute willful and wanton behavior. The
30 liability protections in this section are in addition to any provided under
31 section 5-330, Idaho Code.
