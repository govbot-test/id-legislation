26 SECTION 2. That Section 63-3502B, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
27 amended to read as follows:
28 63-3502B. LEVY OF TAX ON WIND ENERGY PRODUCTION, SOLAR ENERGY PRO-
29 DUCTION, GEOTHERMAL ENERGY ELECTRICAL PRODUCTION, RATE-REGULATED ELECTRIC
30 UTILITY COMPANIES, RATE-REGULATED AFFILIATED GAS COMPANIES, AND RATE-REGU-
31 LATED GAS COMPANIES. (1) A wind energy tax or a geothermal energy tax shall
32 be levied against every producer of electricity by means of wind energy or
33 geothermal energy in the amount of three percent (3%) of such producer's
34 gross wind energy earnings or geothermal energy earnings.
35 (2) A solar energy tax shall be levied against every producer of elec-
36 tricity by means of solar energy in the amount of three and one-half percent
37 (3.5%) of the producer's gross solar energy earnings.
38 (3) A kilowatt-hour tax shall be levied against every rate-regulated
39 electric utility company operating in Idaho on all such electricity and
40 electrical energy sold to retail customers in Idaho, as reported by such
41 company to the Idaho public utilities commission; however, if such a report
42 contains kilowatt-hour sales data for another state, the kilowatt-hour tax
43 established under this section shall not be applied to those out-of-state
44 sales. The kilowatt-hour tax shall be nine hundred twenty-three millionths
45 of a dollar ($0.000923) per kilowatt-hour sold.
46 (4)(a) A thermal energy tax shall be levied against every rate-regu-
47 lated affiliated gas company on all natural gas sold to retail customers
48 in Idaho at a rate of ten thousand eight hundred two millionths of a dol-
49 lar ($0.010802) per therm of natural gas sold.

4
1 (b) A thermal energy tax shall be levied against every rate-regulated
2 gas company on all natural gas sold to retail customers in Idaho at
3 a rate of three hundred twenty-nine hundred-thousandths of a dollar
4 ($0.00329) per therm of natural gas sold. A separate thermal energy tax
5 shall be levied against every rate-regulated gas company on all nat-
6 ural gas delivered to a transport gas customer at a rate of forty-one
7 hundred-thousandths of a dollar ($0.00041) per therm of natural gas
8 delivered to a transport gas customer by a rate-regulated gas company.
9 For the purposes of this subsection, "transport gas customer" means an
10 entity that purchases gas from a supplier other than a rate-regulated
11 gas company but that contracts with a rate-regulated gas company to de-
12 liver gas to its meter.
13 (5) The wind energy tax, solar energy tax, geothermal energy tax, kilo-
14 watt-hour tax, and thermal energy tax provided in this section shall be in
15 lieu of all other taxes on the property of such wind energy producer, solar
16 energy producer, geothermal energy producer, rate-regulated electric util-
17 ity company, rate-regulated affiliated gas company, or rate-regulated gas
18 company exempted pursuant to section 63-602JJ, Idaho Code.
19 (6) For purposes of the certification required by section 63-803, Idaho
20 Code, and the limitations provided by section 63-802, Idaho Code, the taxes
21 levied pursuant to subsection (2) of this section shall reduce the property
22 tax to be levied to finance an annual budget and shall not be included in the
23 amount of property tax revenues to finance an annual budget for purposes of
24 limitations on increases in the annual budget as provided in section 63-802,
25 Idaho Code. Kilowatt-hour Estimated kilowatt-hour taxes and estimated
26 thermal energy taxes apportioned to each taxing district pursuant to section
27 63-3503C, Idaho Code, shall be treated as property tax revenue for the pur-
28 poses of the limitations provided in section 63-802, Idaho Code.
29 (7) By January 1, 2027, rate-regulated electric utility companies,
30 rate-regulated affiliated gas companies, and rate-regulated gas companies
31 shall include the kilowatt-hour and thermal energy tax on the billings to
32 its Idaho customers. The public utilities commission shall adjust the base
33 tariff rates for customers to the extent those rates included a recovery of
34 property taxes on operating property or the kilowatt-hour taxes or thermal
35 energy taxes assessed pursuant to this section.
