1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 12, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE AD-
3 DITION OF A NEW SECTION 33-1204A, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS
4 FOR ALTERNATIVE AUTHORIZATION OF SCHOOL ADMINISTRATORS; AMENDING SEC-
5 TION 33-320, IDAHO CODE, TO AUTHORIZE THE USE OF FUNDS FOR ADMINISTRA-
6 TOR MENTORING AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMER-
7 GENCY.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Chapter 12, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
10 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
11 ignated as Section 33-1204A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 33-1204A. ALTERNATIVE AUTHORIZATION PROGRAM -- ADMINISTRATORS. (1)
13 An individual who does not otherwise hold an administrator certificate
14 required for employment as a school principal or superintendent may sat-
15 isfy the standards of employment for such positions, or similar positions,
16 through the alternative authorization program, which is hereby established
17 to provide a nontraditional pathway to certification under an interim cer-
18 tificate. The alternative authorization program permits a school district
19 board of trustees to request issuance of a three (3) year interim certificate
20 for a qualified candidate. The program shall include two (2) pathways for
21 authorization:
22 (a) A "grow your own" pathway for experienced Idaho educators requiring
23 leadership development; and
24 (b) An "executive leadership" pathway for individuals transitioning
25 from business, military, government, nonprofit, or other sectors who
26 possess leadership and management expertise and require preparation in
27 education systems.
28 (2) To be eligible for alternative authorization as provided in this
29 section, an applicant shall:
30 (a) Pass a criminal history check as provided in section 33-130, Idaho
31 Code;
32 (b) Hold, at a minimum, a bachelor's degree or its equivalent from a re-
33 gionally or nationally accredited college or university;
34 (c) Submit a letter from the school district board of trustees affirm-
35 ing that the applicant demonstrates leadership knowledge and skills,
36 stating the district's intent to employ the applicant, and committing
37 to provide mentoring as required pursuant to subsection (5) of this sec-
38 tion; and
39 (d) Complete coursework consisting of no less than three (3) semester
40 credits in the statewide framework for teacher evaluations, including a
41 laboratory or applied component.

2
1 (3) An applicant who meets the eligibility requirements set forth in
2 subsection (2) of this section shall also satisfy one (1) of the following
3 experience pathways to be eligible for alternative authorization:
4 (a) The "grow your own" pathway, which requires no less than five (5)
5 years of certified teaching experience in one (1) or more Idaho public
6 schools; or
7 (b) The "executive leadership" pathway, which requires demonstrated
8 professional competency in management or leadership through no less
9 than five (5) years of experience in one (1) or more of the following:
10 (i) Higher education;
11 (ii) Government service;
12 (iii) Nonprofit organization service;
13 (iv) Private sector service;
14 (v) Military service at the officer level; or
15 (vi) Equivalent experience as determined by the state department
16 of education.
17 (4) Prior to the issuance of an interim certificate, the sponsoring
18 school district, in collaboration with the state department of education,
19 shall conduct an initial competency assessment using criteria aligned with
20 Idaho administrator standards as adopted by the state board of education to
21 identify any gaps in the applicant's knowledge or skills. If deficiencies
22 are identified, the applicant shall complete targeted coursework, profes-
23 sional development, or learning experiences consisting of no less than six
24 (6) semester credits or the equivalent. Such instruction may include but
25 is not limited to school law, public school finance, special education, and
26 instructional leadership.
27 (5)(a) An individual authorized for an interim certificate pursuant to
28 this section shall receive structured, embedded mentoring from a cer-
29 tified administrator throughout the three (3) year term of the interim
30 certificate. Such mentoring shall include:
31 (i) Assignment by the sponsoring school district, subject to cri-
32 teria established by the state department of education, of a men-
33 tor who holds an Idaho administrator certificate and has no less
34 than five (5) years of experience in a comparable administrative
35 role;
36 (ii) A minimum of seventy (70) clock hours of mentoring during
37 the first two (2) years aligned to a personalized learning plan
38 addressing areas identified for growth in the initial competency
39 assessment, including budgeting, student achievement analysis,
40 employee evaluation, strategic planning, conflict management,
41 school board relations, and special education law;
42 (iii) Ongoing evaluation and support throughout the interim cer-
43 tificate period; and
44 (iv) Submission of an annual mentoring summary by the mentor to
45 the state department of education that includes an assessment of
46 the individual's progress toward required competencies. The de-
47 partment shall review such summary for program oversight purposes
48 and provide feedback to the mentor regarding mentoring effective-
49 ness.

3
1 (b) The sponsoring school district shall compensate the mentor and may
2 use funds appropriated pursuant to section 33-320, Idaho Code, for this
3 purpose.
4 (6) An individual authorized for interim certification pursuant to
5 this section shall be subject to oversight by the professional standards
6 commission as provided in section 33-1208, Idaho Code. The state board of
7 education may deny issuance of an interim certificate for any reason that
8 would constitute grounds for suspension or revocation of a certificate.
9 (7) An interim certificate issued pursuant to this section may be re-
10 newed annually for a period not to exceed three (3) years, contingent on re-
11 ceipt of proficient performance evaluations from the school district board
12 of trustees and satisfactory completion of the mentoring requirements set
13 forth in subsection (5) of this section. Upon completion, the individual
14 shall be eligible for issuance of a standard administrator certificate with
15 principal or superintendent endorsements.
16 (8) An individual who successfully completes the alternative autho-
17 rization program and is issued a standard administrator certificate shall
18 remain subject to ongoing review, including annual professional development
19 requirements aligned with state standards.
20 SECTION 2. That Section 33-320, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
21 amended to read as follows:
22 33-320. CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT PLANS AND TRAINING. (1) Each school
23 district and public charter school in Idaho shall develop an annual plan
24 that is part of a continuous focus on improving the student performance of
25 the district or public charter school. A public charter school may use its
26 performance certificate in lieu of a separate continuous improvement plan.
27 (2)(a) The board of trustees and the superintendent shall collaborate
28 on the plan and engage students, parents, educators, and the community
29 as appropriate. The board of directors and the administrator of a pub-
30 lic charter school shall collaborate on the plan and engage students,
31 parents, educators, and the community as appropriate. All continuous
32 improvement plans must be approved by the local governing board.
33 (b) The annual continuous improvement plan shall:
34 (i) Be data-driven, specifically in student outcomes, and shall
35 include, but not be limited to, analyses of demographic data, stu-
36 dent achievement and growth data, graduation rates, and college
37 and career readiness;
38 (ii) Set clear and measurable targets based on student outcomes;
39 (iii) Include a clearly developed and articulated vision and mis-
40 sion;
41 (iv) Include key indicators for monitoring performance;
42 (v) Include student literacy proficiency goals and targets and
43 how progress toward those outcomes will be measured;
44 (vi) Include, as applicable to the grade ranges served, trajec-
45 tory growth targets toward literacy proficiency;
46 (vii) Include, as applicable to the grade ranges served, college
47 and career advising and mentoring goals and how progress toward
48 those outcomes will be measured;

4
1 (viii) Include the individual staff performance on each of the
2 performance criteria as defined in section 33-1001, Idaho Code,
3 including measurable student achievement and student success
4 indicator targets and the percentage of students meeting those
5 targets. Data will be aggregated at the grade range, subject, or
6 performance indicator, as determined by the commission and al-
7 lowed pursuant to section 33-133, Idaho Code;
8 (ix) Include, at a minimum, the student achievement and growth
9 metrics for the state accountability framework. Student achieve-
10 ment and growth will be reported on each school and district's re-
11 port card as required by the state board of education and published
12 by the state department of education; and
13 (x) Include a report of progress toward the previous year's im-
14 provement goals.
15 (c) The annual continuous improvement plan must be reviewed and updated
16 annually no later than October 1 of each year.
17 (d) The board of trustees or the board of directors shall continuously
18 monitor progress toward the goals by utilizing relevant data to measure
19 growth. The progress shall be included in evaluations of the district
20 superintendent or administrator of a public charter school.
21 (3) The plan must be made available to the public and shall be posted on
22 the school district or charter school website.
23 (4) Of the moneys appropriated in the public schools educational sup-
24 port program, up to six thousand six hundred dollars ($6,600) shall be
25 distributed to each school district and public charter school to be expended
26 for training purposes for district superintendents and boards of trustees,
27 public charter school administrators, and boards of directors. Funds shall
28 be distributed on a reimbursement basis based on a process prescribed by the
29 superintendent of public instruction. Qualified training shall include
30 training for continuous improvement processes and planning, strategic plan-
31 ning, finance, superintendent evaluations, public charter administrator
32 evaluations, ethics, and governance.
33 (5) Funds appropriated pursuant to this section may be used to support
34 administrator mentoring programs, including mentoring provided in section
35 33-1204A, Idaho Code.
36 (5) (6) The state board of education shall be granted rulemaking
37 authority to establish appropriate procedures, qualifications, and guide-
38 lines for qualified training providers and shall prepare a list of qualified
39 training providers within the state of Idaho.
