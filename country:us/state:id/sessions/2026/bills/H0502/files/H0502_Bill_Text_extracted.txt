14 SECTION 1. That Section 38-1502, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
15 amended to read as follows:
16 38-1502. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
17 (1) "Assessment year" means January 1 through December 31 of any calen-
18 dar year in which the commission levies an assessment.
19 (2) "Base year" means the calendar year immediately preceding the as-
20 sessment year.
21 (3) "Business entity" means a person, firm, partnership, corporation,
22 association, trust, or other recognized legal entity.
23 (4) "Commission" means the Idaho forest products commission created by
24 section 38-1503, Idaho Code.
25 (5) "Financial supporter" means entities who have paid assessments
26 pursuant to this chapter.
27 (6) "Forest lands" means federal, state, and private land growing for-
28 est tree species which that are, or could be at maturity, capable of furnish-
29 ing raw material used in the manufacture of lumber or other forest products.
30 The term includes federal, state, and private land from which forest tree
31 species have been removed but have not yet been restocked, but it does not in-
32 clude land affirmatively converted to uses other than the growing of forest
33 tree species.
34 (7) "Forest product manufacturer" means a business entity that engages
35 in the processing, cutting, fabricating or other process which that converts
36 timber, chips, sawdust, or shavings into lumber, paper, plywood, particle
37 board, or other usable products for sale in commerce, provided, however, as
38 used in this chapter, forest products. Forest product manufacturers shall
39 include those business entities that buy timber in Idaho and then sell it to
40 other persons outside of the state for manufacture into finished products.
41 Forest product manufacturers shall not include the following business enti-
42 ties engaged in:

2
1 (a) The production of fence or corral posts or rails;
2 (b) Producing shingles or shakes;
3 (c) Producing firewood or pellets for energy; or
4 (d) Producing logs which that have been shaped or scribed and used in
5 the construction of log structures.
6 (8) "Hog fuel" means wood or wood scraps that have been shredded or pul-
7 verized and used by forest product manufacturers to generate energy.
8 (9) "Private forest lands" means forest lands not owned by the federal
9 government, state government, an Indian tribe, or a political subdivision of
10 the state.
11 SECTION 2. That Section 38-1503, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
12 amended to read as follows:
13 38-1503. FOREST PRODUCTS COMMISSION CREATED -- MEMBERS. (1) There is
14 hereby created and established in the department of self-governing agencies
15 the Idaho forest products commission, to be composed of five (5) voting mem-
16 bers appointed by the governor from a list of names with at least two (2)
17 names for each appointive office for each district submitted to the gover-
18 nor by the financial supporters of the commission in each district. Initial
19 members of the commission shall serve either a three (3) or five (5) year
20 term, with two (2) members of the commission serving three (3) year terms,
21 and three (3) members of the commission serving five (5) year terms. For the
22 initial commission members, the duration of each member's term shall be de-
23 termined by lot. Thereafter, all All commission members shall serve terms
24 of three (3) years, and may be reappointed. The commission shall may adopt
25 rules to, subject to legislative approval, to further define the process for
26 filling vacancies to the commission and to provide for determining the terms
27 of office for all members of the commission after the expiration of the terms
28 of the original members.
29 (2) The governor shall also name as permanent advisory members to the
30 commission, the director of the department of lands, a representative of the
31 United States forest service, the dean of the University university of Idaho
32 college of natural resources or the dean's designee, a representative of the
33 Idaho department of commerce and the Idaho department of agriculture with
34 expertise in marketing and promotion, and the executive director of the as-
35 sociated logging contractors. No advisory member of the commission shall
36 have a vote on the commission.
37 (3) Nominations for expiring seats on the commission shall be made by
38 the financial supporters of the commission from the district in which the
39 seat is expiring, or from all districts if an at-large member seat is expir-
40 ing, no later than June 1 of the year in which the seat is expiring. The com-
41 mission shall provide nomination applications to all financial supporters
42 of the commission. The commission shall send the names of all qualified nom-
43 inees to the governor and may make its own recommendations and nominations.
44 In making the appointments, the governor shall take into consideration the
45 recommendations made by the commission and organizations that represent or
46 are engaged in harvesting, transporting, or manufacturing forest products.
47 (4) Vacancies in any unexpired term shall be filled by the governor.
48 The commission shall identify qualified candidates and send their names to
49 the governor. The appointed member shall serve on the commission for the re-

4 SECTION 3. That Section 38-1506, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
5 amended to read as follows:
6 38-1506. CHAIRMAN AND STAFF, CHAIR, AND VICE CHAIR OF THE COMMIS-
7 SION. (1) The commission shall elect a chairman and may employ clerical or
8 other staff who are not members of the commission.
9 (2) The commission shall nominate and elect, by majority vote, a chair
10 to serve as the presiding officer at all commission meetings.
11 (3) The commission may nominate and elect, by majority vote, a vice
12 chair to accept the duties of the chair in the event that the chair is unable
13 to attend a commission meeting.
14 (4) The term of office for chair and vice chair positions, if elected,
15 shall be one (1) year, commencing on July 30 of each year.
