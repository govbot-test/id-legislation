LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 805
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE PRACTICE OF DENTAL HYGIENE; AMENDING SECTION 54-902, IDAHO
3 CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING THE PRACTICE OF DENTAL HYGIENE;
4 REPEALING SECTION 54-904, IDAHO CODE, RELATING TO THE AUTHORIZATION
5 FOR PROCEDURES PERFORMED UNDER GENERAL SUPERVISION BY DENTAL HYGIEN-
6 ISTS; AMENDING CHAPTER 9, TITLE 54, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A
7 NEW SECTION 54-904, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE AUTHORIZATION FOR DENTAL
8 HYGIENISTS; AMENDING SECTION 54-906, IDAHO CODE, TO REMOVE A PROVISION
9 REGARDING SUPERVISION BY A DENTIST; AMENDING SECTION 54-924, IDAHO
10 CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR LICENSEES AND LICENSURE BY THE BOARD; AND DECLARING
11 AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
12 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
13 SECTION 1. That Section 54-902, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
14 amended to read as follows:
15 54-902. DEFINITION -- PRACTICE OF DENTAL HYGIENE. The practice of
16 dental hygiene is the doing by one (1) person for a direct or indirect con-
17 sideration of one (1) or more of the following with respect to the teeth
18 or dental health of another person, namely, cleaning, polishing, remov-
19 ing stains or concretions; performing nonsurgical periodontal therapy;
20 administering prescribed anesthetics or medicaments; applying preventive
21 agents; performing nonsurgical, clinical and laboratory oral diagnostic
22 tests for interpretation by a dentist; preparation of preliminary records of
23 oral conditions; and such other dental services as specified by the dentist
24 unless prohibited by the board in its adopted rules within such person's edu-
25 cation, training, and experience or as delegated by a supervising dentist.
26 SECTION 2. That Section 54-904, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
27 repealed.
28 SECTION 3. That Chapter 9, Title 54, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
29 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
30 ignated as Section 54-904, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
31 54-904. AUTHORIZATION FOR DENTAL HYGIENISTS. (1) A dental hygienist
32 is authorized to perform any dental intervention within the individual's ed-
33 ucation, training, and experience or as delegated by a supervising dentist,
34 including but not limited to:
35 (a) Dental hygiene assessment;
36 (b) Dental hygiene diagnosis; and
37 (c) Treatment planning and implementation in the identification, pre-
38 vention, and management of oral disease.
39 (2) A dental hygienist who possesses a current and valid certificate
40 pursuant to section 54-1729, Idaho Code, may obtain and administer any topi-

2
1 cal pharmacological agent used in the course of treatment for minor and self-
2 limiting oral diseases.
3 (3) A dental hygienist who practices independently shall refer a pa-
4 tient to a dentist:
5 (a) For any necessary dental care beyond the individual's education,
6 training, and experience; or
7 (b) Upon the patient's request.
8 SECTION 4. That Section 54-906, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 54-906. UNLAWFUL PRACTICE OF DENTAL HYGIENE. (1) Any person, not a
11 dentist, who shall practice, or shall in any manner hold himself out to any
12 other person, or to the public, as qualified or licensed to practice dental
13 hygiene within the state of Idaho without at the time being a licensed dental
14 hygienist, or who performs any act, function, or service permitted a dental
15 hygienist by this act without the supervision of a dentist as specified by
16 the rules of the board, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction
17 shall be fined not less than one hundred dollars ($100) nor more than three
18 hundred dollars ($300), or be imprisoned in the county jail for not less than
19 thirty (30) days nor more than six (6) months, or both.
20 Each act of practice, or holding out, or representation shall consti-
21 tute a separate offense.
22 (2) Conviction under the provisions of this section shall not prevent
23 issuance of an injunction as provided in section 54-933, Idaho Code.
