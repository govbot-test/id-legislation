6 SECTION 1. That Section 18-7008, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby

8 18-7008. CRIMINAL TRESPASS -- DEFINITIONS AND ACTS CONSTITUT-
9 ING. (1) Definitions. As used in this section:
10 (a) "Crops" means field crops including, but not limited to, grains,
11 feed crops, legumes, fruits and vegetables.
12 (b) "Cultivated land" means:
13 (i) Land whose soil is loosened or broken up for the raising of
14 crops;
15 (ii) Land used for the raising of crops; or
16 (iii) Pasturage that is artificially irrigated.
17 (c) "Damage" means any injury or damage to real or personal property
18 and includes, but is not limited to, any of the following actions, when
19 conducted without lawful authority, the consent of the landowner or his
20 agent, or a valid license:
21 (i) Cutting down or carrying off any wood, underbrush, tree or
22 timber, or girdling or otherwise injuring any tree or timber on the
23 land of another;
24 (ii) Severing from the property of another anything attached
25 thereto, or the produce thereof;
26 (iii) Digging, taking or carrying away any earth, soil or stone
27 from the property of another;
28 (iv) Tearing down or otherwise damaging any fence on the land of
29 another person, or opening any gate, bar or fence of another person
30 and leaving it open, or using the corral or corrals of another per-
31 son;
32 (v) Dumping trash or covering up in any manner the property of
33 another person;
34 (vi) The unprovoked, intentional killing or injuring of a domes-
35 tic animal of another on his property;
36 (vii) Removing, mutilating, damaging or destroying any "no tres-
37 passing" signs or markers of similar meaning;
38 (viii) Going through or driving a motor vehicle, as defined in sec-
39 tions 49-114 and 49-123, Idaho Code, into, upon, over or through
40 any cultivated lands; or
41 (ix) Injuring livestock.

1 (d) "Enter" or "enters" means going upon or over real property either
2 in person or by causing any object, substance or force to go upon or over
3 real property.
4 (e) "Navigable streams" shall have the meaning set forth in section
5 36-1601, Idaho Code.
6 (f) "Permission" means written authorization from the owner or his
7 agent to enter upon private land, which shall include the signature of
8 the owner or his agent, the name of the person being given permission,
9 the appropriate dates that the permission is valid and a general de-
10 scription of the property; or another form of permission or invitation
11 recognized by law.
12 (g) "Remains" means to fail to depart from the real property of another
13 immediately when notified to do so by the owner or his agent.
14 (2) Acts constituting criminal trespass.
15 (a) A person commits criminal trespass and is guilty of a misdemeanor,
16 except as provided in subsection (3)(a)(i) of this section, when he
17 enters or remains on the real property of another without permission,
18 knowing or with reason to know that his presence is not permitted. A
19 person has reason to know his presence is not permitted when, except un-
20 der a landlord-tenant relationship, he fails to depart immediately from
21 the real property of another after being notified by the owner or his
22 agent to do so, or he returns without permission or invitation within
23 one (1) year, unless a longer period of time is designated by the owner
24 or his agent. In addition, a person has reason to know that his pres-
25 ence is not permitted on real property that meets any of the following
26 descriptions:
27 (i) The property is reasonably associated with a residence or
28 place of business;
29 (ii) The property is cultivated;
30 (iii) The property is fenced or otherwise enclosed in a manner
31 that a reasonable person would recognize as delineating a private
32 property boundary. Provided, however, if the property adjoins
33 or is contained within public lands, the fence line adjacent to
34 public land is posted with conspicuous "no trespassing" signs or
35 bright orange or fluorescent paint at the corners of the fence
36 adjoining public land and at all navigable streams, roads, gates
37 and rights-of-way entering the private land from the public land,
38 and is posted in a manner that a reasonable person would be put on
39 notice that it is private land; or
40 (iv) The property is unfenced and uncultivated but is posted with
41 conspicuous "no trespassing" signs or bright orange or fluores-
42 cent paint at all property corners and boundaries where the prop-
43 erty intersects navigable streams, roads, gates and rights-of-way
44 entering the land, and is posted in a manner that a reasonable per-
45 son would be put on notice that it is private land.
46 (b) A person commits criminal trespass and is guilty of a misdemeanor
47 when he enters or remains on the premises of a church or house of worship
48 without permission, knowing or with reason to know that his presence is
49 not permitted and with the intent to intimidate or harass or to disrupt a
50 church service or assemblage of people meeting for religious worship.

1 (6) A person shall not be guilty of trespass under this section for en-
2 tering or remaining upon real property if the person entered or remained on
3 the property pursuant to any of the following rights or authorities:
4 (a) An established right of entry or occupancy of the real property in
5 question, including, but not limited to:
6 (i) An invitation, whether express or implied, to enter or remain
7 on real property including, but not limited to, the right to enter
8 property that is, at the time, open to the public, if the person is
9 in compliance with lawful conditions imposed on access;
10 (ii) A license to enter or remain on real property; or
11 (iii) A lease, easement, contract, privilege or other legal right
12 to enter, remain upon, possess or use the real property;
13 (b) A lawful authority to enter onto or remain upon the real property in
14 question, including, but not limited to:
15 (i) Any law enforcement officer during the course and scope of
16 fulfilling his lawful duties;
17 (ii) Any paramedic, firefighter or other emergency personnel dur-
18 ing the course and scope of fulfilling his lawful duties; or
19 (iii) Any licensed professional otherwise authorized to enter or
20 remain on the real property during the course and scope of fulfill-
21 ing his lawful duties; or
22 (c) Any other person with a legally prescribed right to enter or remain
23 upon the real property in question.
24 (7) Examples of the exclusions in subsection (6) of this section in-
25 clude, but are not limited to: a customer entering and remaining in a store
26 during business hours who has not been asked to depart by the property owner
27 or his agent; a person knocking on a front door of a property that is not
28 posted; a meter reader during the scope and course of his employment; a
29 postal employee delivering mail or packages; power company personnel fixing
30 downed power lines; a bail bondsman arresting a person who is in violation of
31 a bail contract; a tenant pursuant to a valid lease; and the owner or operator
32 of any right-of-way or easement for any ditch, canal or other conduit, acting
33 pursuant to the provisions of chapter 11 or chapter 12, title 42, Idaho Code.
34 (8) The exclusions set forth in this section shall not relieve any per-
35 son of civil or criminal liability pursuant to other applicable law for caus-
36 ing damage while entering or remaining on the property in question.
