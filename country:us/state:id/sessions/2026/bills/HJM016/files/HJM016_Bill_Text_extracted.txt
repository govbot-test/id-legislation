LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 16
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE ADMINISTRATOR OF THE CENTERS FOR MEDICARE AND MEDICAID SERVICES, TO
3 THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CON-
4 GRESS ASSEMBLED, AND TO THE CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE
5 STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES.
6 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
7 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
8 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
9 WHEREAS, Idaho seniors and other Medicare beneficiaries rely on ac-
10 cess to affordable Medicare and prescription drug coverage that meets their
11 health care needs; and
12 WHEREAS, federal law provides a limited annual enrollment period during
13 which Medicare beneficiaries must select health coverage options for the up-
14 coming plan year; and
15 WHEREAS, the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) oversees
16 Medicare Advantage plans, including plan approval, bid review, and federal
17 program requirements; and
18 WHEREAS, the Idaho Department of Insurance regulates insurer licen-
19 sure, solvency, and market conduct within the State of Idaho, including
20 Medicare Supplement products and insurance producer activity; and
21 WHEREAS, effective Medicare oversight relies on clear coordination be-
22 tween federal regulators and state insurance regulators; and
23 WHEREAS, CMS and the Idaho Department of Insurance maintain a memoran-
24 dum of understanding allowing cooperation and information sharing regarding
25 violations of applicable laws, rules, and guidance; and
26 WHEREAS, maintaining stability and predictability in Medicare Advan-
27 tage plan availability is essential to protecting Medicare beneficiaries
28 during the annual enrollment period; and
29 WHEREAS, Medicare beneficiaries have limited time during the annual en-
30 rollment period to make informed decisions regarding their health care cov-
31 erage options; and
32 WHEREAS, Medicare beneficiaries often rely on licensed insurance pro-
33 ducers to assist with plan selection, and sudden changes to producer compen-
34 sation structures may reduce consumer access to informed guidance during the
35 annual enrollment period; and
36 WHEREAS, insurers seeking to offer Medicare Advantage plans in Idaho
37 must submit bids to CMS and receive approval to offer plans while complying
38 with applicable federal and state requirements; and
39 WHEREAS, insurance companies are required to actuarially develop their
40 rates using appropriate justification; and
41 WHEREAS, sudden limitations, withdrawal of plan availability, or mar-
42 ket conduct that discourages enrollment during the annual enrollment period

2
1 may disrupt consumer choice and market stability for Medicare beneficia-
2 ries; and
3 WHEREAS, before the 2025 open enrollment period, certain companies,
4 after actuarially pricing, voluntarily applying for, and being awarded the
5 Medicare contract, attempted to dissuade or steer consumers away from their
6 products; and
7 WHEREAS, certain companies withdrew their enrollment applications, de-
8 nied access to Idaho seniors and those who qualify for Medicare, and created
9 market disruption; and
10 WHEREAS, the Idaho Department of Insurance issued a bulletin identify-
11 ing these practices as unfair trade practices under Idaho law; and
12 WHEREAS, due to some companies continuing to operate in a harmful manner
13 to Idaho seniors, Medicare recipients, and to the Medicare market, the Idaho
14 Department of Insurance issued cease and desist orders; and
15 WHEREAS, it is the public policy of the State of Idaho that insurers op-
16 erating within the state comply with all applicable laws governing licen-
17 sure, solvency, and trade practices, and that Medicare beneficiaries have
18 meaningful access to approved coverage options; and
19 WHEREAS, it is further the public policy of the State of Idaho that in-
20 surance companies who apply to offer coverage should not engage in practices
21 that discourage enrollment or limit plan availability after approval to of-
22 fer such coverage.
23 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
24 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
25 the Senate concurring therein, that the Idaho Legislature supports the on-
26 going efforts of the Idaho Department of Insurance to protect Idaho Medicare
27 beneficiaries and promote a stable and fair insurance marketplace.
28 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature encourages the Idaho De-
29 partment of Insurance to continue working with CMS to ensure Idaho Medicare
30 beneficiaries are protected against unfair or disruptive market practices.
31 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature requests CMS to continue
32 collaboration with Idaho and other state insurance regulators and to pro-
33 vide clear guidance regarding federal and state oversight responsibilities
34 related to Medicare Advantage plans.
35 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature requests CMS to provide
36 guidance regarding changes to producer compensation structures after plan
37 approval and its impact on consumer access and market stability.
38 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Represen-
39 tatives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of
40 this Memorial to the Administrator of the Centers for Medicare and Medic-
41 aid Services, to the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of
42 Representatives of the United States in Congress assembled, and to the con-
43 gressional delegation representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the
44 United States.
