19 CHAPTER 28
20 GOVERNMENT UNIONS
21 44-2801. DECLARATION OF POLICY. The legislature of the state of Idaho
22 finds and declares that:
23 (1) Public employees are free to form and join membership organiza-
24 tions, professional associations, labor unions, or similar organizations of
25 their own choosing to advance their mutual interests.
26 (2) As the United States supreme court recognized in Janus v. AFSCME
27 (2018), unlike private sector labor relations, collective bargaining be-
28 tween government entities and labor unions representing public employees is
29 an inherently political process with significant implications for the size,
30 cost, operations, transparency, and accountability of government.
31 (3) The operation of state and local government in Idaho is determined
32 by the people through their elected representatives, not unelected pri-
33 vate interests. However, the legislature has found collective bargaining
34 between public employers and labor organizations representing government
35 employees to be in the public interest in certain contexts.
36 (4) To advance the existing policy of the state of Idaho that special
37 interests be prevented from unduly influencing governmental action and that
38 governmental functions and policies reflect, to the maximum extent possi-
39 ble, the public interest, public funds and taxpayer-funded facilities, sys-
40 tems, and resources should not be used to preferentially aid or subsidize,
41 directly or indirectly, the operations or advocacy of government unions, ex-

2
1 cept where necessary to ensure the uninterrupted delivery of critical emer-
2 gency services by first responders.
3 44-2802. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
4 (1) "Affiliate" means:
5 (a) Any organization subject to the constitution, bylaws, or similar
6 governing documents of a government union;
7 (b) Any organization with a constitution, bylaws, or similar governing
8 documents that a government union is subject to;
9 (c) Any organization that receives a regular portion of the dues or fees
10 collected by a government union from its members pursuant to the consti-
11 tution, bylaws, or similar governing documents of such organization or
12 the government union; or
13 (d) A political committee, political organization, nonprofit entity,
14 or other fund controlled by a government union.
15 (2) "Collective bargaining agreement" means:
16 (a) A written agreement negotiated between a public employer and gov-
17 ernment union that addresses public employee compensation, benefits,
18 or other terms or conditions of employment; or
19 (b) A negotiated agreement governed by sections 33-1271 through
20 33-1276, Idaho Code.
21 (3) "Government union" means:
22 (a) A labor organization, union, or similar entity of any kind in which
23 public employees participate and that exists for the purpose, in whole
24 or in part, of representing public employees in collective bargaining
25 or similar dealings with a public employer concerning public employee
26 compensation, benefits, or other terms or conditions of employment; or
27 (b) A local education organization as defined in section 33-1272, Idaho
28 Code.
29 (4) "Government union activities" means activities undertaken at the
30 direction of, on behalf of, or to advance the purposes of a government union
31 or its affiliate by:
32 (a) Supporting or opposing any candidate for federal, state, or local
33 public office;
34 (b) Influencing the passage or defeat of any federal or state legisla-
35 tion, federal or state regulation, local ordinance, or any ballot mea-
36 sure;
37 (c) Promoting or soliciting membership or participation in, or finan-
38 cial support of, a government union or its affiliate;
39 (d) Seeking to become the exclusive representative of a unit of public
40 employees for the purposes of collective bargaining;
41 (e) Participating in the administration, business, or internal gover-
42 nance of a government union or its affiliate;
43 (f) Preparing, conducting, or attending government union or affili-
44 ate events, conferences, conventions, meetings, or trainings, unless
45 such training is directly related to the performance of public employ-
46 ees' job duties;
47 (g) Distributing government union or affiliate communications;

31 44-2803. TAXPAYER FUNDING OF GOVERNMENT UNIONS PROHIBITED. (1) No
32 public employer, its agents, or representatives may use or authorize the use
33 of taxpayer funds to promote government unions by:
34 (a) Deducting dues, fees, assessments, fines, or contributions from
35 the pay of a public employee on behalf of a government union or its af-
36 filiate;
37 (b) Increasing a public employee's compensation with the intent that
38 the additional compensation, or a part of it, be used to pay government
39 union or affiliate dues, fees, assessments, fines, or contributions;
40 (c) Providing more personal or contact information of a public employee
41 to a government union or its affiliate than the public employer is re-
42 quired to disclose pursuant to chapter 1, title 74, Idaho Code, unless
43 the public employer receives written authorization from the public em-
44 ployee to disclose additional information;
45 (d) Requiring or coercing a public employee to meet, communicate, lis-
46 ten to, or otherwise interact with a government union or its affiliate;
47 (e) Distributing communications or membership solicitations on behalf
48 of a government union or its affiliate;

4
1 (f) Contributing public funds to, or expending public funds on behalf
2 of, a government union or its affiliate; or
3 (g) Except as provided by sections 33-1216 and 33-1279, Idaho Code, and
4 except as otherwise provided by this section, providing any form of com-
5 pensation or paid leave to a public employee, directly or indirectly,
6 for the purpose of engaging in government union activities.
7 (2) If provided by a collective bargaining agreement between a public
8 employer and a government union:
9 (a) A public employee may be granted time off without pay or benefits to
10 engage in government union activities, and a government union may com-
11 pensate a public employee for engaging in government union activities.
12 (b) A public employee may use compensated personal leave, whether the
13 public employee's own leave or leave voluntarily donated by other pub-
14 lic employees, to engage in government union activities if:
15 (i) The leave is accrued at the same rate by similarly situated
16 public employees of the public employer without regard to member-
17 ship or participation in a government union; and
18 (ii) The public employee may freely choose how to use the leave.
19 (c) A public employee may engage in representational activities while
20 in a duty status without loss of pay or benefits if:
21 (i) The government union reports to the public employer no less
22 than twice per calendar year the amount of time, in increments
23 rounded to the nearest quarter of an hour, spent on representa-
24 tional activities each day by each public employee engaged in
25 representational activities;
26 (ii) The public employer calculates the pro rata value of compen-
27 sation, including wages and benefits, paid or accruing to a pub-
28 lic employee for time spent engaged in representational activi-
29 ties and provides an invoice to the government union no less than
30 twice per calendar year for the amounts so calculated; and
31 (iii) Upon receipt of the invoice from the public employer, the
32 government union remits full payment to the public employer within
33 thirty (30) days.
34 44-2804. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES.
35 (1)(a) A person who violates the provisions of section 44-2803, Idaho
36 Code, shall be subject to a civil penalty not to exceed two hundred and
37 fifty dollars ($250).
38 (b) A person who knowingly violates the provisions of section 44-2803,
39 Idaho Code, shall be subject to a civil penalty not to exceed one thou-
40 sand five hundred dollars ($1,500).
41 (c) A person who knowingly violates the provisions of section 44-2803,
42 Idaho Code, and who has previously admitted to committing or has been
43 previously determined to have committed a violation pursuant to para-
44 graph (b) of this subsection shall be subject to a civil penalty not to
45 exceed two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500).
46 (2) The attorney general or appropriate county prosecutor shall inves-
47 tigate complaints regarding the violation of a provision of section 44-2803,
48 Idaho Code, and shall ensure the effective enforcement of this section.
