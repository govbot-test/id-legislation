LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 110
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESEN-
3 TATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, THE CONGRESSIONAL
4 DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED
5 STATES, THE UNITED STATES TRADE REPRESENTATIVE AMBASSADOR, THE SECRE-
6 TARY OF THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE, AND THE SECRETARY OF
7 THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE.
8 We, your Memorialists, the Senate and the House of Representatives
9 of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
10 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
11 WHEREAS, Idaho is the second-largest producer of sugar beets in the
12 United States, with approximately 170,000 acres of sugar beet farmland under
13 production, generating more than $1 billion annually and supporting over 500
14 farm families; and
15 WHEREAS, sugar beets are the heartbeat of Idaho's rural communities,
16 sustaining farm families, processors, and local economies that depend on a
17 strong agricultural base; and
18 WHEREAS, Idaho's sugar processing industry employs approximately 2,000
19 people, providing well-paying jobs in rural Idaho; and
20 WHEREAS, the U.S. sugar market is experiencing a historic oversupply,
21 largely driven by foreign imports that are benefiting from government subsi-
22 dies and the dumping of sugar at prices below the cost of production, thereby
23 eroding domestic sugar beet prices and forcing farmers to harvest at a loss;
24 and
25 WHEREAS, sugar beet market prices have declined by more than 42% over
26 the past two years, placing severe financial strain on domestic producers;
27 and
28 WHEREAS, since the 2018 Farm Bill's enactment, on-farm production costs
29 have increased by more than 30%, while interest costs have surged by 73%
30 since 2020, thereby dramatically increasing the cost of operating a farm;
31 and
32 WHEREAS, since 2000, the U.S. sugar beet industry has been forced to
33 close eleven sugar beet processing factories, resulting in lost processing
34 capacity, lost jobs, and long-term economic harm to rural communities; and
35 WHEREAS, President Trump's America First agenda prioritizes protecting
36 American farmers and workers, confronting unfair foreign trade practices,
37 and strengthening domestic supply chains critical to the nation's economy;
38 and
39 WHEREAS, the tier-2 over-quota duty on imported sugar is intended to
40 function as a prohibitive safeguard against excessive foreign imports, en-
41 suring fair trade practices and protecting domestic sugar beet growers and
42 workers from unfairly traded sugar; and

2
1 WHEREAS, the tier-2 duty has not been adjusted since 2000, reducing its
2 effectiveness in preventing foreign sugar from entering the U.S. market at
3 prices that undermine domestic production.
4 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
5 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of Rep-
6 resentatives concurring therein, that the Legislature urges immediate ac-
7 tion to raise the tier-2 duty on imported sugar to restore its intended pro-
8 tective function and to prioritize American farmers, workers, and rural com-
9 munities.
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature respectfully calls on Con-
11 gress and the Trump Administration to put American interests first by pre-
12 venting policies that enrich foreign governments at the expense of American
13 farmers and workers and to take decisive action to address the devastating
14 effects of oversupply caused by unfairly traded imports.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate be, and she is
16 hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this Memorial to the
17 President of the United States, the President of the Senate and the Speaker
18 of the House of Representatives of Congress, the congressional delegation
19 representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the United States, the
20 United States Trade Representative Ambassador, the Secretary of the United
21 States Department of Commerce, and the Secretary of the United States De-
22 partment of Agriculture.
