22 42-1102. OWNERS OF LAND -- RIGHT TO RIGHT-OF-WAY. (1) Owners or
23 claimants to land that do not have sufficient length of frontage on a stream
24 to afford the requisite fall for a ditch, canal, lateral, drain, or other
25 conduit on their own premises for the proper irrigation or drainage thereof,

27 stream, and convenient facilities otherwise for the watering of said lands
28 cannot be had, such owners or claimants are entitled to a right-of-way
29 through the lands of others for a ditch, canal, lateral, drain, or conduit to
30 convey water to the place of use for the purposes of irrigation or to provide
31 drainage of irrigated land.

29 SECTION 2. That Chapter 12, Title 42, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
30 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
31 ignated as Section 42-1201, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
32 42-1201. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter, "irrigation conduit"
33 means a ditch, canal, lateral, drain, pipeline, or other surface channel or
34 buried conduit that is used to convey water to the place of use for the pur-
35 poses of irrigation or to provide drainage of irrigated land.
36 SECTION 3. That Section 42-1201, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
37 amended to read as follows:
38 42-1201 42-1202. OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE OF IRRIGATION CONVEYANCE
39 FACILITIES. (1) Every person or entity owning or controlling any ditch,
40 canal, lateral, or irrigation conduit for the purpose of irrigation shall:
41 (a) During the time the ditch, canal, lateral, or irrigation conduit is
42 operated to deliver water for irrigation, keep a flow of water therein
43 sufficient to meet the needs of the persons or entities entitled to the
44 use of water therefrom. Such duty may be adjusted when there is insuffi-
45 cient water in the public streams or other natural water sources or when
46 the board of directors or governing body of an entity furnishing water

4
1 deems it in the best interests of that entity to reduce the water supply
2 or dates of availability;
3 (b) Construct the necessary outlets in the ditch, canal, lateral, or
4 irrigation conduit for proper delivery of water to persons and entities
5 having rights to the use of the water;
6 (c) Not permit a greater quantity of water to be turned into the ditch,
7 canal, lateral, or irrigation conduit than it will contain or than can
8 be used for beneficial or useful purposes to prevent the wasting and
9 useless discharge and running away of water; and
10 (d) Carefully keep and maintain the ditch, canal, lateral, or
11 irrigation conduit in good repair and condition so as not to damage or
12 in any way injure the property or premises of others and to prevent the
13 water conveyed therein from wasting during the irrigation season.
14 (2) The duties to operate and maintain an irrigation ditch, canal, lat-
15 eral, or conduit, whether statutory or common law, require reasonable care
16 only and shall not be construed to impose strict liability or to otherwise
17 enlarge the liability of the owners or operators thereof. The owners, con-
18 structors, or operators of irrigation ditches, canals, laterals, or con-
19 duits, while responsible for their own acts or omissions, shall not be liable
20 for damage or injury caused by:
21 (a) The diversion or discharge of water into a ditch, canal, lateral, or
22 irrigation conduit by a third party without the permission of the owner
23 or operator thereof;
24 (b) Any other act or omission of a third party, other than an employee
25 or agent of the owner or operator of the ditch, canal, lateral, or
26 irrigation conduit; or
27 (c) An act of God, including but not limited to fire, earthquake, storm,
28 or similar natural phenomenon.
29 (3) The provisions of this section shall not be construed to impair any
30 defense that an owner, constructor, or operator of a ditch, canal, lateral,
31 or irrigation conduit may assert in a civil action.
