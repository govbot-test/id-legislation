Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 682
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO TERMINATION OF PARENT AND CHILD RELATIONSHIPS; AMENDING SECTION
3 16-2005, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING CONDITIONS UNDER
4 WHICH TERMINATION MAY BE GRANTED; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PRO-
5 VIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 16-2005, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 16-2005. CONDITIONS UNDER WHICH TERMINATION MAY BE GRANTED.
10 (1)(a) The court may grant an order terminating the relationship where
11 it finds that termination of parental rights is in the best interests of
12 the child and that one (1) or more of the following conditions exist:
13 (i) The parent has abandoned the child;
14 (ii) The parent has neglected or abused the child;
15 (iii) The presumptive parent is not the biological parent of the
16 child;
17 (iv) The parent is unable to discharge parental responsibilities
18 and such inability will continue for a prolonged indeterminate pe-
19 riod and will be injurious to the health, morals or well-being of
20 the child; or
21 (v) The parent has been incarcerated and is likely to remain in-
22 carcerated for a substantial period of time during the child's mi-
23 nority.
24 (b) For terminations arising from a case filed pursuant to chapter 16,
25 title 16, Idaho Code, additional factors that inform what is in the best
26 interest of the child, beyond those otherwise identified by the courts,
27 include:
28 (i) The parent's efforts to improve the parent's capacity to
29 safely reunify with the child;
30 (ii) The parent's demonstrated ability to live a law-abiding
31 life, excepting infraction violations; and
32 (iii) When the child has formed a strong and positive bond with the
33 child's substitute caretaker, the strong and positive bond has ex-
34 isted for a substantial portion of the child's life, the removal of
35 the child from the substitute caretaker would likely cause serious
36 psychological harm to the child, and the parent lacks the capacity
37 to meet the needs of the child upon removal.
38 (2) The court may grant an order terminating the relationship and may
39 rebuttably presume that such termination of parental rights is in the best
40 interests of the child where:
41 (a) The parent caused the child to be conceived as a result of rape,
42 incest, lewd conduct with a minor child under the age of sixteen (16)

1 years, or sexual abuse of a child under the age of sixteen (16) years, as
2 defined in sections 18-6101, 18-1508, 18-1506, and 18-6601, Idaho Code;
3 (b) The following circumstances are present:
4 (i) Abandonment, chronic abuse or chronic neglect of the child.
5 Chronic neglect or chronic abuse of a child shall consist of abuse
6 or neglect that is so extreme or repetitious as to indicate con-
7 tinuing the relationship would result in unacceptable risk to the
8 health and welfare of the child;
9 (ii) Sexual abuse against a child of the parent, or a conviction of
10 sexual abuse against any child. Sexual abuse, for the purposes of
11 this section, includes any conduct described in section 18-1506,
12 18-1506A, 18-1507, 18-1508, 18-1508A, 18-6101, or 18-6604, Idaho
13 Code;
14 (iii) Torture of a child; any conduct described in the code sec-
15 tions listed in section 18-8303(1), Idaho Code; battery or an
16 injury to a child that results in serious or great bodily in-
17 jury to a child; voluntary manslaughter of a child, or aiding or
18 abetting such voluntary manslaughter, soliciting such voluntary
19 manslaughter or attempting or conspiring to commit such voluntary
20 manslaughter;
21 (iv) The parent has committed murder, aided or abetted a murder,
22 solicited a murder or attempted or conspired to commit murder; or
23 (c) The court determines the child to be an abandoned infant, except in
24 a parental termination action brought by one (1) parent against another
25 parent.
26 (3) The court shall not grant an order terminating the relationship
27 based on the child's immunization status.
28 (4) The court may grant an order terminating the relationship if termi-
29 nation is found to be in the best interest of the parent and child.
30 (5) The court may grant an order terminating the relationship where a
31 consent to termination in the manner and form prescribed by this chapter has

37 sequent hearing on the merits of the petition shall be held. Consents re-
