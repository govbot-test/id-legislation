1 SECTION 2. That Section 39-4402, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
2 amended to read as follows:
3 39-4402. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND, PURPOSES, AND INTENT. (1) The leg-
4 islature of the State of Idaho finds:
5 (a) That continuing technological progress, increases in manufactur-
6 ing, and the abatement of air and water pollution have resulted in ever-
7 increasing quantities of hazardous waste;
8 (b) That the public health and safety, and the environment, are threat-
9 ened when hazardous wastes are not managed in an environmentally sound
10 manner;
11 (c) That the knowledge and technology necessary for alleviating ad-
12 verse health, environmental, and aesthetic impacts resulting from
13 current hazardous waste management and disposal practices are gener-
14 ally available; and,
15 (d) That the problem of proper management of hazardous waste has become
16 a matter of great statewide concern.
17 (2) Therefore, it is hereby declared that the purposes of this act
18 chapter are:
19 (a) To protect the public health and safety, the health of living organ-
20 isms, and the environment from the effects of the improper, inadequate,
21 or unsound management of hazardous waste;
22 (b) To establish a program to track and control hazardous wastes from
23 the time they are generated through transportation, treatment, stor-
24 age, and disposal; and,
25 (c) To assure the safe and adequate management of hazardous wastes
26 within this state.
27 (3) It is the intent of the legislature that the site licensing process
28 provided for by this chapter not duplicate processes otherwise provided for
29 by state or federal law. The site licensing procedure provided by sections
30 39-4433 through 39-4441, Idaho Code, is intended to be a preliminary, gen-
31 eral review that does not include the type of specific, detailed technical
32 information required for the hazardous waste management act permit process
33 provided for by section 39-4409, Idaho Code.
