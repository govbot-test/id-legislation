LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 14
BY RESOURCES AND CONSERVATION COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, TO THE SECRETARY OF THE INTERIOR, TO
3 THE COMMISSIONER OF THE BUREAU OF RECLAMATION, TO THE SENATE AND THE
4 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED,
5 AND TO THE CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN
6 THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES.
7 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
8 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
9 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
10 WHEREAS, water is the foundation of Idaho's farms, communities, and
11 economy, yet the Eastern Snake Plain Aquifer continues to decline, forcing
12 water curtailments that now threaten farmers and ground water users every
13 year; and
14 WHEREAS, Idaho faces longer, hotter summers and warmer winters that re-
15 duce snowpack and cause runoff to occur earlier in the year, leaving less wa-
16 ter available when it is needed most; and
17 WHEREAS, the State of Idaho irrigates more acres than ever before, but
18 shrinking and inconsistent snowpack has created shortages that harm farms,
19 cities, fish, and industry; and
20 WHEREAS, Idaho loses between 1.5 and 2 million acre-feet of water each
21 year as it flows down the Snake River and out to the ocean simply because the
22 state lacks sufficient reservoir space to capture it; and
23 WHEREAS, while aquifer recharge has proven to be an effective tool, it
24 cannot be sustained without additional late-season water derived from stor-
25 age; and
26 WHEREAS, the Idaho Legislature has demonstrated its commitment to water
27 infrastructure through significant annual appropriations, and the people of
28 Idaho have demonstrated broad public support for the research and construc-
29 tion of new reservoirs and expanded storage capacity; and
30 WHEREAS, to secure the future of our farms, communities, and economy,
31 Idaho must commit to a long-term vision to build 750,000 acre-feet of new wa-
32 ter storage by the year 2100; and
33 WHEREAS, practical near-term solutions have been identified, including
34 modest raises at Minidoka Dam, Jackson Lake, and American Falls Dam; and
35 WHEREAS, mid-range and long-term opportunities exist for new reser-
36 voirs at locations such as Lane Lake, Chester, Soda Springs, Upper Badger
37 Creek, Spring Creek, Moody Creek, and other potential sites, which would
38 offer hydropower, extended irrigation seasons, flood protection, and recre-
39 ation.
40 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
41 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
42 the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislature respectfully urges the

2
1 United States Congress, the Department of the Interior, and the Bureau of
2 Reclamation to cooperate with the State of Idaho to:
3 (1) Move forward immediately with fast-track research on the feasi-
4 bility and desirability of small reservoir raises at Minidoka Dam, Jackson
5 Lake, and American Falls Dam;
6 (2) Support planning and design work for new medium-sized reservoirs
7 across eastern Idaho;
8 (3) Facilitate creative financing options through federal funds and
9 public-private partnerships; and
10 (4) Assist in the completion of an updated basin study in the next two to
11 three years to identify priority projects.
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the State of Idaho commits to the goal of
13 "750k by 2100" by creating 750,000 acre-feet of new water storage by the year
14 2100 to turn today's water shortage into tomorrow's water security.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
16 tives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this
17 Memorial to the President of the United States, to the Secretary of the Inte-
18 rior, to the Commissioner of the Bureau of Reclamation, to the President of
19 the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of Congress, and
20 to the congressional delegation representing the State of Idaho in the Con-
21 gress of the United States.
