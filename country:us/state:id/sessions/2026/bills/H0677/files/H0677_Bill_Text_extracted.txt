Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 677
BY RESOURCES AND CONSERVATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO FISH AND GAME; AMENDING SECTION 36-1101, IDAHO CODE, TO PROHIBIT
3 BLINDS NEAR NON-NATURAL WATERING SITES; AMENDING SECTION 36-1401,
4 IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING VIOLATIONS; AND DECLARING AN

6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 36-1101, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 36-1101. TAKING OF WILDLIFE UNLAWFUL EXCEPT BY STATUTE OR COMMISSION
10 RULE OR PROCLAMATION -- METHODS PROHIBITED -- EXCEPTIONS. (a) It is unlaw-
11 ful, except as may be otherwise provided by Idaho law, including this title
12 or commission rules or proclamations promulgated pursuant thereto, for any
13 person to take any of the game animals, birds or furbearing animals of this
14 state.
15 (b) Except as may be otherwise provided under this title or commission
16 rules or proclamations promulgated pursuant thereto, it is unlawful for any
17 person to:
18 1. Hunt from Motorized Vehicles. Hunt any of the game animals or game
19 birds of this state from or by the use of any motorized vehicle, in-
20 cluding any unmanned aircraft system, except as provided by commission
21 rule; provided however, that the commission shall promulgate rules
22 which shall allow a physically disabled person to apply for a special
23 permit which would allow the person to hunt from a motorized vehicle
24 which is not in motion. A physically disabled person means a person who
25 has lost the use of one (1) or both lower extremities or both hands, or is
26 unable to walk two hundred (200) feet or more unassisted by another per-
27 son, or is unable to walk two hundred (200) feet or more without the aid
28 of a walker, cane, crutches, braces, prosthetic device or a wheelchair,
29 or is unable to walk two hundred (200) feet or more without great diffi-
30 culty or discomfort due to one (1) or more of the following impairments:
31 neurological, orthopedic, respiratory, cardiac, arthritic disorder,
32 blindness, or the loss of function or absence of a limb.
33 The commission shall specify the form of application for and design of
34 the special permit which shall allow a physically disabled person to hunt
35 from a motorized vehicle which is not in motion. No fee shall be charged
36 for the issuance of the special permit and the issuance of a special permit
37 shall not exempt a person from otherwise properly purchasing or obtaining
38 other necessary licenses, permits and tags in accordance with this title and
39 rules promulgated pursuant thereto. The special permit shall not be trans-
40 ferable and may only be used by the person to whom it is issued. A person who
41 has been issued a special permit which allows a physically disabled person
42 to hunt from a motorized vehicle not in motion shall have that permit promi-

3 requested by a conservation officer of the department of fish and game. A
4 person possessing a special permit shall not discharge any firearm from or
5 across a public highway. In addition to other penalties, any unauthorized
6 use of the special permit shall be grounds for revocation of the permit.
7 2. Molest with Motorized Vehicles. Use any motorized vehicle, includ-
8 ing any unmanned aircraft system, to molest, stir up, rally or drive in
9 any manner any of the game animals or game birds of this state.
10 3. Communicate from Aircraft. Make use of aircraft, including any un-
11 manned aircraft system, in any manner to spot or locate game animals,
12 game birds or furbearing animals of this state from the air and communi-
13 cate the location or approximate location thereof by any signals what-
14 soever, whether radio, visual or otherwise, to any person then on the
15 ground.
16 4. Hunt from Helicopter. Make use of any helicopter in any manner in
17 the taking of game or loading, transporting, or unloading hunters,
18 game or hunting gear in any manner except when such use is at recognized
19 airports or airplane landing fields, or at heliports which have been
20 previously established on private land or which have been established
21 by a department or agency of the federal, state or local government or
22 when said use is in the course of emergency or search and rescue oper-
23 ations. Provided however, that nothing in this chapter shall limit
24 or prohibit the lawful control of wolves or predatory or unprotected
25 animals through the use of helicopters when such measures are deemed
26 necessary by federal or state agencies in accordance with existing laws
27 or management plans.
28 5. Hunt with Aid of Aircraft. Make use of any aircraft, including any
29 unmanned aircraft system, to locate any big game animal for the purpose
30 of hunting those animals during the same calendar day those animals were
31 located from the air. Provided however, that nothing in this chapter
32 shall limit or prohibit the lawful control of wolves or predatory or un-
33 protected wildlife through the use of aircraft when such measures are
34 deemed necessary by federal or state agencies in accordance with exist-
35 ing laws or management plans.
36 6. Artificial Light. Hunt any animal or bird except raccoon by the aid
37 of a spotlight, flashlight or artificial light of any kind. The act of
38 casting or throwing, after sunset, the beam or rays of any spotlight,
39 headlight or other artificial light capable of utilizing six (6) volts
40 or more of electrical power upon any field, forest or other place by any
41 person while having in his possession or under his control any uncased
42 firearm or contrivance capable of killing any animal or bird, shall
43 be prima facie evidence of hunting with an artificial light. Provided
44 nothing in this subsection shall apply where the headlights of a motor
45 vehicle, operated and proceeding in a normal manner on any highway or
46 roadway, cast a light upon animals or birds on or adjacent to such high-
47 way or roadway and there is no intent or attempt to locate such animals
48 or birds. Provided further, nothing in this subsection shall prevent
49 the hunting of unprotected or predatory wildlife with the aid of artifi-
50 cial light when such hunting is for the purpose of protecting property

3
1 or livestock, is done by landowners or persons authorized in writing by
2 them to do so and is done on property they own, lease or control; and pro-
3 vided further that the hunting and taking of unprotected or predatory
4 wildlife with the aid of artificial light on public lands is authorized
5 after obtaining a permit to do so from the director. The director may,
6 for good cause, refuse to issue such permit.
7 Other provisions of this subsection notwithstanding, the commission
8 may establish rules allowing the hunting of raccoon with the aid of an
9 artificial light.
10 7. Regulation of Dogs.
11 (A) No person shall make use of a dog for the purpose of pursuing,
12 taking or killing any of the big game animals of this state except
13 as otherwise provided by rules of the commission.
14 (B) Any person who is the owner of, or in possession of, or who har-
15 bors any dog found running at large and which is actively tracking,
16 pursuing, harassing or attacking, or which injures or kills deer
17 or any other big game animal within this state shall be guilty as
18 provided in section 36-1401(a)1.(F), Idaho Code. It shall be no
19 defense that such dog or dogs were pursuing said big game animals
20 without the aid or direction of the owner, possessor, or harborer.
21 (C) Any dog found running at large and which is actively tracking,
22 pursuing, harassing, attacking or killing deer or any other big
23 game animal may be destroyed without criminal or civil liability
24 by the director, or any peace officer, or other persons authorized
25 to enforce the Idaho fish and game laws.
26 8. Attempt to Take Simulated Wildlife.
27 (A) Attempt to take, by firearm or any other contrivance capable
28 of killing an animal or bird, simulated wildlife in violation of
29 any of the provisions of this title or commission rules applica-
30 ble to the taking of the wildlife being simulated, when the sim-
31 ulated wildlife is being used by a conservation officer or other
32 person authorized to enforce Idaho fish and game laws or rules pro-
33 mulgated pursuant thereto. No person shall be found guilty of vio-
34 lating either this subparagraph, or subparagraph (B) of this para-
35 graph, provided that no other law or rule has been violated.
36 (B) Any person pleading guilty to, convicted of or found guilty
37 for attempting to take simulated wildlife within this state shall
38 be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished as provided in
39 either subsection (c) or (e) of section 36-1402, Idaho Code, and
40 shall pay restitution in an amount of no less than fifty dollars
41 ($50.00) for the repair or replacement of the simulated wildlife.
42 9. Devices Accessed via Internet.
43 (A) No person shall shoot at or kill any bird or animal in Idaho,
44 wild or domestic, including domestic cervidae governed under the
45 provisions of chapter 37, title 25, Idaho Code, with any gun or
46 other device accessed and controlled via an internet connection.
47 Accessing, regulating access to, or regulating the control of a
48 device capable of being operated in violation of this paragraph
49 shall be prima facie evidence of an offense under this paragraph.

4
1 (B) Any person pleading guilty to, convicted of or found guilty of
2 a violation of this paragraph shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and
3 shall be punished as provided in section 36-1402, Idaho Code.
4 10. Construct blinds near water troughs or stock ponds.
5 (A) No person shall construct a permanent or temporary blind, pit,
6 platform, or tree stand within three hundred (300) feet of a water
7 trough or stock pond on public land within the state of Idaho.
8 (B) Any person pleading guilty to, convicted of, or found guilty
9 of a violation of this paragraph shall be guilty of a misdemeanor
10 and shall be punished as provided in section 36-1402, Idaho Code.
