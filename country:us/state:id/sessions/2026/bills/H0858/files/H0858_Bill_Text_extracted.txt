LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 858
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO CHILD CUSTODY; AMENDING SECTION 32-717D, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTAB-
3 LISH RESTRICTION ON COURT-APPOINTED PROFESSIONALS; AND DECLARING AN
4 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 32-717D, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 32-717D. COURT-APPOINTED PROFESSIONALS -- PARENTING COORDINATOR --
9 RESTRICTIONS. (1) Provided that a court has entered a judgment or an order
10 establishing child custody in a case, the court may order the appointment of
11 a parenting coordinator to perform such duties as authorized by the court,
12 consistent with any controlling judgment or order of a court relating to
13 the child or children of the parties, and as set forth within the order of
14 appointment. The court shall direct the parenting coordinator to provide
15 a status report to the court at a time and in a manner as determined by the
16 court. Provided however, that the court shall require the parenting coor-
17 dinator to provide a minimum of one (1) status report to the court at least
18 once every six (6) months. At any time during the period of appointment, the
19 court, on its own initiative, or upon request of the parenting coordinator or
20 either party, may hold a status conference to review the continued appoint-
21 ment of the coordinator and/or the status of the case.
22 (2) Qualification, selection, appointment, termination of appoint-
23 ment, and prescribed duties and responsibilities of a parenting coordinator
24 shall be based upon standards and criteria as adopted by the Idaho supreme
25 court. Provided however, that standards and criteria for qualification
26 and selection of a parenting coordinator, as adopted by the Idaho supreme
27 court, shall not apply to a parenting coordinator selected and agreed to by
28 the parties. In addition, as a condition of any appointment, a parenting
29 coordinator shall:
30 (a) Be neutral to the dispute and to the parties;
31 (b) Be either selected pursuant to agreement of the parties or ap-
32 pointed by the court; and
33 (c) Prior to any appointment, and at their own cost, have submitted to a
34 criminal history check through any law enforcement office in the state
35 providing such service. The criminal history check shall include a
36 statewide criminal identification bureau, the federal bureau of inves-
37 tigation criminal history check, the national crime information center
38 and the statewide sex offender register. A record of all background
39 checks shall be maintained in the office of the supreme court of the
40 state of Idaho with a copy going to the applicant and shall be available
41 for review by the court considering a parenting coordinator appointment
42 prior to an appointment.

2
1 (3) In addition to those duties as authorized by the court pursuant to
2 the order of appointment, the responsibilities of a parenting coordinator
3 shall include collaborative dispute resolution in parenting. The parenting
4 coordinator shall act to empower the parties in resuming parenting controls
5 and decision-making, and minimize the degree of conflict between the parties
6 for the best interests of the children.
7 (4) The court shall allocate the fees and costs of the parenting coordi-
8 nator between the parties and may enter an order against either or both par-
9 ties for the reasonable costs, fees and disbursements of the parenting coor-
10 dinator. Any dispute regarding payment of the fees and costs of the parent-
11 ing coordinator shall be subject to review by the court upon request of the
12 parenting coordinator or either party.
13 (5) The court may award attorney's fees and costs to the prevailing
14 party on a motion to set aside or modify the decision of a parenting coordi-
15 nator.
16 (6) Court-appointed professionals shall serve in an advisory capacity
17 only. Final determinations regarding custody, parenting time, and deci-
18 sion-making authority shall remain with the court. Any report relied on by
19 the court shall be disclosed to both parents, and the author must be made
20 available for cross-examination upon request.
21 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
22 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
23 July 1, 2026.
