36 CHAPTER 33
37 DENTURITRY
38 54-3301. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
39 (1) "Board" means the state board of dentistry.
40 (2) "Denture" means any removable full upper or lower prosthetic dental
41 appliance to be worn in the human mouth.
42 (3) "Denturist" means a person licensed pursuant to this chapter to en-
43 gage in the practice of denturitry.

4
1 (4) "Division" means the division of occupational and professional li-
2 censes.
3 (5)(a) "Practice of denturitry" means:
4 (i) The making, fitting, constructing, altering, reproducing,
5 or repairing of a full upper or lower removable prosthetic den-
6 ture, the repairing of a removable partial upper or lower pros-
7 thetic denture, the furnishing or supplying of such a denture di-
8 rectly to a person, or advising the use of any such denture; and
9 (ii) The taking or making, or providing advice, assistance, or fa-
10 cilities respecting the taking or making, of any impression, bite,
11 cast, or design preparatory to or for the purpose of making, con-
12 structing, fitting, furnishing, supplying, altering, repairing,
13 or reproducing any full upper or lower removable prosthetic den-
14 ture.
15 (b) The practice of denturitry within the context of this chapter re-
16 quires that all work except cast framework or work required to meet the
17 needs of the homebound be performed on the licensee's premises.
18 54-3302. LICENSE TO PRACTICE REQUIRED. A person shall hold a license
19 for the practice of denturitry in order to:
20 (1) Engage in or offer to engage in the practice of denturitry; or
21 (2) Use in connection with such person's name the word "denturist" or
22 any other words, letters, abbreviations, or insignia implying that such per-
23 son is engaged in the practice of denturitry.
24 54-3303. EXCEPTIONS. The prohibitions of this chapter do not apply to:
25 (1) A person interning under the supervision of a denturist;
26 (2) The practice of dentistry or medicine by persons authorized to do so
27 by this state;
28 (3) A student of denture technology in pursuit of clinical studies un-
29 der an approved school program; or
30 (4) A denture technician, as defined by board rule, performing services
31 under the direction of a licensed denturist or licensed dentist when the ser-
32 vice does not involve contact with the intended user.
33 54-3304. PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES. Licensed denturists shall not:
34 (1) Perform procedures that would alter any oral tissues or natural
35 teeth;
36 (2) Insert or fit immediate dentures in the mouth of the intended
37 wearer;
38 (3) Diagnose or treat any abnormalities of any human mouth;
39 (4) Prescribe any drugs or treatment for any oral or medical diseases;
40 or
41 (5) Construct or fit orthodontic appliances.
42 54-3305. APPLICATION FOR LICENSE. Upon application and payment of the
43 appropriate fee, the board shall issue a license to practice denturitry to
44 any applicant who has:
45 (1) Satisfactorily completed the examination approved by the board;

5
1 (2) Completed formal training of no less than two (2) years' duration at
2 an educational institution accredited by a national or regional accrediting
3 agency recognized by the Idaho state board of education, the curriculum of
4 which includes courses in oral pathology, physiology, head and oral anatomy,
5 clinical microbiology, clinical jurisprudence, asepsis, and first aid for
6 minor office emergencies; and
7 (3) Completed an internship of at least two (2) years under the supervi-
8 sion of a licensed denturist or have equivalent experience as established by
9 board rule prior to the filing of the application.
10 54-3306. FEES. The board shall be entitled to charge and collect the
11 following fees:
12 (1) An application fee of not to exceed three hundred dollars ($300);
13 (2) An initial license fee of not to exceed six hundred dollars ($600);
14 and
15 (3) A renewal fee of not to exceed two thousand dollars ($2,000).
16 54-3307. SUSPENSION OR REVOCATION OF LICENSE. (1) The board shall have
17 the power to refuse to issue a license, suspend or revoke a license, or place
18 a licensed person on probation for a period specified by the board and sub-
19 ject to such conditions as the board shall impose or to reprimand or censure a
20 licensee for any of the following:
21 (a) Conviction or finding of guilt in this or any other state of a
22 felony or any other crime deemed relevant in accordance with section
23 67-9411(1), Idaho Code;
24 (b) Incompetence or gross negligence in the practice of denturitry;
25 (c) Fraud or misrepresentation in the practice of denturitry;
26 (d) Use of any narcotic or dangerous drug or intoxicating liquor to an
27 extent that such use impairs the ability to safely conduct the practice
28 of denturitry; or
29 (e) A violation of this chapter or any rule adopted pursuant to the pro-
30 visions of this chapter.
31 (2) The division shall have the power to examine and inspect the place
32 of business of any licensed denturist at a reasonable time and in a reason-
33 able manner to assure compliance with this chapter.
34 (3) The board shall have the right to establish standards of conduct and
35 practice and the power to enforce such standards with monetary penalties,
36 revocation or suspension of licensure, or both.
37 54-3308. REVOCATION OF LICENSE STAYS ELIGIBILITY. A denturist whose
38 license has been revoked either by the board or a similar body of another
39 state shall not be eligible to apply for a license until one (1) year after
40 the date of revocation.
41 54-3309. RENEWAL OR REINSTATEMENT OF LICENSE. All licenses issued
42 pursuant to the provisions of this chapter shall be subject to biennial re-
43 newal and shall expire unless renewed in the manner prescribed by the board
44 regarding applications for renewal, continuing education, and fees. Li-
45 cense renewal and reinstatement shall be in accordance with section 67-2614,
46 Idaho Code.

34 54-3313. INJUNCTION PROCEDURE. Whenever any person, corporation,
35 partnership, or association of any kind or nature violates any of the pro-
36 visions of this chapter, the board, without regard to criminal prosecution,
37 may maintain an action in the name of the state of Idaho to enjoin said per-
38 son, corporation, partnership, or association from any further violations,
39 such action to be brought either in the county in which said acts are claimed
40 to have been or are being committed, in the county where the defendant re-
41 sides, or in Ada county. Upon the filing of a verified complaint in the
42 district court, the court, if satisfied that the acts complained of have been
43 or probably are being or may be committed, may issue an injunction pendente
44 lite without bond, upon request of the board, enjoining the defendant from
45 the commission of any such act or acts constituting said violations. A copy
46 of said complaint shall be served on the defendant, and the proceedings shall
47 thereafter be conducted as in any other similar civil action. If the com-
