Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 883
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 10, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE AD-
3 DITION OF A NEW SECTION 33-1035, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS RE-

9 SECTION 1. That Chapter 10, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
10 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
11 ignated as Section 33-1035, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 33-1035. SCHOOL DISTRICT EARNED AUTONOMY. (1) Notwithstanding any
13 provision of law to the contrary, a school district that satisfies the re-
14 quirements set forth in this section may spend its allocated state funding,
15 including but not limited to discretionary funds, categorical funds, and
16 salary apportionment, in the manner it deems best serves its students and
17 schools.
18 (2) A school district shall have the option to participate in the flexi-
19 bility provided in this section if it:
20 (a) Meets the following academic benchmarks:
21 (i) The school district's composite proficiency rate on the Idaho
22 standards achievement test is in the top twenty percent (20%) of
23 school districts statewide or the school district exceeds the
24 state proficiency average by at least fifteen (15) percentage
25 points in both English language arts and math;
26 (ii) Seventy-five percent (75%) or more of the school district's
27 K-3 students score proficient on the spring administration of the
28 Idaho reading indicator or the school district demonstrates a fif-
29 teen percent (15%) reduction in students scoring below basic annu-
30 ally; and
31 (iii) The school district maintains an adjusted cohort graduation
32 rate that is three (3) percentage points higher than the state av-
33 erage or the percentage of graduates who earn a college and career
34 readiness indicator that includes career technical education com-
35 pletion, an associate's degree, qualifying advance placement, an
36 international baccalaureate, or a dual credit course is five (5)
37 percentage points higher than the state average;
38 (b) Provides the following evidence demonstrating financial stability
39 for each of the previous three (3) fiscal years:
40 (i) An unqualified audit opinion or a qualified opinion qualified
41 solely on the basis of not reporting the actuarial value of the
42 public employee retirement system of Idaho sick leave plan pur-

3 (ii) An audit devoid of significant findings and conditions, ma-
4 terial weaknesses, or significant internal control deficiencies;
5 and
6 (iii) An audit that does not include a going concern disclosure in
7 the notes or an explanatory paragraph within the audit report.
8 (3) Participating school districts shall be exempt from the reporting
9 requirements, including those established through rulemaking authority,
10 of sections 33-119, 33-135, 33-1002B, 33-1004E, 33-1028(1)(f) and (2),
11 33-1405, 33-1632, 33-1805, 33-1811, 33-2004, and 33-4805, Idaho Code, with-
12 out incurring a penalty or a reduction in funding.

27 SECTION 2. That Chapter 52, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
28 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
29 ignated as Section 33-5207A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
30 33-5207A. PUBLIC CHARTER SCHOOL EARNED AUTONOMY. (1) Notwithstanding
31 any provision of law to the contrary, a public charter school that is not a
32 virtual school, as defined in section 33-5202A, Idaho Code, and that sat-
33 isfies the requirements set forth in section 33-5218(2)(b), (e), and (g),
34 Idaho Code, may spend its allocated state funding, including but not limited
35 to discretionary funds, categorical funds, and salary apportionment, in the
36 manner it deems best serves students and the public charter school. Partici-
37 pation shall not require a revision to the public charter school's charter or
38 performance certificate.
39 (2) Participating public charter schools shall be exempt from report-
40 ing requirements established in statute or through rulemaking authority
41 that are not federally mandated or required for public school finance
42 purposes. This exemption includes but is not limited to reporting require-
43 ments established pursuant to sections 33-119, 33-135, 33-1002B, 33-1004E,
44 33-1028(1)(f) and (2), 33-1405, 33-1632, 33-1805, 33-1811, 33-2004, and
45 33-4805, Idaho Code, without incurring a penalty or a reduction in funding.
46 (3) Participating public charter schools shall automatically maintain
47 spending flexibility from year to year, except that the state department of
48 education may revoke eligibility if:
