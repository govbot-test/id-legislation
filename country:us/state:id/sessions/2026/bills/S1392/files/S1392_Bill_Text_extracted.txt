LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1392
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO MORTICIANS, FUNERAL DIRECTORS, AND EMBALMERS; AMENDING SEC-
3 TION 54-1143, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING DEFINITIVE
4 IDENTIFICATION AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL CORRECTION; AND DECLARING AN
5 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 54-1143, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 54-1143. RIGHT TO RELY -- DEFINITIVE IDENTIFICATION. (1) Any person
10 signing a funeral service agreement or cremation authorization form or any
11 other authorization for disposition, whether part of a prearranged funeral
12 plan or at time of death, shall be deemed to warrant the truthfulness of any
13 facts set forth therein, including the identity of the deceased whose re-
14 mains are sought to be buried or cremated and the signer's authority to order
15 such disposition.
16 (2) A funeral establishment, cemetery, or crematory establishment
17 shall have the right to rely on such authorization and shall have authority
18 to dispose of human remains upon the receipt of an authorization form signed
19 by the decedent or by the person having the right to control disposition as
20 set forth in section 54-1142, Idaho Code, or upon authorization by the county
21 coroner pursuant to section 31-2802, Idaho Code, as long as such funeral
22 establishment, cemetery, or crematory establishment also complies with the
23 provisions of subsections (3) and (4) of this section. There shall be no lia-
24 bility of a funeral establishment, cemetery or crematory establishment that
25 disposes of human remains pursuant to such authorization, or that releases
26 or disposes of the remains pursuant to such authorization.
27 (3) Prior to making an application for cremation, a funeral establish-
28 ment, cemetery, or crematory establishment shall verify that a definitive
29 identification of the decedent has been made by an attending physician,
30 physician assistant, advanced practice professional nurse, or the county
31 coroner pursuant to the provisions of section 39-260, Idaho Code.
32 (4) Prior to performing a cremation, a funeral establishment, ceme-
33 tery, or crematory establishment shall verify that the decedent to be
34 cremated is the same individual who was definitively identified as provided
35 for in subsection (3) of this section.
36 (5) For the purposes of this section, "definitive identification"
37 means to verify an individual's identity definitively or with high confi-
38 dence through a method such as fingerprinting, DNA analysis, government-is-
39 sued photographic identification, or a visual identification by a legal next
40 of kin to confirm that an individual is accurately identified.

2
1 (6) There shall be no liability for a funeral establishment, cemetery,
2 or crematory establishment that disposes of human remains in compliance with
3 the provisions of this section.
4 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
5 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
6 July 1, 2026.
