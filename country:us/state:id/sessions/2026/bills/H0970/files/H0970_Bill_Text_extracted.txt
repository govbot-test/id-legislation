22 SECTION 2. That Section 67-6610D, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
23 amended to read as follows:
24 67-6610D. FOREIGN CONTRIBUTIONS, FOREIGN INDEPENDENT EXPENDITURES,
25 AND FOREIGN ELECTIONEERING COMMUNICATIONS, AND FOREIGN INFLUENCE PROHIB-
26 ITED.
27 (1)(a) A Except as provided in paragraph (b) of this subsection, a for-
28 eign national shall not make a contribution, directly or indirectly, to
29 any candidate, political committee, or measure or, make electioneer-
30 ing communications or, make independent expenditures, or direct, dic-
31 tate, control, or directly or indirectly participate in the decision-
32 making process related to any election campaign, including any deci-
33 sions concerning the making of contributions or expenditures to influ-
34 ence an election campaign. A foreign national shall not solicit, di-
35 rectly or indirectly, the making of a contribution or expenditure by an-
36 other person to influence an election campaign, and no candidate or po-
37 litical committee shall knowingly or willfully receive, solicit, or ac-
38 cept contributions from any foreign national, whether directly or indi-
39 rectly.
40 (b) A foreign national, pursuant to subsection (2)(e) of this section,
41 may make contributions and independent expenditures if such contri-
42 butions or expenditures originate entirely from funds generated by
43 such entity's United States operations and all decisions concerning
44 such contributions or expenditures, except for setting overall budget
45 amounts, are made by individuals who are United States citizens or per-
46 manent residents.
47 (2) As used in this section, "foreign national" means:
48 (a) An individual who is not a citizen of the United States and is not
49 lawfully admitted for permanent residence;

27 SECTION 3. That Chapter 66, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
28 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
29 ignated as Section 67-6610E, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
30 67-6610E. RESTRICTIONS ON COLLECTION AND RELEASE OF DONOR INFORMATION
31 -- PENALTY. (1) Lawful donors to a tax-exempt organization possess a right
32 of privacy in their donations. Any investigation of an alleged violation of
33 section 67-6607 or 67-6610D, Idaho Code, must occur in a manner that shields
34 the identity of lawful donors as much as possible. The secretary of state,
35 county clerks, and other state or local governmental entities may not col-
36 lect or require the submission of information on the identity of any donor
37 to a tax-exempt organization other than those directly related to an alleged
38 violation of section 67-6607 or 67-6610D, Idaho Code.
39 (2) The secretary of state, county clerks, and other state or local gov-
40 ernmental entities may not disclose to the public, or to another government
41 official not directly involved in the investigation, information revealing
42 the identity of any donor to a tax-exempt organization, unless the informa-
43 tion is regarding the identity of a donor that engaged in conduct prohibited
44 by section 67-6607 or 67-6610D, Idaho Code, after a final determination has
45 been made confirming that such violation occurred.
46 (3) Knowing or willful violations of this section shall be punishable
47 as a misdemeanor.
