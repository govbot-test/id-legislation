LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 124
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND SUPPORTING WILDLIFE CROSSINGS.
3 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
4 WHEREAS, Idaho is home to abundant populations of big game species, in-
5 cluding mule deer, elk, and pronghorn, that require intact, long-distance
6 seasonal migrations to access critical habitat for survival; and
7 WHEREAS, more than 50 big game migration corridors have been mapped
8 across the state, demonstrating that landscape connectivity is vital to
9 Idaho's wildlife and outdoor heritage; and
10 WHEREAS, preserving and promoting big game migration corridors con-
11 tributes to Idaho's robust economy and outdoor traditions by promoting
12 healthy wildlife populations that support hunting, outfitting, and other
13 outdoor recreation industries; and
14 WHEREAS, significant numbers of mule deer, elk, and pronghorn crossing
15 Idaho's highways pose a danger to both wildlife and humans, leading to ele-
16 vated rates of wildlife-vehicle collisions that result in preventable mor-
17 tality for both wildlife and humans; and
18 WHEREAS, each year, hundreds of wildlife-vehicle collisions endanger
19 human lives and cause millions of dollars in property damage and insurance
20 costs; and
21 WHEREAS, wildlife-vehicle collisions pose a clear risk to public
22 safety, but such risk may be mitigated by certain infrastructure projects;
23 and
24 WHEREAS, where appropriate and supported by the local community,
25 strategic investments in wildlife crossings, such as overpasses, under-
26 passes, and related fencing, have proven highly effective in reducing
27 wildlife-vehicle collisions by over 80% while maintaining the integrity of
28 migration corridors, enabling wildlife to travel between summer and winter
29 ranges; and
30 WHEREAS, multiple infrastructure projects in Idaho facilitating the
31 safe movement of wildlife across roadways, including the first wildlife
32 overpass constructed in the state, the Cervidae Peak Wildlife Overpass over
33 Highway 21, have shown significant benefits to wildlife movement and to re-
34 ductions in wildlife-vehicle collisions, demonstrating the effectiveness
35 of crossing structures and providing a basis of success to build on else-
36 where; and
37 WHEREAS, engaging with local communities and providing affected stake-
38 holders with relevant information regarding associated infrastructure
39 projects for facilitating wildlife movement across highways is an important
40 component of implementing initiatives to conserve intact migration corri-
41 dors in Idaho; and
42 WHEREAS, a proactive approach to promoting intact wildlife migration
43 corridors, including coordination with state agencies and local commu-

2
1 nities, will contribute to the long-term viability of Idaho's wildlife
2 resources and enhance public safety.
3 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
4 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of Rep-
5 resentatives concurring therein, that the Legislature strongly supports the
6 advancement of public safety and conservation of Idaho's big game migration
7 corridors through the development and implementation of wildlife crossings
8 where appropriate and supported by local communities.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this resolution be transmitted to
10 the congressional delegation representing the State of Idaho in the Congress
11 of the United States, the Governor of the State of Idaho, the Director of the
12 Idaho Department of Fish and Game, and the Director of the Idaho Transporta-
13 tion Department.
