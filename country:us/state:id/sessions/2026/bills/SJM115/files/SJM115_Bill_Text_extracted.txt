LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 115
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESEN-
3 TATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, THE CONGRESSIONAL
4 DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED
5 STATES, AND THE DIRECTOR OF THE UNITED STATES FISH AND WILDLIFE SERVICE.
6 We, your Memorialists, the Senate and the House of Representatives
7 of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
8 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
9 WHEREAS, Idaho has a plethora of ecosystems, such as Harriman State
10 Park and Island Park, which lie within an 11,000-acre wildlife refuge in the
11 greater Yellowstone Ecosystem, Camas National Wildlife Refuge southwest of
12 Harriman, Deer Flat National Wildlife Refuge on Idaho's eastern border, and
13 Kootenai National Wildlife Refuge in north Idaho, that provide vital habitat
14 for a variety of species, of which migratory birds are particularly impor-
15 tant; and
16 WHEREAS, the Migratory Bird Treaty Act of 1918 (MBTA) was implemented
17 to prevent the extinction of birds due to unsustainable feather-trading and
18 hunting practices and makes it illegal to "take," which includes harassing,
19 pursuing, capturing, killing, selling, trading, buying, and transporting,
20 among other activities, a protected migratory bird species, of which there
21 are over 1,000, without prior authorization by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife
22 Service (USFWS); and
23 WHEREAS, one such protected migratory bird species is the pelican,
24 which is also classified as a predator species as they feast on fish; and
25 WHEREAS, a once-endangered species, the pelican has made a remarkable
26 return and has established a stronghold in Idaho's abundant wetland refuge
27 ecosystems such that its population is beginning to impact the overall use
28 and health of the ecosystem; and
29 WHEREAS, Idaho's streams and rivers are renowned by fishermen across
30 the nation, particularly the Henry's Fork River that meanders for eight
31 miles through Harriman State Park, attracting fly-fishers from all four
32 corners of the state and nation thereby generating revenues for the local
33 community and business owners; and
34 WHEREAS, predatory birds, such as pelicans, have been documented to
35 harvest more fish in Idaho than fisherman; and
36 WHEREAS, pelicans have been documented to consume over three pounds of
37 fish per day; and
38 WHEREAS, over 30% of the electronic fish tags that originated in Idaho's
39 renowned Silver Creek were documented by the Idaho Department of Fish and
40 Game (IDFG) in pelican feces at the Minidoka National Wildlife Refuge pel-
41 ican rookeries; and
42 WHEREAS, millions of sportsmen's dollars are spent by IDFG to create,
43 promote, and enhance Idaho's fishing opportunities; and

2
1 WHEREAS, the pelican, a protected migratory bird under the MBTA, is ac-
2 tively managed and bred by the USFWS and cannot be taken, harassed, killed,
3 or otherwise disturbed without prior authorization by the USFWS; and
4 WHEREAS, the rigorous protections of the MBTA and other environmental
5 acts have successfully ensured this predatory bird's survival but are now
6 hindering the survival of other species and conflicting with areas' tradi-
7 tional use due to the birds' overpopulation.
8 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
9 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of
10 Representatives concurring therein, that the Legislature supports and urges
11 the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service to curtail, or allow curtailment of, the
12 number of pelicans within Idaho.
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature supports and urges the fed-
14 eral government to compensate the State of Idaho for loss of its resources
15 due to the pelican's overpopulation.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate be, and she is
17 hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this Memorial to the
18 President of the United States, the President of the Senate and the Speaker
19 of the House of Representatives of Congress, the congressional delegation
20 representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the United States, and the
21 Director of the United States Fish and Wildlife Service.
