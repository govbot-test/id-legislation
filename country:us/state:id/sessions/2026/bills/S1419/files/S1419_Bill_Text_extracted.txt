26 IDAHO CHILD CARE PROGRAM
27 56-2501. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
28 (1) "Child care" means care, control, supervision, or maintenance of a
29 child provided for compensation by an individual, other than a parent of the
30 child, for less than twenty-four (24) hours in a day.
31 (2) "Department" means the Idaho department of health and welfare.
32 (3) "Eligible child" means a child under thirteen (13) years of age who
33 is either a citizen of or lawfully living in the United States. However, a
34 child who is thirteen (13) years of age or older remains eligible if the child
35 is under court supervision or is verified by either a mental health profes-
36 sional or licensed medical professional to be physically or mentally unable
37 to care for himself.
38 (4) "FPG" means the federal poverty guidelines published annually by
39 the United States department of health and human services.
40 (5) "Intentional program violation" means an intentionally false or
41 misleading action or statement that is one (1) of the following:

2
1 (a) An individual makes a false or misleading statement to the depart-
2 ment, either orally or in writing, in order to participate in the pro-
3 gram;
4 (b) An individual misrepresents or conceals one (1) or more facts to the
5 department, either orally or in writing, to participate in the program;
6 (c) An individual fails repeatedly or substantially to comply with rel-
7 evant state or federal laws or rules promulgated under this chapter;
8 (d) An individual knowingly violates any term of the individual's
9 provider agreement; or
10 (e) A provider knowingly fails to meet the qualifications specifically
11 required in relevant federal law, in this chapter, in chapter 11, title
12 39, Idaho Code, or the rules promulgated under any of the forgoing laws.
13 (6) "Parent" means a person responsible for a child because of birth,
14 adoption, marriage, legal guardianship, or foster care.
15 (7) "Program" means the Idaho child care program.
16 (8) "Provider" means an individual, organization, agency, or other en-
17 tity providing child care.
18 (9) "SMI" means the current state median income estimates determined
19 for the low income home energy assistance program.
20 56-2502. CREATION OF PROGRAM. There is hereby created in the depart-
21 ment the Idaho child care program, which, subject to legislative appro-
22 priation, shall use federal child care funds distributed to the states, as
23 well as any related state matching funds and maintenance-of-effort funds,
24 to improve the quality of child care in Idaho for all children and assist
25 low-income families in Idaho who are working or participating in education
26 or training activities with help paying for child care, to promote parental
27 choice to empower working parents to make decisions regarding the child care
28 services that best suit the needs of individual families, and to maximize
29 options available to parents. The department shall administer the program
30 in a manner consistent with this chapter and with all applicable federal
31 requirements.
32 56-2503. PARENT QUALIFYING ACTIVITIES. (1) Parents in a child's
33 household must participate in qualifying activities as provided in this sub-
34 section to be eligible for assistance under this chapter:
35 (a) For a two (2) parent household:
36 (i) At least one (1) parent is employed or self-employed no less
37 than an average of thirty (30) hours per week per month; and
38 (ii) The other parent is either:
39 1. Employed no less than an average of twenty (20) hours per
40 week per month; or
41 2. Enrolled and participating in a recognized or accredited
42 school, training program, or institution of higher educa-
43 tion for a minimum of twenty (20) hours each week, as deter-
44 mined by the department; or
45 (b) For a one (1) parent household, which shall include a household
46 where the parent is under eighteen (18) years of age and is not the head
47 of the household:

3
1 (i) The parent is employed or self-employed no less than an aver-
2 age of thirty (30) hours per week per month; or
3 (ii) The parent is enrolled and participating in a recognized or
4 accredited school, training program, or institution of higher ed-
5 ucation for a minimum of twenty (20) hours each week, as determined
6 by the department.
7 (2) In a two (2) parent household, an incapacitated parent is not re-
8 quired to participate in a qualifying activity pursuant to subsection (1) of
9 this section if the other parent participates in a qualifying activity. An
10 incapacitated parent in a one (1) parent household is not eligible for the
11 program. As used in this subsection, "incapacitated parent" means a parent
12 who is determined by a physician, physician assistant, nurse practitioner,
13 or clinical nurse specialist licensed under the laws of this state to be un-
14 fit, incapable, or significantly limited in the ability to provide adequate
15 care for the child or ward. A parent's disability diagnosis alone does not
16 qualify a parent as incapacitated without the licensed provider making the
17 findings required by this subsection.
18 (3) The following restrictions shall apply to training or education ac-
19 tivities:
20 (a) Online classes shall not be counted as a qualifying activity under
21 this chapter;
22 (b) Persons who are attending postbaccalaureate classes with no other
23 qualifying activity shall not qualify for assistance under this chap-
24 ter; and
25 (c) No more than forty-eight (48) months of postsecondary education
26 shall count as a qualifying activity.
27 56-2504. APPLICATION -- ELIGIBILITY -- REDETERMINATION. (1) A family
28 who resides in the state of Idaho with no immediate intention of leaving the
29 state may apply to be considered for assistance under this chapter for an el-
30 igible child.
31 (2) An applicant's family assets cannot exceed five hundred thousand
32 dollars ($500,000), as certified by the applicant.
33 (3) When applying, a family's income cannot exceed one hundred thirty-
34 five percent (135%) of FPG for a family of the same size.
35 (4) During the eligibility period, a family's income cannot exceed
36 eighty-five percent (85%) of the SMI for a family of the same size.
37 (5) At least every twelve (12) months, the department shall redeter-
38 mine eligibility for assistance under this chapter. At redetermination, if
39 a family's income exceeds one hundred thirty-five percent (135%) but is less
40 than one hundred seventy percent (170%) of FPG for a family of the same size,
41 the family may be eligible to receive a graduated phaseout of child care as-
42 sistance that is limited to up to twelve (12) months of assistance following
43 the completion of redetermination.
44 56-2505. AUTHORITY TO CREATE A WAITING LIST AND ADJUST ELIGIBIL-
45 ITY. (1) As necessary to administer the program, the department may estab-
46 lish a waiting list for the program. If the department establishes a waiting
47 list, the department shall give priority to the following categories:
48 (a) Families who are homeless;

4
1 (b) Families caring for a child with a disability;
2 (c) Foster families;
3 (d) Families receiving temporary assistance for needy families from
4 the department;
5 (e) Families receiving any child welfare preventative services from
6 the department designed to reduce or eliminate the need for out-of-home
7 placement of a child by the department; and
8 (f) Households where at least one (1) parent is nineteen (19) years of
9 age or younger.
10 56-2506. REQUIREMENTS FOR PROVIDERS. (1) Providers who receive assis-
11 tance on behalf of eligible children under this chapter shall enter into and
12 abide by the terms of a provider agreement with the department and shall gen-
13 erate documentation at the time of service sufficient to support the reim-
14 bursement for child care services. Providers are required to keep the fol-
15 lowing child care records:
16 (a) Proof of enrollment for each eligible child, with authentication of
17 a parent;
18 (b) Proof of attendance each day with authentication of a parent,
19 guardian, or preapproved designee dropping off and retrieving each
20 child;
21 (c) Billing and receipts;
22 (d) Policies or manuals developed by the provider governing procedures
23 for billing, attendance, attendance and enrollment tracking and veri-
24 fication, copayment collection, and operating hours;
25 (e) All rates, fees, and costs of any kind charged by the provider; and
26 (f) Any other related records required by the department for adminis-
27 tration of the program.
28 (2) The department shall be permitted access at any time of business op-
29 erations to the child care facilities of a provider who is receiving assis-
30 tance on behalf of an eligible child and to monitor and inspect the facility.
31 The department shall be permitted to access, inspect, review, copy, or re-
32 move any child care records required by this chapter. A provider shall im-
33 mediately produce records or provide access upon request by the department.
34 Records required by this section shall be kept available by the child care
35 facility at all times for a minimum of three (3) years.
36 (3) Any business entity or individual providing child care who is re-
37 ceiving assistance on behalf of an eligible child under this chapter shall be
38 registered as a business entity with the Idaho secretary of state.
39 (4) A provider shall receive payment for assistance on behalf of an eli-
40 gible child only after substantiating child care attendance and documenting
41 legitimate services provided in a manner and on a cadence prescribed by the
42 department.
43 (5) A provider is prohibited from paying, directly or indirectly or
44 overtly or covertly, for an eligible child to attend the provider's child
45 care facility.
46 (6) Providers are responsible for ensuring families pay the determined
47 child care costs and may not waive these costs.
48 (7) Providers receiving assistance on behalf of an eligible child shall
49 not be considered employees of the department and shall not hold themselves

1 out as employees of the department by virtue of their participation in the

3 (8) If a provider is an individual, the provider must be either a citi-
4 zen of or lawfully living in the United States. If the provider is a business
5 entity, the provider must not knowingly hire or employ an employee who is not
6 either a citizen of or lawfully living in the United States.
7 (9) A provider who is receiving assistance on behalf of an eligible
8 child under this chapter shall develop policies, procedures, and controls
9 to govern fiscal responsibility. A provider operating a daycare center, as
10 defined in 39-1102, Idaho Code, shall:
11 (a) In the case of a nonprofit provider, cause an annual financial audit

14 accounting principles and maintain financial statements and documenta-
15 tion of business-related expenses.
16 56-2507. PROGRAM INTEGRITY. (1) When the department has reasonable
17 suspicion to believe that a provider seeks payment on behalf of an eligible
18 child in a manner contrary to state or federal law, the department is autho-
19 rized to immediately suspend payment and conduct an investigation. Reasons
20 to suspend payment include but are not limited to:
21 (a) Any claims for child care services for which the department has a
22 reasonable suspicion were not provided;
23 (b) Child care services not documented by the provider as required by
24 law or the provider agreement;
25 (c) Child care services provided contrary to state or federal law or the
26 provider agreement;
27 (d) Failure to maintain records or provide immediate access to records
28 as required by law;
29 (e) The department believes a provider is paying for an eligible child
30 to attend the provider's child care facility in violation of law or the
31 provider agreement; or
32 (f) The department has determined that an intentional program viola-
33 tion has occurred and has provided notice of this determination.
34 (2) If the department determines that an intentional program violation
35 has occurred, the department shall send written notice of the determination
36 to the family or provider stating the reasons for the determination. The
37 decision of the department may be appealed to the office of administrative
38 hearings within twenty-eight (28) days of the date of the issuance of the no-
39 tice to the family or provider. The department may continue any suspension
40 of payment for the duration of the administrative appeal.
41 (a) The office of administrative hearings shall, within twenty-eight
42 (28) days of receipt of the appeal, review the full record regarding the
43 department's decision and hold a hearing. After the hearing, the ad-
44 ministrative law judge shall submit a written recommendation to the de-
45 partment and the family or provider. The recommendation by the adminis-
46 trative law judge either to affirm or reverse the department's decision
47 shall be based on the full record of the department's decision and rel-
48 evant state and federal law. The recommendation by the administrative
49 law judge shall be in writing and accompanied by a reasoned opinion.

7 (ii) Reverses its initial decision, then there shall be no fur-

18 (b) Any family or provider who is established to have committed a second

20 period of no less than two (2) years but up to a period of five (5) years,
21 as determined by the department.
22 (c) Any family or provider who is established to have committed a third
23 or subsequent intentional program violation shall be permanently inel-
24 igible for the program.
25 (4) When the department establishes an intentional program violation,
26 it shall verify that any other benefits an individual receives pursuant to
