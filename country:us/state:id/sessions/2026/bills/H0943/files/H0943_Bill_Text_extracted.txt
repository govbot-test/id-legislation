10 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
11 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS. The Legislature finds and declares
12 that:
13 (1) Reliable access to motor fuels and refined petroleum products is
14 essential to Idaho's economy, public safety, and quality of life, including
15 but not limited to agriculture, food production, manufacturing, construc-
16 tion, transportation, emergency services, health care, and commerce.
17 (2) Idaho does not currently have an in-state petroleum refining capac-
18 ity and therefore relies heavily on interstate transportation systems and
19 out-of-state fuel supplies to meet the needs of residents and businesses.
20 (3) Disruptions, constraints, and increased costs affecting in-
21 terstate fuel supply chains can result in higher fuel prices, reduced
22 availability, and economic harm to Idaho consumers, employers, and critical
23 services, particularly in a rural state where travel and transportation are
24 necessary.
25 (4) A resilient and diversified fuel supply system, including access to
26 multiple interstate pipeline routes and sources, reduces vulnerability to
27 supply disruptions, market volatility, and external policy or infrastruc-
28 ture constraints.
29 (5) Energy security is a matter of public safety and economic stability
30 for the State of Idaho. Ensuring access to reliable, sufficient, and diver-
31 sified supplies of motor fuels and refined petroleum products is essential
32 to protect Idaho residents, businesses, agriculture, emergency services,
33 and critical infrastructure from supply disruptions, price volatility, and
34 external market or policy constraints.
35 (6) Investment in modern, expanded, and reliable interstate pipeline
36 infrastructure promotes fuel supply stability, enhances energy security,
37 supports economic growth, and benefits consumers through improved market
38 access and competition.
39 (7) High-cost pipeline infrastructure projects require substantial
40 up-front capital investment and long-term regulatory certainty, and such
41 projects may not proceed without targeted incentives and a predictable,
42 timely permitting framework.

5 SECTION 2. That Title 63, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
6 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
7 ter 46, Title 63, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
8 CHAPTER 46
9 PROTECTING IDAHO PIPELINE ENERGY SECURITY ACT
10 63-4601. SHORT TITLE. This chapter shall be known and may be cited as
11 the "Protecting Idaho Pipeline Energy Security Act" or the "PIPES Act."
12 63-4602. DEFINITIONS. For the purposes of this chapter:
13 (1) "Construction" means the development, installation, or creation of
14 a new pipeline or related facilities, including laterals, interconnections,
15 pumping stations, storage facilities, and associated infrastructure.
16 (2) "Developer" means any person or business entity, including an
17 owner, operator, developer, or investor, who engages in or proposes to en-
18 gage in the interstate transmission, storage, distribution, or delivery of
19 crude oil or refined petroleum products and is eligible for any incentive
20 provided in this chapter.
21 (3) "Energy security" means the availability of reliable, sufficient,
22 and diversified fuel supplies necessary to support economic activity, pub-
23 lic safety, and essential services within the state.
24 (4) "Pipeline" means real and personal property, including gathering
25 lines and fixtures, owned or operated to facilitate the interstate trans-
26 mission, storage, distribution, or delivery of crude oil or refined petro-
27 leum products through a pipe that has an outside diameter of at least six (6)
28 inches.
29 (5) "Refined petroleum products" means gasoline, diesel fuel, aviation
30 fuel, and other liquid products derived from crude oil that are intended for
31 use as motor fuel.
32 (6) "Strategic pipeline infrastructure project" means any phased or
33 multistage project where the developer certifies to the state tax commission
34 in writing that the aggregate investment will meet or exceed fifty million
35 dollars ($50,000,000) in new investment in construction of a new pipeline
36 that:
37 (a) Is not a replacement of an existing pipeline;
38 (b) Crosses into Idaho from an adjacent state or nation from which no
39 pipeline already exists; and
40 (c) Delivers crude oil or refined petroleum products to Idaho cus-
41 tomers.
42 63-4603. INCOME TAX CREDIT, PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTION, AND SALES TAX EX-
43 EMPTION FOR STRATEGIC PIPELINE INFRASTRUCTURE PROJECTS.
44 (1)(a) For taxable years beginning on or after January 1, 2027, a devel-
45 oper that places a strategic pipeline infrastructure project into ser-
46 vice is allowed an investment tax credit against any applicable tax im-

1 posed by chapter 30, title 63, Idaho Code. The credit shall be equal to
2 thirty-five percent (35%) of the new infrastructure-related revenue.
3 For purposes of this section, "infrastructure-related revenue" means
4 an increase, if any, in the amount of revenue, in a taxable year, that is
5 attributable to the strategic pipeline infrastructure project.
6 (b) The credit allowed under this section may be claimed annually
7 through the taxable year that the earliest of the following occurs:
8 (i) The economic life of the strategic pipeline infrastructure
9 project has ended, provided that a developer shall provide the
10 state tax commission with such information as the state tax com-
11 mission may require to determine and certify the economic life of
12 the strategic pipeline infrastructure project;
13 (ii) Twenty (20) years have passed from the time the initial
14 credit was claimed; or
15 (iii) The developer has recovered fifty percent (50%) of the total
16 cost of the strategic pipeline infrastructure project through the
17 credit provided in this section.
18 (c) A developer seeking a credit under this section shall, for each tax-
19 able year, file a report with the state tax commission showing the in-
20 frastructure-related revenue. The report shall be prepared by an inde-
21 pendent certified public accountant that verifies the infrastructure-
22 related revenue described in paragraph (a) of this subsection in accor-
23 dance with generally accepted accounting principles.
24 (2) The construction of a pipeline on or after January 1, 2027, that is
25 the result of a completed strategic pipeline infrastructure project in Idaho
26 shall be exempt from real and personal property taxation.
27 (3) Beginning on January 1, 2027, the sale, lease, or purchase of tan-
28 gible property that is incorporated into the pipeline system as a component
29 part of the strategic pipeline infrastructure project is exempt from any
30 sales or use tax under Idaho law.
31 63-4604. REGULATORY RELIEF FOR STRATEGIC PIPELINE INFRASTRUCTURE
32 PROJECTS. (1) Except as otherwise required by federal law, a strategic
33 pipeline infrastructure project shall be exempt from any additional state
34 permitting, licensing, certification, environmental review, or any other
35 applicable statutory requirement.
36 (2) If a state agency is charged with implementing or enforcing any fed-
37 eral regulatory review and approval process in connection with a strategic
38 pipeline infrastructure project, such agency shall expedite and prioritize
39 such review or approval to the extent permitted by federal law.
40 (3) This section shall not be construed to prevent any county or city
41 permitting, licensing, certification, environmental review, or any other
42 applicable requirements.
43 (4) In the event a state agency, county, or city fails to issue a fi-
44 nal decision on an application for a permit, license, certification, or any
45 other applicable regulatory authorization within sixty (60) days, or within
46 the timeline mandated by federal law, whichever is shorter, the application
47 for any permit, license, certification, or other similar application shall
48 be deemed approved.

4
1 (5) State agencies, counties, cities, and all other political subdi-
2 visions shall give priority consideration to strategic pipeline infrastruc-
3 ture projects that materially enhance Idaho's energy security by:
4 (a) Increasing access to diversified interstate fuel supply sources;
5 (b) Improving redundancy or resiliency of fuel transportation infra-
6 structure; or
7 (c) Reducing Idaho's reliance on a limited number of supply corridors
8 or single-source markets.
9 63-4605. APPLICATION FOR FEDERAL GRANTS AND FINANCING PROGRAMS. State
10 agencies are authorized and directed to seek out all available federal
11 grants, state or federal low-interest or no-interest financing options, or
12 other similar state or federal programs that may be available to financially
13 or otherwise assist a business seeking to invest in and complete a strategic
14 pipeline infrastructure project.
15 SECTION 3. This act shall be in full force and effect on and after Jan-
