17 57-1901. CREATION OF OFF-ROAD MOTOR VEHICLE ACCOUNT -- PURPOSE. (1)
18 There is hereby created and established in the state treasury an account to
19 be known as the "off-road motor vehicle account" to which shall be credited
20 or deposited all moneys accruing for the purposes of the account.
21 (2) The purposes for which moneys in the account may be used shall be to:
22 (a) acquire Acquire, purchase, improve, repair, maintain, furnish, and
23 equip off-road motor vehicle facilities and sites or areas used by off-
24 road vehicles on public or private land, and to assist;
25 (b) Assist with the enforcement of laws and regulations governing the
26 use of off-road vehicles in the state of Idaho.;
27 (c) Secure special leases, use licenses, or recreation easements or
28 permits, or to purchase land under private, state, or federal ownership
29 to be used for public recreational off-road vehicle activity;
30 (d) Secure, maintain, construct, or develop trails and other public
31 recreational facilities for off-road vehicle use on private, state, and
32 federal lands;
33 (e) Finance the formulation and implementation under the park and
34 recreation board's direction of an off-the-road rider education pro-
35 gram; and
36 (f) Acquire applicable federal matching funds.
37 (3) The park and recreation board is charged with the administration of
38 the account for the purposes specified herein.
39 (4) The provisions of section 67-4228, Idaho Code, are made applicable
40 for the provisions of to this section.
41 (5) All claims against the account shall be examined, audited, and al-
42 lowed in the same manner now or hereafter provided by law for claims against

1 the state, except that the board is hereby empowered to enter into agreements
2 with legal governmental agencies in Idaho and Idaho nonprofit entities, for

3 SECTION 3. That Section 67-7107A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
4 amended to read as follows:
5 67-7107A. STATE SNOWMOBILE AVALANCHE FUND -- STATE SNOWMOBILE
6 AVALANCHE FUND COMMITTEE. (1) There is hereby established in the state
7 treasurer's office an account to be known and designated as the "state snow-
8 mobile avalanche fund." This fund shall receive one dollar ($1.00) from each
9 snowmobile certificate of number fee, one dollar ($1.00) from each rental
10 certificate of number fee, and one dollar ($1.00) from each nonresident
11 snowmobile user certificate issued by vendors, as provided for in section
12 67-7106, Idaho Code. Any amounts credited to this fund that remain or are
13 unencumbered after three (3) fiscal years shall be returned to the state
14 treasurer for deposit in the state snowmobile fund. This fund shall be man-
15 aged by the state snowmobile avalanche fund committee.
16 (2) The board shall appoint a state snowmobile avalanche fund committee
17 (SSAFC) of five (5) members. The membership of the SSAFC shall consist of the
18 director of the United States forest service national avalanche center, or
19 his designee, who shall chair the SSAFC, and one (1) active snowmobiler from
20 each of the following districts: north Idaho, central Idaho, south Idaho,
21 and east Idaho. These four (4) regional members of the SSAFC shall be ap-
22 pointed by the board from a list of no fewer than two (2) persons nominated by
23 the Idaho state snowmobile association, and said members shall serve a term
24 of four (4) years, except that the term of the initial appointees shall com-
25 mence on the date of appointment and shall be staggered such that at least
26 one (1) position is up for appointment each year. Each member of the SSAFC
27 must be a qualified elector of the state, maintaining a residence and domi-
28 cile within the district of appointment. For purposes of SSAFC membership
29 and representation, the four (4) districts shall be as follows:
30 (a) North Idaho consists of Boundary, Bonner, Kootenai, Benewah,
31 Latah, Shoshone, Clearwater, Nez Perce, Lewis, and Idaho counties.
32 (b) Central Idaho consists of Adams, Washington, Valley, Payette, Gem,
33 Boise, Canyon, Ada, Elmore, and Owyhee counties.
34 (c) South Idaho consists of Lemhi, Custer, Butte, Camas, Blaine, Good-
35 ing, Lincoln, Jerome, Minidoka, Twin Falls, and Cassia counties.
36 (d) East Idaho consists of Clark, Fremont, Jefferson, Madison, Teton,
37 Bonneville, Bingham, Power, Bannock, Caribou, Bear Lake, Franklin, and
38 Oneida counties.
39 (3) The duties of the SSAFC include:
40 (a) Determining and directing distributions from the state snowmobile
41 avalanche fund, at least annually, to avalanche centers within the
42 state of Idaho or avalanche centers providing reporting services within
43 the state of Idaho operated and administered by the national avalanche
44 center, United States forest service, or United States department of
45 agriculture. The SSAFC may require that applications or proposals be
46 submitted for review and consideration from avalanche centers seeking
47 funds from the state snowmobile avalanche fund. The SSAFC must require
48 the funds so distributed be used for existing avalanche center oper-
49 ations and maintenance within or for the benefit of areas within the

4
1 state of Idaho, which include: avalanche forecaster labor; website
2 and information technology improvements and maintenance; snowmobiles
3 and related equipment; remote weather station installation and upkeep;
4 avalanche education costs; and safety equipment;
5 (b) Representing the best interests of the snowmobile users in the dis-
6 trict from which each member is appointed; and
7 (c) Advising existing avalanche centers in Idaho and registered non-
8 profit organizations that support them as to whether current projects
9 meet the needs of snowmobile users in that area.
10 (4) The SSAFC shall be compensated as provided in section 59-509(b),
11 Idaho Code.
12 SECTION 4. That Section 67-7113, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
13 amended to read as follows:
14 67-7113. VIOLATIONS -- ACCOUNTABLE FOR PROPERTY DAMAGE. (1) Unless
15 otherwise provided in this chapter, any person who violates any provision
16 of this chapter, or any rule promulgated by the department pursuant to this
17 chapter, shall be guilty of an infraction and shall be punished by a fine of
18 one hundred dollars ($100).
19 (2) In addition thereto, the operator and/or owner of the snowmobile or
20 OHV shall be responsible and held accountable to the owner of any lands where
21 trees, shrubs, or other property have been damaged as the result of travel
22 over their premises.
23 (3) Whenever any owner of public or private land gives notice of and
24 imposes closures, restrictions, rules, or prohibitions in relation to the
25 use of the owner's land by snowmobile or OHV operators, any person who vio-
26 lates the same shall be guilty of trespass, punishable as provided by section
27 18-7008, Idaho Code. For the purposes of this subsection, "notice" means ac-
28 tual or constructive notice.
