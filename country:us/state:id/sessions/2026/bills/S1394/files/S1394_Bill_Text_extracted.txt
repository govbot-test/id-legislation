LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1394
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO CHILD CUSTODY; AMENDING SECTION 32-717E, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE
3 PROVISIONS REGARDING SUPERVISED VISITATION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
4 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 32-717E, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 32-717E. SUPERVISED VISITATION -- SUPERVISED ACCESS PROVIDERS --
9 RECORD CHECKS. (1) If the court orders supervised visitation in proceedings
10 between parents in which custody and parenting time of a minor child is at
11 issue, the court shall state in its decision the reasons for supervised visi-
12 tation. The supervised visitation order shall be reviewed within forty-nine
13 (49) calendar days of the initial order. Absent good cause shown by clear and
14 convincing evidence, supervised visitation shall not be ordered or contin-
15 ued.
16 (2) All supervised visitation orders shall include clear, objective
17 conditions for transition to unsupervised parenting time and periodic re-
18 views at intervals not to exceed ninety (90) days, unless otherwise ordered
19 by the court for good cause.
20 (3) Preference shall be given to unpaid supervised access providers
21 agreed on by the parties.
22 (4) In cases in which a court has ordered that contact between a per-
23 son and one (1) or more children shall take place only in the presence of an
24 approved provider, or where the court has ordered supervised exchanges or
25 transfers of one (1) or more children, the court may appoint an individual
26 or entity as a supervised access provider to provide such supervised access
27 or to facilitate such exchanges or transfers. The qualifications and duties
28 of supervised access providers shall be as specified in rules adopted by the
29 supreme court. A supervised access provider who is paid for providing super-
30 vised access services shall, prior to acting in such capacity and at his or
31 her own cost, submit to a fingerprint-based criminal history check through
32 any law enforcement office in the state providing such service. The crimi-
33 nal history check shall include a statewide criminal identification bureau
34 check, federal bureau of investigation criminal history check, child abuse
35 registry check, adult protection registry check and statewide sex offender
36 registry check. A record of all background checks shall be maintained in the
37 office of the supreme court of the state of Idaho.
38 (5) A parent's legitimate inability to attend supervised visitation
39 shall not be deemed a voluntary relinquishment of time.
40 (6) Nothing in this section shall be construed to permit the use of
41 supervised visitation as a punitive measure, leverage, or a substitute for
42 proof of unfitness.

2
1 (7) The provisions of this section apply only to custody and parenting
2 time determinations between parents and shall not modify, limit, or super-
3 sede proceedings under chapter 16, title 16, Idaho Code.
4 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
5 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
6 July 1, 2026.
