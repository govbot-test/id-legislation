LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 579
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO JUSTIFIABLE HOMICIDE; AMENDING SECTION 18-4009, IDAHO CODE, TO
3 REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING JUSTIFIABLE HOMICIDE; AND DECLARING AN
4 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 18-4009, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 18-4009. JUSTIFIABLE HOMICIDE BY ANY PERSON. (1) Homicide is justifi-
9 able when committed by any person in any of the following cases:
10 (a) When resisting any attempt to murder any person, or to commit a
11 felony, or to do some great bodily injury upon any person;
12 (b) When committed in defense of habitation, a place of business or
13 employment, occupied vehicle, property or person, against one who
14 manifestly intends or endeavors, by violence or surprise, to commit a
15 felony, or against one who manifestly intends and endeavors, in a vio-
16 lent, riotous or tumultuous manner, to enter the habitation, place of
17 business or employment or occupied vehicle of another for the purpose of
18 offering violence to any person therein;
19 (c) When committed in the lawful defense of such person, or of a wife
20 or husband, parent, child, master, mistress or servant of such person
21 another person, when there is reasonable ground to apprehend a design to
22 commit a felony or to do some great bodily injury, and imminent danger of
23 such design being accomplished; but such person, or the person in whose
24 behalf the defense was made, if he was the assailant or engaged in mor-
25 tal combat, must really and in good faith have endeavored to decline any
26 further struggle before the homicide was committed; or
27 (d) When necessarily committed in attempting, by lawful ways and means,
28 to apprehend any person for any felony committed, or in lawfully sup-
29 pressing any riot, or in lawfully keeping and preserving the peace.
30 (2) For purposes of subsection (1)(b) of this section, a person who un-
31 lawfully and by force or by stealth enters or attempts to enter a habitation,
32 place of business or employment or occupied vehicle is presumed to be doing
33 so with the intent to commit a felony.
34 (3) For purposes of this section:
35 (a) "Habitation" means any building, inhabitable structure or con-
36 veyance of any kind, whether the building, inhabitable structure or
37 conveyance is temporary or permanent, mobile or immobile, including a
38 tent, and is designed to be occupied by people lodging therein at night,
39 and includes a dwelling in which a person resides either temporarily or
40 permanently or is visiting as an invited guest, and includes the cur-
41 tilage of any such dwelling.

2
1 (b) "Place of business or employment" means a commercial enterprise or
2 establishment owned by a person as all or part of the person's liveli-
3 hood or is under the owner's control or under control of an employee or
4 agent of the owner with responsibility for protecting persons and prop-
5 erty and shall include the interior and exterior premises of the place
6 of business or employment.
7 (c) "Vehicle" means any motorized vehicle that is self-propelled and
8 designed for use on public highways to transport people or property.
9 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
10 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
11 July 1, 2026.
