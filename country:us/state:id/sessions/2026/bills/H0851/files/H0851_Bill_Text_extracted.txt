LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 851
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO LIBEL AND SLANDER; AMENDING SECTION 6-712, IDAHO CODE, TO PRO-
3 VIDE FOR PUNITIVE DAMAGES IN CASES OF LIBEL PER SE AND SLANDER PER SE,
4 TO DEFINE A TERM, AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL CORRECTION; AND DECLARING AN
5 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 6-712, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 6-712. RETRACTION BY NEWSPAPER, RADIO OR TELEVISION BROADCASTING STA-
10 TION OR NETWORK OF STATIONS -- LIMIT OF RECOVERY. (1) In Except as provided
11 in subsection (2) of this section, in any action for damages for the publi-
12 cation of a libel, in a newspaper, or of a slander or libel by radio or tele-
13 vision broadcast, plaintiff shall recover no more than actual damages unless
14 a correction be demanded and be not published or broadcast, as hereinafter
15 provided. Plaintiff shall serve upon the publisher, at the place of publi-
16 cation or broadcaster at the place of broadcast, a written notice specify-
17 ing the statements and the manner in which said statements are claimed to be
18 slanderous or libelous and demanding that the same be corrected. Said notice
19 and demand must be served within twenty (20) days after knowledge of the pub-
20 lication or broadcast of the statement claimed to be slanderous or libelous.
21 If a correction be demanded within said period and be not published or broad-
22 cast in substantially as conspicuous a manner in said newspaper or on said
23 radio or television broadcasting station as were the statements claimed to
24 be slanderous or libelous, in a regular issue thereof published or broad-
25 cast within three (3) weeks after such service, plaintiff, if he proves such
26 notice, demand and failure to correct, and if his cause of action be main-
27 tained, may recover general, actual and exemplary damages; provided that
28 no exemplary damages may be recovered unless the plaintiff shall prove that
29 defendant made the publication or broadcast with actual malice, and actual
30 malice shall not be inferred or presumed from the publication or broadcast.
31 A correction published or broadcast in substantially as conspicuous a manner
32 in said newspaper or on said broadcasting station as the statements claimed
33 in the complaint to be defamatory, prior to receipt of a demand therefor,
34 shall be of the same force and effect as though such correction has been pub-
35 lished or broadcast within three (3) weeks after a demand therefor.
36 (2) In any action for damages for the publication of a libel in a news-
37 paper or of a slander or libel by a radio or television broadcast that con-
38 stitutes libel per se or slander per se, a plaintiff may recover punitive
39 damages regardless of whether a retraction has been published or broadcast.
40 As used in this section, "libel per se or slander per se" means a malicious
41 defamation, as described in section 18-4801, Idaho Code, that is knowingly

2
1 and deliberately false, or in reckless disregard of the truth, without re-
2 gard to context.
3 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
4 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
5 July 1, 2026.
