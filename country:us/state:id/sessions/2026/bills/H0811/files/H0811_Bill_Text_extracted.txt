16 SECTION 1. That Section 50-3101, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
17 amended to read as follows:
18 50-3101. PURPOSE, RELATIONSHIP WITH OTHER LAWS AND SHORT TITLE. (1)
19 The purpose of this chapter is:
20 (a) To encourage the funding and construction of regional community in-
21 frastructure in advance of actual developmental growth that creates the
22 need for such additional infrastructure;
23 (b) To provide a means for the advance payment of development impact
24 fees established in chapter 82, title 67, Idaho Code, and the community
25 infrastructure that may be financed thereby; and
26 (c) To create additional financial tools and financing mechanisms that
27 allow new growth to more expediently pay for itself.
28 (2) Only community infrastructure to be publicly owned by this state or
29 a political subdivision thereof may be financed pursuant to this chapter.
30 (3) A community infrastructure district may only be formed pursuant to
31 this chapter only by a city in the city's incorporated area, or by a county
32 in an area contained within a city's comprehensive plan with the city's con-
33 sent, or by a county when the district is outside of a city's comprehensive
34 plan.
35 (4) A community infrastructure district may be formed only after (i)
36 prior review and approval by the governing body of each county or city in
37 which the district is proposed to be located of a petition requesting the
38 formation of the district, and (ii) the necessary approvals for site de-
39 velopment under the local land use planning act, sections 67-6501 et seq.,
40 Idaho Code, and the planning and zoning ordinances of each county and city
41 in which the district is proposed to be located have been obtained; provided
42 however, that where there will be phased development, approvals obtained

2
1 for the first phase of site development shall be sufficient for the initial
2 creation and organization of the district. The formation of a district
3 pursuant to this chapter shall not prevent the exercise by a county, city
4 or other political subdivision of any of its powers on the same basis as on
5 all other land within its jurisdiction. Notwithstanding the formation of a
6 district, the development of real property located within the district shall
7 remain subject to the provisions of chapter 65, title 67, Idaho Code, and
8 the applicable planning and zoning ordinances of the counties and cities in
9 which the district is located. The formation of a district pursuant to this
10 chapter shall not prevent the subsequent establishment of other districts or
11 the improvement or assessment of land within the district by a county, city
12 or other political subdivision.
13 (5) This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Community In-
14 frastructure District Act."

25 SECTION 5. That Chapter 31, Title 50, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
26 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
27 ignated as Section 50-3122, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
28 50-3122. FIRE PROTECTION SERVICES AND EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES FEES
29 FOR DISTRICTS FORMED AFTER JANUARY 1, 2026. (1) A community infrastructure
30 district formed after January 1, 2026, may impose an annual fee for fire pro-
31 tection services and emergency medical services if authorized by the govern-
32 ing body in the district development agreement.
33 (2) A fee under this section may be imposed on each residential property
34 for a term not to exceed five (5) years, commencing upon the transfer of title
35 to the first residential homeowner. Notwithstanding any provision of law to
36 the contrary, the district shall be exempt from providing public notice when
37 a fee is imposed on a property for the first time. The existence, terms, and
38 any applicable amounts of the fee shall be disclosed in the property disclo-
39 sure notice and shall be signed and acknowledged by any purchaser prior to
40 purchase. The obligation to pay the fee shall run with the property, and any
41 subsequent owner who acquires title within the original five (5) year period
42 shall be responsible for payment of the fee for the remainder of that five (5)
43 year term. No fee under this section may be extended or renewed beyond its
44 initial five (5) year term, and each such fee shall expire at the end of the
45 five (5) year term without further notice or action. Upon the expiration of
46 the fee for a property as provided in this section, the district shall have no
47 further authority to impose a fee under this section on that same property.
48 (3) The initial amount of the fee and any provision for annual increases
49 shall be as set forth in the district development agreement, provided that

8
1 the amount of the fee shall not increase by more than two percent (2%) in any
2 year.
3 (4) The fee shall be collected and accounted for at the time and in the
4 form and manner as property taxes are collected and accounted for under the
5 laws of this state. All revenues from the fee shall be kept separate from
6 other district funds in a dedicated fund and may accumulate from year to year
7 to ensure the availability of moneys to pay for or offset the costs of fire
8 protection services and emergency medical services as authorized in this
9 section.
10 (5) The district board shall budget and disburse the fee proceeds col-
11 lected pursuant to this section solely to pay for or offset the costs of
12 fire protection services and emergency medical services for the district.
13 Permissible expenditures shall include but not be limited to salaries and
14 benefits of fire protection services personnel and emergency medical ser-
15 vices personnel; procurement, maintenance, repair, or replacement of fire
16 protection services or emergency medical services equipment; fire protec-
17 tion services training; emergency medical services training; and other
18 operational expenses necessary for fire protection and emergency response.
19 Notwithstanding the provisions of this subsection, the fee may be pledged to
20 bonds issued by the district if a portion of the net proceeds of bonds is used
21 to fund the costs of fire protection services and emergency medical services
22 for the district.
23 (6) The district may enter into agreements with cities, fire protection
24 districts, ambulance districts, or other service providers to furnish fire
25 protection services or emergency medical services to the district and may
26 distribute fee revenues to such service providers pursuant to such agree-
27 ments or as provided in the district development agreement. In no event
28 shall the district be required to pay any amount in excess of collections
29 from the fees.
