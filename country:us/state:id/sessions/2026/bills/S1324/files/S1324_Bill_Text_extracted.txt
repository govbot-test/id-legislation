3 VISIONS REGARDING CANDIDATE QUALIFICATIONS FOR THE OFFICE OF COURT
4 OF APPEALS JUDGE; AMENDING SECTION 34-615, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PRO-
5 VISIONS REGARDING CANDIDATE QUALIFICATIONS FOR THE OFFICE OF SUPREME

9 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
10 SECTION 1. That Section 1-2404, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
11 amended to read as follows:
12 1-2404. NUMBER OF JUDGES -- QUALIFICATIONS -- CONDUCT AND DISCIPLINE
13 -- TERM -- ELECTION -- SELECTION -- COMPENSATION. (1) The court of appeals
14 shall consist of four (4) judges, and shall sit in panels of not less than
15 three (3) judges each.
16 (2) To be elected or appointed to the office of judge of the court of ap-
17 peals a person must, at the time of such election or appointment, meet all of
18 the following qualifications:
19 (a) Be at least thirty (30) years of age;
20 (b) Be a citizen of the United States and an elector of the state of
21 Idaho;
22 (c) Have been a legal resident of the state of Idaho for at least two (2)
23 continuous years immediately preceding such election or appointment;
24 (d) Have been in good standing as an active or judicial member of the
25 Idaho state bar for at least two (2) continuous years immediately pre-
26 ceding such election or appointment; and
27 (e) Have held a license to practice law or held a judicial office in one
28 (1) or more jurisdictions for at least ten (10) continuous years immedi-
29 ately preceding such election or appointment.
30 (3) No person who has been appointed by the governor to fill a vacancy on
31 the court of appeals is eligible to be a candidate for election to that same
32 seat at the next election held for such seat following the appointment.
33 (4) For purposes of this section, the following terms have the follow-
34 ing meanings:
35 (a) "Active," "judicial" and "good standing" have the same definitions
36 as those terms are given by rule 301 of the Idaho bar commission rules or
37 any successors to those rules;
38 (b) "Jurisdiction" means a state or territory of the United States, the
39 District of Columbia or any branch of the United States military; and
40 (c) "Elector" means one who is lawfully registered to vote.
41 (3) (5) A judge of the court of appeals shall be governed by the code of
42 judicial conduct as promulgated by the Idaho supreme court, and shall be sub-

2
1 ject to removal, discipline, or retirement pursuant to section 1-2103, Idaho
2 Code.
3 (4) (6)(a) Judges of the court of appeals shall be appointed by the gov-
4 ernor effective the first Monday of January, 1982, for the following
5 initial terms: one (1) judge shall be appointed for a term to expire on
6 the first Monday of January, 1985, one (1) judge shall be appointed for a
7 term expiring two (2) years later, and one (1) judge shall be appointed
8 for a term expiring two (2) further years later. Thereafter, the term of
9 office of a judge of the court of appeals shall be six (6) years.
10 (b) Vacancies in the office of judge of the court of appeals shall be
11 filled in the same manner as vacancies in the office of supreme court
12 justice or district judge.
13 (c) The positions of judges of the Idaho court of appeals shall first
14 be filled as vacancies. The judicial council shall submit to the gov-
15 ernor its recommendations for the offices at the earliest practicable
16 time after the effective date of this act. The governor may make the ap-
17 pointment at any time thereafter, to be effective the first Monday of
18 January, 1982, for the terms set forth in subsection (4) paragraph (a)
19 of this subsection.
20 (d) In making its nominations for the initial vacancies to be created
21 by this act, the Idaho judicial council shall submit the names of not
22 less than six (6) nor more than nine (9) qualified persons for the ini-
23 tial three (3) vacancies to be created by this act. Otherwise, the ju-
24 dicial council shall submit the names of not less than two (2) nor more
25 than four (4) persons for each vacancy. The governor shall appoint the
26 judges, identifying each appointment by the length of the term of ap-
27 pointment.
28 (e) Nominations and appointments to fill initial or subsequent vacan-
29 cies shall be made with due regard for balanced geographical membership
30 of the court of appeals.
31 (f) Subsequent terms of office of a judge who has been appointed to the
32 court of appeals shall be subject to a statewide nonpartisan election
33 to be held in the primary election next preceding the expiration of an
34 appointed term in the same method and manner as a justice of the supreme
35 court.
36 (g) A fourth judge of the court of appeals shall be appointed by the gov-
37 ernor effective the first Monday of January, 2009, for an initial term
38 to expire on the first Monday of January, 2013. Thereafter, the term of
39 office for this position shall be six (6) years. The judicial council
40 shall submit the names of not less than two (2) nor more than four (4)
41 persons for the initial vacancy in this position under the procedure set
42 forth in section 1-2102, Idaho Code. This position shall be subject to
43 all of the provisions relating to qualifications, removal, discipline,
44 retirement, filling of vacancies, election and compensation set forth
45 in this chapter.
46 (5) (7) Judges of the court of appeals, except for judges who have made
47 an election to remain in the public employee retirement system of Idaho pur-
48 suant to section 1-2011, Idaho Code, shall receive compensation upon retire-
49 ment as provided in chapter 20, title 1, Idaho Code.
