17 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-

19 63-3029N. IDAHO PARENTAL CHOICE TAX CREDIT. (1) There is hereby estab-
20 lished the Idaho parental choice tax credit.
21 (2) For the purpose of this section:
22 (a) "Academic instruction" means kindergarten through grade 12 in-
23 struction that includes, at a minimum, English language arts, mathemat-
24 ics, science, and social studies.
25 (b) "Eligible student" means:
26 (i) A full-time resident of Idaho who is five (5) to eighteen (18)
27 years of age; or
28 (ii) A child with disabilities requiring ancillary personnel as
29 defined in section 33-2001, Idaho Code, who is five (5) to twenty-
30 one (21) years of age.
31 (c) "Federal poverty level" means the federal poverty guidelines pub-
32 lished and updated annually by the United States department of health
33 and human services.
34 (d) "Nonpublic school" means a private school, microschool, or learn-
35 ing pod that provides academic instruction to eligible students, which
36 academic instruction may be provided in person, online, virtually, or
37 through any combination thereof, and such academic instruction is:
38 (i) Provided by an accredited school; or
39 (ii) Documented in a portfolio of evidence or learning record that
40 indicates the student's growth. Records of the instruction shall
41 be retained sufficient to establish that the instruction meets

2
1 the requirements of paragraph (a) of this subsection and shall be
2 available in the case of any audit by the state tax commission.
3 (e) "Parent" means an eligible student's parent, legal guardian, or
4 foster parent, if such foster parent is licensed and in good standing
5 pursuant to chapter 12, title 39, Idaho Code.
6 (f) "Qualified expenses" means kindergarten through grade 12 tuition
7 or fees for attending a nonpublic school, tutoring, nationally stan-
8 dardized assessments, assessments used to determine college admission,
9 advance placement examinations, industry-recognized certification ex-
10 ams, and preparatory courses for nationally standardized assessments.
11 "Qualified expenses" also means costs for textbooks, curricula used for
12 kindergarten through grade 12 academic instruction, and transporta-
13 tion costs to and from a facility incurred for the purposes of receiving
14 kindergarten through grade 12 academic instruction, including public
15 transportation, ridesharing, and the use of privately owned vehicles,
16 for which reimbursement shall use the mileage rate applicable to state
17 employee travel in effect at the time the travel occurs. "Qualified ex-
18 penses" shall not include expenses paid with funds received from a grant
19 under section 33-1031, Idaho Code, or reimbursement pursuant to section
20 33-4603, Idaho Code.
21 (3) The Idaho parental choice tax credit shall be administered as pro-
22 vided in this section. For tax year 2028 and subsequent tax years, there
23 shall be allowed a refundable tax credit up to five thousand dollars ($5,000)
24 per eligible student against the tax imposed by this chapter for any parent:
25 (a) Who incurs qualified expenses of up to five thousand dollars
26 ($5,000) per eligible student during the tax year, if each eligible
27 student is a dependent and is properly claimed on the parent's full-time
28 Idaho resident individual income tax return; and
29 (b) Who timely and properly files an application.
30 (4) The state tax commission shall prescribe forms for the purpose of
31 applying for and claiming the credit authorized by this section. The forms
32 shall identify the civil and criminal penalties provided in sections 63-3046
33 and 63-3075, Idaho Code, for providing false and fraudulent tax information
34 and shall require the parents to affirm under oath that the information sub-
35 mitted in the form is true and accurate. The forms shall also require any
36 parent who has previously received a credit authorized by this section to
37 certify and attach evidence that the parent completed the satisfaction and
38 engagement survey required under subsection (14) of this section. Begin-
39 ning in January 2029, and each year thereafter, the application period shall
40 open on January 15 and shall remain open for a period of sixty (60) days. The
41 state tax commission shall notify parents whether they will receive a credit
42 award. Such notification must be within thirty (30) days of the close of the
43 application period.
44 (5) The state tax commission shall calculate the total amount of cred-
45 its claimed in completed applications that qualify under subsection (3) of
46 this section. If the total amount does not exceed the annual maximum limit
47 provided in subsection (12) of this section, the state tax commission shall
48 re-open the application period to receive new applications. Such applica-
49 tions shall be accepted until August 15 or until the annual maximum limit is
50 reached, whichever is first.

2 ceived in 2029, the state tax commission shall give priority to parents whose
3 modified adjusted gross income as indicated on their most recently filed tax
4 return does not exceed three hundred percent (300%) of the federal poverty
5 level. Starting in the 2030 application period, the state tax commission
6 shall give priority status to applications from parents who received a

11 tion shall be increased to seven thousand five hundred dollars ($7,500) for
12 each eligible student up to twenty-one (21) years of age who is also con-
13 sidered a child with disabilities requiring ancillary personnel as defined
14 in section 33-2001, Idaho Code. Parents claiming an increased credit under
15 this subsection shall document the student's diagnosis or other determina-
16 tion made by an Idaho licensed health care provider or the student's eligi-
17 bility for services under an individualized education program in accordance
18 with the federal individuals with disabilities education act.
19 (8) The refundable tax credit provided in this section, including the
20 advance payment, shall not constitute Idaho taxable income.
21 (9) The application forms pursuant to subsection (4) of this section
22 shall permit parents to elect a onetime advance payment of the credit for
23 each eligible student. When a parent elects to receive an advance payment
24 pursuant to this subsection, the credit shall be issued in one (1) install-
25 ment, to be paid within sixty (60) days of notifying a parent of such credit
26 award but not later than August 30. The state tax commission shall approve
27 advance payments only for eligible students whose parents' modified ad-
28 justed gross income as indicated on their most recently filed tax return
29 does not exceed three hundred percent (300%) of the federal poverty level.
30 Advance payments made under this subsection may be spent only on qualified
31 expenses for which the parent may claim a credit under the provisions of this
32 section. In order to enable parents whose modified adjusted gross income
33 does not exceed three hundred percent (300%) of the federal poverty level to
34 pay for a student's education, an advance payment that is expended on quali-
35 fied expenses for an eligible student may be claimed for such expenditure in
36 that year and a parental choice tax credit shall be allowed on a parent's tax
37 return for that year, subject to the remaining requirements of this chapter.
38 (10) Parents claiming the Idaho parental choice tax credit shall:
39 (a) Claim the credit for only qualified expenses incurred on behalf of
40 an eligible student;
41 (b) Not claim the credit for any semester in which a student was
42 enrolled full-time or part-time in a public school, public char-
43 ter school, public virtual charter school, public magnet school, or
44 part-time public kindergarten.
45 (c) Not claim the credit for any tuition or fees related to academic in-
46 struction provided by the parent to such parent's eligible student;
47 (d) Ensure no other parent claims a credit for an eligible student. In
48 the event more than one (1) parent claims a credit under this section for
49 the same eligible student in the same year, the provisions of section

4
1 63-3029L(2), Idaho Code, shall be used to determine which parent is en-
2 titled to receive the credit; and
3 (e) Fill out and submit the satisfaction and engagement survey provided
4 for in subsection (14) of this section. No parent shall be eligible to
5 receive a credit under the provisions of this section in a subsequent
6 year if such parent has not submitted such survey for the last year that
7 such parent received the credit.
8 (11) If the credit exceeds the tax imposed by this chapter, the excess
9 credit amount shall be refunded to the taxpayer. The state tax commission
10 shall design the tax return forms to account for advance payment of the
11 credit when calculating the tax imposed by this chapter.
12 (12) The total amount of tax credits authorized for all taxpayers under
13 this section shall not exceed fifty million dollars ($50,000,000) each tax
14 year. In the event the total amount of claims for tax credits allowed by this
15 section exceeds fifty million dollars ($50,000,000), the credits shall be
16 allowed in full to parents whose applications were properly and timely filed
17 and who have priority status under subsection (6) of this section, followed
18 by the remaining parents who filed complete applications on a first-come,
19 first-served basis, until the annual maximum limit is reached. The state tax
20 commission shall create and maintain a waiting list demonstrating who would
21 be eligible on a first-come, first-served basis if the annual maximum limit
22 for credits allowed under this section is increased.
23 (13)(a) Beginning with the second regular session of the seventieth
24 Idaho legislature in 2030, the state tax commission shall provide an
25 annual report to the governor, the senate local government and taxation
26 committee, the house revenue and taxation committee, and the joint fi-
27 nance-appropriations committee that shall include:
28 (i) The total amount of tax credits provided each year under this
29 section;
30 (ii) The number of parents who applied each year for the Idaho
31 parental choice tax credit;
32 (iii) The amount of the average tax credit allowed;
33 (iv) The number of credits distributed to parents with an adjusted
34 gross income below three hundred percent (300%) of the federal
35 poverty level;
36 (v) The number of parents who applied each year for the advance
37 payment pursuant to subsection (9) of this section, the number of
38 advance payments awarded, and the median amount of the advance
39 payments awarded;
40 (vi) The geographic area of parents applying for the credit;
41 (vii) The number of eligible students on the waiting list for each
42 year; and
43 (viii) A list of the categories of qualified expenses for which
44 recipients used the tax credit, which shall include the dollar
45 amounts for each category listed in subsection (2)(f) of this sec-
46 tion.
47 (b) The report shall not include any personally identifying informa-
48 tion of eligible students, their parents, or their households.
49 (14) Beginning in 2029, and each year thereafter, the legislative ser-
50 vices office shall deliver to each parent who has received a tax credit under

16 (15) Any taxpayer receiving an advance payment or claiming a tax credit
17 without submission of a timely and properly prescribed form shall be ineli-
18 gible to receive such payment or claim such credit. The state tax commission
19 shall deny or recapture credits claimed by a parent for expenditures not au-
20 thorized by the provisions of this section. If a parent has received an ad-
21 vance payment for an eligible student in a previous year, the parent shall
22 not claim the credit for qualified expenses incurred in a year that a stu-
23 dent ceases to qualify as an eligible student, except for any portion of the
24 credit that exceeds the amount of the advance payment received. If a student

26 the credit for that year, an amount of the credit, not exceeding the amount
27 of the advance payment received, shall be repaid to or recovered by the state
28 tax commission and deposited into the general fund.
29 (16) Parents who claim the credit on a tax return without receiving
30 prior approval of an application may not receive a credit, and the state
31 shall have no liability to such parent.
32 (17) The state tax commission shall, in as close to real time as practi-
33 cable, make available on its website the annual maximum limit, the cumula-
34 tive amount of credits claimed in applications under this section, and the
35 cumulative amount of advance payments issued under this section until the
36 annual maximum limit is reached.
37 (18) The state tax commission shall also keep records of the total ap-
38 plications for credits and total claim of credits that exceed the annual max-
39 imum limit.
40 (19) The state tax commission shall ensure that parents of eligible
41 students receive notice in the application that participation in the program
42 is a parental placement under 20 U.S.C. 1412 of the federal individuals with
43 disabilities education act, along with an explanation of the rights that
44 parentally placed students possess under the federal individuals with dis-
45 abilities education act, including eligibility for equitable services, and
46 any applicable state laws and regulations.

6 SECTION 3. That Section 67-1230, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 67-1230. IDAHO PARENTAL CHOICE TAX CREDIT ADVANCE PAYMENT FUND. (1)
9 There is hereby established in the state treasury the Idaho parental choice
10 tax credit advance payment fund, to be administered by the state tax commis-
11 sion.
12 (2) Moneys in the fund shall:
13 (a) Consist of the following:
14 (i) Legislative appropriations and transfers;
15 (ii) Donations and contributions made to the fund;
16 (iii) Reversions of unused, paid back, or recovered advance pay-
17 ment funds; and
18 (iv) Interest earned on idle moneys in the fund; and
19 (b) Be continuously appropriated to pay advance payments awarded un-
20 der the Idaho parental choice tax credit program established in section
21 63-3029N, Idaho Code.
22 (3) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the state
23 controller shall transfer the unobligated cash balance remaining in the
24 fund, established pursuant to this section, to the general fund. Such trans-
25 fer shall occur on July 1, 2027, or as soon thereafter as practicable but no
26 later than December 1, 2027.

28 repealed.
