LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1238
BY RESOURCES AND ENVIRONMENT COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO FISH AND GAME; AMENDING SECTION 36-105, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
3 FOR PUBLIC PARTICIPATION PRIOR TO THE IDAHO FISH AND GAME COMMISSION
4 SETTING OF ANY SEASON OR LIMIT ON THE TAKING OF FISH AND WILDLIFE AND TO
5 MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN
6 EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 36-105, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 36-105. COMMISSION ORDERS, RULES AND PROCLAMATIONS. (1) Adoption and
11 Publication of Rules and Orders. All rules and orders adopted pursuant to
12 the provisions of this title shall be made in accordance with chapter 52, ti-
13 tle 67, Idaho Code. Said rules and orders may also be given such other pub-
14 licity as the commission may deem desirable.
15 (2) Violation of Rules, Proclamations and Orders. All rules, proclama-
16 tions and orders made as herein provided shall have full force and effect as
17 law and any person violating any such rule, proclamation or order of the com-
18 mission, adopted and published as herein set forth, shall be found guilty as
19 set forth in section 36-1401, Idaho Code.
20 (3) Notwithstanding any other provision of chapter 52, title 67, Idaho
21 Code, the Idaho fish and game commission and the director of the Idaho fish
22 and game department shall be excepted from the requirements of rulemaking
23 when adopting, repealing, or amending any proclamation relating to setting
24 of any season or limit on numbers, size, sex, or species of wildlife clas-
25 sified by the commission as game animals, game birds, furbearers, migratory
26 birds, salmon, steelhead, and resident fish which that may be taken in this
27 state if:
28 (a) Notice of the proposed proclamation is published in the Idaho ad-
29 ministrative bulletin and is provided in the same manner as an open
30 meeting under section 74-204, Idaho Code;
31 (b) A minimum of a thirty (30) day public comment period is provided
32 when adopting, repealing, or amending any season-setting proclamation.
33 Emergency proclamations are exempt from the public comment period re-
34 quirement;
35 (b) (c) Notice is given to the director of the legislative services of-
36 fice for review by the germane joint subcommittee as soon as possible
37 after adoption by the commission; and
38 (c) (d) The proclamation shall be is published in a pamphlet or brochure
39 as provided in section 59-1012, Idaho Code, and distributed without
40 charge to the public. The text of the proclamation published in a pam-
41 phlet or brochure shall be the official text of the proclamation. Judi-
42 cial notice shall be taken of the proclamation pamphlet or brochure.
