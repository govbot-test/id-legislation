LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1371
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 1, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDI-
3 TION OF A NEW SECTION 33-145, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH THE IDAHO STUDENT
4 SAFETY AND EDUCATOR DISCLOSURE ACT; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PRO-
5 VIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Chapter 1, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
8 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
9 ignated as Section 33-145, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 33-145. IDAHO STUDENT SAFETY AND EDUCATOR DISCLOSURE ACT. (1) The
11 legislature finds that student safety is a paramount responsibility of the
12 state and that uniform statewide reporting and disclosure requirements in
13 the hiring of school employees is necessary to protect good faith disclo-
14 sures and prevent concealment of misconduct against a student.
15 (2) For the purposes of this section:
16 (a) "Covered individual" means any school personnel.
17 (b) "Educational entity" means any public school district, public
18 charter school, private school, the state department of education,
19 or any other entity that provides K-12 education and receives public
20 funds, including any program, school, or activity operated by or under
21 the authority of such entity.
22 (c) "Misconduct" means any conduct by a covered individual that vio-
23 lates the code of ethics for Idaho professional educators relating to
24 educator-student relationships.
25 (d) "Pending investigation" means any internal, administrative, reg-
26 ulatory, or criminal investigation that has been initiated and has not
27 reached a final disposition.
28 (3) An educational entity shall not conduct an internal review or in-
29 vestigation of alleged abuse, abandonment, or neglect of a child in lieu of
30 reporting to law enforcement as required by section 16-1605, Idaho Code.
31 Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to prohibit an educational
32 entity from taking administrative action or conducting an internal review
33 or investigation after a report has been made in compliance with section
34 16-1605, Idaho Code.
35 (4) Prior to hiring an employee, an educational entity shall obtain
36 disclosure statements from applicants and all educational employers or ed-
37 ucational contractors with the applicant for the prior ten (10) years and
38 shall specifically request information about any pending investigations,
39 resignations during investigations, and disciplinary actions involving the
40 applicant. Any applicant who makes a material misrepresentation or willful
41 omission regarding such disclosure statements or information required by

2
1 this subsection shall be subject to immediate termination of employment or
2 recission of such applicant's employment contract.
3 (5) An educational entity or administrator that knowingly withholds or
4 falsifies a disclosure statement required under this section shall be sub-
5 ject to administrative penalties, civil liability, and professional disci-
6 pline.
7 (6) Educational entities and administrators have immunity from civil
8 liability for disclosures made in good faith under this section.
9 (7) Any resignation, termination, or administrative leave during an
10 investigation of misconduct by a covered individual shall be referred to the
11 Idaho professional standards commission. Upon referral, the educational
12 entity shall confirm that the covered individual's certification status is
13 accurately reflected in the state department of education's certification
14 lookup application.
15 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
16 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
17 July 1, 2026.
