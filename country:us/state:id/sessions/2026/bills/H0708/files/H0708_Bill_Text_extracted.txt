LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 708
BY BUSINESS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE HOMEOWNER'S ASSOCIATION ACT; AMENDING CHAPTER 32, TITLE 55,
3 IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 55-3204C, IDAHO CODE, TO ES-
4 TABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING THE AUTOMATIC DISSOLUTION OF HOMEOWNER'S
5 ASSOCIATIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
6 DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Chapter 32, Title 55, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
9 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
10 ignated as Section 55-3204C, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 55-3204C. AUTOMATIC DISSOLUTION OF HOMEOWNER'S ASSOCIATIONS. (1) Any
12 homeowner's association that has been in existence for ten (10) years or
13 more, pursuant to the date of filing of the articles of incorporation, as
14 of July 1, 2026, shall automatically dissolve on July 1, 2029, unless its
15 members vote against such dissolution pursuant to the provisions of this
16 section. Any homeowner's association that has been in existence for less
17 than ten (10) years as of July 1, 2026, or that is created after July 1, 2026,
18 shall automatically dissolve after ten (10) years from the date of filing of
19 the articles of incorporation unless its members vote against such dissolu-
20 tion pursuant to the provisions of this section.
21 (2) A homeowner's association shall hold a meeting of its membership
22 before the automatic dissolution dates provided for in subsection (1) of
23 this section to provide an opportunity for its members to vote against
24 the automatic dissolution of the homeowner's association. A homeowner's
25 association shall give notice to its members of the proposed meeting in ac-
26 cordance with the provisions of section 30-30-505, Idaho Code. The notice
27 shall state that the purpose, or one (1) of the purposes, of the meeting is
28 to consider the automatic dissolution of the homeowner's association and
29 shall contain or be accompanied by a copy or summary of a plan of dissolution.
30 Automatic dissolution of a homeowner's association shall not occur if a ma-
31 jority of the eligible voters vote against such automatic dissolution.
32 (3) Unless otherwise provided for in the declaration or governing doc-
33 uments, all common property, including common areas, limited common areas,
34 other property or improvements owned or maintained by a homeowner's associa-
35 tion, or property owned in common by the members of a homeowner's association
36 shall remain dedicated to the common use of the members of the homeowner's
37 association. The plan of dissolution shall provide for the disposition of
38 the assets of a homeowner's association by transfer to a successor entity,
39 the lot owners, or a local government.
40 (4) If members of a homeowner's association do not vote against the au-
41 tomatic dissolution of the homeowner's association, the homeowner's asso-
42 ciation shall dissolve pursuant to the plan of dissolution. If members of a

2
1 homeowner's association vote against the automatic dissolution of the home-
2 owner's association, the homeowner's association shall continue under the
3 same terms. Homeowner's associations shall automatically dissolve ten (10)
4 years after the date of the first successful vote against automatic disso-
5 lution. Automatic dissolution shall occur every ten (10) years thereafter
6 while a homeowner's association is in existence unless and until the members
7 fail to vote against such dissolution and the homeowner's association is au-
8 tomatically dissolved as provided in this section.
9 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
10 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
11 July 1, 2026.
