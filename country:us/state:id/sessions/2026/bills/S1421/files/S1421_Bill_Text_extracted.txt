LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1421
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO GOLD AND SILVER; AMENDING SECTION 57-720, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
3 FOR INVESTMENT IN PHYSICAL GOLD AND SILVER IN CERTAIN INSTANCES, TO PRO-
4 VIDE FOR STORAGE OF PHYSICAL GOLD AND SILVER, AND TO PROVIDE FOR A MAX-
5 IMUM ALLOWED INVESTMENT; AMENDING SECTION 59-1312, IDAHO CODE, TO PRO-
6 VIDE FOR INVESTMENT IN PHYSICAL GOLD AND SILVER IN CERTAIN INSTANCES,
7 TO PROVIDE FOR STORAGE OF PHYSICAL GOLD AND SILVER, AND TO PROVIDE FOR A
8 MAXIMUM ALLOWED INVESTMENT; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN
9 EFFECTIVE DATE.
10 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
11 SECTION 1. That Section 57-720, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
12 amended to read as follows:
13 57-720. INVESTMENT AUTHORITY -- INVESTMENT POLICIES -- ANNUAL AU-
14 DIT. (1) The investment board or its investment manager(s) may, and are
15 hereby authorized to, invest the permanent endowment funds and the earnings
16 reserve funds of the state of Idaho and other moneys as required by law. The
17 investment board may, with the approval of the state board of land commis-
18 sioners, invest other funds that are exempt from section 67-1210, Idaho
19 Code, provided however, that the costs of investment of such funds may be
20 deducted by the investment board from investment proceeds.
21 (2) The funds invested by the investment board may be combined or pooled
22 for investment.
23 (3) Earnings reserve funds shall be accounted for separately from per-
24 manent endowment funds.
25 (4) Prior to the annual calculation of gains and losses pursuant to sec-
26 tion 57-724, Idaho Code, the investment board shall allocate the end of fis-
27 cal year market value between the permanent endowment funds and the earnings
28 reserve funds. This allocation shall be made based upon the proportion that
29 the market value of the permanent endowment funds and the market value of
30 the earnings reserve funds bear to the combined market value of both sets of
31 funds, at the end of the fiscal year.
32 (5)(a) Investments may include physical gold and silver. Except as
33 provided in paragraph (b) of this subsection, physical gold and silver,
34 owned directly and held under this subsection, shall be maintained se-
35 curely in a depository for precious metals constructed, at a minimum,
36 to currently accepted industry standards for secure storage and located
37 within the geographical boundaries of this state, a state contiguous to
38 this state, or the state of Texas. The Idaho department of finance shall
39 ensure that the gold and silver held in a qualifying depository under
40 this subsection is insured, independently audited, and physically seg-
41 regated from other assets.

2
1 (b) Physical gold and silver, owned directly and held under this sub-
2 section, may be stored by a bank chartered under the Idaho bank act or a
3 credit union organized under the Idaho credit union act when such facil-
4 ity is located within the geographical boundaries of this state and the
5 gold and silver is stored in a class 1 vault.
6 (c) The maximum allowed investment in physical gold and silver shall
7 not exceed seven and one-half percent (7.5%) of the moneys invested pur-
8 suant to this section.
9 (5) (6) The investment board shall formulate investment policies gov-
10 erning the investment of permanent endowment funds and earnings reserve
11 funds and the investment of other funds accepted for investment by the in-
12 vestment board pursuant to subsection (1) of this section. The policies
13 shall pertain to the types, kinds or nature of investment of any of the funds,
14 and any limitations, conditions or restrictions upon the methods, practices
15 or procedures for investment, reinvestments, purchases, sales or exchange
16 transactions, provided such policies shall not conflict with nor be in dero-
17 gation of any Idaho constitutional provision or of the provisions of this
18 chapter.
19 (6) (7) Annually, the investment board shall cause an audit to be con-
20 ducted of the investment of permanent endowment funds and earnings reserve
21 funds, such audit to be conducted by a recognized certified public accoun-
22 tant. The certified public accountant conducting the audit shall not be an
23 employee of the state. The expense of such audit shall be paid from earnings
24 reserve funds.
25 (7) (8) The state treasurer shall invest the income funds of the respec-
26 tive endowment funds and distribute the moneys in the income funds according
27 to legislative appropriation.
