LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 520
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE IDAHO ADMINISTRATIVE PROCEDURE ACT; AMENDING SECTION
3 67-5242, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING HEARING NOTICES
4 AND TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING PROCEDURE AT A HEARING; PROVIDING
5 SEVERABILITY; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
6 DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 67-5242, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 67-5242. HEARING NOTICES AND PROCEDURE AT HEARING. (1) When an agency
11 gives a person notice of an opportunity for a contested case hearing for
12 which the person is entitled to notice by law, the notice shall:
13 (a) Be in writing;
14 (b) Set forth the agency action being taken;
15 (c) Identify the agency statute or rule authorizing the action;
16 (d) Inform the person of the right, procedure, and time limit to file a
17 request for hearing; and
18 (e) Provide citation to any agency rules and procedures governing the
19 contested case hearing.
20 (1) (2) In a contested case other than an emergency proceeding Other
21 than emergency proceedings held pursuant to section 62-5247, Idaho Code, all
22 parties to a contested case hearing shall receive notice that shall include:
23 (a) A statement of the time, place, and nature of the hearing;
24 (b) A statement of the legal authority under which the hearing is to be
25 held; and
26 (c) A short and plain statement of the matters asserted or the issues
27 involved.
28 (2) (3) At the hearing, the presiding officer:
29 (a) Shall regulate the course of the proceedings to assure that there
30 is a full disclosure of all relevant facts and issues, including such
31 cross-examination as may be necessary.
32 (b) Shall afford all parties the opportunity to respond and present ev-
33 idence and argument on all issues involved, except as restricted by a
34 limited grant of intervention or by a prehearing order.
35 (c) May give nonparties an opportunity to present oral or written
36 statements. If the presiding officer proposes to consider a statement
37 by a nonparty, the presiding officer shall give all parties an opportu-
38 nity to challenge or rebut it and, on motion of any party, the presiding
39 officer shall require the statement to be given under oath or affirma-
40 tion.
41 (d) Shall cause the hearing to be recorded at the agency's expense. Any
42 party, at that party's expense, may have a transcript prepared or may

2
1 cause additional recordings to be made during the hearing if the making
2 of the additional recording does not cause distraction or disruption.
3 (e) May conduct all or part of the hearing by telephone, video confer-
4 ence, or other electronic means, if each participant in the hearing has
5 an opportunity to participate in the entire proceeding while it is tak-
6 ing place.
7 SECTION 2. SEVERABILITY. The provisions of this act are hereby declared
8 to be severable and if any provision of this act or the application of such
9 provision to any person or circumstance is declared invalid for any reason,
10 such declaration shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of
11 this act.
12 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
13 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
14 July 1, 2026.
