7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. The Legislature seeks to
9 increase transparency for taxpayers and to support appropriation reductions
10 for Medicaid rates paid to residential habilitation providers. Medicaid
11 pays more than $176.5 million in general funds to help people with disabil-
12 ities live independently with residential habilitation and other home and
13 community-based services. The Governor's recommendation calls for the Leg-
14 islature to pursue policy changes to support the Department of Health and
15 Welfare in achieving budget reductions for Medicaid, including an option
16 to reduce rates for residential habilitation. In 2022, the Legislature ap-
17 propriated funds pursuant to Chapter 252, Laws of 2022, to increase payment
18 rates for these services to implement a new service array and budget tool
19 associated with the K.W. v. Armstrong lawsuit, but these funds are no longer
20 appropriate since a court order halted implementation of the new services
21 and budget tool. To support ongoing accountability for taxpayer dollars and
22 ensure the Legislature has the needed information to effectuate its appro-
23 priation responsibilities, this act requires the department to report how
24 those funds are used.
