LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 904
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO PROPERTY SALES; AMENDING CHAPTER 1, TITLE 55, IDAHO CODE, BY THE
3 ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 55-116, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS
4 PROHIBITING THE PURCHASE OF SINGLE-FAMILY HOMES BY FOREIGN PERSONS, TO
5 DEFINE A TERM, TO PROVIDE PROCEDURES, TO PROVIDE FOR ENFORCEMENT, AND
6 TO PROVIDE FOR IMMUNITY FROM LIABILITY; AMENDING SECTION 55-103, IDAHO
7 CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING EXCEPTIONS TO WHO MAY OWN PROPERTY
8 AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL CORRECTION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PRO-
9 VIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
10 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
11 SECTION 1. That Chapter 1, Title 55, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
12 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
13 ignated as Section 55-116, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
14 55-116. PROHIBITION ON PURCHASE OF SINGLE-FAMILY HOMES BY FOREIGN
15 PERSONS. (1) On and after July 1, 2026, a foreign person, as defined in this
16 section, shall not be permitted to purchase or acquire a controlling inter-
17 est in the ownership of a single-family home property in Idaho.
18 (2) For the purpose of this section, "foreign person" means:
19 (a) An individual who is not a citizen or legal resident of the United
20 States, or an agent, trustee, or fiduciary thereof;
21 (b) A foreign corporation incorporated under the laws of a jurisdiction
22 outside of the United States that would be a business corporation if in-
23 corporated under the laws of this state, or an agent, trustee, or fidu-
24 ciary thereof;
25 (c) A foreign government, meaning a government other than the federal
26 government of the United States, the government of any territory or pos-
27 session of the United States, or the government of any state, political
28 subdivision of a state, or Indian tribe;
29 (d) A real estate investment trust, whether publicly traded or not, the
30 majority of whose shareholders are restricted persons described in this
31 subsection;
32 (e) A company that is registered with the United States securities and
33 exchange commission as an investment adviser under the investment ad-
34 visers act of 1940, as amended, when acting on behalf of clients who are
35 restricted persons described in this subsection; or
36 (f) Any other corporation, business trust, estate, trust, partnership,
37 limited liability company, association, joint venture, agency, or in-
38 strumentality, or any other legal or commercial entity, acting on be-
39 half of a nonresident alien, foreign corporation, or foreign govern-
40 ment, or an agent, trustee, or fiduciary thereof.
41 (3) A foreign person that acquires, directly or indirectly, control of
42 a single-family home property on or after July 1, 2026, by devise, by de-

2
1 scent, through the enforcement of security interests, or through the collec-
2 tion of debts, must sell, transfer, or otherwise divest itself of the prop-
3 erty within one hundred eighty (180) days of acquiring control of the prop-
4 erty.
5 (4) If a foreign person does not divest the single-family home property
6 as required by this section, the attorney general shall commence an action in
7 the district court within the jurisdiction of the property.
8 (5) If the property is held in violation of this section, the district
9 court shall order that the property be sold through judicial foreclosure.
10 Proceeds of the sale shall be disbursed to lienholders, in the order of pri-
11 ority, except for liens that, under the terms of the sale, are to remain on
12 the property.
13 (6) On or after January 1, 2027, any individual may act as a whistle-
14 blower and provide a referral to the office of the attorney general for vio-
15 lations of this section.
16 (7) If a whistleblower referral results in a divestiture of land or
17 other assets held in violation of this section, the whistleblower shall be
18 entitled to a reward equal to thirty percent (30%) of the proceeds of the
19 property sale resulting from a violation of this section after disbursements
20 have been made pursuant to paragraphs (a) through (c) of this subsection.
21 Proceeds of the sale shall be disbursed in the following order:
22 (a) The payment of authorized costs of the sale, including all approved
23 fees and expenses of the referee and any taxes and assessments due;
24 (b) The payment, in an amount approved by the court, to the attorney
25 general for reimbursement of investigation and litigation costs and ex-
26 penses;
27 (c) To bona fide lienholders, in the order of priority, except for liens
28 that, under the terms of the sale, are to remain on the property;
29 (d) To whistleblowers; and
30 (e) To the restricted foreign person.
31 (8) The responsibility for determining whether a foreign person is sub-
32 ject to the provisions of this section rests solely with the foreign person,
33 the attorney general, or any qualifying whistleblower. An individual or en-
34 tity who is not a foreign person shall not be required to determine or inquire
35 whether another person or entity is or may be subject to the provisions of
36 this section and shall bear no civil or criminal liability under this sec-
37 tion.
38 SECTION 2. That Section 55-103, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
39 amended to read as follows:
40 55-103. WHO MAY OWN PROPERTY. (1) Except as provided in subsection (2)
41 or (3) of this section or as otherwise provided in this chapter, any person,
42 whether citizen or alien, may take, hold, and dispose of property, real or
43 personal.
44 (2) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, on and af-
45 ter the effective date of this subsection, a foreign government or a foreign
46 state-controlled enterprise shall not purchase, acquire, or hold any con-
47 trolling interest in agricultural land, forest land, water rights, mining
48 claims, or mineral rights in the state of Idaho. This subsection does not
49 apply to any interest in agricultural land, forest land, water rights, min-

3
1 ing claims, or mineral rights acquired by a foreign government or foreign
2 state-controlled enterprise prior to the effective date of this subsection
3 or to any foreign pension fund.
4 (3) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, a for-
5 eign principal from a foreign adversary shall not purchase, acquire, or hold
6 any controlling interest, except a de minimis indirect interest, in agricul-
7 tural land, water rights, mining claims, or mineral rights in the state of
8 Idaho. A foreign principal has a de minimis indirect interest if any owner-
9 ship is the result of the foreign principal's ownership of registered equi-
10 ties in a publicly traded company owning the land and if the foreign princi-
11 pal's ownership in the company is either:
12 (a) Less than five percent (5%) of any class of registered equities or
13 less than five percent (5%) in the aggregate in multiple classes of reg-
14 istered equities; or
15 (b) A noncontrolling interest in an entity controlled by a company that
16 is both registered with the United States securities and exchange com-
17 mission as an investment advisor under the investment advisers act of
18 1940, as amended, and is not a foreign entity.
19 (4) A foreign principal that directly or indirectly owns or otherwise
20 controls agricultural land, water rights, mining claims, or mineral rights
21 in the state of Idaho must sell, transfer, or otherwise divest itself of
22 the agricultural land, mining claims, or mineral rights within one hundred
23 eighty (180) days of the effective date of this act.
24 (5) A foreign principal that directly or indirectly owns or acquires
25 agricultural land, water rights, or any interest in such land in this state
26 must register with the Idaho state department of agriculture within sixty
27 (60) days of the effective date of this act or the date of acquisition,
28 whichever is latest. The department must establish a form for such registra-
29 tion, which, at a minimum, must include all of the following:
30 (a) The name of the owner of the agricultural land, water rights, or the
31 owner of the interest in such land;
32 (b) The address of the agricultural land, the property appraiser's par-
33 cel identification number, and the property's legal description; and
34 (c) The number of acres of agricultural land.
35 (6) A foreign principal that directly owns or acquires mining claims
36 or mineral rights or any interest in such claims in this state must register
37 with the Idaho department of lands and the Idaho secretary of state within
38 sixty (60) days of the effective date of this act or the date of acquisition,
39 whichever is latest. The foreign principal shall be registered to do busi-
40 ness with the Idaho secretary of state's office. The Idaho department of
41 lands shall provide the form for such registration, which at a minimum must
42 include the following:
43 (a) The mineral lease number with lease length and premises by parcel
44 and acres; and
45 (b) The application for use of state land by the individual or legal en-
46 tity.
47 (7) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (3) of this section, a
48 foreign principal from a foreign adversary country may acquire agricultural
49 land, mining claims, or mineral rights on or after July 1, 2025, by devise
50 or descent, through the enforcement of security interests, or through the

1 collection of debts, provided that the foreign principal sells, transfers,
2 or otherwise divests itself of the agricultural land, water rights, mining
3 claims, or mineral rights within one hundred eighty (180) days of acquiring
4 the agricultural land, mining claims, or mineral rights.
5 (8) The provisions of subsections (3) through (7) of this section shall
6 not apply to an entity that has a national security agreement with the com-
7 mittee on foreign investment in the United States as of July 1, 2025, and
8 maintains such national security agreement.
9 (9) The Idaho state department of agriculture and department of lands
10 shall adopt rules, subject to legislative approval, to implement the provi-
11 sions of this section.
12 (10) The responsibility for determining whether an entity is subject to
13 this section rests solely with the foreign entity, the attorney general, or
14 any qualifying whistleblower, and no other individual or entity. An indi-
15 vidual or entity who is not a foreign entity shall not be required to deter-
16 mine or inquire whether another person or entity is or may be subject to the
17 provisions of this section and shall bear no civil or criminal liability un-
18 der this section.
19 (11) If a foreign principal from a foreign adversary country does not
20 divest the public or private lands, mining claims, or mineral rights as re-
21 quired by this section, the attorney general shall commence an action in the
22 district court within the jurisdiction of the public or private land, mining
23 claims, or mineral rights.
24 (12) If the public or private land is held in violation of this sec-
25 tion, the district court shall order that the public or private land be sold
26 through judicial foreclosure.
27 (13) Proceeds of the sale shall be disbursed to lienholders, in the or-
28 der of priority, except for liens that, under the terms of the sale, are to
29 remain on the public or private land.
30 (14) Any individual may act as a whistleblower and provide a referral to
31 the office of the attorney general for violations of this section.
32 (15) If a whistleblower referral results in a divestiture of land, min-
33 ing claims, or mineral rights or other assets held in violation of this sec-
34 tion, the whistleblower shall be entitled to a reward equal to thirty percent
35 (30%) of the proceeds of the land, mining claims, mineral rights, or asset
36 sale resulting from a violation of this section after payment of lienhold-
37 ers. Proceeds of the sale shall be disbursed in the following order:
38 (a) To bona fide lienholders, in order of priority, except for liens
39 that, under the terms of the sale, are to remain on the property;
40 (b) The payment of authorized costs of the sale, including all approved
41 fees and expenses of the referee and any taxes and assessments due;
42 (c) The payment, in an amount approved by the court, to the attorney
43 general for reimbursement of investigation and litigation costs and ex-
44 penses;
45 (d) To whistleblowers; and
46 (e) To the restricted foreign entity.
47 (16) The whistleblower enforcement provision takes effect one hundred
48 eighty (180) days after the enforcement date of this section.

5 (i) Possession of more than fifty percent (50%) of the ownership

16 (d) "Foreign government" means a government other than the federal gov-
17 ernment of the United States or the government of any state, political
18 subdivision of a state, territory, federally recognized Indian tribe,
19 or possession of the United States.
20 (e) "Forest land" means privately owned or state-owned land being held
21 and used primarily for the continuous purpose of growing and harvesting
22 trees of a marketable species. Having met the above criteria, forest
23 land includes:
24 (i) Forest land is land evidenced by present use and silvicul-
25 tural treatment.
26 (ii) Forest land is land that has a dedicated use that is further
27 evidenced by a forest land management plan that includes eventual
28 harvest of the forest crop.
29 (iii) Forest land is land bearing forest growth or land that has
30 not been converted to another use.
31 (iv) Forest land is land that has had the trees removed by man
32 through harvest, including clear-cuts or by natural disaster,
33 such as but not limited to fire, and which within five (5) years af-
34 ter harvest or initial assessment will be reforested as specified
35 in the forest practices act, chapter 13, title 38, Idaho Code.
36 (f) "Foreign pension fund" means a trust, corporation, or other entity
