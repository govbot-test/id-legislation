26 SECTION 2. That Chapter 66, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
27 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
28 ignated as Section 67-6628B, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
29 67-6628B. PAID IN-PERSON CANVASSING ACTIVITIES -- DISCLOSURES. (1)
30 For the purposes of this section:
31 (a) "Paid in-person canvassing activities" means canvassing activi-
32 ties performed by an individual who accepts financial compensation for
33 the purpose of influencing how a person votes, including but not limited
34 to circulating any petition for an initiative or referendum; soliciting
35 personal residences or public locations to communicate with or distrib-
36 ute materials to possible voters about political candidates or ballot
37 measures; conducting in-person get-out-the-vote efforts in relation to
38 any candidate or ballot measure; and any other direct, in-person con-
39 tact or interaction intended to affect an individual's electoral par-
40 ticipation or preferences.
41 (b) "Phone banking activities" means any organized outreach activity
42 in which telephone calls are made to Idaho qualified electors for the
43 purpose of persuading such electors to vote or to vote in a particular
44 manner, providing information to such electors regarding an election or
45 voting, or mobilizing such electors to vote.
46 (2) Persons who engage in any paid in-person canvassing activities
47 shall:
48 (a) Be a resident of the state of Idaho;

4
1 (b) Verbally notify any person with whom they communicate that they are
2 being compensated to perform paid in-person canvassing activities and
3 provide the name of the persons or entities providing payment to such
4 person; and
5 (c) At all times, while engaging in paid in-person canvassing activi-
6 ties, wear a badge that is clearly visible to any person with whom they
7 intend to communicate with that contains the words "paid political ad-
8 vocate" as well as the name of the persons or entities providing payment
9 to such person.
10 (3) The Idaho secretary of state may promulgate rules to implement the
11 provisions of this section, including specifications regarding font, shape,
12 color, and size requirements for the badge required pursuant to subsection
13 (2)(c) of this section.
14 (4) The provisions of this section shall not apply to:
15 (a) Volunteers acting without compensation or reimbursement beyond in-
16 cidental expenses;
17 (b) Individuals whose paid in-person canvassing activities are inci-
18 dental to broader campaign employment responsibilities;
19 (c) Phone banking activities; or
20 (d) Candidates engaged in in-person canvassing activities for their
21 own election campaign.
22 SECTION 3. That Section 67-6625, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
23 amended to read as follows:
24 67-6625. VIOLATIONS -- CIVIL FINE -- MISDEMEANOR PENALTY -- PROSECU-
25 TION -- LIMITATION -- VENUE. (1) Any person who violates the provisions of
26 sections 67-6603, 67-6604, 67-6606 through 67-6614A, 67-6624, 67-6627 or,
27 67-6628, or 67-6628B, Idaho Code, shall be liable for a civil fine not to ex-
28 ceed two hundred fifty dollars ($250) if an individual, and not more than two
29 thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500) if a person other than an individual.
30 The burden of proof for such civil liability shall be met by showing a prepon-
31 derance of the evidence.
32 (2) Any person who violates section 67-6605, Idaho Code, and any per-
33 son who knowingly and willfully violates sections 67-6603 through 67-6614A,
34 67-6624, 67-6627 or, 67-6628, or 67-6628B, Idaho Code, is guilty of a misde-
35 meanor and, upon conviction, in addition to the fines set forth in subsection
36 (1) of this section, may be imprisoned for not more than six (6) months or be
37 both fined and imprisoned.
38 (3) The attorney general or the appropriate prosecuting attorney may
39 prosecute any violations of this chapter.
40 (4) Prosecution for a civil or misdemeanor violation of this chapter
41 must be commenced within two (2) years after the date on which the violation
42 occurred. Prosecution for a felony violation of this chapter must be com-
43 menced pursuant to the provisions of section 19-402, Idaho Code.
44 (5) Venue for prosecution under the provisions of this chapter shall be
45 in the county of residence of the defendant if the defendant is a resident of
46 the state of Idaho, otherwise venue shall be in Ada county.
47 SECTION 4. SEVERABILITY. The provisions of this act are hereby declared
48 to be severable and if any provision of this act or the application of such
