Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 572
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE PEDIATRIC SECRETIVE TRANSITIONS PARENTAL RIGHTS ACT; AMEND-
3 ING CHAPTER 10, TITLE 32, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION
4 32-1016, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE A SHORT TITLE, TO PROVIDE LEGISLATIVE
5 FINDINGS AND INTENT, TO DEFINE TERMS, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING
6 CERTAIN PROHIBITED ACTS, TO PROVIDE FOR CIVIL ACTIONS, AND TO PROVIDE
7 FOR ATTORNEY GENERAL ENFORCEMENT; PROVIDING SEVERABILITY; AND DECLAR-
8 ING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
9 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
10 SECTION 1. That Chapter 10, Title 32, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
11 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
12 ignated as Section 32-1016, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
13 32-1016. PEDIATRIC SECRETIVE TRANSITIONS PARENTAL RIGHTS ACT. (1)
14 This section shall be known and may be cited as the "Pediatric Secretive
15 Transitions Parental Rights Act."
16 (2) The legislature finds that:
17 (a) Parents have a fundamental right to raise their children and make
18 medical and educational decisions on their behalf;
19 (b) Medical and social sex transitions can inflict severe and irre-
20 versible harm to a child's well-being; and
21 (c) It is undoubtedly a parental purview and prerogative to have knowl-
22 edge of, and the authority to deny, a child's efforts at sex transition
23 procedures or social transitions.
24 (3) It is the intent of the legislature to prohibit any health care
25 provider, educational institution, or mandated reporter of child neglect
26 or abuse from facilitating a pediatric sex transition or social transition
27 without informing and obtaining informed consent from a minor child's par-
28 ents or guardians.
29 (4) As used in this section:
30 (a) "Aggrieved person" means the parent or guardian of a child sub-
31 jected to social transition or sex transition procedures.
32 (b) "Child" means an individual who is less than eighteen (18) years of
33 age and who is not married or legally emancipated.
34 (c) "Covered entity" means a mandated reporter of child abuse or
35 neglect pursuant to state law, a primary or secondary educational in-
36 stitution, a child care provider, or a medical, behavioral, or mental
37 health care provider.
38 (d)(i) "Sex transition procedure" means engaging in any one (1)
39 or more of the following for the purpose of: (i) intentionally de-
40 laying, halting, or disrupting the natural development of an in-
41 dividual's body, including the onset or progression of puberty, so
42 that it does not develop or halts developing to correspond to the

2
1 individual's sex; or (ii) intentionally changing an individual's
2 body, including the individual's external appearance or biologi-
3 cal functions, to no longer correspond to the individual's sex:
4 1. The use of puberty blockers, including gonadotropin re-
5 leasing hormone agonists and antagonists;
6 2. The use of sex hormones, such as androgen blockers, es-
7 trogen, anti-estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, or di-
8 hydrotestosterone blockers; or
9 3. Surgical procedures that attempt to transform an indi-
10 vidual's physical appearance or that attempt to alter or re-
11 move an individual's sexual organs.
12 (ii) "Sex transition procedure" does not include:
13 1. Appropriate and medically necessary procedures to treat
14 a verifiable disorder of sexual development, including an
15 individual born with forty-six (46) XX chromosomes with vir-
16 ilization, an individual born with forty-six (46) XY chromo-
17 somes with undervirilization, or an individual having both
18 ovarian and testicular tissue;
19 2. The treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or dis-
20 order that has been caused or exacerbated by the performance
21 of an intervention described in paragraph (d)(i) of this
22 section without regard to whether the intervention was per-
23 formed in accordance with state or federal law; or
24 3. Any intervention undertaken because an individual suf-
25 fers from any diagnosed and verifiable condition of the
26 body's organ systems, including traumatic bodily injuries,
27 congenital structural anomalies of major organs or systems,
28 or acute illnesses with a high probability of rapid mortal-
29 ity.
30 (e) "Social transition" means the nonmedical process by which an in-
31 dividual adopts a name, pronouns, appearance, or mannerisms associated
32 with a gender other than from such individual's biological sex.
33 (5) A covered entity shall not withhold information from a child's par-
34 ent or guardian related to the parent's child's expressed interest in or de-
35 sire for sex transition procedures.
36 (6) A covered entity shall not facilitate or contribute to a child's ef-
37 forts to obtain sex transition procedures without the express written con-
38 sent of such child's parent or guardian.
39 (7) An aggrieved person may commence an action at law or equity in a
40 court of competent jurisdiction against a covered entity for violations of
41 this section.
42 (8) If an aggrieved person proves that a covered entity violated the
43 provisions of this section, such aggrieved person is entitled to recover:
44 (a) Declaratory relief;
45 (b) Injunctive relief;
46 (c) Statutory damages of not less than fifty thousand dollars ($50,000)
47 assessed against the covered entity found by a court or jury to have vio-
48 lated this section;
49 (d) Compensatory damages; and
50 (e) Reasonable costs and attorney's fees.

10 SECTION 2. SEVERABILITY. The provisions of this act are hereby declared
11 to be severable and if any provision of this act or the application of such

14 this act.
15 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
