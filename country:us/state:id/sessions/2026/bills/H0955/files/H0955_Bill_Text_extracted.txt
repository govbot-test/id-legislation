13 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS. The Legislature finds that:
14 (1) The Fifth and Fourteenth Amendments to the Constitution of the
15 United States prohibit the government from depriving individuals of their
16 liberty or property interests without due process of law.
17 (2) The interests and role of parents in the care, custody, and control
18 of their children are deeply rooted in our nation's history and tradition and
19 are also among the unalienable rights retained by the people under the Ninth
20 Amendment to the Constitution of the United States.
21 (3) Idaho has established procedures for reporting on child abuse and
22 child neglect under the Child Protective Act, Chapter 16, Title 16, Idaho
23 Code.
24 (4) Idaho has established laws for protecting parental rights under
25 Chapter 10, Title 32, Idaho Code.
26 (5) The Idaho Legislature wishes to ensure that Idaho's procedures for
27 child and adult protection cases comport with constitutional due process re-
28 quirements.
29 SECTION 2. That Chapter 16, Title 16, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
30 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
31 ignated as Section 16-1650, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
32 16-1650. MEDICAL KIDNAPPING -- PARENTAL RIGHTS. (1) As used in this
33 section, "medical kidnapping" means the wrongful removal of a minor child
34 under eighteen (18) years of age or a vulnerable adult from a parent or
35 guardian by law enforcement, a social worker, or any other official when:
36 (a) A parent or guardian questions, denies, or declines a potential
37 medical intervention for the child or vulnerable adult or chooses to
38 seek a second opinion when there is cause for concern that the potential
39 medical intervention would do harm to the child or vulnerable adult,
40 including but not limited to an allergic reaction;

2
1 (b) A parent or guardian brings the child or vulnerable adult for medi-
2 cal intervention at a health care facility and:
3 (i) A screening establishes that the child or vulnerable adult is
4 not abused, abandoned, or neglected; and
5 (ii) The child or vulnerable adult is nevertheless removed from
6 the parent's or guardian's custody by law enforcement, a social
7 worker, or another official for invalid reasons, including but not
8 limited to imminent harm or danger;
9 (c) A child or vulnerable adult has his or her belongings taken by a
10 doctor, nurse, staff, volunteer, social worker, or any other official
11 without parental or guardian consent, including but not limited to a
12 service or therapy animal, clothes, books, games, cellular phone, wal-
13 let, eye glasses, water, or food;
14 (d) A child or vulnerable adult is denied the freedom to continue care
15 with his or her own medical physicians and specialists as established by
16 the parent or guardian;
17 (e) A child abuse specialist visits with a child when it has previously
18 been verified that no child abuse or neglect has occurred;
19 (f) A child or vulnerable adult is coerced by medical staff or a volun-
20 teer into medical intervention;
21 (g) A doctor, nurse, medical staff, or volunteer takes photos, videos,
22 lab work, or x-rays or conducts medical testing, care, or treatment
23 of or on the child or vulnerable adult without consent of a parent or
24 guardian;
25 (h) A child or vulnerable adult is forced by medical staff into a medi-
26 cal intervention without consent of the parent or guardian;
27 (i) A child or vulnerable adult is unreasonably denied food, water, or
28 other drinks, including supplemental food or drinks; or
29 (j) A child or vulnerable adult is ordered to have a medical interven-
30 tion against parental or guardian consent when it is not required and
31 may cause a medical condition or harm.
32 (2) A parent or guardian has the final decision in a child or vulner-
33 able adult's medical care and treatment. A parent or guardian shall have a
34 parental and constitutional right to seek medical treatment and make deci-
35 sions for a child or vulnerable adult when the parent or guardian believes
36 it is in the best interests of the child or vulnerable adult, including but
37 not limited to accepting or denying medical intervention, imaging, medica-
38 tion, or a second opinion. Such choices shall not be deemed child abuse or
39 neglect and may not be disputed, discriminated against, reported, or inves-
40 tigated by law enforcement, a social worker, or any other official. A child
41 or vulnerable adult shall not be removed from a parent or guardian and placed
42 in state care, a hospital, or any other medical treatment facility by law en-
43 forcement, a social worker, or any other official unless there is a known in-
44 stance of abuse or neglect and a criminal complaint has been filed in an in-
45 vestigation. Treating medical doctors, nurses, specialists, staff, and any
46 other medical facility employees or volunteers shall abide by a parent's or
47 guardian's wishes. Any reports made by a reporting person must be substan-
48 tiated by clear and convincing evidence that a parent or guardian has acted
49 with negligence or malicious intent to harm the child.

2 not limited to influenza, gastrointestinal issues, or a compromised immune

14 (5) When a social worker, psychiatrist, or psychologist visits with a
15 parent, guardian, child, or vulnerable adult and concludes that no further
16 investigation is warranted, then no doctor, nurse, staff, social worker,
17 law enforcement, or any other person or official shall conduct any further

20 (6) If a parent or guardian determines that such parent or guardian, the
21 child, or the vulnerable adult is being mistreated by a doctor, nurse, staff,

26 (7) If a doctor, nurse, staff, volunteer, social worker, or any other
27 person at a medical facility contacts child protective services, adult pro-

29 neglect is found, such person may be subject to the provisions of sections
30 16-1607, 18-5415, and 56-1008, Idaho Code, and the provisions of chapter 45,
31 title 18, and chapter 10, title 32, Idaho Code, and any resulting child pro-
32 tection case shall be terminated.
33 (8) Any person who commits medical kidnapping as described in this sec-
34 tion shall be subject to:
35 (a) Loss of licensure as provided in the applicable provisions of title
36 54, Idaho Code;
37 (b) The provisions of chapter 45, title 18, Idaho Code;
38 (c) Registration in the national child abuse and neglect data system
39 and the Idaho child protection central registry as level one; and
40 (d) An investigation by child and family services or adult protective
41 services if the individual has full or partial custody of a child or vul-
42 nerable adult. The individual shall complete all requirements and rec-
43 ommendations imposed as a result of such investigation, including but
44 not limited to:
45 (i) A full psychological evaluation; and
46 (ii) Attendance at in-depth courses addressing parenting, nu-
47 trition, special needs of individuals, and trauma. Such courses
48 shall not be taught by a social worker, the Idaho department of
49 health and welfare, or any state employee or state contractor.
