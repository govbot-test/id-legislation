36 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. (1) The Legislature finds
37 that:
38 (a) Parenthood is a natural and fundamental relationship from which
39 inherent parental rights and responsibilities arise, protected by the
40 United States Constitution and the Constitution of the State of Idaho;
41 (b) A parent has the right, obligation, responsibility, and authority
42 to raise, manage, train, educate, provide for, and reasonably disci-
43 pline the parent's child;

2
1 (c) The role of the state is secondary and supportive to the primary
2 role of a parent;
3 (d) A parent's fundamental rights may be restricted only upon a judi-
4 cial finding of unfitness or necessity, supported by clear and convinc-
5 ing evidence; and
6 (e) The interests of the state favor preservation and not severance of
7 natural familial bonds in circumstances where a positive, nurturing
8 parent-child relationship can exist, including extended family associ-
9 ation and support. The procedural safeguards contained in this act are
10 essential to the best interests of the child.
11 (2) The Legislature declares that family court proceedings affecting
12 the custody, care, or visitation of a child implicate fundamental liberty
13 interests.
14 (3) It is the intent of the Legislature to:
15 (a) Protect children by preserving stable relationships with fit par-
16 ents;
17 (b) Ensure that restrictions on parental rights are narrowly tailored,
18 based on clear and convincing evidence, and are the least restrictive
19 means available;
20 (c) Promote transparency, accountability, and timely judicial action
21 in all custody matters; and
22 (d) Promote stability, reduce unnecessary litigation, and support
23 the child's meaningful relationship with fit parents by establishing
24 clear, uniform standards for custody determinations.

12 32-717C. ALLEGATIONS OF ABUSE -- INVESTIGATION. (1) When, in any di-
13 vorce proceeding or upon request for modification of a divorce decree, an in
14 any proceeding affecting child custody or parenting time, a credible alle-
15 gation of child abuse or child sexual abuse, neglect, or other criminal acts
16 is made, implicating either party, the court shall order by a written notice

27 the investigating agency shall provide written status updates to the court

30 and convincing standard set forth in section 32-717B , Idaho Code.

32 measures only for good cause and in compliance with sections 32-717B and
33 32-717F, Idaho Code. Any ex parte or temporary order shall state an expira-
34 tion date and create no presumption for or against either parent.
35 (4) Findings regarding allegations shall not be based solely on the
36 opinions or recommendations of guardians ad litem, evaluators, or other
37 court-appointed professionals.

39 services, and a parent's compliance with it, shall not be used as evidence of
40 fault, unfitness, restriction, or risk of harm in any custody or parenting
41 time proceeding.
