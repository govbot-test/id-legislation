LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 20
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES
3 SENATE, THE SPEAKER OF THE UNITED STATES HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES, THE
4 SECRETARY OF STATE OF THE UNITED STATES, AND THE CONGRESSIONAL DELE-
5 GATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED
6 STATES.
7 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
8 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
9 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
10 WHEREAS, the State of Idaho is constitutionally obligated to maintain a
11 general, uniform, and thorough system of public, free common schools; and
12 WHEREAS, federal immigration policy and enforcement are powers vested
13 exclusively in the United States Congress; and
14 WHEREAS, pursuant to the decision of the United States Supreme Court in
15 Plyler v. Doe, 457 U.S. 202 (1982), states are required to provide access to
16 public K-12 education to students residing within their borders regardless
17 of immigration status; and
18 WHEREAS, the fiscal responsibility for complying with such federal con-
19 stitutional requirements rests primarily with state and local governments,
20 without a dedicated federal funding stream tied to immigration status or
21 lawful presence; and
22 WHEREAS, the average per-pupil expenditure in the State of Idaho is ap-
23 proximately $11,000 per student per year, and the aggregate cost to Idaho of
24 educating students whose presence in the United States is governed by fed-
25 eral immigration policy represents a significant and ongoing financial com-
26 mitment borne primarily by state and local taxpayers; and
27 WHEREAS, students requiring English language acquisition services, re-
28 medial instruction, and supplemental academic supports often generate addi-
29 tional instructional costs beyond base per-pupil funding; and
30 WHEREAS, federal funding under Title I of the Elementary and Secondary
31 Education Act (ESEA), intended to support low-income students, and Title
32 III of ESEA, intended to support English learners, does not fully reimburse
33 states or school districts for the actual costs of providing required ser-
34 vices; and
35 WHEREAS, the allocation formulas under Title I and Title III of ESEA are
36 not designed to offset the full fiscal impact on states arising from federal
37 immigration policy decisions or constitutional requirements; and
38 WHEREAS, Idaho's local school districts must balance constitutional
39 educational obligations with limited state and local revenues, creating
40 measurable budgetary pressures that may affect staffing levels, class
41 sizes, and program offerings for all students; and
42 WHEREAS, principles of fiscal federalism support the proposition that
43 when federal policy decisions generate substantial financial obligations

2
1 for states, the federal government should provide commensurate funding sup-
2 port.
3 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
4 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
5 the Senate concurring therein, that:
6 (1) The Idaho Legislature respectfully urges the Congress of the United
7 States to establish a dedicated federal reimbursement or block grant program
8 to assist states in covering the costs of educating students residing in the
9 United States regardless of immigration status. Such funding should be de-
10 signed to supplement, not supplant, existing Title I and Title III ESEA al-
11 locations and should reflect actual per-pupil expenditures incurred by the
12 State of Idaho;
13 (2) Congress should review and revise federal education funding for-
14 mulas to ensure that states disproportionately impacted by federal immigra-
15 tion policy receive equitable fiscal support; and
16 (3) Congress should commission a comprehensive fiscal impact study
17 evaluating the aggregate cost to states of complying with federal constitu-
18 tional and immigration-related educational obligations.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
20 tives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this
21 Memorial to the President of the United States, the President of the United
22 States Senate, the Speaker of the United States House of Representatives,
23 the Secretary of State of the United States, and the congressional delega-
24 tion representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the United States.
