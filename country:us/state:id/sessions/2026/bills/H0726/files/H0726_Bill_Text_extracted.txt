14 SECTION 1. That Section 22-2721, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
15 amended to read as follows:
16 22-2721. ELECTION, APPOINTMENT, QUALIFICATIONS AND TENURE OF SUPER-
17 VISORS. (1) The governing body of the district shall consist of five (5)
18 supervisors, elected or appointed as provided in this chapter. Elections
19 shall be conducted pursuant to the provisions of this section and the uni-
20 form district election law, chapter 14, title 34, Idaho Code. If at any time
21 the supervisors of a district deem it necessary, upon majority vote of the
22 district board, they may increase the number of supervisors to seven (7) or
23 decrease the number of supervisors to five (5). If increased, the additional
24 supervisors shall then be appointed as outlined in this section until such
25 time as regular district elections for two (2) supervisors in each district.
26 At that time, those districts having seven (7) supervisors shall then elect
27 four (4) supervisors for four (4) year terms. The two (2) supervisors ap-
28 pointed by the district shall be persons who are by training and experience
29 qualified to perform the specialized services that will be required of them
30 in the performance of their duties. In the event the supervisors of a dis-
31 trict, upon majority vote of the district board, deem it necessary to reduce
32 the number of supervisors to five (5), there must either be two (2) vacant
33 positions on the board or two (2) of the supervisors must first volunteer to
34 give up their seats. Written notification of the reduction in supervisors,
35 signed by the district board chairman, shall then be sent to the state soil
36 and water conservation commission. Such notification shall also be sent
37 to the secretary of state to ensure only two (2) or three (3) supervisors
38 shall be on the ballot for the next election cycle. All supervisors shall be
39 landowners or farmers of the district where they are elected or appointed and
40 shall be registered to vote in the state of Idaho.
41 (2) Within thirty (30) days after the date of issuance by the secretary
42 of state of a certificate of organization of a soil conservation district,

1 nominating petitions may be filed with the state soil and water conservation
2 commission to nominate candidates for supervisors of each district. The
3 county clerk shall conduct the election for the district in compliance with
4 chapter 14, title 34, Idaho Code, and shall be the election official for the
5 district. The election official shall have authority to extend the time
6 within which nominating petitions may be filed. Nominating petitions shall
7 be filed with the secretary of the district, and no such nominating petition
8 shall be accepted by the election official unless it shall be subscribed
9 by not less than five (5) persons who are qualified electors owning land
10 or residing within the boundaries of the district. The election official
11 shall give due notice of an election to be held, subject to the provisions
12 of section 34-106, Idaho Code, for the election of three (3) supervisors for
13 the district. The names of all nominees on behalf of whom such nominating
14 petitions have been filed within the time herein designated shall appear
15 upon ballots, with directions to choose three (3) names to indicate the
16 voter's preference. The three (3) candidates who receive the largest num-
17 ber, respectively, of the votes cast in such election shall be the elected
18 supervisors for such district.
19 (3) All elections in districts shall be conducted by the county clerk.
20 Such election shall be held on the first Tuesday succeeding the first Monday
21 of November in each even-numbered year. Such elections shall be in compli-
22 ance with the provisions of chapter 14, title 34, Idaho Code, and shall be
23 supervised and conducted by the county clerk. The cost of conducting such
24 elections shall be borne by the county that conducted the election. The
25 county clerk shall certify to the soil and water conservation district the
26 names of the elected supervisors. The soil and water conservation district
27 shall issue certificates of election to each elected supervisor so certi-
28 fied. The county clerk or county clerks of the county or counties in which
29 the district is located shall conduct the election for the soil conservation
30 district, and the county clerk must provide a ballot for the district elec-
31 tion and must provide a process that allows only qualified electors of the
32 district to vote in that district's election.
33 (4) In any election for supervisor, if, after the deadline for filing
34 a declaration of intent as a write-in candidate, it appears that the num-
35 ber of qualified candidates who have been nominated is equal to the number
36 of supervisors to be elected, it shall not be necessary for the candidates to
37 stand for election. The board of supervisors shall declare such candidates
38 elected as supervisors, and the soil and water conservation district shall
39 immediately make and deliver to such persons certificates of election.
40 (5) The supervisors shall designate a chairman and may, from time to
41 time, change such designation. The term of office of each supervisor shall
42 be four (4) years commencing on the first day of January next following elec-
43 tion, except that the two (2) supervisors who are first appointed shall be
44 designated to serve for terms of two (2) years. Vacancies shall be filled for
45 the unexpired term. The selection of successors to fill an unexpired term
46 or for a full term shall be made by a vote of the majority of the supervi-
47 sors duly qualified and acting at the time the vacancy shall arise, and the
48 supervisors shall certify the name of the appointed supervisor to the state
49 soil and water conservation commission. In the event supervisors fail to
50 appoint a replacement within ninety (90) days following a vacancy, and the

3
1 vacancy results in the loss of a quorum of the district board, the governor
2 shall appoint a replacement sufficient to reestablish a quorum of the dis-
3 trict board. The soil conservation district shall issue a certificate of
4 such appointment.
5 (6) A quorum shall consist of three (3) supervisors for those districts
6 with a total of five (5) supervisors and four (4) supervisors for those dis-
7 tricts with a total of seven (7) supervisors, and the concurrence of a major-
8 ity in any matter within their duties shall be required for its determina-
9 tion. A supervisor shall be entitled to expenses, including travel expense,
10 necessarily incurred in the discharge of duties. A supervisor shall receive
11 no compensation for services from regular district funds, county funds au-
12 thorized in section 22-2726, Idaho Code, or state funds authorized in sec-
13 tion 22-2727, Idaho Code.
14 (7) In the event the district has a special project, approved by the
15 state soil and water conservation commission, making project funds avail-
16 able from federal or other sources, a supervisor may receive compensation
17 not to exceed thirty-five dollars ($35.00) per day plus actual and necessary
18 expenses from project funds for services directly related to the project.
19 (8) The supervisors may employ a secretary, technical experts, and such
20 other officers, agents, and employees, permanent and temporary, as they may
21 require and shall determine their qualifications, duties and compensation.
22 The supervisors may call upon the attorney general of the state for such le-
23 gal services as they may require or may employ their own counsel and legal
24 staff. The supervisors may delegate to their chairman, to one (1) or more
25 supervisors, or to one (1) or more agents or employees such powers and du-
26 ties as they may deem proper. The supervisors shall furnish to the state soil
27 and water conservation commission, upon request, copies of such ordinances,
28 rules, orders, contracts, forms and other documents as they shall adopt or
29 employ and such other information concerning the supervisors' activities as
30 the commission may require in the performance of the commission's duties un-
31 der this chapter.
32 (9) The supervisors shall provide for the execution of surety bonds for
33 all employees and officers entrusted with funds or property; shall provide
34 for the keeping of a full and accurate record of all proceedings and of all
35 resolutions and orders issued or adopted; and shall provide for independent
36 financial audits in accordance with the provisions of section 67-450B, Idaho
37 Code. Supervisors shall be subject to recall in accordance with the provi-
38 sions of chapter 17, title 34, Idaho Code.
39 (10) The supervisors may invite the legislative body of a municipality
40 or county located near the territory comprised within the district to desig-
41 nate a representative to advise and consult with the supervisors of the dis-
42 trict on all questions of program and policy that may affect the property,
43 water supply, or other interests of such municipality or county.
44 (11) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the
45 election for supervisor scheduled for November 2026 shall instead be held
46 on the third Tuesday in May 2027 and shall be for a term of four (4) years;
47 and the election for supervisor scheduled for November 2028 shall instead
48 be held on the third Tuesday in May 2029 and shall be for a term of four (4)
49 years.

28 SECTION 4. That Section 34-308, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
29 amended to read as follows:
30 34-308. MAIL BALLOT PRECINCT. (1) A precinct within the county that
31 contains no more than one hundred forty (140) registered electors at the
32 last general election may be designated by the board of county commissioners
33 as a mail ballot precinct no later than April 1 in an even-numbered year.
34 Such a designation shall apply thereafter to all elections conducted within
35 the precinct until revoked by the board of county commissioners or until
36 the precinct contains one hundred fifty-one (151) registered electors at
37 the last general election. Having designated a mail ballot precinct, there
38 shall be no voting place established within the precinct. Elections in a
39 mail ballot precinct shall be conducted in a manner consistent with absentee
40 voting with the special provisions provided in this section.
41 (2) The clerk shall issue a ballot, by mail, to every registered voter
42 in a mail ballot precinct and shall affix postage to the return envelope suf-
43 ficient to return the ballot.
44 (3) The ballot shall be mailed no sooner than twenty-four (24) forty-
45 five (45) days prior to the election day and no later than the fourteenth
46 eleventh day prior to the election.
47 (4) The clerk shall make necessary provisions to segregate mail bal-
48 lot precinct ballots by precinct and, for all purposes of the election, the
49 precinct integrity shall be maintained.

1 (5) The clerk shall make registration available in the office of the

4 shall provide an official polling place in the office of the clerk, and a
5 qualified elector who registers on election day and resides in a mail ballot
6 precinct shall be allowed to vote at the office of the clerk.
7 (6)(a) Except as provided in paragraph (b) of this subsection, electors
8 who have designated a political party affiliation pursuant to section

17 Idaho Code.
18 (7) For "unaffiliated" electors, in order to receive a political
19 party's primary election ballot pursuant to section 34-904A, Idaho Code, the
20 county clerk shall mail a ballot request form for the primary election ballot
21 to the electors in a mail ballot precinct for the electors to use in selecting

24 designated as "unaffiliated" to vote in their party's primary election
