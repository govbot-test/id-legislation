LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 118
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A SENATE RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE SENATE AND PROPOSING THE CREATION OF A STATE PLAN FOR
3 KINSHIP CAREGIVERS IN ORDER TO PROVIDE A COMPREHENSIVE BLUEPRINT TO EN-
4 SURE THAT INITIATIVES AND ACTIONS IMPLEMENTED TO SUPPORT KINSHIP CARE-
5 GIVERS ARE COORDINATED AND TO MAXIMIZE THE USE OF PUBLIC AND COMMUNITY-
6 BASED RESOURCES.
7 Be It Resolved by the Senate of the State of Idaho:
8 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers are grandparents, other adult family mem-
9 bers, or close family friends who are raising children when such children's
10 parents are unable to do so; and
11 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers offer children permanency and improved
12 well-being and reduce dependency on the foster care system; and
13 WHEREAS, for every one Idaho child in foster care being raised by kin,
14 there are 18 being raised by kin outside of foster care; and
15 WHEREAS, more than 10,000 children in Idaho are being raised by their
16 grandparents; and
17 WHEREAS, more than 43,000 children are living in homes where a relative
18 is the head of household; and
19 WHEREAS, the dedication and selflessness of kinship caregivers enhance
20 the quality of life for those they care for, often sacrificing their time,
21 energy, emotional and physical health, financial stability, and personal
22 goals to provide care and support; and
23 WHEREAS, the absence of a solid support structure for kinship care-
24 givers, including access to resources and respite care, takes a serious toll
25 on the economic and social well-being of families and businesses and commu-
26 nities across Idaho; and
27 WHEREAS, kinship caregivers and those they care for would benefit from a
28 multi-sector state-planning initiative to connect public, private, and in-
29 dependent sectors to transform the infrastructure and coordination of ser-
30 vices.
31 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Senate, assembled
32 in the Second Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, that
33 the Senate endorses the efforts of the Idaho caregiver alliance to develop
34 a state plan for kinship caregivers and recommendations to increase pub-
35 lic awareness, to provide training for caregivers, to improve caregiver
36 supports, including respite resources, to integrate support for kinship
37 caregivers into education and health care systems, and to develop methods
38 for ongoing data collection and reporting to monitor changes and assure re-
39 sponsible use of public and private resources.
