LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 828
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE MERIT-BASED HEALTH CARE ACT; AMENDING TITLE 56, IDAHO CODE,
3 BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW CHAPTER 25, TITLE 56, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
4 A SHORT TITLE, TO PROVIDE LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT, TO DEFINE
5 TERMS, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING PROHIBITED ACTS, TO ESTABLISH
6 PROVISIONS REGARDING MEDICAID CONTRACTUAL COMPLIANCE, TO ESTABLISH
7 PROVISIONS REGARDING ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES, TO ESTABLISH PROVI-
8 SIONS REGARDING A LIMITED PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION, AND TO ESTABLISH
9 PROVISIONS REGARDING FEDERAL PREEMPTION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
10 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
11 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
12 SECTION 1. That Title 56, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
13 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
14 ter 25, Title 56, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
15 CHAPTER 25
16 MERIT-BASED HEALTH CARE ACT
17 56-2501. SHORT TITLE. This chapter shall be known and may be cited as
18 the "Merit-Based Health Care Act."
19 56-2502. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. (1) The legislature finds
20 that:
21 (a) Medicaid participation is voluntary and subject to reasonable con-
22 ditions imposed by the state as a steward of public funds;
23 (b) Employment decisions based on merit and professional qualifica-
24 tions promote patient trust and high-quality care; and
25 (c) Employment practices unrelated to job-related qualifications or
26 business necessity undermine program integrity, workforce competency,
27 and the efficient use of public funds.
28 (2) It is the intent of the legislature to:
29 (a) Ensure that health care employment and contracting decisions
30 funded by medicaid are based on individual merit, qualifications, and
31 clinical competency;
32 (b) Prohibit the use of state medicaid funds to support ideological or
33 discriminatory employment practices; and
34 (c) Preserve compliance with all applicable federal civil rights and
35 medicaid requirements.
36 (3) Nothing in this chapter is intended to authorize discrimination
37 prohibited by federal law or to interfere with patient-specific medical
38 decision-making.
39 56-2503. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:

2
1 (1) "Discriminatory hiring" means granting a preference or imposing a
2 disadvantage in employment or contracting based on race, sex, sexual orien-
3 tation, gender identity, or national origin, except as expressly required by
4 federal law.
5 (2) "Diversity, equity, and inclusion" or "DEI" means the policies com-
6 monly known and practiced as DEI, critical race theory, or anti-racism, any
7 form of race or sex stereotyping or scapegoating, or the concepts that:
8 (a) One race or sex is inherently superior to another race or sex;
9 (b) The United States is fundamentally racist or sexist;
10 (c) An individual, by virtue of the individual's race or sex, is in-
11 herently racist, sexist, or oppressive, whether consciously or uncon-
12 sciously;
13 (d) An individual should be discriminated against or receive adverse
14 treatment solely or partly because of the individual's race or sex;
15 (e) Members of one race or sex cannot and should not attempt to treat
16 others without respect to race or sex;
17 (f) An individual's moral character is necessarily determined by the
18 individual's race or sex;
19 (g) An individual, by virtue of the individual's race or sex, bears re-
20 sponsibility for actions committed in the past by other members of the
21 same race or sex;
22 (h) An individual should feel discomfort, guilt, anguish, or any other
23 form of psychological distress on account of the individual's race or
24 sex;
25 (i) Meritocracy or traits such as a hard work ethic are racist or sexist
26 or were created by a particular race to oppress another race; or
27 (j) All people should be compelled to believe in and speak in a manner
28 consistent with the concept that an individual can change the individ-
29 ual's sex or gender.
30 (3) "Health care provider" means any individual or entity that volun-
31 tarily participates in the Idaho medicaid program or receives medicaid reim-
32 bursements in whole or in part through a state-contracted managed care net-
33 work.
34 (4) "Prohibited DEI conduct" means any policy, practice, training, or
35 requirement that includes:
36 (a) Consideration of DEI in hiring, promotion, compensation, disci-
37 pline, termination, or contracting decisions;
38 (b) Race-based or sex-based preferences, targets, benchmarks, quotas,
39 or equity goals;
40 (c) Mandatory bias, implicit bias, systemic bias, or similar training
41 that assigns responsibility, disadvantage, or professional outcomes
42 based on protected characteristics rather than individual merit or per-
43 formance; or
44 (d) Required statements, pledges, attestations, or affirmations en-
45 dorsing diversity, equity, and inclusion principles or related ideo-
46 logical frameworks as a condition of employment or contracting.
47 (5) "Public-facing communication" means any communication made avail-
48 able to the public, including marketing materials, websites, mission or
49 value statements, publicly presented staff trainings, facility signage, or
50 social media.

3
1 56-2504. PROHIBITED ACTS. (1) With respect to employment, policies,
2 training, or communications, a health care provider shall not:
3 (a) Engage in discriminatory hiring or prohibited DEI conduct;
4 (b) Adopt or implement internal policies or trainings that constitute
5 prohibited DEI conduct; or
6 (c) Use state funds, including medicaid reimbursements, for any pub-
7 lic-facing communication that promotes prohibited DEI conduct.
8 (2) Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit:
9 (a) Compliance with title VI of the civil rights act of 1964 or other
10 applicable federal civil rights laws;
11 (b) Collection of demographic data for legitimate clinical care, qual-
12 ity reporting, or public health purposes;
13 (c) Patient-specific medical discussions related to biological, ge-
14 netic, or epidemiological risk factors;
15 (d) Non-ideological training required for clinical competency, licen-
16 sure, accreditation, or federal program compliance; or
17 (e) Workforce training, quality improvement initiatives, or dispar-
18 ity-reduction programs required or encouraged by federal law, centers
19 for medicare and medicaid services guidance, or accreditation bodies.
20 56-2505. MEDICAID CONTRACTUAL COMPLIANCE. (1) Compliance with this
21 chapter shall be a material condition of all Idaho medicaid provider agree-
22 ments as applied to medicaid-funded activities.
23 (2) The department of health and welfare shall ensure that all managed
24 care contracts require network-wide compliance with the provisions of this
25 chapter for medicaid-funded activities.
26 (3) This section shall be implemented in a manner consistent with fed-
27 eral medicaid requirements and centers for medicare and medicaid services
28 guidance.
29 56-2506. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES. (1) The attorney general may in-
30 vestigate alleged violations of this chapter upon receipt of a formal com-
31 plaint.
32 (2) Prior to the imposition of civil penalties, the department shall
33 provide written notice and an opportunity for corrective action.
34 (3) The attorney general may seek civil penalties for violations of
35 this chapter as follows:
36 (a) For a health care provider with fifty (50) or more employees:
37 (i) A penalty not to exceed ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for a
38 first violation;
39 (ii) A penalty not to exceed fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) for
40 a second violation; and
41 (iii) A penalty not to exceed one hundred thousand dollars
42 ($100,000) for a third or subsequent violation; and
43 (b) For a health care provider with fewer than fifty (50) employees:
44 (i) A penalty not to exceed five thousand dollars ($5,000) for a
45 first violation;
46 (ii) A penalty not to exceed twenty-five thousand dollars
47 ($25,000) for a second violation; and

4
1 (iii) A penalty not to exceed fifty thousand dollars ($50,000) for
2 a third or subsequent violation.
3 56-2507. LIMITED PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION. A health care professional
4 may bring an action solely for retaliation arising from refusing to partici-
5 pate in prohibited DEI conduct. Remedies may include reinstatement, injunc-
6 tive relief, compensatory damages, and reasonable attorney's fees.
7 56-2508. SAVINGS CLAUSE AND FEDERAL PREEMPTION. Nothing in this chap-
8 ter shall be construed to restrict conduct required or encouraged by fed-
9 eral civil rights law, centers for medicare and medicaid services guidance,
10 or federal conditions of participation. If any provision of this chapter is
11 preempted by federal law, such provision shall be severed while the remain-
12 der remains in full force and effect.
13 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
14 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
15 July 1, 2026.
