LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 917
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO AI REGULATORY REVIEW; AMENDING TITLE 67, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDI-
3 TION OF A NEW CHAPTER 34, TITLE 67, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS
4 FOR THE AI REGULATORY REVIEW ACT; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
7 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
8 ter 34, Title 67, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
9 CHAPTER 34
10 AI REGULATORY REVIEW ACT
11 67-3401. SHORT TITLE. This chapter shall be known and may be cited as
12 the "AI Regulatory Review Act."
13 67-3402. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. (1) The legislature finds
14 that:
15 (a) Outdated and unnecessarily burdensome regulations impose substan-
16 tial costs to the state and on individuals and businesses;
17 (b) Artificial intelligence can systematically analyze regulatory
18 frameworks at scales impractical by manual human review; and
19 (c) Regular systematic review ensures regulations remain relevant, ef-
20 fective, and minimally burdensome.
21 (2) Therefore, it is the intent of the legislature to establish AI-em-
22 powered regulatory review so as to reduce unnecessary regulatory burdens
23 while maintaining public protections.
24 67-3403. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
25 (1) "Agency" means any department, board, commission, or other entity
26 of the state authorized by the legislature to adopt rules or regulations.
27 (2) "Artificial intelligence" or "AI" or "AI system" means advanced ar-
28 tificial intelligence systems capable of autonomous analysis and recommen-
29 dation generation regarding regulatory requirements.
30 (3) "Guidance document" means any agency statement that lacks the force
31 and effect of law but provides direction regarding regulatory compliance or
32 interpretation.
33 (4) "Plain language" means clear, concise, well-organized writing that
34 is appropriate to the subject matter and intended audience.
35 (5) "Regulation" means any rule, standard, or other mandate adopted by
36 an agency that has the force and effect of law.
37 (6) "Qualified agency personnel" means a person employed by an agency
38 that has subject matter expertise or legal or regulatory analysis expertise.

2
1 67-3404. AI REVIEW REQUIRED. All agencies shall cause their regula-
2 tions and guidance documents to be reviewed by an AI system when conducting
3 their periodic review pursuant to section 67-5292, Idaho Code. The AI system
4 shall:
5 (1) Analyze:
6 (a) The mandates provided for by the regulation or guidance document
7 and determine:
8 (i) Whether such mandates are required by state or federal law
9 or regulation or another binding authority or are discretionary,
10 including relevant binding and persuasive state and federal case
11 law;
12 (ii) Whether such mandates conflict with or are duplicative of
13 other regulatory or statutory provisions of the state;
14 (iii) How such mandates compare to those of neighboring states and
15 federal laws and regulations; and
16 (iv) Whether such mandates can be simplified and written in plain
17 language while achieving the same mandated objective.
18 (b) The language of the regulation and guidance document to determine
19 whether the text uses plain language;
20 (c) The cost of complying with the regulation or guidance document, in-
21 cluding:
22 (i) Agency and public training hours required to ensure compli-
23 ance;
24 (ii) Time associated with filing and processing various forms and
25 paperwork required to be done by the agency or public;
26 (iii) Fees and direct costs associated with compliance for the
27 agency and public; and
28 (iv) Other resources required for compliance with the regulation
29 or guidance document; and
30 (d) The economic impact of its proposed modifications to the agency's
31 regulation or guidance document; and
32 (2) Provide:
33 (a) Recommendations for proposed modifications based on its analysis;
34 (b) A cost-benefit analysis of the proposed modifications; and
35 (c) An estimate of the potential savings from accepting a proposed mod-
36 ification.
37 67-3405. HUMAN REVIEW REQUIRED. (1) All AI-generated analyses and
38 recommendations shall be reviewed and approved by qualified agency person-
39 nel. No agency regulation or guidance document shall be amended or repealed
40 based solely on AI analysis without human review and approval conducted by
41 qualified agency personnel.
42 (2) Within thirty (30) days of the AI completing its review, the quali-
43 fied agency personnel shall:
44 (a) Verify the factual accuracy of the AI analyses;
45 (b) Assess appropriateness of the AI-proposed modification recommen-
46 dations;
47 (c) Ensure AI-proposed modifications maintain and align with applica-
48 ble statutory authority and legislative intent;

3
1 (d) Approve or reject each of the AI-proposed modification recommenda-
2 tions;
3 (e) Eliminate mandates duplicative of or contradictory to state or fed-
4 eral law or regulation;
5 (f) Correct plain language deficiencies;
6 (g) Streamline and reduce any unnecessary regulatory burden on the
7 agency or public;
8 (h) Ensure references and citations remain current and remove or revise
9 such provisions; and
10 (i) Initiate rulemaking pursuant to the provisions of this chapter and
11 chapter 52, title 67, Idaho Code.
12 (3) If, after conducting the review, the qualified agency personnel de-
13 termines that certain provisions identified by the AI as potentially unnec-
14 essary should, nevertheless, be retained, the agency shall document:
15 (a) The rationale for such determination;
16 (b) The benefit provided by such provision; and
17 (c) Why less burdensome alternatives or use of plain language is insuf-
18 ficient.
19 67-3406. RULEMAKING AND REPORTING REQUIRED. (1) Rulemaking, pursuant
20 to the Idaho administrative procedures act, chapter 52, title 67, Idaho
21 Code, shall be promptly initiated by an agency to incorporate changes based
22 on review conducted pursuant to the provisions of this chapter. Agencies
23 shall use such act's expedited rulemaking procedures for non-substantive
24 corrections and burden-reducing changes whenever possible.
25 (2) Each agency shall annually publish a report containing:
26 (a) The number of regulations and guidance documents reviewed;
27 (b) The AI-generated review for each regulation and guidance document,
28 including its analysis and recommendations;
29 (c) The review process conducted by the qualified agency personnel;
30 (d) The number of AI-generated recommendations that were accepted by
31 the agency and appear as proposed changes to the rule;
32 (e) The estimated quantified burden reductions achieved; and
33 (f) The estimated cost savings to the agency and the public, including
34 regulated persons.
35 (3) Such report shall:
36 (a) Be publicly available and published on the respective agency's web-
37 site; and
38 (b) Be delivered and presented to the appropriate legislative commit-
39 tee for review and approval pursuant to section 29, article III of the
40 constitution of the state of Idaho and chapter 52, title 67, Idaho Code.
41 (4) An agency may combine the report required by this section with the
42 legislative report required by section 67-5292, Idaho Code.
43 67-3407. IMMEDIATE AGENCY ACTION REQUIRED. Within six (6) months of
44 the effective date of this chapter, each agency shall:
45 (1) Acquire or develop AI systems capable of performing the analyses
46 required by this chapter;
47 (2) Complete an AI-assisted comprehensive review of all regulations
48 and guidance documents under its authority;
