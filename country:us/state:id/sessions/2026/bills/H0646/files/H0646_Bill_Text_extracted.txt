20 48-2104. LITIGATION FINANCING PROTECTIONS. (1) A litigation fi-
21 nancier shall not:
22 (a) Pay or offer commissions, referral fees, rebates, or other forms
23 of consideration to any person in exchange for referring a consumer or a
24 consumer's legal representative to a litigation financier;
25 (b) Accept any commissions, referral fees, rebates, or other forms of
26 consideration from any person providing any goods or rendering any ser-
27 vices to the consumer;
28 (c) Charge a rate of interest that exceeds the greater of fifteen
29 percent (15%) or an amount that is six (6) percentage points per year
30 above the prime rate published by the federal reserve system in its
31 statistical release publication, H.15 selected interest rates for bank
32 prime loans, dated three (3) business days prior to the execution of
33 the agreement. Interest shall be allowed according to the terms of the
34 agreement;
35 (d) Receive or recover any payment that exceeds twenty-five percent
36 (25%) of the amount of any judgment, award, settlement, verdict, or
37 other form of monetary relief obtained in the legal claim that is the
38 subject of the litigation contract;
39 (e) Advertise false or misleading information regarding its products
40 or services;
41 (f) Refer or require any consumer to hire or engage any person providing
42 any goods or rendering any services to the consumer;
43 (g) Fail to promptly deliver a fully completed and signed litigation
44 financing contract to the consumer and the consumer's legal representa-
45 tive;
46 (h) Attempt to secure a remedy or obtain a waiver of any remedy, includ-
47 ing but not limited to compensatory, statutory, or punitive damages,
48 that the consumer may or may not be entitled to pursue or recover other-
49 wise;

6
1 (i) Offer or provide legal advice to the consumer;
2 (j) Assign, including securitizing, a litigation financing contract in
3 whole or in part;
4 (k) Make, influence, or direct a decision regarding a legal claim, in-
5 cluding decisions in appointing or changing counsel, choice or use of
6 expert witnesses, litigation strategy, and settlement or other resolu-
7 tion; or
8 (l) Demand, request, receive, or exercise any right to influence, af-
9 fect, or otherwise make any decision in the handling, conduct, admin-
10 istration, litigation, settlement, or resolution of any legal claim in
11 which the litigation financier has provided litigation financing. All
12 rights remain solely with the consumer and the consumer's legal repre-
13 sentative.
14 (2) A person who provides any goods or renders any services to the con-
15 sumer shall not have a financial interest in litigation financing and shall
16 not receive any commissions, referral fees, rebates, or other forms of con-
17 sideration from any litigation financier or the litigation financier's em-
18 ployees, owners, or affiliates.
19 (3) A party, attorney or law firm representing a party, or affiliated
20 attorney or law firm shall not disclose or share any proprietary information
21 with a litigation financier.
22 48-2105. LITIGATION FINANCING CONTRACT -- DISCLOSURES. (1) The terms
23 and conditions of a litigation financing contract shall be set forth in a
24 fully completed written contract with no terms or conditions omitted. The
25 litigation financing contract shall contain all terms and conditions at the
26 time it is signed by any party to the litigation financing contract.
27 (2) Upon execution of a litigation financing contract, a litigation fi-
28 nancier shall not amend the terms or conditions of the litigation financing
29 contract without full disclosure to and the prior written consent of all par-
30 ties to the litigation financing contract.
31 (3) A litigation financing contract shall set forth the name, physical
32 street address, and mailing address of the litigation financier on page one
33 (1) of the litigation financing contract.
34 (4) A litigation financing contract shall contain the following dis-
35 closures that constitute material terms and conditions of the litigation
36 financing contract and shall be typed in at least fourteen (14) point bold
37 font and be placed clearly and conspicuously immediately above the con-
38 sumer's signature line in the litigation financing contract:
39 IMPORTANT DISCLOSURES -- PLEASE READ CAREFULLY
40 1. Right to Cancellation: You may cancel this litigation financ-
41 ing contract without penalty or further obligation within five (5)
42 business days from the date you sign this contract or the date you
43 receive financing from the litigation financier, whichever date is
44 later. You may cancel by sending a notice of cancellation to the
45 litigation financier and returning to the litigation financier any
46 funds received from the litigation financier at the litigation fi-
47 nancier's address set forth on page 1 of this contract.

7
1 2. The maximum amount the litigation financier may receive or re-
2 cover from any payment may not exceed twenty-five percent (25%) of
3 the amount of any judgment, award, settlement, verdict, or other
4 form of monetary relief obtained in the civil action, administra-
5 tive proceeding, claim, or cause of action that is the subject of
6 this litigation contract.
7 3. The litigation financier agrees that it has no right to, and
8 will not demand, request, receive, or exercise any right to, in-
9 fluence, affect, or otherwise make any decision in the handling,
10 conduct, administration, litigation, settlement, or resolution of
11 your civil action, administrative proceeding, claim, or cause of
12 action. All of these rights remain solely with you and your legal
13 representative.
14 4. If there is no recovery of any money from your civil action, ad-
15 ministrative proceeding, claim, or cause of action, or if there is
16 not enough money to satisfy in full the portion assigned to the lit-
17 igation financier, you will not owe anything in excess of your re-
18 covery.
19 5. You are entitled to a fully completed contract with no terms or
20 conditions omitted prior to signing. Before signing this contract,
21 you should read the contract completely and consult an attorney.
22 (5) If the consumer is represented by a legal representative in the le-
23 gal claim that is the subject of the litigation financing contract, the legal
24 representative shall acknowledge in the contract that the legal representa-
25 tive and the legal representative's employer and employees have not received
26 or paid a referral fee or any other consideration from or to the litigation
27 financier and have no obligation to do so in the future.
28 (6) If the consumer's legal representative is a party to a litigation
29 financing contract related to the consumer's legal claim that is the subject
30 of the litigation financing contract, the legal representative shall dis-
31 close and deliver the litigation financing contract to the consumer. Fol-
32 lowing this disclosure and delivery, the consumer shall sign an acknowledg-
33 ment that the consumer has read and understands the terms and conditions of
34 the litigation financing contract and the consumer shall be provided with a
35 copy of the acknowledgment.
36 48-2106. DISCLOSURE AND DISCOVERY OF LITIGATION FINANCING CON-
37 TRACTS. (1) Except as otherwise stipulated or ordered by a court of competent
38 jurisdiction, a consumer or the consumer's legal representative shall,
39 without awaiting a discovery request, disclose and deliver to the following
40 persons the litigation financing contract:
41 (a) Each party to the legal claim, or to each party's legal representa-
42 tive;
43 (b) The court, agency, or tribunal in which the legal claim may be pend-
44 ing; and
45 (c) Any known person, including an insurer, with a preexisting contrac-
46 tual obligation to indemnify or defend a party to the legal claim.
47 (2) The disclosure obligation pursuant to subsection (1) of this sec-
48 tion exists regardless of whether a legal claim has commenced.

8
1 (3) The disclosure obligation pursuant to subsection (1) of this sec-
2 tion is a continuing obligation, and within thirty (30) days of entering into
3 a litigation financing contract or amending an existing litigation financ-
4 ing contract, the consumer or the consumer's legal representative shall dis-
5 close and deliver any new or amended litigation financing contracts.
6 (4) The existence of a litigation financing contract and all partici-
7 pants or parties to the litigation financing contract are permissible sub-

11 48-2107. EXEMPTIONS. This chapter does not apply to the following:
12 (1) A nonprofit entity that is exempt from federal income taxation pur-
13 suant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code or a nonprofit entity
14 that provides litigation financing, funding, or money to a legal representa-
15 tive to represent a consumer on a pro bono basis, provided that the nonprofit
16 entity does not receive in consideration for the litigation financing the
17 payment of interest, fees, or other consideration in excess of the amount of
18 attorney's fees and litigation expenses incurred in conjunction with or in
19 pursuit of the legal claim;
20 (2) Any litigation financing provided by an entity engaged in commerce
21 or business activity, but only if the entity does not:
22 (a) Charge or collect any interest, fees, or other consideration;
23 (b) Retain or receive any financial interest in the outcome of the legal
24 claim; or
25 (c) Receive any right to recovery or payment from the amount of any
26 judgment, award, settlement, verdict, or other form of monetary relief
27 obtained in conjunction with or in pursuit of the legal claim; or
28 (3) A regulated lender that does not receive, in consideration for
29 loaning money to any person, a right to receive payment from the value of any
30 proceeds or other consideration realized from any judgment, award, settle-
31 ment, verdict, or other form of monetary relief any person may receive or
32 recover in conjunction with or in pursuit of the legal claim.
33 48-2108. CLASS ACTIONS. In addition to all other actions to which this
34 chapter applies, this chapter shall also apply to any civil action filed or
35 certified as a class action in which litigation financing is provided. A
36 litigation financier owes a fiduciary duty to all class members or intended
37 beneficiaries of a certified class and shall act in a manner consistent with
38 the litigation financier's fiduciary duty throughout the civil action. In
39 addition to the disclosure requirements set forth in this chapter, the legal
40 representative of the putative class shall disclose to all parties, putative
41 class members, and the court any legal, financial, or other relationship be-
42 tween the legal representative and the litigation financier. A class member
43 is entitled to receive from the class counsel a true and correct copy of the
44 litigation financing contract on request.
45 48-2109. JOINT AND SEVERAL LIABILITY FOR COSTS. A litigation fi-
46 nancier is jointly and severally liable for any award or order imposing or
47 assessing costs or monetary sanctions against a consumer or a party's legal

3 48-2110. PROHIBITION ON LITIGATION FUNDING BY FOREIGN ADVERSARIES OR
4 FOREIGN PERSONS OF CONCERN. (1) A foreign adversary or a foreign person of
5 concern may not engage in litigation financing in this state.
6 (2) A foreign adversary or a foreign person of concern may not, directly
7 or indirectly, invest in, finance, fund, or loan money to a litigation fi-
8 nancier engaged in litigation financing in this state.
9 (3) A litigation financier engaged in litigation financing in this
10 state may not, directly or indirectly:
11 (a) Become affiliated with or maintain an affiliation with a foreign
12 adversary or a foreign person of concern;
13 (b) Receive or accept money or funds from a foreign adversary or a for-
14 eign person of concern; or
15 (c) Allow a foreign adversary or a foreign person of concern to invest
16 in, maintain an ownership interest in, or exercise any control over the
17 litigation financier.
18 48-2111. RESTRICTION ON DISCLOSURE OF PROPRIETARY INFORMATION. A
19 party to a legal claim in this state shall not disclose to or share with a
20 foreign person, a foreign adversary, or a foreign person of concern any pro-
21 prietary information.
22 48-2112. LIMITATIONS ON FOREIGN LITIGATION FUNDING. A foreign person
23 who, directly or indirectly: engages in litigation financing; becomes af-
24 filiated with or maintains an affiliation with a litigation financier; fi-
25 nances, funds, or loans money to a litigation financier; or invests in, main-
26 tains an ownership interest in, or exercises any control over a litigation
27 financier shall not:
28 (1) Become affiliated with or maintain an affiliation with a foreign
29 adversary or a foreign person of concern;
30 (2) Receive or accept money or funds from a foreign adversary or a for-
31 eign person of concern; or
32 (3) Allow a foreign adversary or a foreign person of concern to invest
33 in, maintain an ownership interest in, or exercise control over the foreign
34 person.

36 eign person who is qualified to engage in litigation financing in this state
37 shall comply with this chapter if the foreign person engages in litigation

41 litigation financier engages in any of the following:
42 (a) Becomes affiliated with or maintains affiliation with a foreign
43 person;
44 (b) Receives or accepts money or funds from a foreign person for any
45 purpose; or

10
1 (c) Allows a foreign person to invest in, maintain an ownership inter-
2 est in, or exercise any control over the litigation financier.
3 (3) The following information shall be disclosed in writing to the sec-
4 retary of state:
5 (a) The name and address of the foreign person and the citizenship of
6 the foreign person or the country of incorporation, formation, or reg-
7 istration of the foreign person; and
8 (b) A copy of each litigation financing contract in which the foreign

14 48-2114. REGULATORY OVERSIGHT -- RULEMAKING. The secretary of state
15 is authorized to adopt rules and other policies in overseeing the practice of
16 litigation financing consistent with this chapter, subject to legislative
17 approval.
18 48-2115. ACT VIOLATION -- UNENFORCEABLE CONTRACT. (1) Any violation
19 of this chapter by a litigation financier shall render the litigation fi-
20 nancing contract unenforceable by the litigation financier or any succes-
21 sor-in-interest to the litigation financing contract.
22 (2) If a litigation financier charges a rate of interest that exceeds
23 the rate of interest allowed pursuant to section 48-2104(1)(c), Idaho Code,
24 the litigation financier shall be subject to a penalty for usury and an ac-
25 tion to recover excessive interest.
26 48-2116. APPLICABILITY. This chapter shall apply to any legal claim
27 pending or commenced on or after July 1, 2026.
28 SECTION 2. SEVERABILITY. The provisions of this act are hereby declared
29 to be severable and if any provision of this act or the application of such
30 provision to any person or circumstance is declared invalid for any reason,
31 such declaration shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of
32 this act.
33 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
34 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
35 July 1, 2026.
