LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1389
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO POLLING PLACES; AMENDING SECTION 34-302, IDAHO CODE, TO PRO-
3 VIDE A LIMITATION OF LIABILITY FOR OWNERS OF PROPERTY USED AS A POLLING
4 PLACE; AMENDING SECTION 63-602, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS RE-
5 GARDING PROPERTY EXEMPT FROM TAXATION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND
6 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 34-302, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 34-302. DESIGNATION OF PRECINCT POLLING PLACES -- LIMITATION OF LI-
11 ABILITY. (1) The board shall, by the fifth Friday before any election,
12 designate a suitable polling place for each election precinct. Insofar as
13 possible, the board shall designate the same polling place for the general
14 election that it designated for the primary election. The physical arrange-
15 ments of the polling place shall be sufficient to guarantee all voters the
16 right to cast a secret ballot. Public school facilities shall be made avail-
17 able to the board as precinct polling places. All polling places designated
18 as provided herein shall conform to the accessibility standards adopted by
19 the secretary of state pursuant to the voting accessibility for the elderly
20 and handicapped act, 52 U.S.C. 20101 et seq. The expense of providing such
21 polling places shall be a public charge and paid out of the county treasury.
22 (2) An owner of a property who either directly or indirectly invites
23 or permits without charge any person to use such property as a polling place
24 owes no duty of care to keep the property safe for entry by others for polling
25 purposes or to give any warning of a dangerous condition, use, structure,
26 or activity on such land to persons entering for polling purposes. The in-
27 stallation of a sign or other form of warning of a dangerous condition, use,
28 structure, or activity; any modification made for the purpose of improving
29 the safety of others; or the failure to maintain or keep in place any sign,
30 other form of warning, or modification made to improve safety shall not cre-
31 ate liability on the part of an owner of property where there is no other ba-
32 sis for such liability. Additionally, such owner of a property does not, by
33 permitting such use as a polling place:
34 (a) Extend any assurance that the property is safe for any purpose;
35 (b) Confer on any person entering on such owner's property the legal
36 status of an invitee or licensee to whom a duty of care is owed; or
37 (c) Assume responsibility for or incur liability for any injury to per-
38 son or property caused by an act or omission of any person entering on
39 such owner's property.
