LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1249
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO JUVENILE CORRECTIONS; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; AMENDING
3 SECTION 20-524A, IDAHO CODE, TO REMOVE OUTDATED LANGUAGE; AND DECLARING
4 AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
7 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
8 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
9 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
10 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
11 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.
12 SECTION 2. That Section 20-524A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
13 amended to read as follows:
14 20-524A. DEPARTMENT'S PAYMENT OF DETENTION COSTS. If the juvenile of-
15 fender is committed to the custody of the department of juvenile corrections
16 pursuant to chapter 5, title 20, Idaho Code, the department shall reimburse
17 the county for the period of time in excess of five (5) calendar days during
18 which the juvenile offender is housed at a detention center. This time pe-
19 riod shall begin to run on the first business day the department receives a
20 copy of the order of commitment, executed by the court. Orders received by
21 the department after 3 o'clock p.m., mountain standard time, on a business
22 day, will be considered to have been received the next business day. Facsim-
23 ile transmissions of the order are acceptable.
24 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
25 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
26 July 1, 2026.
