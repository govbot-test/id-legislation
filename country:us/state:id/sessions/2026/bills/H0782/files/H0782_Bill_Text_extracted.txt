14 SECTION 1. That Section 63-3024, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
15 amended to read as follows:
16 63-3024. INDIVIDUALS' TAX AND TAX ON ESTATES AND TRUSTS. (1) For each
17 taxable year, a tax measured by Idaho taxable income as defined in this chap-
18 ter is hereby imposed upon every individual, trust, or estate required by
19 this chapter to file a return.
20 (2)(a) The tax imposed upon individuals, trusts, and estates shall be
21 computed at the rate of five and three-tenths percent (5.3%) five and
22 three hundred twenty-five thousandths percent (5.325%) of taxable in-
23 come over two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500).
24 (b) For taxpayers filing a joint return pursuant to the provisions of
25 section 63-3031, Idaho Code, the tax imposed shall be computed at the
26 rate of five and three-tenths percent (5.3%) five and three hundred
27 twenty-five thousandths percent (5.325%) of taxable income over five
28 thousand dollars ($5,000). For the purposes of this section, a return
29 of a surviving spouse, as defined in section 2(a) of the Internal Rev-
30 enue Code, and a head of household, as defined in section 2(b) of the
31 Internal Revenue Code, shall be treated as a joint return.
32 (3) For taxable year 2000 and each year thereafter, the state tax com-
33 mission shall prescribe a factor that shall be used to compute the Idaho in-
34 come tax thresholds provided in subsection (2) of this section. The factor
35 shall provide an adjustment to the Idaho tax thresholds so that inflation
36 will not result in a tax increase. The Idaho tax thresholds shall be adjusted
37 as follows: multiply the last threshold amount by the percentage (the con-
38 sumer price index for the calendar year immediately preceding the calendar
39 year to which the adjusted threshold amount will apply divided by the con-
40 sumer price index for calendar year 1998). For the purpose of this computa-
41 tion, the consumer price index for any calendar year is the average of the
42 consumer price index as of the close of the twelve (12) month period for the

2
1 immediately preceding calendar year, without regard to any subsequent ad-
2 justments, as adopted by the state tax commission. This adoption shall be
3 exempt from the provisions of chapter 52, title 67, Idaho Code. The consumer
4 price index shall mean the consumer price index for all U.S. urban consumers
5 published by the United States department of labor. The state tax commission
6 shall annually include the factor as provided in this subsection to multiply
7 against Idaho taxable income using the thresholds in this section to arrive
8 at that year's Idaho taxable income for tax threshold purposes.
9 (4) In the case of a trust that is an electing small business trust as
10 defined in section 1361 of the Internal Revenue Code, the special rules for
11 taxation of such trusts contained in section 641 of the Internal Revenue Code
12 shall apply, except that the individual rate provided in subsection (2)(a)
13 of this section shall apply in computing tax due under this chapter.
14 (5) The state tax commission may promulgate rules defining the condi-
15 tions upon which such returns shall be filed.

19 hereby imposed on the Idaho taxable income of a corporation, other than an
20 S corporation, that transacts or is authorized to transact business in this
21 state or has income attributable to this state. The tax rate imposed by this
22 section shall be as follows:
23 (a) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2001, the
24 tax imposed by this section shall be equal to seven and six-tenths per-
25 cent (7.6%) of Idaho taxable income.
26 (b) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2012, the
27 tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to seven and
28 four-tenths percent (7.4%) of Idaho taxable income.
29 (c) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2018, the
30 tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to six and nine
31 hundred twenty-five thousandths percent (6.925%) of Idaho taxable in-
32 come.
33 (d) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2021,
34 the tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to six and
35 five-tenths percent (6.5%) of Idaho taxable income.
36 (e) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2022, the
37 tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to six percent
38 (6%) of Idaho taxable income.
39 (f) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2023, the
40 tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to five and
41 eight-tenths percent (5.8%) of Idaho taxable income.
42 (g) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2024, the
43 tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to five and six
44 hundred ninety-five thousandths percent (5.695%) of Idaho taxable in-
45 come.
46 (h) For each taxable year, commencing on and after January 1, 2025, the
47 tax imposed by this section is changed and shall be equal to five and
48 three-tenths percent (5.3%) of Idaho taxable income.

24 SECTION 3. That Section 63-3029L, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
25 amended to read as follows:
26 63-3029L. CHILD TAX CREDIT. (1) For taxable years beginning on or af-
27 ter January 1, 2018, and before January 1, 2026, there shall be allowed to a
28 taxpayer a nonrefundable credit against the tax imposed by this chapter in
29 the amount of two hundred five dollars ($205) with respect to each qualifying
30 child of the taxpayer. For purposes of this section, the term "qualifying
31 child" has the meaning as defined in section 24(c) of the Internal Revenue
32 Code. In no event shall more than one (1) taxpayer be allowed this credit for
33 the same qualifying child. This credit is available only to Idaho residents.
34 Any part-year resident entitled to a credit under this section shall receive
35 a proportional credit reflecting the part of the year in which the part-year
36 resident was domiciled in Idaho.
37 (2) In the case of divorced parents or parents who do not live together,
38 if the qualifying child is in the custody of one or both of the child's par-
39 ents for more than one-half of a calendar year, such child is the qualifying
40 child of the custodial parent for the taxable year beginning during such cal-
41 endar year. However, the child may be the qualifying child of the noncusto-
42 dial parent if either of the following requirements are met:
43 (a) A court of competent jurisdiction has unconditionally awarded, in
44 writing, to the noncustodial parent the tax benefits associated with
45 the child pursuant to section 32-706, Idaho Code, and the noncustodial
46 parent attaches a copy of the court order to the noncustodial parent's
47 income tax return for the taxable year; or
48 (b) The custodial parent signs a written declaration that such custo-
49 dial parent will not claim the credit of this section with respect to

4

2 noncustodial parent attaches such written declaration to the noncusto-
3 dial parent's income tax return for the taxable year beginning during

7 SECTION 5. That Section 67-1230, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 67-1230. IDAHO PARENTAL CHOICE TAX CREDIT ADVANCE PAYMENT FUND. (1)
10 There is hereby established in the state treasury the Idaho parental choice
11 tax credit advance payment fund, to be administered by the state tax commis-
12 sion.
13 (2) Moneys in the fund shall:
14 (a) Consist of the following:
15 (i) Legislative appropriations and transfers;
16 (ii) Donations and contributions made to the fund;
17 (iii) Reversions of unused, paid back, or recovered advance pay-
18 ment funds; and
19 (iv) Interest earned on idle moneys in the fund; and
20 (b) Be continuously appropriated to pay advance payments awarded un-
21 der the Idaho parental choice tax credit program established in section
22 63-3029N, Idaho Code.
23 (3) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the state
24 controller shall transfer the unobligated cash balance remaining in the
25 Idaho parental choice tax credit advance payment fund to the general fund on
26 July 1, 2026, or as soon thereafter as practicable but no later than December
27 1, 2026.
28 SECTION 6. That Section 67-1230, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
29 repealed.
