9 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
10 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. (1) The Legislature finds
11 that the rapid proliferation of non-primary residential properties and
12 high-occupancy short-term rentals has placed an unprecedented and dispro-
13 portionate strain on Idaho's public safety infrastructure. The increased
14 demand for law enforcement, emergency medical services, and fire protec-
15 tion, particularly in areas with high seasonal populations, frequently
16 outpaces the revenue generated by traditional local property tax struc-
17 tures. Furthermore, the displacement of Idaho's workforce by out-of-state
18 investment interests has created a critical shortage of local first respon-
19 ders who can afford to live in the communities they serve. It is therefore
20 the intent of the Legislature to:
21 (a) Establish a sustainable revenue source dedicated to the funding of
22 emergency services and law enforcement; and
23 (b) Ensure that those who utilize Idaho's residential market for sea-
24 sonal or investment purposes contribute proportionally to the public
25 safety systems that protect their investments.
26 (2) The Legislature finds that while residential expansion adds to
27 the tax base, the growth-related costs associated with infrastructure,
28 maintenance, and the constitutional mandate to provide a "general, uniform
29 and thorough system of public, free common schools" frequently exceed the
30 revenue generated by primary residential homesteads. Under the current
31 tax system, the financial burden of rapid growth falls unfairly on Idaho's
32 long-term residents and workforce. By distinguishing between primary
33 dwellings and secondary residential investments, the state can mitigate
34 this imbalance. It is therefore the intent of the Legislature to:
35 (a) Utilize a state-level levy on non-primary residential properties
36 to offset the increased financial obligations of the state and its local
37 taxing districts caused by rapid expansion; and
38 (b) Direct revenue from such state-level levy toward direct property
39 tax credits for primary homesteads, thereby lowering the tax burden for
40 Idaho citizens.
41 (3) In accordance with Section 2, Article VII of the Constitution of
42 the State of Idaho, the Legislature hereby declares that this state property
43 tax is needful to ensure the long-term viability of Idaho's physical infra-

2
1 structure. The Legislature finds that the conversion of residential neigh-
2 borhoods into high-turnover investment properties places unique stress on
3 local road networks, water systems, and sewer facilities originally engi-
4 neered for lower-intensity use. Seasonal surges and high-occupancy periods
5 require accelerated maintenance of public thoroughfares and necessitate
6 expanding water treatment and sewage processing capacities to handle peak
7 demand. Because these capital-intensive utilities must be maintained at
8 maximum capacity regardless of fluctuating occupancy rates, the costs of
9 such readiness must be shared by those driving the demand. It is therefore
10 the intent of the Legislature to ensure that the development and mainte-
11 nance of roads, water systems, and sewer infrastructure are funded through
12 a system where secondary residential investments contribute to community
13 infrastructure that sustains their property value.
14 SECTION 2. That Section 63-216, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
15 repealed.
16 SECTION 3. That Chapter 6, Title 63, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
17 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
18 ignated as Section 63-602PP, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
19 63-602PP. EXEMPTIONS FROM STATE PROPERTY TAX. (1) The following prop-
20 erty is exempt from the state property tax authorized in section 63-801,
21 Idaho Code:
22 (a) Personal property;
23 (b) Operating property;
24 (c) Forest land as defined in section 63-1701, Idaho Code;
25 (d) Property primarily devoted to the exploration, development, or ex-
26 traction of any mineral as that term is defined in chapter 7, title 47,
27 Idaho Code, and any mining claims whether patented or unpatented;
28 (e) Property that qualifies for the exemption provided in section
29 63-602G, Idaho Code;
30 (f) Real property classified as commercial property, industrial prop-
31 erty, or land actively devoted to agriculture;
32 (g) Any additional residential property owned by a natural person who
33 is a resident of the state of Idaho and who has been granted a homestead
34 exemption pursuant to section 63-602G, Idaho Code, for a primary resi-
35 dence within the state; however, this exemption shall not apply to any
36 property utilized as a short-term rental for more than thirty (30) days
37 in a calendar year, unless said short-term rental has a maximum occu-
38 pancy level of six (6) or fewer individuals; and
39 (h) Residential property that is not a primary homestead but is occu-
40 pied by a tenant under a long-term lease of at least seven (7) months and
41 is not subleased by such tenant.
42 (2) Any person who knowingly provides false information regarding the
43 occupancy, use, or homestead status of residential property to avoid the
44 state property tax shall be subject to the penalties for escaped assessments
45 as provided in section 63-1401, Idaho Code, and the recovery of back taxes
46 and interest as provided in section 63-602G, Idaho Code.

3 63-801. ANNUAL STATE PROPERTY TAX LEVY -- STATE PROPERTY TAX FUND --
4 COLLECTION AND DISTRIBUTION. (1) The county commissioners in each county in
5 this state must meet on the second Monday of September in each year to as-
6 certain the tax rate necessary to be levied on each dollar of the valuation
7 of all the taxable property in the county for such year in order to raise the
8 amount of state taxes apportioned to such county by the state tax commission.
9 The total of all levies must be within the limits prescribed by the laws of
10 this state.
11 (2) In any period during which a sales tax is in force in this state,
12 there shall be no levy of the general state property tax permitted by section
13 9, article VII, of the constitution of the state of Idaho. There is hereby
14 levied a state property tax on real and personal property not otherwise ex-
15 empted by section 63-602PP, Idaho Code, as follows:
16 (a) The rate of such tax shall be two and one-half (2.5) mills on each
17 dollar of the taxable value of all real and personal property subject to
18 such tax; and
19 (b) The county treasurer shall collect the state property tax imposed
20 pursuant to this section in the same manner as other property taxes pur-
21 suant to section 63-901, Idaho Code, and all revenues collected from the
22 state property tax shall be remitted to the state treasurer for deposit
23 into the state property tax fund.
24 (3) There is hereby created in the state treasury the state property tax
25 fund. The fund shall consist of moneys deposited into it pursuant to this
26 section, and such moneys are continuously appropriated to be distributed by
27 the state tax commission to taxing districts as provided in this section.
28 The funds shall be distributed by the state tax commission to taxing dis-
29 tricts within the county where the taxes were collected as follows:
30 (a) Fifty percent (50%) of funds collected within such county shall
31 be distributed to the county and the cities and fire districts therein
32 to be used exclusively to fund law enforcement services, fire pro-
33 tection services, and emergency medical services. Such funds shall
34 be allocated in proportion to each entity's share of the total public
35 safety-related expenses of the county and all cities within the county.
36 For the purposes of this paragraph, "public safety-related expenses"
37 means the sum of any revenue raised from:
38 (i) A county levy pursuant to section 63-805, Idaho Code, to the
39 extent such revenues are expended for sheriff services, jails,
40 fire services, or ambulance services;
41 (ii) An ambulance service district levy pursuant to section
42 31-3901, Idaho Code;
43 (iii) Any fire protection district levy pursuant to chapter 14,
44 title 31, Idaho Code;
45 (iv) A city general fund levy pursuant to section 50-235, Idaho
46 Code, to the extent such revenues are expended for police ser-
47 vices, fire services, or ambulance services; and
48 (v) A city police retirement levy pursuant to section 50-1512,
49 Idaho Code;
