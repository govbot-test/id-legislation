28 74-3-101. PURPOSE OF CHAPTER. The purpose of this chapter is:
29 (1) To promote openness and public confidence in government; and
30 (2) To promote transparency by those giving financial support to elec-
31 tion campaigns and to persons advocating for or against candidates or ballot
32 measures.

40 precinct committeeman.

25 74-3-103. FOREIGN CONTRIBUTIONS, FOREIGN INDEPENDENT EXPENDITURES,
26 AND FOREIGN ELECTIONEERING COMMUNICATIONS PROHIBITED. (1) A foreign na-
27 tional shall not make a contribution, directly or indirectly, to any
28 candidate, political action committee, or measure or make electioneering
29 communications or independent expenditures.
30 (2) As used in this section:
31 (a) "Foreign national" means:
32 (i) An individual who is not a citizen of the United States and is
33 not lawfully admitted for permanent residence;
34 (ii) A government or subdivision of a foreign country;
35 (iii) A foreign political party; or
36 (iv) Any entity, such as a partnership, association, corporation,
37 organization, union, or other combination of persons, that is or-
38 ganized under the laws of or has its principal place of business in
39 a foreign country.
40 (b) "Indirectly" means making a contribution or payment to a person
41 with a designation, instruction, or encumbrance, whether direct or
42 indirect, express or implied, oral or written, or involving interme-
43 diaries or conduits, that results in any part of the contribution or
44 payment being used for a purpose described in subsection (1) of this
45 section.
46 (3) A violation of the provisions of this section shall be prosecuted
47 and punished as provided in part 5 of this chapter. However, any person who
48 knowingly and willfully violates the provisions of this section is guilty of
49 a felony when:

12 74-3-104. COMMERCIAL RECORDKEEPING. Each newspaper, periodical,
13 broadcasting station, direct mailing company, printer, and advertising
14 agency shall keep a current record of any and all obligations incurred by
15 a candidate or political action committee and payments made by a candidate
16 or political action committee. Such records shall be made available for
17 inspection upon request by the secretary of state. Failure to make such
18 records available shall result in a civil fine not to exceed one thousand
19 dollars ($1,000).

21 with the secretary of state under this chapter shall be signed and certi-
22 fied as true and correct by the person required to file the same. Electronic
23 signatures and certifications shall be governed by the uniform electronic
24 transactions act, chapter 50, title 28, Idaho Code.
25 74-3-106. IDENTIFICATION OF SOURCE OF CONTRIBUTIONS AND EXPENDITURES
26 -- COMMUNICATIONS. (1) No contribution shall be made and no expenditure
27 shall be incurred, directly or indirectly, in a fictitious name, anony-
28 mously, or by one (1) person through an agent, relative, or other person in
29 such a manner as to conceal the identity of the source of the contribution.
30 (2)(a) To ensure the proper reporting and identification of the source
31 of expenditures, any public communication made that is reportable as an
32 expenditure under this chapter shall clearly state: "Paid for by [the
33 official name of the candidate, political action committee, or fil-
34 ing entity]" and shall further state the filing entity identification
35 number, if available, as registered or reported with the secretary of
36 state.
37 (b) The provisions of this subsection shall not apply to campaign mate-
38 rials that are being reused by any candidate who has run for public of-
39 fice prior to 2026, as long as the person responsible for such communi-
40 cations is clearly indicated on such communications.
41 (c) Failure to comply with the provisions of this section shall result
42 in a civil fine payable to the secretary of state in the amount of fifty
43 dollars ($50.00) plus five percent (5%) of the monetary value of the ex-
44 penditure being made, rounded up to the nearest whole number. The sec-
45 retary of state shall deposit any civil fines collected pursuant to this
46 section to the general fund.

23 74-3-201. CANDIDATES FOR PUBLIC OFFICE -- ESTABLISHMENT OF CAMPAIGN
24 FINANCE ACCOUNT. (1) A candidate shall be subject to the requirements of this
25 chapter once the candidate has taken any of the following actions:
26 (a) Announced the individual's candidacy publicly;
27 (b) Established a campaign finance account with the secretary of
28 state's office;
29 (c) Received a contribution for the purpose of promoting the individ-
30 ual's candidacy for office; or
31 (d) Made an expenditure, contracted for services, or reserved space
32 with the intent of promoting the individual's candidacy for office.
33 (2) For purposes of this chapter, an incumbent shall be presumed to be
34 a candidate in the subsequent election for the incumbent's office until the
35 incumbent has failed to file for office by the statutory deadline.
36 (3) Each candidate shall establish a campaign finance account with the
37 secretary of state's office as prescribed by the secretary of state. The
38 candidate shall provide the full name and address of the candidate, the of-
39 fice being sought by the candidate, and the name and address of the candi-
40 date's political treasurer.
41 74-3-202. APPOINTMENT OF POLITICAL TREASURER FOR CANDIDATE. (1) Each
42 candidate shall appoint a political treasurer who is a registered elector of
43 this state. The candidate shall certify the full name and complete address
44 of the political treasurer to the secretary of state at the time the candi-
45 date establishes a campaign finance account with the secretary of state's
46 office or as soon thereafter as practicable.

39 74-3-210. USE OF SYNTHETIC MEDIA. (1) This section shall be known and
40 may be cited as the "Freedom from AI-Rigged (FAIR) Elections Act."
41 (2) For purposes of this section:
42 (a) "Information content provider" means any person or entity that is
43 responsible, in whole or in part, for the creation or development of in-
44 formation provided through the internet or any other interactive com-
45 puter service.
46 (b) "Interactive computer service" means any information service, sys-
47 tem, or access software provider that provides or enables computer ac-
48 cess by multiple users to a computer server, including specifically a

14
1 service or system that provides access to the internet and such systems
2 operated or services offered by libraries or educational institutions.
3 (c) "Synthetic media" means an audio recording or a video recording of
4 an individual's speech or conduct that has been created through the use
5 of generative adversarial network techniques or other digital technol-
6 ogy in a manner to create a realistic but false audio or video that:
7 (i) Appears to a reasonable individual to be of a real event, ac-
8 tion, or speech that did not actually occur in reality; and
9 (ii) Provides a fundamentally different understanding or impres-
10 sion of the event, action, or speech than a reasonable person would
11 have from the unaltered, original version of the audio recording
12 or video recording.
13 (3) A candidate whose action or speech is deceptively represented
14 through the use of synthetic media in an electioneering communication, ex-
15 penditure by a candidate, or independent expenditure may seek injunctive or
16 other equitable relief prohibiting the publication of such synthetic media.
17 (4) A candidate whose action or speech is deceptively represented
18 through the use of synthetic media in an electioneering communication, ex-
19 penditure by a candidate, or independent expenditure may bring an action for
20 general damages, special damages, or both against the information content
21 provider. The court may award a prevailing party reasonable attorney's fees
22 and costs. The provisions of this subsection do not limit or preclude a
23 plaintiff from securing or recovering any other available remedy.
24 (5) It shall be an affirmative defense for any action brought pursuant
25 to this section that the electioneering communication, expenditure by a
26 candidate, or independent expenditure containing synthetic media includes
27 a disclosure stating, "This (video/audio) has been manipulated" in the fol-
28 lowing manner:
29 (a) If the media is a video, the text of the disclosure must be promi-
30 nently displayed and appear in a size easily readable by the average
31 viewer, and the disclosure must appear for the duration of the video; or
32 (b) If the media consists of audio only, the disclosure must be read in
33 a clearly spoken manner and in a pitch that can be easily heard by the
34 average listener at the beginning of the audio, at the end of the au-
35 dio, and, if the audio is greater than two (2) minutes in length, in-
36 terspersed within the audio at intervals of no more than two (2) minutes
37 each.
38 (6) In any action commenced pursuant to this section, the plaintiff
39 bears the burden of establishing the use of synthetic media by clear and
40 convincing evidence.
41 (7) Courts are encouraged to determine matters pursuant to this section
42 expediently.
43 (8) For an action brought pursuant to this section, the information
44 content provider of the electioneering communication, expenditure by a
