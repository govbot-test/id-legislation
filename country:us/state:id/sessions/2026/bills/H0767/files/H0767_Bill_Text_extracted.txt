10 67-8203. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
11 (1) "Affordable housing" means housing affordable to families whose

21 subsection (24)(f) of this section.

32 SECTION 2. That Section 67-8210, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
33 amended to read as follows:
34 67-8210. EARMARKING AND EXPENDITURE OF COLLECTED DEVELOPMENT IMPACT
35 FEES. (1) An ordinance imposing development impact fees shall provide that
36 all development impact fee funds shall be maintained in one (1) or more
37 interest-bearing accounts within the capital projects fund. Accounting
38 records shall be maintained for each category of system improvements and the
39 service area in which the fees are collected. Interest earned on development
40 impact fees shall be considered funds of the account on which it is earned,
41 and not funds subject to section 57-127, Idaho Code, and shall be subject
42 to all restrictions placed on the use of development impact fees under the
43 provisions of this chapter.
44 (2) Expenditures of development impact fees shall be made only for the
45 category of system improvements and within or for the benefit of the service
46 area for which the development impact fee was imposed as shown by the capi-
47 tal improvements plan and as authorized in this chapter. Development impact
48 fees shall not be used for any purpose other than system improvement costs

5
1 to create additional improvements to serve new growth and the replacement of
2 fire apparatus pursuant to section 67-8203(24)(f), Idaho Code.
3 (3) As part of its annual audit process, a governmental entity shall
4 prepare an annual report:
5 (a) Describing the amount of all development impact fees collected, ap-
6 propriated, or spent during the preceding year by category of public fa-
7 cility and service area; and
8 (b) Describing the percentage of tax and revenues other than impact
9 fees collected, appropriated or spent for system improvements during
10 the preceding year by category of public facility and service area.
11 (4) Collected development impact fees must be expended within eight
12 (8) years from the date they were collected, on a first-in, first-out (FIFO)
13 basis, except that the development impact fees collected for wastewater
14 collection, treatment and disposal and drainage facilities must be expended
15 within twenty (20) years. Any funds not expended within the prescribed times
16 shall be refunded pursuant to section 67-8211, Idaho Code. A governmental
17 entity may hold the fees for longer than eight (8) years if it identifies, in
18 writing:
19 (a) A reasonable cause why the fees should be held longer than eight (8)
20 years; and
21 (b) An anticipated date by which the fees will be expended but in no
22 event greater than eleven (11) years from the date they were collected.
23 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby

25 July 1, 2026.
