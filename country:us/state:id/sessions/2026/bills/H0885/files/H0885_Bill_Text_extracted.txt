Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 885
BY REVENUE AND TAXATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO TAXATION; AMENDING SECTION 63-602G, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVI-

5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 63-602G, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 63-602G. PROPERTY EXEMPT FROM TAXATION -- HOMESTEAD. (1) For each tax
9 year, the first one hundred twenty-five thousand dollars ($125,000) of the
10 market value for assessment purposes of the homestead as that term is defined
11 in section 63-701, Idaho Code, or fifty percent (50%) of the market value
12 for assessment purposes of the homestead as that term is defined in section
13 63-701, Idaho Code, whichever is the lesser, shall be exempt from property
14 taxation.
15 (2) The exemption allowed by this section may be granted only if:
16 (a) The homestead is owner-occupied and used as the primary dwelling
17 place of the owner. The homestead may consist of part of a multidwelling
18 or multipurpose building and shall include all of such dwelling or
19 building except any portion used exclusively for anything other than
20 the primary dwelling of the owner. The presence of an office in a home-
21 stead, which office is used for multiple purposes, including business
22 and personal use, shall not prevent the owner from claiming the exemp-
23 tion provided in this section; and
24 (b) The state tax commission has certified to the board of county com-
25 missioners that all properties in the county subject to appraisal by the
26 county assessor have, in fact, been appraised uniformly so as to secure
27 a just valuation for all property within the county; and
28 (c) The owner has certified to the county assessor that:
29 (i) He is making application for the exemption allowed by this
30 section;
31 (ii) The homestead is his primary dwelling place; and
32 (iii) He has not made application in any other county for the ex-
33 emption and has not made application for the exemption on any other
34 homestead in the county.; and
35 (iv) He has no similar homestead exemption in any other state in
36 the United States.
37 (d) For the purpose of this section, the definition of "owner" shall be
38 the same definition set forth in section 63-701(7), Idaho Code. When an
39 owner, pursuant to the provisions of section 63-701(7), Idaho Code, is
40 any person who is the beneficiary of a revocable or irrevocable trust,
41 or who is a partner of a limited partnership, a member of a limited lia-
42 bility company, or a shareholder of a corporation, he or she may provide

4 documents submitted with the affidavit set forth in section 63-703(4),
5 Idaho Code, that are held by a county assessor, and the copies shall
6 be returned by the county assessor upon submission of the affidavit in
7 proper form.
8 (f) For the purpose of this section, the definition of "primary
9 dwelling place" shall be the same definition set forth in section
10 63-701(8), Idaho Code.
11 (g) For the purpose of this section, the definition of "occupied" shall
12 be the same definition set forth in section 63-701(6), Idaho Code.

35 sued driver's license number or Idaho state-issued identification card
36 number, submit evidence of filing an Idaho individual income tax return
37 in the prior or current calendar year showing the address of the claimed
38 homestead property.

41 tions are met:
42 (a) The owner has received the exemption during the previous year as a
43 result of making a valid application as set forth in subsection (2)(c)
44 of this section.
45 (b) The owner or beneficiary, partner, member or shareholder, as appro-

7 date of eligibility status change provided on the approved application
8 for the current tax year and must be taken before the reduction in taxes

25 eligible for the exemption.

21 idence, facts or circumstances from the improperly approved ex-

28 an amount equal to the amount of property tax recovered pursuant
