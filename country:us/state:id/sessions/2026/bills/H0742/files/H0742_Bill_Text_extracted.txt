Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 742
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO VEHICLE TITLES; AMENDING SECTION 49-504, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
3 FOR ELECTRONIC RECORDS OF OWNERSHIP; AMENDING SECTION 49-511, IDAHO
4 CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING CANCELLATION OR DISCHARGE OF

12 made upon a form furnished by the department and shall contain the owner's

38 SECTION 2. That Section 49-511, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
39 amended to read as follows:
40 49-511. CANCELLATION OR DISCHARGE OF LIEN OR ENCUMBRANCE. (1) When a
41 lien or encumbrance is canceled or discharged, the lienholder shall provide
42 notice of such cancellation or discharge to the department within thirty
43 (30) days. If the lienholder was holding the paper certificate of title,
44 he shall note the cancellation or discharge on the certificate of title in
45 the space provided, over his signature, or by some other legal document,
46 discharging the encumbrance, and shall deliver the paper certificate of
47 title to the owner within thirty (30) days of receipt of payoff of the encum-
48 brance. If the lienholder was holding an electronic title, he shall send the
49 department an electronic transaction that directs the department to provide

4
1 a paper title to the owner free of the lienholder's lien within thirty (30)
2 days of receipt of payoff of the encumbrance.
3 (2)(a) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (1) of this sec-
4 tion, when an Idaho vehicle dealer licensed pursuant to chapter 16,
5 title 49, Idaho Code, cancels or discharges a lien or encumbrance, the
6 lienholder shall provide notice of such cancellation or discharge to
7 the department within ten (10) business days of receipt of verified
8 funds. If the lienholder was holding the paper certificate of title,
9 such lienholder shall note the cancellation or discharge on the cer-
10 tificate of title in the space provided, over his signature, or by some
11 other legal document, discharging the encumbrance. If the lienholder
12 has received authorization from the current owner to do so, by personal
13 notification from the current owner, by power of attorney, or by other
14 legal document granted to a dealer, the lienholder shall send the paper
15 certificate of title to the dealer within ten (10) business days of re-
16 ceipt of verified funds to pay off the encumbrance.
17 (b) For the purposes of this subsection, "verified funds" means funds
18 when such funds credit to the lienholder's account and become final in
19 accordance with the law or rules applicable to the method by which the
20 funds were transmitted to the lienholder.
21 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
22 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
