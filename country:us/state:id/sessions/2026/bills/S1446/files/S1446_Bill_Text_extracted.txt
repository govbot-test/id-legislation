7 SECTION 3. EXEMPTIONS FROM PROGRAM TRANSFER LIMITATIONS FOR BEHAVIORAL
8 HEALTH PROGRAMS. Notwithstanding any provisions of law to the contrary, the
9 Department of Health and Welfare is hereby exempted from the provisions of
10 Section 67-3511(2), Idaho Code, allowing unlimited transfers into the Ba-
11 sic Medicaid Plan Program and the Adult Mental Health Program from other pro-
12 grams within the department for all moneys appropriated to it for the period
13 July 1, 2025, through June 30, 2026, for the purpose of funding assertive
14 community treatment and peer support services.
15 SECTION 4. OPIOID SETTLEMENT FUND USAGE. The Legislature finds and de-
16 clares that assertive community treatment and peer support services serve
17 the overarching purposes of the national opioid settlements even though not
18 every person assertive community treatment and peer support services serves
19 suffers from opioid use disorder (OUD). In a time of fiscal exigency, every
20 dollar matters to ensure assertive community treatment and peer support ser-
21 vices remain viable. Therefore, in accordance with provisions in the opi-
22 oid settlement agreements that allow the state to use a limited percentage
23 of settlement funds for purposes not directly related to OUD, and notwith-
24 standing the provisions of section 57-825, Idaho Code, or any other provi-
25 sion of law to the contrary, of the amounts appropriated in Section 1 and
26 Section 2 of this act from the State-Directed Opioid Settlement Fund, up to
27 $5,808,000 may be used for assertive community treatment and peer support
28 services participants who face substance abuse disorders or mental health
29 conditions (SUD/MH) without co-occurring OUD as those terms are used in Ex-
30 hibit E, Schedule B of the settlement agreements.
31 SECTION 5. CONDITIONS, LIMITATIONS, AND RESTRICTIONS. Appropriations
32 made pursuant to this act are subject to the conditions, limitations, and re-
33 strictions specified in this act.
34 SECTION 6. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
35 declared to exist, Section 3 of this act shall be in full force and effect on
36 and after passage and approval, and Sections 1, 2, 4, and 5 of this act shall
37 be in full force and effect on and after July 1, 2026.
