LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 836
BY APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE STATE BUDGET; AMENDING CHAPTER 35, TITLE 67, IDAHO CODE, BY
3 THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 67-3520, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR THE
4 IDAHO PERSONNEL REDUCTION ACT; AMENDING CHAPTER 35, TITLE 67, IDAHO
5 CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 67-3520A, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
6 FOR THE REPORTING OF STATE EMPLOYEE TRAVEL EXPENSES; AND DECLARING AN
7 EMERGENCY.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Chapter 35, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
10 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
11 ignated as Section 67-3520, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 67-3520. IDAHO PERSONNEL REDUCTION ACT. (1) It is the intent of the
13 legislature that the Idaho state government performs its duties and serves
14 the public interest as well and efficiently as practicable. Discovering and
15 eliminating government waste will save taxpayer dollars while increasing
16 public confidence in the responsibility and integrity of Idaho state govern-
17 ment.
18 (2) For the purposes of this section:
19 (a) "Department" means a department, agency, institution, or office of
20 the state of Idaho that receives a legislative appropriation for per-
21 sonnel costs.
22 (b) "FTP" means full-time equivalent positions.
23 (3) On or before July 1, 2026, and annually on or before September 1
24 in each year thereafter, every department shall identify the number of cur-
25 rently vacant appropriated FTP, including portions of FTP, within such de-
26 partment that were vacant for more than one hundred eighty (180) calendar
27 days as of the end of the previous fiscal year.
28 (4) Vacant FTP identified in subsection (3) of this section shall be
29 eliminated, and the amount budgeted for the payment of salaries and wages,
30 including associated fringe benefits, of such eliminated FTP shall be re-
31 duced in the department's annual budget submission pursuant to section
32 67-3502, Idaho Code. Alternatively, if the vacant FTP, or portion of FTP,
33 has been vacant for less than one (1) year, a department may request to retain
34 it and shall fully fund the vacant FTP, or portion of FTP, from its existing
35 funds and shall fill the vacancy as soon as practicable.
36 (5) In 2026 and 2027, one-third (1/3) of any executive branch FTP elim-
37 inated pursuant to subsection (4) of this section and one-third (1/3) of any
38 associated fringe benefits shall be annually transferred to the legislative
39 account pursuant to an ongoing transfer and shall be managed by the speaker
40 of the house of representatives and the president pro tempore of the senate.
41 Of the moneys associated with any FTP and fringe benefits transferred to the
42 legislative account pursuant to this subsection, ninety-five percent (95%)

2
1 of such resources shall be allocated by the speaker of the house of represen-
2 tatives and the president pro tempore of the senate for district support per-
3 sonnel for individual legislators and associated administrative staff.
4 (6) The number of FTP identified in subsection (3) of this section shall
5 be detailed in the department's annual budget submission required pursuant
6 to section 67-3502, Idaho Code, together with the following information:
7 (a) Each individual vacant FTP or portion of FTP by job title;
8 (b) The length of time the position has been vacant;
9 (c) The job description associated with the position;
10 (d) Whether there is funding available to fill the FTP and, if so, the
11 source of such funds;
12 (e) The reason why the FTP remains vacant; and
13 (f) Whether the department has reduced the FTP from its budget submis-
14 sion or is requesting that the FTP be retained.
15 SECTION 2. That Chapter 35, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
16 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
17 ignated as Section 67-3520A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
18 67-3520A. REPORTING OF IDAHO STATE EMPLOYEE TRAVEL EXPENSES. (1) Con-
19 sistent with the legislative intent provided for in section 67-3520, Idaho
20 Code, it is also the intent of the legislature to discover and eliminate gov-
21 ernment waste involving state employee travel in order to save taxpayer dol-
22 lars while increasing public confidence in the responsibility and integrity
23 of Idaho state government.
24 (2) For the purposes of this section:
25 (a) "Department" means a department, agency, institution, or office of
26 the state of Idaho that receives a legislative appropriation for per-
27 sonnel costs.
28 (b) "Travel expenses" means costs associated with reimbursement of
29 travel or subsistence pursuant to the standard travel pay and allowance
30 act of 1949, chapter 20, title 67, Idaho Code.
31 (3) All travel expenses from the preceding fiscal year shall be de-
32 tailed in the department's annual budget submission required pursuant to
33 section 67-3502, Idaho Code, including detail regarding the following in-
34 formation:
35 (a) Total travel expenses of the department;
36 (b) Identification of all travel occurrences and the purposes of such
37 travel;
38 (c) Travel destinations;
39 (d) Airfare, mileage, car rental, lodging, food, conference fees, and
40 other expenses;
41 (e) Dates of travel; and
42 (f) Portions of any travel expenses that were paid by an entity other
43 than the state of Idaho.
44 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
45 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its
46 passage and approval.
