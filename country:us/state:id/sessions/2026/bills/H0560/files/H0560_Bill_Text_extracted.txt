Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 560
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO JURIES; AMENDING SECTION 2-211, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE AN EXEMP-
3 TION TO JURY SERVICE; AMENDING SECTION 2-212, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR
4 VOLUNTEER ELECTION SERVICE IN LIEU OF JURY SERVICE AND TO MAKE A TECHNI-
5 CAL CORRECTION; AMENDING SECTION 34-303, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE AN EX-
6 CEPTION TO CERTAIN COMPENSATION REQUIREMENTS FOR ELECTION PERSONNEL;
7 AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Section 2-211, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
10 amended to read as follows:
11 2-211. NO EXEMPTIONS. No exemptions for any qualified prospective ju-
12 ror may be granted, except as provided in section 2-212(4), Idaho Code.
13 SECTION 2. That Section 2-212, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
14 amended to read as follows:
15 2-212. EXCUSING OR POSTPONING JURY SERVICE -- INQUIRY BY COURT --
16 GROUNDS FOR EXCUSING OR POSTPONING -- VOLUNTEER ELECTION SERVICE IN LIEU OF
17 JURY SERVICE. (1) The court, or a member of the jury commission designated
18 by the court, upon request of a prospective juror or on its own initiative,
19 shall determine on the basis of information provided on the qualification
20 questionnaire form or interview with the prospective juror or other com-
21 petent evidence whether the prospective juror should be excused from jury
22 service or have their jury service postponed. The clerk or the jury commis-
23 sioner shall keep a record of this determination.
24 (2) A person who is seventy (70) years of age or older shall be perma-
25 nently excused if the person indicates on the qualification questionnaire
26 form that he or she wishes to be excused. A person who requests to be ex-
27 cused on this basis shall be reinstated to the county jury list by submitting
28 a written request asking to be reinstated for jury service.
29 (3) A person who is not disqualified for jury service under section
30 2-209, Idaho Code, may have jury service postponed by the court or the jury
31 commissioner only upon a showing of undue hardship, extreme inconvenience,
32 or public necessity, or upon a showing that the juror is a mother breastfeed-
33 ing her child.
34 (a) Any person requesting a postponement shall provide a written state-
35 ment setting forth the reason for the request and the anticipated date
36 that the reason will no longer exist.
37 (b) The court or the jury commissioner may require a person requesting a
38 postponement for any medical reason to provide a statement from a medi-
39 cal provider supporting the request.

15 (b) If a request is made by a county clerk pursuant to paragraph (a)

20 service in lieu of jury service provided in this subsection shall be
21 granted only for the number of positions requested by the county clerk
22 and at the discretion of the court.
23 (c) If a prospective juror accepts the volunteer election service op-
24 portunity pursuant to this subsection and completes such service in a
25 manner acceptable to the county clerk, the prospective juror shall be
26 excused from jury service for the next two (2) years. The county clerk
27 shall furnish to the jury commission the names of each prospective ju-
28 ror who accepted the election service in lieu of jury service provided
29 in this subsection and shall indicate whether such prospective juror
30 completed the election service. A prospective juror who accepts the
31 election service pursuant to this subsection, but fails to complete the
32 election service shall be reinstated to the county jury list.
33 SECTION 3. That Section 34-303, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
34 amended to read as follows:
35 34-303. APPOINTMENT OF ELECTION JUDGES BY COUNTY CLERK. (1) The
36 county clerk shall appoint two (2) or more election judges, one (1) of whom
37 shall be designated chief judge, and the number of clerks deemed necessary by
38 him for each polling place. In the event a single polling place is designated
39 for two (2) or more precincts, an individual may serve simultaneously on the
40 election board for two (2) or more precincts thus served by a single polling
41 place. The precinct committeemen shall recommend persons for the position
42 in their respective precincts to the county clerk in writing by the fifth
43 Friday prior to the primary election and the county clerk shall appoint the
44 judges from such lists if the persons recommended are qualified.
45 (2) The chief election judge shall be responsible for the conduct of the
46 proceedings in the polling place. Compensation for all election personnel
47 shall be determined by the board of county commissioners at no less than the
48 minimum wage as prescribed by the laws of the state of Idaho, except for per-
