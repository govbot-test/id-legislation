10 SECTION 1. That Chapter 3, Title 41, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
11 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
12 ignated as Section 41-349A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
13 41-349A. DISPENSING FEES. (1) A plan sponsor, pharmacy benefit man-
14 ager (PBM), or third-party payer shall ensure that reimbursement to indepen-
15 dent pharmacies for each drug dispensed is an amount that is not less than the
16 sum of the national average drug acquisition cost (NADAC) as provided for in
17 subsection (2) of this section and the professional dispensing fee as pro-
18 vided for in subsection (3) of the section.
19 (2) The NADAC shall be the published price in effect for the day that a
20 drug claim is billed by a pharmacy. However, if a particular drug does not
21 have a published NADAC, the reimbursement to an independent pharmacy shall
22 be:
23 (a) For generic drugs, one hundred percent (100%) of published whole-
24 sale acquisition costs; and
25 (b) For brand name drugs, one hundred percent (100%) of wholesale ac-
26 quisition costs.
27 (3) The minimum professional dispensing fee for independent pharmacies
28 shall be twelve dollars and thirty-five cents ($12.35), subject to an an-
29 nual increase as provided for in this subsection. On January 1 of each year,
30 every plan sponsor, PBM, and third-party payer shall increase the amount of
31 the minimum professional dispensing fee for independent pharmacies to ad-
32 just for inflation. Inflation shall be measured by the annual percentage in-
33 crease, if any, in the consumer price index for all urban consumers (CPI-U)
34 as published by the United States department of labor, bureau of labor sta-
35 tistics, for all items.
36 (4) The Idaho department of insurance shall issue a letter and guidance
37 to every plan sponsor, PBM, and third-party payer registered with the state
38 of Idaho no later than thirty (30) days after the effective date of this sec-
39 tion. All reimbursement rates shall be in full force and effect as of that
40 date, and plan sponsors, PBMs, and third-party payers shall issue additional
41 payments as needed to independent pharmacies to cover any deficiencies in
42 payment made after the effective date of this section.
