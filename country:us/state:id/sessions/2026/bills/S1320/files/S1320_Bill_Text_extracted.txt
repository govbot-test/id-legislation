LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1320
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE STATE CONTROLLER; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; REPEALING
3 SECTION 67-1021C, IDAHO CODE, RELATING TO THE BUSINESS INFORMATION IN-
4 FRASTRUCTURE FUND; AMENDING SECTION 67-1021A, IDAHO CODE, TO REMOVE OB-
5 SOLETE PROVISIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFEC-
6 TIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
9 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
10 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
11 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
12 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
13 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.
14 SECTION 2. That Section 67-1021C, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
15 repealed.
16 SECTION 3. That Section 67-1021A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
17 amended to read as follows:
18 67-1021A. BUSINESS INFORMATION INFRASTRUCTURE PROJECT. (1) Notwith-
19 standing any laws to the contrary, the state controller shall engage in a
20 project to modernize and replace the state's aging business information in-
21 frastructure, including its financial, payroll, human capital management,
22 budget and procurement systems. The purpose of the project shall be to mod-
23 ernize the state's business information infrastructure and to consolidate
24 duplicative business systems into a centralized enterprise resource plan-
25 ning system in order to achieve standardized business practices and greater
26 transparency in the state's data.
27 (2) The cost of modernizing the state's business information infra-
28 structure shall be equitably distributed among and between all state and
29 public entities that use the services and functions outlined in subsection
30 (1) of this section. On or before June 30 of each year from the effective
31 date of this act until and including June 30, 2023, all moneys deposited to
32 the indirect cost recovery fund resulting from the assessment of the amounts
33 allocated in the annual statewide indirect cost allocation plan pursuant
34 to section 67-3531, Idaho Code, shall be transferred to the business infor-
35 mation infrastructure fund established in section 67-1021C, Idaho Code.
36 Transfers under this section shall occur as requested by the state con-
37 troller and no later than June 30 of each year.
38 SECTION 4. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
39 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
40 July 1, 2026.
