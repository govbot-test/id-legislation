9 16-1602. DEFINITIONS. For purposes of this chapter:
10 (1) "Abused" means any case in which a child has been the victim of:
11 (a) Conduct or omission resulting in skin bruising, bleeding, mal-
12 nutrition, burns, fracture of any bone, head injury, soft tissue
13 swelling, failure to thrive or death, and such condition or death is not
14 justifiably explained, or where the history given concerning such con-
15 dition or death is at variance with the degree or type of such condition
16 or death, or the circumstances indicate that such condition or death may
17 not be the product of an accidental occurrence; or
18 (b) Sexual conduct, including rape, molestation, incest, commercial
19 sexual activity, obscene or pornographic photographing, filming or de-
20 piction for commercial purposes, human trafficking as defined in chap-
21 ter 86, title 18, Idaho Code, or other similar forms of sexual exploita-
22 tion harming or threatening the child's health or welfare or mental in-
23 jury to the child.

25 parental relationship with his child including, but not limited to, reason-
26 able support or regular personal contact. Failure to maintain this rela-

37 protective supervision or vesting legal custody of the child in an au-

36 16-2005. CONDITIONS UNDER WHICH TERMINATION MAY BE GRANTED.
37 (1)(a) The court may grant an order terminating the relationship where
38 it finds that termination of parental rights is in the best interests of
39 the child and that one (1) or more of the following conditions exist:
40 (i) The parent has abandoned the child;
41 (ii) The parent has neglected or abused the child;
42 (iii) The presumptive parent is not the biological parent of the
43 child;
44 (iv) The parent is unable to discharge parental responsibilities
45 and such inability will continue for a prolonged indeterminate pe-
46 riod and will be injurious to the health, morals or well-being of
47 the child; or

8 of the department.
