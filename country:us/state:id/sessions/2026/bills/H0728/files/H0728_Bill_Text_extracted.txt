29 SECTION 2. That Section 33-512D, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
30 amended to read as follows:
31 33-512D. SELF-DIRECTED LEARNER DESIGNATION. (1) A student attending
32 public school in Idaho shall be eligible to be designated as a self-directed
33 learner. For the purposes of this section, a "self-directed learner" means a
34 full-time student:
35 (a) Who demonstrates mastery of content knowledge through grades, as-
36 sessments, or mastery-based learning rubrics;
37 (b) Whose teacher or teachers designate the student as such. The
38 teacher may consider the student's mastery of the content, academic
39 growth, timeliness for assignments, self-motivation, ability to estab-
40 lish goals, and reaching age-appropriate learning outcomes;
41 (c) Who, starting in grade 5, demonstrates mastery of addition and mul-
42 tiplication for numbers 0-10, as well as related subtraction and divi-
43 sion problems, known collectively as "math facts"; and
44 (d) Who, starting in grade 8, demonstrates an informed choice of post-
45 secondary career and education goals by:
46 (i) Completing and updating his student learning plan as defined
47 in section 33-1001(30)(32), Idaho Code;
48 (ii) Supplementing his student learning plan, as applicable, with
49 the following that further his postsecondary goals:

7
1 1. Extended learning opportunities as defined in section
2 33-6401, Idaho Code;
3 2. Courses and examinations funded in chapter 46, title 33,
4 Idaho Code; or
5 3. Any other credits or programs permitted under Idaho Code
6 or district policy as applicable to the student's learning
7 plan; and
8 (iii) Identifying and writing down self-determined personal life
9 goals, including an explanation of how attending specific classes
10 will lead to the fulfillment of personal life goals.
11 (2) Each school district or public charter school may adopt a self-di-
12 rected learner policy to provide processes:
13 (a) Through which students may seek a self-directed learner designa-
14 tion;
15 (b) By which teachers may designate a student as a self-directed
16 learner;
17 (c) To monitor and support self-directed learners;
18 (d) By which a student's teacher or teachers rescind the self-directed
19 learner designation; and
20 (e) As otherwise necessary for implementation.
21 (3) Once a student is designated a self-directed learner, the student
22 has the right to flexible learning. Flexible learning may be different for
23 each student and may include flexible attendance, attending school virtu-
24 ally, extended learning opportunities, and any other agreed-upon learning
25 inside or outside the classroom. Starting in grade 8, flexible learning
26 should further the student's progress toward postsecondary goals. Any flex-
27 ible learning permitted under this section must be agreed upon by the stu-
28 dent, his teacher or teachers, and the student's parents or legal guardian.
29 (4) In order to remain a self-directed learner, the student must meet
30 criteria agreed upon by him, his teacher or teachers, and his parents or le-
31 gal guardian. Criteria may include continued mastery of content knowledge
32 and skills, academic growth, progress toward postsecondary goals, or other
33 measures of student learning. If a student fails to meet the agreed-upon
34 criteria or fails to stay current on classroom assignments, and does not cure
35 the failure within an agreed-upon time frame, the building administrator
36 shall rescind the self-directed learner designation upon recommendation by
37 the teacher or teachers.
38 (5) A self-directed learner will be reported as enrolled as one (1.0)
39 FTE or in attendance for a full day in school for the purpose of calculat-
40 ing support units and public school funding. The district or charter school
41 will receive full funding for its self-directed learners, regardless of at-
42 tendance or actual hours of instruction up to one (1) full day of attendance
43 or one (1.0) FTE, or the remaining day or FTE if the student is shared between
44 two (2) or more school districts or public charter schools.
45 (6) Each school district or public charter school must report the num-
46 ber of self-directed learners to the state department of education annually.

9 shall consist of moneys provided pursuant to sections 57-811, 63-3638, and
10 67-7434, Idaho Code, and any legislative transfers or appropriations. In-
11 terest earned from the investment of moneys in the fund shall be returned to
12 the fund. Moneys in the fund are hereby continuously appropriated for the
13 purposes stated in this section.
14 (2) The moneys in the fund shall be distributed by the state controller
15 to the state department of education by August 1 each year for the purpose of
16 construction or renovation of school facilities. The moneys shall be dis-
17 tributed by the state department of education to each school district, as
18 defined in section 33-1001(21)(23), Idaho Code, first as provided in sub-
19 section (7) of this section and then on a per-pupil basis using the average
20 daily attendance calculation provided in section 33-1003A, Idaho Code, for
21 each student in kindergarten through grade 12 at physical facilities that
22 are part of and on school grounds of the school district in which the student
23 is enrolled with verification, as needed, by the office of the state board
24 of education. Upon formal approval by the state board of education, a school
25 district may receive an exemption to the physical facility requirement pur-
26 suant to this subsection if the student would have attended a physical facil-
27 ity in the school district if not for a stated emergency. The state depart-
28 ment of education shall transfer the moneys by no later than August 31 each
29 year to each school district. Such moneys shall be used in place of property
30 tax levy moneys and shall be expended by a school district for one (1) or more
31 of the purposes set forth in paragraphs (a) through (f) of this subsection.
32 Moneys in the fund must be used by a school district in the following order of
33 priority:
34 (a) Payment of existing school bonds authorized pursuant to chapter 11,
35 title 33, Idaho Code, and issued prior to July 1, 2025;
36 (b) Payments required pursuant to section 33-909, Idaho Code;
37 (c) Payment of existing school bonds authorized pursuant to chapter 11,
38 title 33, Idaho Code, and issued on or after July 1, 2025;
39 (d) Payment of supplemental school levies authorized pursuant to sec-
40 tion 33-802, Idaho Code, excluding indefinite term supplemental levies
41 described in section 33-802(5), Idaho Code;
42 (e) Payment of school plant facility levies authorized pursuant to sec-
43 tions 33-804 and 33-804A, Idaho Code; and
44 (f) Any moneys that remain following the payments provided in para-
45 graphs (a) through (e) of this subsection may be: used for construction
46 of a new school facility, renovation, or maintenance needs; used to
47 secure and make payments on a new school facilities bond; or saved in
48 a reserve account by the school district for future school facility
49 needs. Uses of funds shall include regular and routine facilities
