22 SECTION 1. USES OF THE IDAHO MILLENNIUM INCOME FUND. The Joint Millen-
23 nium Fund Committee, pursuant to Section 67-1808(6), Idaho Code, recommends
24 that all moneys available for appropriation from the Idaho Millennium Income
25 Fund beginning in fiscal year 2024 and each year thereafter be prioritized
26 for programs that focus on prevention, cessation, and treatment related to
27 tobacco, vape, alcohol, illegal substances, and other substances that may be
28 abused beyond the intended uses and for the overall safety and well-being of
29 Idaho youth. Funding should be prioritized first to programs that benefit
30 Idahoans under 18 years of age and then for adults with higher risk factors
31 for abusing or using various substances described in this section. It is the
32 recommendation of the Joint Millennium Fund Committee that no moneys from
33 the Idaho Millennium Income Fund be used for the payment of Medicaid claims.
34 In accordance with the priorities of this section, any requested funds not
35 already an ongoing appropriation in fiscal year 2027 shall be presented in
36 the Legislative Budget Book as a request for new onetime appropriation in the
37 Idaho Millennium Fund Program and in the respective agency budgets. Consis-
38 tent with Section 67-1806, Idaho Code, the Joint Millennium Fund Committee
39 shall be responsible for the official recommendation on the uses of the Idaho
40 Millennium Income Fund.

15 covery services. Funds shall be used by the community-based recovery cen-

17 personnel costs, rent or lease expenses, basic supplies, and other operating
18 costs. Community-based recovery centers shall provide to the Department of
19 Health and Welfare a document that demonstrates operational and fiscal ac-
20 countability for the center. Community-based recovery centers shall work in
21 partnership with the Department of Health and Welfare and sign an attesta-
22 tion letter or equivalent document that ensures funds will be used in accor-
23 dance with this section, with Section 3 of this act, and with any other appli-
24 cable rule or law, and that all moneys received will be accounted for using
25 generally accepted accounting principles. Funding is allocated for eligi-
26 ble centers for the cities of Boise, Caldwell or Nampa, Coeur d'Alene, Em-
27 mett, Idaho Falls, Kamiah, Lewiston, Moscow, Pocatello, and Twin Falls.
28 SECTION 3. COMMUNITY-BASED RECOVERY CENTERS OVERSIGHT. (1) The Depart-
29 ment of Health and Welfare shall provide oversight for the funds appropri-
30 ated in the fiscal year 2027 maintenance bill and in Section 2 of this act
31 for community-based recovery centers. The Department of Health and Welfare
32 shall:
33 (a) Provide four distributions of $37,500 for a total distribution of
34 $150,000 to each eligible center. Distributions shall occur no later
35 than July 15, 2026, October 15, 2026, January 15, 2027, and April 15,
36 2027;
37 (b) Receive and compile two reports from each center and transmit each
38 report to the Budget and Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative
39 Services Office on behalf of the Joint Millennium Fund Committee by
40 December 30, 2026, and by June 30, 2027, or as soon thereafter as prac-
41 ticable. The format of the report and the content therein shall be
42 developed by the department and approved by the Budget and Policy Analy-
43 sis Division of the Legislative Services Office;
44 (c) Withhold or recover distributions pursuant to paragraph (a) of this
45 subsection if the center is not operating or has indicated it intends to
46 no longer operate prior to the close of the fiscal year, if it does not
47 provide any of the required reports, or otherwise as needed to address
48 legal or fiscal concerns regarding funds provided to a center. The
49 department shall notify the center immediately with detailed informa-

3
1 tion on identified concerns and potential amounts subject to recovery.
2 Amounts subject to withholding or recovery shall be based on the time
3 frame in question; and
4 (d) Assist each center, as needed, to ensure services are being pro-
5 vided as intended by this act and to meet the needs of that local commu-
6 nity.
7 (2) The Department of Health and Welfare shall not have the authority to
8 directly manage any center or direct any center to perform functions not re-
9 quired in this section or Section 2 of this act. If the Department of Health
10 and Welfare has reason to believe that appropriated funds are used in viola-
11 tion of this section or Section 2 of this act or any other rule or law, the

13 Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative Services Office, and the Execu-

15 Department of Health and Welfare shall work collaboratively with such cen-
16 ter and the Executive Office of the Governor to develop an appropriate cor-
17 rective action plan. If funds were withheld pursuant to subsection (1)(c)
18 of this section while a center made necessary adjustments according to the
19 corrective action plan and the center continued operations according to the
20 provisions of this act, then the department shall provide any withheld fund-
21 ing no later than 30 days after completion of the corrective action plan.
22 (3) Should any of the community-based recovery centers as defined in
23 Section 2 of this act cease to operate or not be operational in accordance
24 with the requirements of this section and Section 2 of this act within 30 days
25 of any quarterly distribution date identified in this section, the State

27 transfer any quarterly distribution moneys back to the Idaho Millennium
28 Income Fund as soon as practicable. If a delay occurs as a result of action
29 or inaction by the Department of Health and Welfare, funds shall be retained
30 until such time that a payment can be made to an eligible center or it can
31 be determined that a center will no longer be able to operate. A decision
32 to no longer operate requires agreement from the identified center, the De-
33 partment of Health and Welfare, and the Executive Office of the Governor. If
34 a resolution is not reached by the end of the fiscal year, all moneys shall
35 be returned to the Idaho Millennium Income Fund and adequate documentation

37 and Welfare to the Budget and Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative
38 Services Office no later than 30 days after the end of the fiscal year docu-
39 menting why a resolution could not be reached.
