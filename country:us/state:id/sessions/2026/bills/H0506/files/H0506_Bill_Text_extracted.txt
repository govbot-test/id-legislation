LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 506
BY REVENUE AND TAXATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO TAXATION; AMENDING SECTION 63-3641, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PRO-
3 VISIONS REGARDING THE REBATE OF SALES TAX COLLECTED; AND DECLARING AN
4 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING RETROACTIVE APPLICATION.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 63-3641, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 63-3641. REBATE OF SALES TAXES COLLECTED. (1) As provided in and
9 subject to the limitations of this section, a developer of a retail complex
10 shall receive a rebate of sales taxes collected and remitted to the state
11 tax commission under this chapter by qualified retailers within the retail
12 complex to reimburse the developer for project expenses incurred for the
13 installation of approved transportation improvements.
14 (2) As used in this section:
15 (a) "Approved transportation improvements" means a highway project the
16 cost of which is in excess of six million dollars ($6,000,000) for the
17 installation of an interchange from an interstate highway or expended
18 on for the improvement of a highway as defined in section 40-109(5),
19 Idaho Code, the cost of which is in excess of ten million dollars
20 ($10,000,000). To qualify as an approved highway improvement the de-
21 veloper of a retail complex must enter into an agreement with the Idaho
22 transportation board and/or or a political subdivision. An approved
23 highway improvement shall include those costs directly associated with
24 the highway project but shall not include any improvement not within the
25 right-of-way of the proposed public highway improvement, improvements
26 not specifically authorized in the agreement entered into, or developer
27 financed improvements required by state or local agencies as part of
28 the permitting and development process not within the public highway
29 right-of-way.
30 (b) "Political subdivision" means a city, county or highway district
31 that receives highway funding pursuant to section 40-709, Idaho Code.
32 (c) "Qualified retailer" means a specific location within a retail com-
33 plex operated by a retailer in regard to which the retailer:
34 (i) Has obtained a separate seller's permit pursuant to section
35 63-3620, Idaho Code, applicable only to that location and has col-
36 lected sales or use taxes in regard to retail sales made at that lo-
37 cation and has remitted all such taxes to the state tax commission
38 with returns related to that permit;
39 (ii) Has been identified in the manner required by rules of the
40 state tax commission as associated with the specific retail com-
41 plex; and

2
1 (iii) Did not directly or by any related party, (as defined in sec-
2 tion 63-3615A(2), Idaho Code), operate a retail business in the
3 same location before construction of the retail complex.
4 (d) "Retail complex" means:
5 (i) One (1) or more buildings in a single location constructed by
6 a developer applying for a rebate under this section;
7 (ii) Facilities reasonably related to the buildings, such as
8 parking lots, sidewalks, lighting, traffic signs and accessory
9 equipment; and
10 (iii) For which the developer has expended a minimum of four mil-
11 lion dollars ($4,000,000).
12 (e) "Retailer" has the same meaning as provided in section 63-3610,
13 Idaho Code;
14 (f) "Retail sales" has the same meaning as that term is defined in sec-
15 tion 63-3609, Idaho Code.
16 (3) To obtain the rebate provided by this section, the developer of a
17 retail complex shall file a written claim with the state tax commission.
18 (a) The claim shall:
19 (i) Identify the location and boundaries of the retail complex;
20 (ii) Identify the qualified retailers making retail sales within
21 the complex;
22 (iii) Include verification that the developer has met the expendi-
23 ture requirements of paragraph (2)(d)(iii) of this section;
24 (iv) Include certification from the Idaho transportation depart-
25 ment or political subdivision of the amount expended on the ap-
26 proved transportation improvements related to the retail complex;
27 (v) Contain such additional information as the state tax commis-
28 sion may require by rule.
29 (b) The claim shall be subject to such reasonable documentation and
30 verification as the state tax commission may require.
31 (c) A developer of a retail complex must submit a claim under this sub-
32 section within two (2) years of the developer's last expenditure on ap-
33 proved transportation improvements.
34 (4)(a) Upon approval by the state tax commission, the developer is en-
35 titled to receive a rebate of sixty percent (60%) of all sales and use
36 taxes imposed by this chapter and remitted to the state tax commission
37 after the date of approval by qualified retailers in the retail complex
38 but not to exceed the lesser of:
39 (i) The amount certified pursuant to subsection (3)(a)(iv) of
40 this section; or
41 (ii) The limitation imposed by subsection (5)(c) of this section.
42 (b) No interest shall be paid on the amounts rebated.
43 (c) All sales and use tax information remitted by retailers shall be
44 deemed a trade secret, shall be confidential and shall not be disclosed
45 by the state tax commission.
46 (5)(a) When a retailer certifies to the state tax commission and the
47 commission determines that the requirements of subsection (3)(a)(i),
48 (ii) and (iii) of this section have been met, sixty percent (60%) of all
49 sales and use taxes imposed by this chapter and remitted to the state tax
50 commission after the date of approval by qualified retailers in the re-

3
1 tail complex, shall be deposited into the demonstration pilot project
2 fund, which is hereby created in the state treasury.
3 (b) All moneys rebated shall be paid by the state tax commission from
4 the demonstration pilot project fund in a timely manner not to exceed
5 sixty (60) calendar days after receipt as funds are available in the
6 demonstration pilot project fund. Payments shall be specific to and
7 accounted for by each project.
8 (c) Once a total of thirty-five million dollars ($35,000,000) one hun-
9 dred million dollars ($100,000,000) has been paid in as a rebate on a
10 particular approved transportation improvement, no additional rebates
11 shall be paid in regard to that approved transportation improvement.
12 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
13 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its
14 passage and approval, and retroactively to January 1, 2026.
