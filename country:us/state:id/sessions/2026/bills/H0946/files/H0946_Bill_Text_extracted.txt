24 "Litigation Financing Transparency, National Security, and Consumer Pro-
25 tection Act."
26 48-2102. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
27 (1) "Consumer" means any person who is or may become a plaintiff,
28 claimant, or complainant in conjunction with or in pursuit of a legal claim.
29 (2) "Entity" means any domestic or foreign corporation, partnership,
30 limited partnership, limited liability company, trust, fund, plan, or any
31 other business, enterprise, association, or organization of any kind or na-
32 ture.
33 (3) "Foreign adversary" means:
34 (a) A foreign government of a foreign country identified or designated
35 as a foreign adversary pursuant to 15 CFR 791.4, as in effect on July 1,
36 2026;
37 (b) Any political subdivision, agency, department, or political party
38 of a foreign government identified or designated as a foreign adversary
39 pursuant to 15 CFR 791.4, as in effect on July 1, 2026; or

2
1 (c) A foreign nongovernment person identified or designated as a for-
2 eign adversary pursuant to 15 CFR 791.4, as in effect on July 1, 2026.
3 (4) "Foreign person" means:
4 (a) An individual who is not a citizen of the United States or is not an
5 alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States;
6 (b) An entity that is not formed, organized, incorporated, or created
7 in the United States;
8 (c) An entity of which a majority of the persons who own or control the
9 entity are not citizens of the United States or are not aliens lawfully
10 admitted for permanent residence in the United States;
11 (d) The government, a political subdivision, an agency, a department,
12 or a political party of a country other than the United States;
13 (e) An entity that has a place of business in a country other than the
14 United States and that has shares or other ownership interest held by
15 the government or a government official of a country other than the
16 United States; or
17 (f) An employee, officer, director, official, or member of an entity
18 described in this subsection.
19 (5) "Foreign person of concern" means a person that:
20 (a) Is formed, organized, incorporated, or created in a foreign country
21 identified as a foreign adversary;
22 (b) Is formed, organized, incorporated, owned, controlled, or affili-
23 ated, directly or indirectly, with a foreign adversary;
24 (c) Maintains a personal residence or place of business in a foreign
25 country identified as a foreign adversary; or
26 (d) Is formed, organized, incorporated, owned, controlled, or affili-
27 ated, directly or indirectly, with a foreign person that has been:
28 (i) Placed on the federal office of foreign assets control's spe-
29 cially designated nationals and blocked persons list, also known
30 as the SDN list, as amended; or
31 (ii) Designated by the United States secretary of state as a for-
32 eign terrorist person or organization.
33 (6) "Legal claim" means a civil action, administrative proceeding,
34 claim, or cause of action to recover damages or any form of monetary or in-
35 junctive relief in this state.
36 (7) "Legal representative" means an attorney or attorneys engaged to
37 represent a consumer in conjunction with or in pursuit of a legal claim.
38 (8)(a) "Litigation financier" means any person or group of persons en-
39 gaged in, or formed, created, or established for the purpose of engaging
40 in, the business of litigation financing or any other business or eco-
41 nomic activity in which a person or group of persons receive considera-
42 tion of any kind in exchange for providing litigation financing.
43 (b) "Litigation financier" does not include:
44 (i) A party to the legal claim;
45 (ii) A legal representative engaged directly, or indirectly
46 through another legal representative, to represent a party in con-
47 junction with or in pursuit of the legal claim; or
48 (iii) An entity or insurer with a preexisting contractual obliga-
49 tion to indemnify or defend a party to a legal claim.
50 (9) "Litigation financing" means:

3

17 (b) Any agreement or arrangement involving the financing, funding, ad-

21 claims in which the legal representative represents one (1) or more con-

23 (10)(a) "Litigation financing contract" means a written contract in
24 which a person agrees to provide litigation financing to any person in
25 consideration for:
26 (i) The payment of interest, fees, or other consideration to the
27 person providing the litigation financing; or
28 (ii) Granting or assigning to the person providing the litigation
29 financing a right to receive payment from the value of any proceeds
30 or other consideration realized from any judgment, award, settle-
31 ment, verdict, or other form of monetary relief any consumer, le-
32 gal representative, or other person may receive or recover in re-

34 (b) "Litigation financing contract" does not include any agreement,
35 contract, or engagement of a legal representative to render legal ser-
36 vices to a consumer on a contingency fee basis, including the advance-

39 sentative representing the consumer in the legal claim.
40 (11) "Person" means an individual or an entity.
41 (12) "Proprietary information" means any documents, records, or infor-
42 mation received by a party in conjunction with or in pursuit of a legal claim

44 (a) Designated as proprietary information by a party to the legal
45 claim; or
46 (b) Subject to a court order to protect, seal, or otherwise restrict the

48 (13) "Regulated lender" has the same meaning as provided for in section

1 48-2103. REGISTRATION REQUIRED. (1) A person shall not engage in liti-
2 gation financing in this state unless such person is registered with the sec-
3 retary of state as a litigation financier pursuant to the provisions of this
4 chapter.
5 (2) To register as a litigation financier, if the person registering is
6 an entity, the entity shall be active and in good standing as reflected in the
7 office of the secretary of state.
8 (3) To register as a litigation financier, a person shall file a reg-
9 istration statement with the secretary of state setting forth the following
10 information:
11 (a) The legal name of the litigation financier;
12 (b) The physical street address and mailing address of the litigation
13 financier; and
14 (c) The name, physical street address, and mailing address of the lit-
15 igation financier's registered agent in this state who is authorized to
16 accept service of process on behalf of the litigation financier.
17 (4) If the person seeking to register as a litigation financier is an
18 entity, the following information shall be set forth in the registration
19 statement with respect to each person that owns, controls, or holds with the
20 power to vote five percent (5%) or more of the beneficial interests or voting
21 interests of the litigation financier:
22 (a) The legal name, physical street address, and mailing address of
23 each person;
24 (b) If the person is an individual, a disclosure of any conviction of a
25 felony involving theft, fraud, deception, or dishonesty during the past
26 ten (10) years;
27 (c) If the person is an entity, and if such information is not other-
28 wise disclosed in the entity's filing with the secretary of state for
29 authorization to do business in this state, a list of all individuals
30 who are directors, officers, trustees, partners, members, or managers
31 of the entity and each subsidiary of the entity, if any. Such list shall
32 include, for each individual, the information required to be provided
33 pursuant to this subsection.
34 (5) A litigation financier subject to registration pursuant to this
35 chapter shall file an updated registration:
36 (a) Annually; and
37 (b) Within thirty (30) days of any change in ownership, control, or man-
38 agement of the entity that causes the information required to be pro-
39 vided pursuant to subsection (4) of this section to become inaccurate.
40 (6) The secretary of state is authorized to prescribe the forms and the
41 filing fees the secretary of state considers necessary for the purposes of
42 this chapter.
43 48-2104. LITIGATION FINANCING PROTECTIONS. (1) A litigation fi-
44 nancier shall not:
45 (a) Pay or offer commissions, referral fees, rebates, or other forms
46 of consideration to any person in exchange for referring a consumer or a
47 consumer's legal representative to a litigation financier;

5
1 (b) Accept any commissions, referral fees, rebates, or other forms of
2 consideration from any person providing any goods or rendering any ser-
3 vices to the consumer;
4 (c) Receive or recover any payment that exceeds twenty-five percent
5 (25%) of the amount of any judgment, award, settlement, verdict, or
6 other form of monetary relief obtained in the legal claim that is the
7 subject of the litigation contract;
8 (d) Advertise false or misleading information regarding its products
9 or services;
10 (e) Refer or require any consumer to hire or engage any person providing
11 any goods or rendering any services to the consumer;
12 (f) Fail to promptly deliver a fully completed and signed litigation
13 financing contract to the consumer and the consumer's legal representa-
14 tive;
15 (g) Attempt to secure a remedy or obtain a waiver of any remedy, includ-
16 ing but not limited to compensatory, statutory, or punitive damages,
17 that the consumer may or may not be entitled to pursue or recover other-
18 wise;
19 (h) Offer or provide legal advice to the consumer;
20 (i) Assign, including securitizing, a litigation financing contract in
21 whole or in part;
22 (j) Make, influence, or direct a decision regarding a legal claim, in-
23 cluding decisions in appointing or changing counsel, choice or use of
24 expert witnesses, litigation strategy, and settlement or other resolu-
25 tion; or
26 (k) Demand, request, receive, or exercise any right to influence, af-
27 fect, or otherwise make any decision in the handling, conduct, admin-
28 istration, litigation, settlement, or resolution of any legal claim in
29 which the litigation financier has provided litigation financing. All
30 rights remain solely with the consumer and the consumer's legal repre-
31 sentative.
32 (2) A person who provides any goods or renders any services to the con-
33 sumer that are related to the litigation being financed shall not have a fi-
34 nancial interest in litigation financing for the consumer and shall not re-
35 ceive any commissions, referral fees, rebates, or other forms of considera-
36 tion from any litigation financier or the litigation financier's employees,
37 owners, or affiliates.
38 (3) A party, attorney or law firm representing a party, or affiliated
39 attorney or law firm shall not disclose or share any proprietary information
40 with a litigation financier.
41 48-2105. LITIGATION FINANCING CONTRACT -- DISCLOSURES. (1) The terms
42 and conditions of a litigation financing contract shall be set forth in a
43 fully completed written contract with no terms or conditions omitted. The
44 litigation financing contract shall contain all terms and conditions at the
45 time it is signed by any party to the litigation financing contract.
46 (2) Upon execution of a litigation financing contract, a litigation fi-
47 nancier shall not amend the terms or conditions of the litigation financing
48 contract without full disclosure to and the prior written consent of all par-
49 ties to the litigation financing contract.

6
1 (3) A litigation financing contract shall set forth the name, physical
2 street address, and mailing address of the litigation financier on page one
3 (1) of the litigation financing contract.
4 (4) A litigation financing contract shall contain clear and conspicu-
5 ous disclosures immediately above the consumer's signature line informing
6 the consumer that:
7 (a) The contract may be canceled by sending written notice to the liti-
8 gation financier within five (5) days after signing the contract or re-
9 ceiving funds from the litigation financier, whichever is later;
10 (b) The fee amount shall be disclosed and may not exceed twenty-five
11 percent (25%) of the monetary amount received from a verdict, judgment,
12 or settlement;
13 (c) A litigation financier shall not seek to influence any aspect of the
14 litigation, as such right shall belong only to the consumer and its le-
15 gal representative; and
16 (d) If there is no recovery of money in the litigation or if the recov-
17 ery is insufficient to pay the litigation financier's full fee, the con-
18 sumer shall owe nothing in excess of its monetary recovery.
19 (5) If the consumer's legal representative is a party to a litigation
20 financing contract related to the consumer's legal claim that is the subject
21 of the litigation financing contract, the legal representative shall dis-
22 close and deliver the litigation financing contract to the consumer.
23 48-2106. DISCLOSURE AND DISCOVERY OF LITIGATION FINANCING CON-
24 TRACTS. (1) Except as otherwise stipulated or ordered by a court of competent
25 jurisdiction, a consumer or the consumer's legal representative shall,
26 without awaiting a discovery request, disclose and deliver to the following
27 persons the litigation financing contract:
28 (a) Each party to the legal claim, or to each party's legal representa-
29 tive; and
30 (b) The court, agency, or tribunal in which the legal claim may be pend-
31 ing.
32 (2) If a demand for payment is made before a suit is filed, a party who
33 makes that demand shall, upon request of the party on whom demand is made,
34 disclose and deliver any litigation financing contract to the party on whom
35 demand is made.
36 (3) The disclosure obligations pursuant to subsections (1) and (2) of
37 this section are continuing obligations, and within thirty (30) days of en-
38 tering into a litigation financing contract or amending an existing litiga-
39 tion financing contract, the consumer or the consumer's legal representa-
40 tive shall disclose and deliver any new or amended litigation financing con-
41 tract.
42 (4) The existence of a litigation financing contract and all partici-
43 pants or parties to the litigation financing contract are permissible sub-
44 jects of discovery in any legal claim to which litigation financing is pro-
45 vided under the litigation financing contract.
46 48-2107. EXEMPTIONS. This chapter does not apply to the following:
47 (1) A nonprofit entity that is exempt from federal income taxation pur-
48 suant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code or a nonprofit entity

7
1 that provides litigation financing, funding, or money to a legal representa-
2 tive to represent a consumer on a pro bono basis, provided that the nonprofit
3 entity does not receive in consideration for the litigation financing the
4 payment of interest, fees, or other consideration in excess of the amount of
5 attorney's fees and litigation expenses incurred in conjunction with or in
6 pursuit of the legal claim;
7 (2) Any litigation financing provided by an entity engaged in commerce
8 or business activity, but only if the entity does not:
9 (a) Charge or collect any interest, fees, or other consideration;
10 (b) Obtain or receive any financial interest in the outcome of the legal
11 claim; or
12 (c) Receive any right to recovery or payment from the amount of any
13 judgment, award, settlement, verdict, or other form of monetary relief
14 obtained in conjunction with or in pursuit of the legal claim; or
15 (3) A regulated lender that does not receive, in consideration for
16 loaning money to any person, a right to receive payment pursuant to a litiga-
17 tion financing contract.
18 48-2108. CLASS ACTIONS. In addition to all other actions to which this
19 chapter applies, this chapter shall also apply to any civil action filed or
20 certified as a class action in which litigation financing is provided. A
21 litigation financier owes a fiduciary duty to all class members or intended
22 beneficiaries of a certified class and shall act in a manner consistent with
23 the litigation financier's fiduciary duty throughout the civil action. In
24 addition to the disclosure requirements set forth in this chapter, the legal
25 representative of the putative class shall disclose to all parties, putative
26 class members, and the court any legal, financial, or other relationship be-
27 tween the legal representative and the litigation financier. A class member
28 is entitled to receive from the class counsel a true and correct copy of the
29 litigation financing contract on request.
30 48-2109. PROHIBITION ON LITIGATION FUNDING BY FOREIGN ADVERSARIES OR
31 FOREIGN PERSONS OF CONCERN. (1) A foreign adversary or a foreign person of
32 concern may not engage in litigation financing in this state.
33 (2) A foreign adversary or a foreign person of concern may not, directly
34 or indirectly, invest in, finance, fund, or loan money to a litigation fi-
35 nancier engaged in litigation financing in this state.
36 (3) A litigation financier engaged in litigation financing in this
37 state may not, directly or indirectly:
38 (a) Become affiliated with or maintain an affiliation with a foreign
39 adversary or a foreign person of concern;
40 (b) Receive or accept money or funds from a foreign adversary or a for-
41 eign person of concern; or
42 (c) Allow a foreign adversary or a foreign person of concern to invest
43 in, maintain an ownership interest in, or exercise any control over the
44 litigation financier.
45 48-2110. RESTRICTION ON DISCLOSURE OF PROPRIETARY INFORMATION. A
46 party to a legal claim in this state shall not disclose to or share with a

1 foreign person, a foreign adversary, or a foreign person of concern any pro-

3 48-2111. LIMITATIONS ON FOREIGN LITIGATION FUNDING. A foreign person
4 who, directly or indirectly: engages in litigation financing; becomes af-
5 filiated with or maintains an affiliation with a litigation financier; fi-
6 nances, funds, or loans money to a litigation financier; or invests in, main-
7 tains an ownership interest in, or exercises any control over a litigation
8 financier shall not:
9 (1) Become affiliated with or maintain an affiliation with a foreign
10 adversary or a foreign person of concern;
11 (2) Receive or accept money or funds from a foreign adversary or a for-
12 eign person of concern; or
13 (3) Allow a foreign adversary or a foreign person of concern to invest
14 in, maintain an ownership interest in, or exercise control over the foreign
15 person.
16 48-2112. REGISTRATION AND TRANSPARENCY OF FOREIGN PERSONS. (1) A for-
17 eign person who is qualified to engage in litigation financing in this state
18 shall comply with this chapter if the foreign person engages in litigation
19 financing in this state.
20 (2) A litigation financier shall disclose in writing to the secretary
21 of state the information set forth in subsection (3) of this section if the
22 litigation financier engages in any of the following:
23 (a) Becomes affiliated with or maintains affiliation with a foreign
24 person;
25 (b) Receives or accepts money or funds from a foreign person for any
26 purpose; or
27 (c) Allows a foreign person to invest in, maintain an ownership inter-
28 est in, or exercise any control over the litigation financier.
29 (3) The following information shall be disclosed in writing to the sec-
30 retary of state:
31 (a) The name and address of the foreign person and the citizenship of
32 the foreign person or the country of incorporation, formation, or reg-
33 istration of the foreign person; and
34 (b) A copy of each litigation financing contract in which the foreign
35 person is entitled to receive any payment under the contract.
36 (4) The litigation financier shall provide the written notice required
37 pursuant to subsection (2) of this section within thirty (30) days after the
38 date on which any litigation financing agreement is executed by the con-
39 sumer.
40 48-2113. ACT VIOLATION -- UNENFORCEABLE CONTRACT. (1) Any violation
41 of this chapter by a litigation financier shall render the litigation fi-
42 nancing contract unenforceable by the litigation financier or any succes-
43 sor-in-interest to the litigation financing contract.
44 (2) The prevailing party in any litigation under this chapter shall be
45 entitled to costs and reasonable attorney's fees as awarded by the court.
