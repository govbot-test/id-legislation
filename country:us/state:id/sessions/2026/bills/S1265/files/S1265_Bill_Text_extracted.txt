15 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
16 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
17 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
18 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
19 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
20 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.

23 41-4904. BOARD OF TRUSTEES OF THE FUND. (1) The governor shall appoint
24 seven (7) persons to be the board of trustees of the Idaho petroleum clean
25 water trust fund. One (1) member shall be a member of the state senate, one

20 amended to read as follows:
21 41-4911. ISSUANCE OF CONTRACTS OF INSURANCE BY THE ADMINISTRATOR OF
22 THE IDAHO PETROLEUM CLEAN WATER TRUST FUND -- DEFERRAL. (1) The administra-
23 tor may issue a contract of insurance to an owner or operator of a petroleum
24 storage tank that, based upon a consideration of the owner or operator's ap-
25 plication for insurance and appropriate investigation by the administrator,
26 meets the eligibility provisions of this chapter and the underwriting re-
27 quirements established by the administrator.
28 (2) The administrator may defer issuing contracts of insurance to cer-
29 tain categories of petroleum storage tank owners or operators if necessary
30 for the sound operation of the trust fund.
31 (3) The administrator shall consider the following factors in deter-
32 mining whether to defer the issuance of contracts of insurance to any cate-
33 gory of petroleum storage tank owners or operators:
34 (a) The underwriting capacity of the trust fund;
35 (b) Any requirement of federal or state law or regulation imposed on any
36 category of petroleum storage tank owners or operators to demonstrate
37 financial responsibility for corrective action and compensation to
38 third parties for bodily injury and property damage arising from acci-
39 dental releases from petroleum storage tanks;
40 (c) The ability of the administrator to process insurance applications
41 from different categories of petroleum storage tank owners or opera-
42 tors.
43 (4) Any decision by the administrator to defer issuing contracts of in-
44 surance to any category of petroleum storage tank owners or operators shall
45 be documented in the plan of operation, or an amendment thereto, submitted to
46 the director of the department of insurance pursuant to sections 41-4925 or
47 41-4925A, Idaho Code, and subject to the director's approval.

11 41-4925A. AMENDMENTS TO PLAN OF OPERATION. (1) Any amendment to the
12 plan of operation prepared by the administrator for the purpose of deferring
13 the issuance of contracts of insurance to any category of petroleum storage
14 tank owners or operators or for issuing contracts of insurance to any de-
15 ferred category of petroleum storage tank owners or operators shall be sub-
16 mitted to the director of the department of insurance.
17 (2) The director shall review the amendment and shall, with all reason-
18 able promptness, approve, approve as modified, or disapprove of the amend-
19 ment to the plan of operation. If the amendment is approved, the adminis-
20 trator may issue contracts of insurance and otherwise operate the trust fund
21 in a manner consistent with the amended plan of operation. If the amend-
22 ment is disapproved, the administrator must operate the trust fund in a man-
23 ner consistent with the provisions of the plan of operation as submitted to
24 the director in the trust fund's application for registration under section
25 41-4925, Idaho Code.
26 (3) The director may request such relevant documentation and informa-
27 tion, including an actuarial analysis of the underwriting capacity of the
28 trust fund, as is reasonably necessary to evaluate the proposed amendment to
29 the plan of operation.
30 (4) All procedures and policies concerning the approval, modification
31 or disapproval of any amendment to the plan of operation are subject to the
32 provisions of chapter 52, title 67, Idaho Code, as well as the rules of prac-
33 tice and procedure of the department of insurance.
