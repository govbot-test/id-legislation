11 54-1807A. PHYSICIAN ASSISTANTS -- PHYSICIAN ASSISTANT ADVISORY COM-
12 MITTEE. (1) Physician assistants must be licensed by the board prior to the
13 commencement of activities that may involve the practice of medicine in this
14 state. The licensure requirements for physician assistants shall include
15 passage of an examination acceptable to the board and submission of a com-
16 pleted application to the board on forms furnished by the board. All appli-
17 cants for original licensure as a physician assistant shall submit to a fin-
18 gerprint-based criminal history check in accordance with section 67-9411A,
19 Idaho Code. All physician assistants shall renew their licenses biennially.
20 (2) A physician assistant may practice at a licensed health care
21 facility, a facility with a credentialing and privileging system, a physi-
22 cian-owned facility or practice, or another facility or practice allowed
23 by this chapter. A physician assistant shall collaborate with, consult
24 with, or refer to the appropriate member of the facility health care team as
25 indicated by: the condition of the patient; the education, experience, and
26 competence of the physician assistant; and the community standard of care.
27 The degree and nature of collaboration shall be determined by the facility
28 or practice in which the physician assistant works and shall be set forth in
29 facility bylaws or procedures for facilities that have credentialing and
30 privileging systems or in a written collaborative practice agreement for all
31 other facilities and practices. Such provisions may provide for collabo-
32 rative oversight to be provided by the employer, group, hospital service,
33 or the credentialing and privileging systems of a licensed facility, but
34 at a minimum shall require a physician assistant to collaborate with one
35 (1) or more physicians licensed pursuant to this chapter. Such physicians
36 need not be identified individually in the facility bylaws or procedures
37 or collaborative practice agreement if more than one (1) physician works
38 in the facility or practice. A physician assistant is responsible for the
39 care provided by the physician assistant and is responsible for obtaining
40 professional liability insurance if the physician assistant is not covered
41 by the facility or practice in which the physician assistant works. A physi-
42 cian assistant may be employed by nonphysician health care providers if the

33 SECTION 2. That Section 54-1814, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
34 amended to read as follows:
35 54-1814. GROUNDS FOR MEDICAL DISCIPLINE. Every person licensed to
36 practice medicine, or registered as an intern or resident in this state is
37 subject to discipline by the board pursuant to the procedures set forth in
38 this chapter and rules promulgated pursuant thereto upon any of the follow-
39 ing grounds:
40 (1) Being convicted of a felony, pleading guilty to a felony, or the
41 finding of guilt by a jury or court of commission of a felony.
42 (2) Using false, fraudulent or forged statements or documents, diplo-
43 mas or credentials in connection with any licensing or other requirements of
44 this act.
45 (3) Practicing medicine under a false or assumed name in this or any
46 other state.
47 (4) Advertising the practice of medicine in any unethical or unprofes-
48 sional manner.

1 (5) Knowingly aiding or abetting any person to practice medicine who is
2 not authorized to practice medicine as provided in this chapter.

5 (7) Providing health care which fails to meet the standard of health
6 care provided by other qualified physicians or physician assistants in the
7 same community or similar communities, taking into account his training, ex-
8 perience and the degree of expertise to which he holds himself out to the pub-
9 lic.
10 (8) Dividing fees or gifts or agreeing to split or divide fees or gifts
11 received for professional services with any person, institution or corpora-

28 SECTION 3. That Section 54-1867, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
29 amended to read as follows:
30 54-1867. LIMITED LICENSE FOR BRIDGE YEAR PHYSICIANS. (1) As used in
31 this section:
32 (a) "Board" means the state board of medicine.
33 (b) "Bridge year physician" means a person who:
34 (i) Is within the first year of graduation from a medical school
35 accredited or provisionally accredited by an entity recognized by
36 the board;
37 (ii) Is a United States citizen or attended medical school in the
38 United States; and
39 (iii) Applied to, but was not accepted into, an accredited medical
40 residency training program.
41 (2) The board shall establish a one (1) year, nonrenewable limited li-
42 cense under which bridge year physicians may practice medicine under terms,
43 conditions, and a scope of practice determined by the board. If there is a
44 limit to the number of limited licenses that may be issued, priority shall be
45 granted to bridge year physicians who live in Idaho or who have longstanding
46 ties to the state of Idaho, as determined by the board.
47 (3) Persons practicing under a limited license established pursuant to
48 this section shall:
49 (a) Practice only within the scope of practice determined by the board;

6
1 (b) Practice under the supervision of a licensed physician or pursuant
2 to a collaborative practice agreement. The person practicing under a
3 limited license shall qualify as one (1) of a supervising physician's
4 permitted advanced practice professionals. The board shall prescribe
5 supervision requirements for limited licensees, provided that such re-
6 quirements shall be no less stringent than supervision requirements for
7 physician assistants;
8 (c) Have prescriptive authority as determined by the board; and
9 (d) Be subject to the same professional discipline, civil liability,
10 and criminal liability as a fully licensed physician.
11 (4) The services provided by a person practicing under a limited li-
12 cense shall be compensable in accordance with customary medical billing
13 practices.
14 (5) The board is authorized to:
15 (a) Take such actions as are necessary to implement the provisions of
16 this section, including the promulgation of any necessary rules;
17 (b) Charge a fee of up to three hundred dollars ($300) for a limited li-
18 cense; and
19 (c) Cooperate with the department of health and welfare and other rel-
20 evant entities, including hospitals and health care clinics, whether
21 public or private, in establishing a limited license.
22 (6) No later than January 31, 2033, the board shall provide a report to
23 the senate and house of representatives health and welfare committees on:
24 (a) Requirements for a limited license;
25 (b) The number of limited licenses issued and the number of limited li-
26 cense holders who were later accepted into a residency program; and
27 (c) Whether and how limited licenses have increased the supply of
28 health care providers in health professional shortage areas.
29 SECTION 4. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
30 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
31 July 1, 2026.
