LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 888
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY; AMENDING SECTION 67-827A, IDAHO CODE,
3 TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING THE OFFICE OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY
4 SERVICES AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
5 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 67-827A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 67-827A. POWERS AND DUTIES. (1) The office of information technology
10 services is hereby authorized and directed:
11 (1)(a)(i) To control and approve the acquisition, purchase, and instal-
12 lation of all information technology and telecommunications equipment
13 and facilities for all departments and institutions of state gov-
14 ernment, except as provided in subparagraphs (ii), (iii) and (iv)
15 paragraphs (b), (c), and (d) of this paragraph subsection;
16 (ii) (b) To coordinate the acquisition, purchase, and installation of
17 all information technology and telecommunications equipment and facil-
18 ities for the institutions of higher education and the elected officers
19 in the executive branch;
20 (iii) (c) To coordinate the acquisition, purchase, and installation of
21 all information technology and telecommunications equipment and facil-
22 ities for the legislative and judicial branches;
23 (iv) (d) Provided however, that the acquisition, purchase, and in-
24 stallation of all public safety and microwave equipment shall be under
25 the control of the military division. The military division is autho-
26 rized to charge and receive payment for actual and necessary expenses
27 incurred in providing services to any unit of state government under the
28 provisions of this subparagraph.
29 (b) (2)(a) In approving or directing the acquisition, purchase, or in-
30 stallation of information technology or telecommunications equipment
31 or facilities, the office shall first consult with and consider the
32 recommendations and advice of the directors or executive heads of the
33 various departments or institutions. For each purchase of information
34 technology or telecommunications equipment or facilities, the office
35 shall conduct a technical and financial analysis that best meets the
36 needs of the departments and institutions of state government. The of-
37 fice, in its discretion, may use a third-party broker for the technical
38 and financial analysis as long as:
39 (i) The third-party broker has a fiduciary duty to the office for
40 the analysis being performed;
41 (ii) The third-party broker is not also a direct vendor of the in-
42 formation technology being procured;

2
1 (iii) The third-party broker does not accept gifts, gratuities, or
2 favors from vendors and is prohibited from unauthorized ex parte
3 communications during the analysis;
4 (iv) The analysis being conducted is neutral, transparent, and
5 documented; and
6 (v) Fees are not charged to the office, except that the third-
7 party broker may charge a reasonable commission to the vendors en-
8 gaged by the office for its services.
9 (b) The office, in its discretion, may also use and enter into a
10 memorandum of understanding with the division of purchasing in the de-
11 partment of administration for procurements. Except when using the
12 division of purchasing for procurements, the office's purchase of in-
13 formation technology and telecommunications equipment and facilities
14 is exempt from the provisions of chapter 92, title 67, Idaho Code. Any
15 procurements less than fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000) shall be
16 exempt from the provisions of this section and may be purchased di-
17 rectly by the departments and institutions of state government. Any
18 acquisition, purchase, or installation of any information technology
19 or telecommunications equipment or facilities that is contrary to the
20 office's direction or is not in harmony with the state's overall plan
21 for telecommunications and information sharing shall be reported in
22 writing to the governor and the legislature.
23 (2) (3) To provide a system of information technology and telecommu-
24 nications for all departments and institutions of state government. Funds
25 received pursuant to this subsection shall be appropriated for payment of
26 any charges related to information technology and telecommunications and
27 telephone charges incurred by the various agencies and institutions of state
28 government.
29 (3) (4) To provide a means whereby political subdivisions of the state
30 may use the state telecommunications system, on such terms and under such
31 conditions as the office of information technology services may establish.
32 (4) (5) To accept federal funds granted by congress or by executive or-
33 der for all or any of the purposes of this chapter, as well as gifts and dona-
34 tions from individuals and private organizations or foundations.
35 (5) (6) To oversee implementation of cybersecurity policies that fos-
36 ter risk and cybersecurity management telecommunications and decision-mak-
37 ing with both internal and external organizational stakeholders.
38 (6) (7) To consult with and direct state agencies and officials regard-
39 ing information security needs.
40 (7) (8) To direct state agencies and officials on penetration tests and
41 vulnerability scans of state technology systems in order to identify steps
42 to mitigate identified risks.
43 (8) (9) To direct state agencies and officials to ensure that state
44 agencies implement mandatory education and training of state employees and
45 provide guidance on appropriate levels of training for various classifica-
46 tions of state employees.
47 (9) (10) To direct appropriate state agencies to create, coordinate,
48 publish, routinely update and market a statewide cybersecurity website as
49 an information repository for intelligence-sharing and cybersecurity best
50 practices.
