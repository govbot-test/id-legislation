28 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
29 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-

26 SECTION 13. That Section 59-1316, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
27 amended to read as follows:
28 59-1316. MEMBER'S RETIREMENT RECORDS CONFIDENTIAL -- PENALTY FOR DI-
29 VULGING INFORMATION. (1) Each member shall furnish the board with such in-
30 formation as the board shall deem necessary for the proper operation of the
31 system. As provided in section 74-106, Idaho Code, information contained in
32 the retirement system mortgage portfolio loan documents and in each member's
33 retirement system records is confidential and may not be divulged except as
34 ordered by a court or except as may be required by the employer member or by
35 the retirement board and its staff in order to carry into effect the purposes
36 of this chapter.
37 (2) A member may by his written authorization release specific infor-
38 mation from his own retirement system records to a stated designee. If the
39 member is deceased, the member's contingent annuitant or beneficiary may by
40 written authorization release specific information from the member's re-
41 tirement system records to a stated designee.

3
1 (3) The retirement system may disclose the identity of a deceased mem-
2 ber's beneficiary to the member's spouse, children, and the court-appointed
3 administrator of the member's estate.
4 (4) Should a court order direct distribution or partial distribution of
5 a member's benefit as defined in either chapter 13, title 59, Idaho Code, or
6 chapter 14, title 72, Idaho Code, to the member's spouse or former spouse,
7 the system may release to the spouse, former spouse, or the court issuing the
8 order information pertaining to the division or segregation of the member's
9 accounts or benefit. This information includes account balances, service
10 accumulations, and related information and histories, but does not include
11 current addresses and phone numbers. The system may release the same in-
12 formation to a member's current spouse at any time, regardless of whether a
13 court has ordered a distribution or division of the member's account.
14 (5) No board member, director, deputy, employee, contractor, or any
15 person formerly employed in any such position shall knowingly divulge or
16 make known to any person in any manner any member's retirement system records
17 obtained directly or indirectly in the discharge of duties except as pro-
18 vided by statute, court order, or rules of the retirement system promulgated
19 under this section or sections 50-1507, 50-1508, 50-1524, 59-1301, 59-1314,
20 59-1383, 59-1392, 72-1405, Idaho Code.
21 (6) Any board member, director, deputy, employee, contractor, or any
22 person formerly employed in any such position who knowingly violates any of
23 the provisions of this section shall be guilty of a felony and, upon convic-
24 tion, shall be punished by a fine of not less than one hundred dollars ($100)
25 nor more than five thousand dollars ($5,000) or by imprisonment for not more
26 than five (5) years. Upon such conviction, such person shall also forfeit
27 his office, employment, or contract, if any, and shall be ineligible to hold

8 59-1394. EXCESS COSTS -- ADDITIONAL CONTRIBUTIONS. (1) In addition
9 to the employee and employer contributions required by chapter 14, title
10 72, Idaho Code, additional contributions shall be required to fund the pro-
11 visions of section 59-1397, Idaho Code this section. These costs shall be
12 borne by employers and by the state of Idaho as hereinafter provided.
13 (a) Fifty percent (50%) of the gross receipts by the state of the tax on
14 fire insurance premiums, as provided by section 41-402, Idaho Code, is
15 hereby perpetually appropriated to the public employee retirement ac-
16 count for the purpose of partially funding the benefit payment require-
17 ments imposed by the provisions of chapter 14, title 72, Idaho Code.
18 (b) The board shall conduct studies from time to time of the benefits
19 prescribed by section 59-1397, Idaho Code, to determine the additional
20 contributions required to fund the rights conferred by chapter 14,
21 title 72, Idaho Code, above and beyond the initial contribution from
22 the fire insurance premium tax required by subsection (1) (a) of this
23 section. If such studies indicate the value of the benefits exceeds
24 the required contributions otherwise prescribed, the board shall es-
25 tablish an additional contribution rate necessary to bring the amounts
26 into balance. The cost of such additional contribution shall be borne
27 equally by the employers through additional contributions and the state
28 of Idaho through the fire insurance premium tax. In addition to appro-
29 priation of the fire insurance premium tax contained in subsection (1)
30 (a) of this section, the amount of the gross receipts by the state of the
31 tax on fire insurance premiums, as provided by section 41-402, Idaho
32 Code, necessary to match dollar for dollar the additional contribution
33 required of employers is hereby perpetually appropriated commencing
34 July 1, 1980 to the public employee retirement account for the purpose
35 of subsection (1) (b) of this section. If the matching funds herein
36 provided equal one hundred percent (100%) of the gross receipts from the
37 fire insurance premium tax, the employers shall contribute the balance
38 of the monies required to meet the required contribution rate. The
39 additional contribution rate from the employers commencing October 1,
40 1980 shall be ten percent (10%) of the pay period salary of each paid
41 firefighter until next determined by the board.
42 (2) Nothing herein contained shall prevent the board from contracting
43 with employers to provide a schedule of contributions which will retire any
44 excess cost over a given period of time, not to exceed fifty (50) years. In
45 the event that such agreements are reached, the amount of the fire insurance
46 premium tax necessary to match additional employer contributions is contin-
47 uously appropriated for that purpose.
