26 gram. The format and content of this report will be determined in consul-
27 tation with the Budget and Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative Ser-
28 vices Office.
29 SECTION 7. IDAHO CHILD CARE PROGRAM CAPACITY. (1) Of the amount appro-
30 priated or reappropriated for year two of the Idaho Child Care Program Capac-
31 ity through the Cooperative Welfare (Federal) Fund, the Department of Health
32 and Welfare shall prioritize: executing contracts, programs and grants to
33 expand the availability of licensed, license-exempt, and unlicensed home-
34 based child care, out-of-school child care programs, or center-based child
35 care. No less than $9 million shall be reserved for home-based child care,
36 out-of-school child care programs, and underserved children. For the pur-
37 poses of this funding, underserved populations are foster children, chil-
38 dren with disabilities, infants and toddlers, children needing non-tradi-
39 tional hours care, homeless children, and school-aged children. After all
40 qualifying providers are approved, the department may make available to all
41 child care providers unused funding from the set-aside. The funds shall be
42 used to start or expand capacity for such programs and may not be used to sup-
43 plant existing costs.
44 (2) The director of the Department of Health and Welfare shall not allo-
45 cate funds:
46 (a) For construction or major renovations as defined in 45 CFR 98.2;

4
1 (b) For activities prohibited by 45 CFR 98.56;
2 (c) For minor remodeling;
3 (d) To a school district;
4 (e) To any provider who is currently under investigation by the depart-
5 ment, the attorney general, or a county prosecutor for fraud or the mis-
6 use of public funds; or
7 (f) To any provider who has admitted or was previously found by a court,
8 administrative tribunal, jury, or other neutral factfinder to have com-
9 mitted fraud or misused public funds.
10 (3) For purposes of subsection (2)(e) of this section, a provider shall
11 not be deemed to be under investigation solely on the basis of having re-
12 ceived a civil investigation demand requesting information, where no subse-
13 quent investigative or enforcement action has been taken.

26 vision of Youth Safety and Permanency is hereby exempted from the provision

28 Southwest Idaho Treatment Center Program for all moneys appropriated to the
29 division for the period July 1, 2026, through June 30, 2027.
30 SECTION 11. CONDITIONS, LIMITATIONS, AND RESTRICTIONS. Appropriations
31 made pursuant to this act are subject to the conditions, limitations, and re-
32 strictions specified in this act.
