LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 754
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE IDAHO DIRECT PRIMARY CARE ACT; AMENDING SECTION 39-9203,
3 IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A DEFINITION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PRO-
4 VIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 39-9203, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 39-9203. DEFINITIONS. For purposes of this chapter, the following
9 definitions apply:
10 (1) "Direct fee" means an agreed-upon fee charged by a primary care
11 provider as consideration for providing and being available to provide di-
12 rect primary care services described in a direct primary care agreement.
13 (2) "Direct primary care agreement" means a written contract between a
14 primary care provider and an individual patient or a patient's representa-
15 tive in which the primary care provider agrees to provide direct primary care
16 services to the patient over a specified period of time for payment of a di-
17 rect fee.
18 (3) "Direct primary care services" means those services that a primary
19 care provider is licensed or otherwise legally authorized to provide and may
20 include, but are not limited to, such services as screening, assessment, di-
21 agnosis and treatment for the purpose of promoting health; detection, man-
22 agement and care of disease or injury; or routine preventive or diagnostic
23 dental treatment. Such services may be provided in a primary care provider's
24 office, the patient's home or other locations where a patient visit with the
25 primary care provider needs to occur.
26 (4) "Patient" means a person who is entitled to receive direct primary
27 care services under a direct care agreement.
28 (5) "Patient's representative" means a person identified in section
29 39-4504(1)(a) through (g), Idaho Code.
30 (6) "Primary care provider" means a natural person licensed or other-
31 wise legally authorized to provide health care services in the state of Idaho
32 in the field of pediatrics, family medicine, internal medicine, physical
33 therapy, or dentistry, who provides such services either alone or in profes-
34 sional association with others in a form and within a scope permitted by such
35 licensure or legal authorization for the provision of such services, and who
36 enters into a direct primary care agreement.
37 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
38 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
39 July 1, 2026.
