1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE CAPITOL MALL; AMENDING SECTION 67-1613, IDAHO CODE, TO RE-
3 VISE PROVISIONS REGARDING CAMPING, TO PROVIDE FOR TEMPORARY CANOPIES,
4 AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AMENDING SECTION 67-1613A, IDAHO
5 CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING DISPOSITION OF PROPERTY; AMENDING

10 SECTION 1. That Section 67-1613, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
11 amended to read as follows:
12 67-1613. CAPITOL MALL AND OTHER STATE PROPERTY AND FACILITIES -- CAMP-
13 ING PROHIBITED. (1) No person shall camp on or in any state-owned or leased
14 property or facility, including, but not limited to, the capitol mall, ex-
15 cept those that are designated as a recreational camping ground, area or fa-
16 cility. Any symbolic tents or other enclosed structures shall be removed
17 from 9:00 p.m. local time to 6:00 a.m. local time, unless otherwise provided
18 for in any applicable rules promulgated by the department of administration.
19 The provisions of this section shall not apply or affect policies, rules,
20 statutes or leases on endowment lands, department of parks and recreation
21 lands or department of fish and game lands. For the purposes of this section,
22 the term "camp" or "camping" means to use as a temporary or permanent place of
23 dwelling, lodging or living accommodation, and which indicia of camping may
24 include, but are not limited to, storing personal belongings, using tents or
25 other temporary structures for storing personal belongings or for sleeping,
26 carrying on cooking activities, laying out bedding or making any fire. Any
27 person who violates the provisions of this section shall be guilty of an in-
28 fraction. Such persons shall be required to remove all their personal prop-
29 erty from the state-owned or leased property.
30 (2) As used in this section:
31 (a) "Camp" or "camping" means to erect tents or other enclosed struc-
32 tures on state-owned or leased property or facilities at any time be-
33 tween sunset and sunrise, the use of state-owned or leased property or
34 facilities as a temporary or permanent place of dwelling, lodging, res-
35 idence, or living accommodation at any time, or any conduct that consti-
36 tutes the indicia of camping.
37 (b) "Indicia of camping" may include but is not limited to storing per-
38 sonal belongings, storing food for future days, using tents or other
39 temporary structures for storing personal belongings or food or for
40 sleeping or lying down, carrying on cooking activities, sleeping or
41 making preparations to sleep, including laying out a sleeping bag,

2
1 blanket, or other material used for bedding, making any fire, or doing
2 any digging or earth breaking.
3 (3) Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall
4 be guilty of an infraction. Regardless of whether a citation is issued,
5 such person shall be required to remove all his personal property from the
6 state-owned or leased property or facility.
7 (4) Nothing in this section shall preclude a person from erecting a tem-
8 porary side-free canopy or other similar cover without side walls to stay dry
9 during periods of rain or snow, including from sunset to sunrise, as long as
10 it is not used for sleeping or camping and it complies with any rules promul-
11 gated by the department of administration.
12 SECTION 2. That Section 67-1613A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
13 amended to read as follows:
14 67-1613A. DISPOSITION OF PROPERTY. Any property remaining after is-
15 suance of a citation, any property on state property in violation of an Idaho
16 statute or administrative rule, or any property left unattended shall be
17 held by the agency or its agent removing the property in a secure location for
18 a period of not less than ninety (90) days. Notice shall be posted and remain
19 at the nearest reasonable location to the place of removal with the agency's
20 or agent's contact information for the ninety (90) day period. If property
21 is not claimed within the ninety (90) day period, the property shall be
22 deemed abandoned and the agency shall have the right to dispose of the prop-
23 erty. A reasonable storage fee as determined by the agency may be assessed at
24 the time an owner claims the property. The individual claiming the property
25 shall produce identification and shall sign a release form providing his or
26 her name and contact information and swearing that the property belongs to
27 the claiming party. If the provisions of this section are complied with, the
28 state of Idaho, its agents, employees and contractors shall be immune from
29 legal liability for the administration of this section.
