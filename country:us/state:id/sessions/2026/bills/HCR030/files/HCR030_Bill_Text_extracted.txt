LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 30
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND DIRECTING THE MEDICAID LEGISLATIVE
3 REVIEW PANEL TO STUDY THE IMPACT OF AND PROVIDE GUIDANCE AND RECOMMEN-
4 DATIONS ON THE IMPLEMENTATION OF COMPREHENSIVE MEDICAID MANAGED CARE IN
5 THE STATE OF IDAHO.
6 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 WHEREAS, in March 2025, the Idaho Legislature passed House Bill 345,
8 which directs the Department of Health and Welfare to submit state plan
9 amendments and federal waivers to the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Ser-
10 vices in order to implement a plan for comprehensive Medicaid managed care;
11 and
12 WHEREAS, the Medicaid Legislative Review Panel was established by the
13 Legislature in 2024 in order to review contracts related to Medicaid, to make
14 recommendations on key indicators and performance measures to be included
15 in any Medicaid contract, to recommend any data to be collected as part of a
16 Medicaid contract, and to perform other duties as determined by the Legisla-
17 ture, Legislative Council, or initiative of the cochairs of the panel; and
18 WHEREAS, the most recent national data available shows that 75% of Med-
19 icaid beneficiaries receive their care through comprehensive managed care
20 organizations, and states have the authority to decide which services are
21 included in managed care organization contracts; and
22 WHEREAS, a transition of Medicaid delivery to a comprehensive managed
23 care system is a momentous and highly complex undertaking, with impacts on
24 myriad aspects of health care delivery and participant health outcomes in
25 addition to budgetary concerns.
26 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
27 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
28 the Senate concurring therein, that the Medicaid Legislative Review Panel,
29 created pursuant to Section 56-274, Idaho Code, shall study the impact of and
30 provide guidance and recommendations on the implementation of comprehensive
31 Medicaid managed care in the state of Idaho.
32 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED the Medicaid Legislative Review Panel is autho-
33 rized to retain the services of a consultant or analyst, with the prior ap-
34 proval of the Speaker of the House of Representatives and the President Pro
35 Tempore of the Senate, who is familiar with comprehensive Medicaid managed
36 care and who can provide necessary economic and policy research that can as-
37 sist the panel and the Legislature in making an informed decision on this im-
38 portant topic.
