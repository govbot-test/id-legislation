LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 846
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTION 33-1027, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PRO-
3 VISIONS REGARDING STUDENT ENROLLMENT COUNTS AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL
4 CORRECTION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 33-1027, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 33-1027. STUDENT ENROLLMENT COUNTS AND RULEMAKING. The state board of
9 education shall promulgate rules that set forth the procedures for determin-
10 ing student enrollment counts by school, school district, and statewide, and
11 the process for reporting such counts. Such rules shall be consistent with
12 the following:
13 (1) Full-time enrollment (FTE) shall be based on enrollment in any
14 school district or public charter school;
15 (2) A student shall not exceed a total of one (1.0) unweighted FTE in a
16 single school year, except as provided in subsection (4) of this section;
17 (3) A kindergarten student shall not exceed a total of one-half (0.5)
18 unweighted enrollment in a single school year;
19 (4) A student attending a summer school or night school program shall
20 not exceed a total of one-fourth (0.25) unweighted enrollment. Such student
21 may be counted pursuant to both this subsection and subsection (2) of this
22 section;
23 (5) A fractional enrollment count schedule shall be specified for any
24 student enrolled less than one (1.0) FTE in a given school district or public
25 charter school;
26 (6) FTE is based on the courses a student is enrolled in at the time of
27 the official count, as specified in board rule, except that a student may be
28 counted as enrolled if the term for which such student is enrolled begins af-
29 ter the time of the official count;
30 (7) Each school district or public charter school shall conduct an of-
31 ficial count of enrolled students in the district or school on the first day
32 of October, the first day of December, the first day of February, and the
33 first day of April, or the previous school day if those dates do not fall on
34 a school day; and
35 (8) A school district or public charter school may shall not count as
36 enrolled any student who has unexcused absences totaling eleven (11) or more
37 consecutive school days immediately prior to and including the official
38 count date.
39 (9) A school district or public charter school shall not count as en-
40 rolled any student who has unexcused absences for the first ten (10) consecu-
41 tive days of the school year. If a student attends at a later date, the school

2
1 district or public charter school may count the student as enrolled as of the
2 date of attendance.
3 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
4 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
5 July 1, 2026.
