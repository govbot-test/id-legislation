LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 125
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND ENCOURAGING THE CITIZENS' COMMITTEE
3 ON LEGISLATIVE COMPENSATION TO REDUCE THE COMPENSATION AND EXPENSE AL-
4 LOWANCES FOR LEGISLATORS SERVING IN THE SIXTY-NINTH IDAHO LEGISLATURE
5 SO THAT LEGISLATORS SHARE IN THE BURDENS BEING IMPOSED ON THE CITIZENS
6 AND EMPLOYEES OF THE STATE OF IDAHO.
7 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 WHEREAS, in response to falling state revenue projections and in antic-
9 ipation of the estimated $175 million cost to the State of Idaho conforming
10 to the federal tax bill in 2027, Idaho is proposing across-the-board state
11 agency budget cuts and no increase in state employee salaries; and
12 WHEREAS, the executive budget recommended by Governor Brad Little for
13 fiscal year 2027 includes an across-the-board 3% budget cut to state agency
14 budgets; and
15 WHEREAS, the Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee voted to include
16 an additional across-the-board budget cut of 2% to state agency budgets, for
17 a total 5% cut to state agency budgets; and
18 WHEREAS, the impact of these budget reductions and General Fund spend-
19 ing authority reductions on the citizens and employees of the State of Idaho
20 are severe; and
21 WHEREAS, the Citizens' Committee on Legislative Compensation is given
22 the authority under section 23, article III of the Constitution of the State
23 of Idaho to establish the rate of compensation and expense allowances for
24 services to be rendered by members of the Idaho Legislature; and
25 WHEREAS, the committee will meet prior to the commencement of the First
26 Regular Session of the Sixty-ninth Idaho Legislature to set legislative com-
27 pensation and expense allowances for 2027 and 2028.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
29 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of
30 Representatives concurring therein, that the Citizens' Committee on Leg-
31 islative Compensation is encouraged to reduce the compensation and expense
32 allowances for legislators serving in the Sixty-ninth Idaho Legislature
33 so that legislators share in the burdens being imposed on the citizens and
34 employees of the State of Idaho.
35 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate is directed to
36 send a copy of this resolution to each member of the Citizens' Committee on
37 Legislative Compensation.
