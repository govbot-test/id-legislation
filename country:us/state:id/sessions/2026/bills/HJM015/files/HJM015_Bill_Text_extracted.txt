LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 15
BY ENVIRONMENT, ENERGY AND TECHNOLOGY COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRE-
3 SENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, AND THE CONGRES-
4 SIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE
5 UNITED STATES.
6 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
7 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
8 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
9 WHEREAS, it is documented that federal agencies, military contractors,
10 nongovernmental organizations, and private entities acting on behalf of
11 or at the request of the federal government are engaging in geoengineering
12 experiments, including but not limited to solar radiation modification,
13 stratospheric aerosol injection, and other weather modification attempts
14 by dispersing chemicals or particulates into the atmosphere, all without
15 public knowledge or consent; and
16 WHEREAS, such activities involve substances such as aluminum oxide,
17 barium, sulfur dioxide, or other compounds with unknown or unreported im-
18 pacts on human health, livestock, crops, ecosystems, and weather stability
19 being emitted into the atmosphere without public knowledge or consent; and
20 WHEREAS, such activities are currently allowed without oversight in
21 Idaho's airspace; and
22 WHEREAS, under the Tenth Amendment to the United States Constitu-
23 tion, it is the constitutional duty of state government to safeguard public
24 health, defend private property, protect agricultural integrity, and assert
25 state sovereignty over activities conducted within its borders and above its
26 skies; and
27 WHEREAS, the Idaho Department of Environmental Quality is charged with
28 protecting the safety and quality of the state's air, water, and soil and
29 must be empowered to investigate and stop unauthorized atmospheric contam-
30 ination within Idaho's jurisdiction; and
31 WHEREAS, multiple states, including Tennessee and Florida, have now
32 enacted legislation prohibiting geoengineering modifications, and dozens
33 more are considering similar laws to preserve natural climate systems and
34 ensure transparency in all atmospheric activity.
35 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
36 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
37 the Senate concurring therein, that the Idaho Legislature supports and urges
38 the United States Congress passing legislation to: prohibit the intentional
39 injection, dispersion, or release of chemical, biological, or radiological
40 substances into the atmosphere for the purpose of weather modification,
41 solar radiation modification, or environmental geoengineering without the
42 express approval of the State of Idaho; ban the use of unmarked aircraft,
43 drones, balloons, or space-based platforms to conduct geoengineering mod-

2
1 ifications over the State of Idaho; make such unauthorized acts a felony
2 offense, with substantial fines and criminal penalties per violation; en-
3 courage partnerships between the relevant federal law enforcement agencies,
4 Idaho Department of Environmental Quality, and relevant state law enforce-
5 ment entities to enforce such felonies; and formally reject any federal,
6 international, or foreign-sponsored geoengineering programs conducted over
7 Idaho airspace without full disclosure, state consent, and public trans-
8 parency.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Idaho Legislature urges all elected
10 officials, agencies, and candidates to support legislation that preserves
11 Idaho's natural skies, defends individual and agricultural health, and as-
12 serts constitutional control over state airspace.
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
14 tives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this
15 Memorial to the President of the United States, the President of the Senate
16 and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of Congress, and the con-
17 gressional delegation representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the
18 United States.
