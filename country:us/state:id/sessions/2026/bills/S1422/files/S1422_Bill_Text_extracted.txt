28 74-3-101. PURPOSE OF CHAPTER. The purpose of this chapter is:
29 (1) To promote openness and public confidence in government; and
30 (2) To promote transparency by those giving financial support to elec-
31 tion campaigns and to persons advocating for or against candidates or ballot
32 measures.
33 74-3-102. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter, the following terms
34 have the following meanings:
35 (1) "Candidate" means an individual who seeks nomination, election,
36 or reelection to public office in Idaho and includes individuals seeking
37 statewide, judicial, legislative, or local government office. For the
38 purposes of this chapter, "candidate" does not include individuals seek-
39 ing nomination, election, or reelection to federal office or the office of
40 precinct committeeman.
41 (2) "Contribution" includes any advance, conveyance, forgiveness of
42 indebtedness, deposit, distribution, loan, gift, pledge, subscription, or
43 transfer of money or anything of value, any contract, agreement, and promise
44 or other obligation, whether or not legally enforceable, and any payment,

1 whether by cash, check, or electronic transfer, to make a contribution in
2 support of or in opposition to any candidate, political action committee,
3 or measure. Such term also includes personal funds or other property of a
4 candidate or members of his household expended or transferred to cover ex-
5 penditures incurred in support of such candidate but does not include per-
6 sonal funds used to pay the candidate filing fee. Such term also includes
7 the rendering of personal and professional services for less than full con-
8 sideration but does not include ordinary home hospitality or the rendering
9 of part-time services of the sort commonly performed by volunteer campaign
10 workers or advisors or incidental expenses not in excess of twenty-five dol-
11 lars ($25.00) personally paid for by any volunteer campaign worker. "Part-
12 time services," for the purposes of this definition, means services in addi-
13 tion to regular full-time employment, or, in the case of an unemployed person
14 or persons engaged in part-time employment, services rendered without com-
15 pensation or reimbursement of expenses from any source other than the candi-
16 date or political action committee for whom such services are rendered. For
17 the purposes of this chapter, contributions other than money or its equiva-
18 lent shall be deemed to have a monetary value equivalent to the fair market
19 value of the contribution.
20 (3) "Earmark" or "earmarked" means a designation, instruction, or en-
21 cumbrance, whether directly or indirectly, express or implied, oral or writ-
22 ten, that results in all or any part of a contribution being used to make
23 a contribution, independent expenditure, electioneering communication, or
24 measure expenditure. A donor's authorization for a commercial payment pro-
25 cessor, whose usual and normal business is to process payments, to transmit
26 funds from the donor to a nonprofit corporation, association, or other or-
27 ganization in the commercial payment processor's ordinary course of busi-
28 ness does not in itself constitute an earmark.
29 (4) "Election" means any state or local general, special, recall, or
30 primary election.
31 (5) "Election campaign" means any campaign in support of or in opposi-
32 tion to a candidate for election to public office and any campaign in support
33 of or in opposition to a measure.
34 (6)(a) "Electioneering communication" means any paid public communi-
35 cation that:
36 (i) Unambiguously refers to any candidate;
37 (ii) Is broadcast, printed, mailed, delivered, made, dis-
38 tributed, or disseminated to a recipient by telephone, in a digi-
39 tal format online, or by other electronic means during the thirty
40 (30) day period before a primary election or the sixty (60) day
41 period before a general election; and
42 (iii) Is directed to an audience that includes members of the elec-
43 torate for the candidate's public office.
44 (b) An electioneering communication does not include:
45 (i) Any news articles, editorial endorsements, opinion or com-
46 mentary, writings, or letters to the editor printed in a newspa-
47 per, magazine, or other periodical not owned or controlled by a
48 candidate, political action committee, or political party;
49 (ii) Media appearances by a candidate on television, radio shows,
50 podcasts, or any other type of digital media not owned or con-

3 (iii) Any editorial endorsements or opinions aired by a broadcast

6 (iv) Any communication by persons made in the regular course and
7 scope of their business or any communication made by a membership

15 (7) "Employee" means an individual who performs a service for wages or
16 other compensation from which the individual's employer withholds federal
17 employment taxes under a contract for hire, written or oral.
18 (8) "Expenditure" includes any payment, contribution, subscription,
19 distribution, loan, advance, deposit, or gift of money or anything of value
20 and includes a contract, promise, or agreement, whether or not legally en-
21 forceable, to make an expenditure. The term "expenditure" also includes a
22 promise to pay, a payment or a transfer of anything of value in exchange for
23 goods, services, property, facilities, or anything of value for the purpose
24 of assisting, benefiting, or honoring any public official or candidate, or
25 assisting in furthering or opposing any election campaign. For the purpose
26 of determining when an expenditure report should be filed under this chap-
27 ter, an expenditure is made on the day that the communication paid for with
28 the expenditure is publicly distributed or otherwise viewed by the public or
29 on the date payment is made, whichever is earlier.
30 (9)(a) "Independent expenditure" means any expenditure by a person
31 for a communication expressly advocating the election or defeat of a
32 clearly identified candidate that is not made in coordination with the
33 candidate.
34 (b) As used in this subsection, "expressly advocating" means any com-
35 munication containing a message advocating the election or defeat of a
36 candidate by using the name of the candidate and using phrases includ-
37 ing but not limited to "vote for," "elect," "support," "cast your ballot
38 for," "vote against," "defeat," or "reject."
39 (c) For the purpose of determining whether an expenditure is made in
40 coordination with a candidate or political action committee, the term
41 "coordination" means and includes but is not limited to an expenditure
42 made with the cooperation of, with the prior consent or knowledge of, in
43 consultation with, or at the request or suggestion of, or using nonpub-
44 lic information obtained from, a candidate or the candidate's agent or
45 paid or unpaid staff or volunteer, a person acting on behalf of a can-
46 didate, or a person acting as a conduit for messages to or from a candi-
47 date's campaign.
48 (10) "Indirectly" means making a contribution or payment to a person
49 with a designation, instruction, or encumbrance, whether direct or indi-
50 rect, express or implied, oral or written, or involving intermediaries or

40 74-3-103. FOREIGN CONTRIBUTIONS, FOREIGN INDEPENDENT EXPENDITURES,
41 AND FOREIGN ELECTIONEERING COMMUNICATIONS PROHIBITED. (1) A foreign na-
42 tional shall not make a contribution, directly or indirectly, to any
43 candidate, political action committee, or measure or make electioneering
44 communications or independent expenditures.
45 (2) As used in this section, "foreign national" means:
46 (a) An individual who is not a citizen of the United States and is not
47 lawfully admitted for permanent residence;
48 (b) A government or subdivision of a foreign country;
49 (c) A foreign political party; or

6
1 (d) Any entity, such as a partnership, association, corporation, or-
2 ganization, union, or other combination of persons, that is organized
3 under the laws of or has its principal place of business in a foreign
4 country.
5 (3) A violation of the provisions of this section shall be prosecuted
6 and punished as provided in part 5 of this chapter. However, any person who
7 knowingly and willfully violates the provisions of this section is guilty of
8 a felony when:
9 (a) The aggregate amount of contributions, independent expenditures,
10 or cost of electioneering communications made in violation of this sec-
11 tion exceeds one thousand dollars ($1,000) in a consecutive twelve (12)
12 month period; or
13 (b) The person pleads guilty to or is found guilty of a knowing and will-
14 ful violation of the provisions of this section for a second time within
15 ten (10) years, notwithstanding the form of the judgment or withheld
16 judgment.
17 (4) If any provision of this section or its application to any person or
18 circumstance is held invalid, the remainder of the section or the applica-
19 tion of the provision to other persons or circumstances is not affected.
20 74-3-104. COMMERCIAL RECORDKEEPING. Each newspaper, periodical,
21 broadcasting station, direct mailing company, printer, and advertising
22 agency shall keep a current record of any and all obligations incurred by
23 a candidate or political action committee and payments made by a candidate
24 or political action committee. Such records shall be made available for
25 inspection upon request by the secretary of state. Failure to make such
26 records available shall result in a civil fine not to exceed one thousand
27 dollars ($1,000).
28 74-3-105. REPORTS TO BE CERTIFIED. All reports required to be filed
29 with the secretary of state under this chapter shall be signed and certi-
30 fied as true and correct by the person required to file the same. Electronic
31 signatures and certifications shall be governed by the uniform electronic
32 transactions act, chapter 50, title 28, Idaho Code.

9 74-3-107. PERSUASIVE POLL CONCERNING CANDIDATE OR MEASURE MUST IDEN-
10 TIFY PERSON OR ENTITY PAYING FOR POLL. (1) If a person, candidate, politi-
11 cal party, or political committee requests or compensates a person to con-
12 duct or cause to be conducted a persuasive poll by telephone concerning a
13 candidate or to produce automated or computerized messages by telephone to
14 conduct a persuasive poll concerning a candidate, the person conducting the
15 poll shall, at the end of the poll, disclose the name and telephone number
16 of the person, candidate, political party, or political committee that re-
17 quested or compensated the person for the poll.
18 (2) As used in this section, "persuasive poll" means the canvassing of
19 persons, by means other than an established method of scientific sampling,
20 by asking questions concerning a candidate that are designed to provide in-
21 formation to advocate for the election, approval, or defeat of a candidate or
22 measure. The term does not include a poll that is conducted only to measure
23 the public's opinion about or reaction to an issue, fact, or theme.
24 (3) A violation of the provisions of this section shall be punishable as
25 provided in part 5 of this chapter.

32 FINANCE ACCOUNT. (1) A candidate shall be subject to the requirements of this
33 chapter once the candidate has taken any of the following actions:
34 (a) Announced the individual's candidacy publicly;
35 (b) Established a campaign finance account with the secretary of
36 state's office;
37 (c) Received a contribution for the purpose of promoting the individ-
38 ual's candidacy for office; or
39 (d) Made an expenditure, contracted for services, or reserved space
40 with the intent of promoting the individual's candidacy for office.
41 (2) For purposes of this chapter, an incumbent shall be presumed to be
42 a candidate in the subsequent election for the incumbent's office until the
43 incumbent has failed to file for office by the statutory deadline.
44 (3) Each candidate shall establish a campaign finance account with the
45 secretary of state's office as prescribed by the secretary of state. The
46 candidate shall provide the full name and address of the candidate, the of-

3 74-3-202. APPOINTMENT OF POLITICAL TREASURER FOR CANDIDATE. (1) Each
4 candidate shall appoint a political treasurer who is a registered elector of
5 this state. The candidate shall certify the full name and complete address
6 of the political treasurer to the secretary of state at the time the candi-
7 date establishes a campaign finance account with the secretary of state's
8 office or as soon thereafter as practicable.
9 (2) No contribution shall be received or expenditure made by or on be-
10 half of a candidate until a political treasurer has been appointed pursuant
11 to the provisions of this section. Contributions must be received and expen-
12 ditures must be made by or through the candidate's political treasurer.
13 (3) A candidate may appoint himself to serve as his own political trea-
14 surer.
15 (4) An individual may be appointed and serve as political treasurer for
16 more than one (1) candidate and political action committee.
17 (5) A candidate may remove his political treasurer. In the case of the
18 death, resignation, or removal of a political treasurer before all obliga-
19 tions of a political treasurer under this chapter have been met, the candi-
20 date shall appoint a successor political treasurer and certify the name and
21 address of the successor in the manner provided in the case of an original ap-
22 pointment.
23 74-3-203. DUTIES OF POLITICAL TREASURER FOR CANDIDATE. (1) The polit-
24 ical treasurer or candidate shall serve as the official point of contact for
25 the secretary of state for the candidate's campaign.
26 (2) The political treasurer for each candidate shall maintain at least
27 one (1) checking account with a financial institution. All moneys received
28 by the candidate's campaign shall be deposited in such account. The polit-
29 ical treasurer for each candidate shall keep detailed accounts of all con-
30 tributions received and all expenditures made by or on behalf of the candi-
31 date. The political treasurer shall keep the accounts current within seven
32 (7) days after the date of receiving a contribution or making an expenditure.
33 A candidate's campaign funds shall be segregated from, and may not be commin-
34 gled with, any other account. A candidate who loans his campaign his own per-
35 sonal funds must transfer those funds to his campaign account.
36 (3) The political treasurer shall be responsible for making and filing
37 all reports that are required of a candidate under this chapter. Accounts
38 kept by the political treasurer for a candidate are subject to inspection by
39 the office of the secretary of state in the case of an investigation pursuant
40 to section 74-3-501 or 74-3-502, Idaho Code.
41 (4) Accounts kept by a political treasurer shall be preserved by him for
42 at least one (1) year after the date of the election to which the accounts re-
43 fer or at least one (1) year after the date the last report is filed under sec-
44 tion 74-3-205, Idaho Code, whichever is later.

1 (a) "Information content provider" means any person or entity that is
