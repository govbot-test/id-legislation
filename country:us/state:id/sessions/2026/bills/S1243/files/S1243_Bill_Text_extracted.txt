8 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
9 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
10 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
11 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
12 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
13 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.

19 SECTION 3. That Section 22-2718, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
20 amended to read as follows:
21 22-2718. IDAHO STATE SOIL AND WATER CONSERVATION COMMISSION. (1)
22 There is hereby established and created in the department of agriculture of
23 the state of Idaho the Idaho state soil and water conservation commission
24 which shall perform all functions conferred upon it by this chapter and shall
25 be a nonregulatory agency.
26 (a) The commission shall consist of seven (7) members appointed by the
27 governor from divisions of the Idaho association of soil conservation
28 districts as follows: one (1) member from division I, one (1) member
29 from division II, one (1) member from division III, one (1) member from
30 division IV, one (1) member from division V, one (1) member from divi-
31 sion VI, and one (1) at-large member appointed at the governor's discre-
32 tion.
33 (b) Commission members shall be chosen with due regard to their demon-
34 strated expertise, including knowledge of conservation districts and
35 their functions, knowledge of production agriculture, knowledge of
36 banking or other similar financial experience, or experience as a
37 county commissioner.
38 (c) Commissioners serving on July 1, 2022, will be assigned to the di-
39 visions that they would represent. On July 1, 2022, current commission-
40 ers, at the will of the governor, will be reappointed to the position
41 representing the divisions in which they live.
42 (d) For divisions that have vacant positions, or divisions with no cur-
43 rent commissioner residing there, the division shall submit a list of
44 up to three (3) names for each open commission position. The governor
45 shall appoint commission seats from the list submitted.
46 (e) The term of each commissioner shall be for five (5) years, with the
47 ability to serve two (2) terms; except that on July 1, 2022, the terms of
48 each commission position will be reset: The term of the commissioners
49 from districts I and IV will be set for two (2) years. The term of the
