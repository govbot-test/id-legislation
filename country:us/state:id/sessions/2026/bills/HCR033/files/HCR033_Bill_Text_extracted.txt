LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 33
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND ESTABLISHING AN INTERIM COMMITTEE TO
3 STUDY THE CRIME OF SEXTORTION AND TO MAKE RECOMMENDATIONS TO THE LEGIS-
4 LATURE.
5 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 WHEREAS, predators threatening to disclose intimate images of another
7 in exchange for money, more sexual content, or other favors is becoming an
8 increasing problem throughout the country; and
9 WHEREAS, these images can be obtained through coercion, deception, or
10 hacking and can be either real or created artificially so as to be indistin-
11 guishable from a real image; and
12 WHEREAS, such a crime is commonly referred to as "sextortion," and those
13 under 18 years of age are the most common victims; and
14 WHEREAS, victims of sextortion can experience mental health issues such
15 as anxiety and depression, financial loss, reputational damage, and in some
16 cases may attempt suicide or other self-harm; and
17 WHEREAS, victims of sextortion, especially those who are minors, often
18 feel fear, shame, or embarrassment and fail to tell anyone or ask for help;
19 and
20 WHEREAS, the Idaho Office of the Attorney General states that sextor-
21 tion cases are one of the main issues currently being reported to the office;
22 and
23 WHEREAS, it would greatly benefit the people of Idaho for the Legisla-
24 ture to study the crime of sextortion, the effects on its victims, and means
25 of prevention; to review ways to strengthen or expand current statutes re-
26 garding sextortion; and to address ways to help and support the victims of
27 sextortion.
28 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
29 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
30 the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislative Council is authorized to
31 establish an interim committee to undertake and complete a study of the crime
32 of sextortion, means of prevention, and the effects on its victims, in par-
33 ticular those victims under 18 years of age, and to make recommendations re-
34 garding ways to strengthen current statutes to address the effects on vic-
35 tims of sextortion. The Legislative Council shall determine the number of
36 legislators and membership from each house appointed to the interim commit-
37 tee, shall appoint members of both the majority party and minority party, and
38 shall authorize the interim committee to receive input, advice, and assis-
39 tance from interested and affected parties who are not members of the Legis-
40 lature. Other members of the interim committee shall include a representa-
41 tive from the Office of the Attorney General's Internet Crimes Against Chil-
42 dren Unit, the Idaho Department of Education, and the Idaho Crisis and Sui-
43 cide Hotline.

2
1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the cochairs of the interim committee who
2 are appointed by the Legislative Council may appoint additional nonvoting
3 members of the interim committee to represent stakeholders.
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that nonlegislative members of the interim com-
5 mittee shall not be reimbursed from legislative funds for per diem, mileage,
6 or other expenses.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislative Services Office shall pro-
8 vide staff support to the interim committee.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the interim committee shall, by January 31,
10 2027, report its findings, recommendations, and proposed legislation, if
11 any, to the First Regular Session of the Sixty-ninth Idaho Legislature.
