5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 18-310, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 18-310. IMPRISONMENT -- EFFECT ON CIVIL RIGHTS AND OFFICES. (1) A
9 sentence of custody to the Idaho state board of correction suspends all the
10 civil rights of the person so sentenced, including the right to refuse treat-
11 ment authorized by the sentencing court, and forfeits all public offices and
12 all private trusts, authority or power during such imprisonment: provided
13 that any such person may bring an action for damages or other relief in the
14 courts of this state or have an action brought against such person; and pro-
15 vided further that any such person may lawfully exercise all civil rights
16 that are not political during any period of parole or probation, except the
17 right to ship, transport, possess or receive a firearm, and the right to
18 refuse treatment authorized by the sentencing court.
19 (2) Upon final discharge, a person convicted of any Idaho felony shall
20 be restored the full rights of citizenship, except that for persons con-
21 victed of treason or those offenses enumerated in paragraphs (a) through
22 (hh) of this subsection the right to ship, transport, possess or receive a
23 firearm shall not be restored. As used in this subsection, "final discharge"
24 means satisfactory completion of imprisonment, probation and parole as the
25 case may be.
26 (a) Aggravated assault (18-905, 18-915, Idaho Code);

37 (3) A person not restored to the civil right to ship, transport, pos-
38 sess or receive a firearm may make application to the commission of pardons
39 and parole to restore the civil right to ship, transport, possess or receive
40 a firearm. The commission shall not accept any such application until five
41 (5) years after the date of final discharge. The commission shall conduct
42 the proceeding upon such application pursuant to rules adopted in accordance
43 with the law. The commission shall not restore the right to ship, transport,
44 possess or receive a firearm to any person convicted of murder in the first
45 degree (18-4003, Idaho Code), murder in the second degree (18-4003, Idaho
46 Code), or any felony enumerated in paragraphs (a) through (hh) of subsection
47 (2) of this section, upon which the sentence was enhanced for the use of a
48 firearm during the commission of said felony.
49 (4) Persons convicted of felonies in other states or jurisdictions, ex-
50 cept for persons convicted of treason, shall be allowed to register and vote
