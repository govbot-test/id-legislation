LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 628
BY TRANSPORTATION AND DEFENSE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES; AMENDING SECTION 49-307, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE
3 PROVISIONS REGARDING PARENT-STUDENT ON-ROAD DRIVER'S TRAINING; AND
4 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 49-307, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 49-307. CLASS D DRIVER'S TRAINING INSTRUCTION PERMIT -- CLASS D SU-
9 PERVISED INSTRUCTION PERMIT -- APPLICATION FOR A CLASS D DRIVER'S LICENSE
10 -- RESTRICTIONS ON CLASS D DRIVER'S LICENSE. (1) No enrollee of any class D
11 driver's training course shall be allowed to attend classes or participate
12 in driving instruction unless he has obtained a class D driver's training in-
13 struction permit or a class D instruction permit as provided in subsection
14 (4) of this section.
15 (2) Every enrollee of a class D driver's training course shall pay
16 a nonrefundable fee of fifteen dollars ($15.00), except that a pupil who
17 participates in parent-student on-road driver's training pursuant to sub-
18 section (6) of this section shall pay a nonrefundable fee of ten dollars
19 ($10.00). Five dollars ($5.00) of each fee so imposed shall be deposited in
20 the state highway account and five dollars ($5.00) shall be deposited in the
21 county current expense fund. The additional five dollars ($5.00) for every
22 class D driver's training course enrollee shall be:
23 (a) Deposited in the driver training account if the person is taking
24 driver's training from a public school; or
25 (b) Paid to the division of occupational and professional licenses
26 and deposited in the state treasury to the credit of the occupational
27 licenses fund if the person is taking driver's training from a private
28 driver's training program. The amount to be remitted to the division
29 of occupational and professional licenses shall be annually calculated
30 and paid. To calculate such amount, the total number of public driver's
31 training students as submitted to the state department of education
32 shall be subtracted from the total number of permits sold as reported by
33 the Idaho transportation department, and the resulting number shall be
34 multiplied by five dollars ($5.00).
35 (3) Each enrollee of a class D driver's training course shall provide
36 the type of information required for a driver's license or instruction per-
37 mit. If an enrollee of a class D driver's training course cannot provide a
38 certified copy of his birth certificate at the time of application for a per-
39 mit, the department may issue a class D driver's training instruction permit
40 or a class D instruction permit upon receipt of identification acceptable to
41 the department. The certified copy of an applicant's birth certificate or
42 another government-issued identity document and documentation to establish

5 (5) days after the permittee's eighteenth birthday for permittees fourteen
6 and one-half (14 1/2) years of age through seventeen and one-half (17 1/2)
7 years of age. The class D driver's training instruction permit shall ex-

10 (17) years or older may attend classes or participate in driver's training
11 instruction while operating with a class D instruction permit or a class D
12 driver's training instruction permit.
13 (5) The class D driver's training instruction permit shall be issued to
14 the instructor of the course or to the qualifying parent or legal guardian
15 pursuant to subsection (6) of this section.
16 (6)(a) An eligible student may satisfy the requirements of section
17 33-1702, Idaho Code, regarding driver's training courses through par-
18 ent-student on-road driver's training pursuant to this subsection.
19 Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, students
20 eligible for driver's training pursuant to section 33-1703, Idaho Code,
21 may receive the in-car observation and behind-the-wheel instruction
22 components of a driver's training course from a qualifying parent or
23 legal guardian instead of a licensed instructor only if the parent or
24 legal guardian and the student live in a rural school district as de-
25 scribed in section 33-319, Idaho Code, or in a school district that does
26 not offer a driver's training course.
27 (b) A student participating in parent-student on-road driver's train-
28 ing pursuant to this subsection shall enroll and successfully complete
29 the thirty (30) hours of classroom instruction for driver's training
30 required under section 33-1702, Idaho Code, through the Idaho digital
31 learning alliance online driver education course or through any other
32 online driver's training course approved by the state department of
33 education. The Idaho digital learning alliance and any other approved
34 online driver's training program shall make online driver's training
35 courses available to all students eligible for driver's training. The
36 state department of education shall publish a list of all approved on-
37 line driver's training programs on the department's website and such
38 list shall also be made available at each county driver's license of-
39 fice.
40 (c) A parent or legal guardian who is qualified to provide parent-stu-
41 dent on-road driver's training pursuant to this subsection may teach
42 the driving portion of a driver's training course, including the six (6)
43 hours of in-car observation and the six (6) hours of behind-the-wheel
44 instruction required under section 33-1702, Idaho Code. Such training
45 shall be done concurrently with the classroom component of the driver's
46 training course. The in-car observation and behind-the-wheel instruc-
47 tion components shall satisfy all content standards and benchmarks of
48 the Idaho driver education curriculum approved by the state department
49 of education. More than one (1) qualifying parent or legal guardian may
50 provide the parent-student on-road driver's training to a student.

41 by a driver who holds a valid driver's license, is twenty-one (21) years

43 driver. The supervising driver and the permittee shall be the only oc-

47 ten (10) hours of which shall be during hours of darkness.
48 (c) The permit shall be in the permittee's immediate possession at all
49 times while operating a vehicle.
