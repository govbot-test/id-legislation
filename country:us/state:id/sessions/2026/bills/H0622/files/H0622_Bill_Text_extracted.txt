Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTION 33-1020, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PRO-
3 VISIONS REGARDING IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY FUNDING; AND DECLAR-
4 ING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 33-1020, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 33-1020. IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY FUNDING.
9 (1)(a) An amount of four hundred forty-five dollars ($445) four hundred
10 twenty-seven dollars ($427) per enrollment shall be distributed to sup-
11 port the Idaho digital learning academy, created pursuant to chapter
12 55, title 33, Idaho Code. For the purposes of this section, an "enroll-
13 ment" shall be counted each time an Idaho school age child enrolls in
14 an Idaho digital learning academy class and continues to be enrolled in
15 such class past the deadline to withdraw, where such withdrawal would
16 not result in the class appearing on such child's transcript in any way.
17 A single child enrolled in multiple classes shall count as multiple en-
18 rollments may be counted for only up to three (3) enrollments per school
19 year, not including summer enrollments. Summer enrollments shall be
20 included in the fiscal year that begins that summer. An enrollment
21 shall be eligible for state funding only if:
22 (i) The student is enrolled full time in an Idaho school district
23 or public charter school that is not entirely virtual;
24 (ii) The student is in grades 6 through 12;
25 (iii) The class is not a driver's education class; and
26 (iv) The student is not enrolled in a public school or public char-
27 ter school that is located in a city with a population of more than
28 fifty thousand (50,000) and is not enrolled in a custom section. A
29 "custom section" means an Idaho digital learning academy class in
30 which all enrollments are from one (1) school district.
31 (b) If a student wants to enroll in an Idaho digital learning academy
32 class that does not meet the criteria for state funding under this sec-
33 tion, such student may enroll in such class if the school district or
34 public charter school pays the fees for the class as set by the Idaho
35 digital learning academy.
36 (2) The state department of education shall make an estimated distri-
37 bution of funds to the Idaho digital learning academy by no later than July
38 31 of each fiscal year, consisting of eighty percent (80%) of the estimated
39 funding for the fiscal year. The balance of all remaining funds to be dis-
40 tributed, pursuant to the calculations in this section, shall be distributed
41 by no later than May 15 of the same fiscal year. The state department of
42 education shall reconcile the enrollments prior to the May 15 payment and

8 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
