LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 576
BY TRANSPORTATION AND DEFENSE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE IDAHO CONSUMER ASSET RECOVERY FUND; AMENDING SECTION
3 49-1608E, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS FOR IDAHO RESIDENTS; AND
4 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 49-1608E, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 49-1608E. SUBMISSION OF CLAIMS. (1) Except as otherwise provided in
9 this section, whenever any person an Idaho resident or a person who was an
10 Idaho resident at the time of purchase is awarded a final judgment certified
11 in a court of competent jurisdiction in the state of Idaho for:
12 (a) Any actual loss or damage in connection with the purchase or lease
13 of a motor vehicle by reason of any fraud practiced on him or fraudulent
14 representation made to him by a licensed motor vehicle dealer; or
15 (b) Any actual loss or damage by reason of a violation by a dealer of any
16 of the provisions of chapter 6, title 48, Idaho Code, chapter 5, title
17 49, Idaho Code, or section 49-1418, Idaho Code, in connection with the
18 purchase or lease of a motor vehicle on or after July 1, 2014,;
19 the judgment creditor may file a verified claim with the board requesting
20 payment from the fund of the amount unpaid on the judgment.
21 (2) Verified claims filed pursuant to subsection (1) of this section
22 shall be subject to the following conditions:
23 (i) (a) Unless the judgment has been appealed, the claim shall be filed
24 with the department, acting on behalf of the board, no sooner than
25 forty-five (45) days and no later than one (1) year after the judgment
26 becomes final.
27 (ii) (b) The board shall not consider claims submitted by motor vehicle
28 dealers, financial institutions or institutions providing floorplans
29 for motor vehicle dealers.
30 (2) (3) To For a person to be eligible to receive any payment from the
31 fund, any such person must be an Idaho resident or an Idaho resident at the
32 time of the purchase of the motor vehicle. Any action instituted by a person
33 against a licensee that may become a claim against the fund shall be served to
34 the board in a manner consistent with the provisions of section 48-613, Idaho
35 Code.
36 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
37 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
38 July 1, 2026.
