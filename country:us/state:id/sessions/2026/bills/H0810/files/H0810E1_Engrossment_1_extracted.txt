LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 810, As Amended in the Senate
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO ELECTIONS; AMENDING SECTION 34-614, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PRO-
3 VISION REGARDING THE QUALIFICATIONS OF STATE REPRESENTATIVES AND SENA-
4 TORS; AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 34-614, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 34-614. ELECTION OF STATE REPRESENTATIVES AND SENATORS -- QUALIFICA-
9 TIONS. (1) At the general election, 1972, and every alternate year there-
10 after, there shall be elected in each legislative district such representa-
11 tives and senators as they may be severally entitled.
12 (2) No person shall be elected to the office of representative or sen-
13 ator unless he shall have attained the age of twenty-one (21) years at the
14 time of the general election, is a citizen of the United States and shall
15 have, has been a registered elector within the legislative district for one
16 (1) year next immediately preceding the general election at which he offers
17 his candidacy, and has maintained a fixed habitation within the legislative
18 district boundaries for one (1) year immediately preceding the date such
19 candidate files a declaration of candidacy for the office of representative
20 or senator. For the purposes of this subsection, "fixed habitation" means a
21 residence as defined in section 34-107, Idaho Code, at which the candidate
22 maintains actual physical presence and occupancy for at least one hundred
23 twenty (120) nights during the one (1) year period immediately preceding
24 the date such candidate files a declaration of candidacy for the office of
25 representative or senator. A candidate shall receive an exemption to the
26 fixed habitation requirement due to active military service during the one
27 (1) year period.
28 (3) Each candidate shall file his declaration of candidacy with the
29 secretary of state.
30 (4) Each candidate who files a declaration of candidacy shall at the
31 same time pay a filing fee of thirty dollars ($30.00) which shall be de-
32 posited in the general fund.
33 SECTION 2. This act shall be in full force and effect on and after Jan-
34 uary 1, 2027.
