Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 760
BY REVENUE AND TAXATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO PROPERTY TAXES; AMENDING SECTION 63-602GG, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE
3 PROVISIONS REGARDING PROPERTY TAX EXEMPTIONS FOR LOW-INCOME HOUSING
4 OWNED BY NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PRO-
5 VIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 63-602GG, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 63-602GG. PROPERTY EXEMPT FROM TAXATION -- LOW-INCOME HOUSING OWNED
10 BY NONPROFIT ORGANIZATIONS. (1) As provided in this section, the board of
11 county commissioners may, at its discretion, exempt all or a portion of low-
12 income housing owned by nonprofit organizations shall be exempt from taxa-
13 tion.
14 (2) In order to qualify as a nonprofit organization under this section,
15 an organization must demonstrate that:
16 (a) It is organized as a nonprofit corporation pursuant to chapter 30,
17 title 30, Idaho Code, or pursuant to equivalent laws in the applicable
18 state of incorporation; and
19 (b) It has received an exemption from taxation from the internal rev-
20 enue service pursuant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue
21 Code; and.
22 (c) No proceeds or tax benefits of the organization or from the low-in-
23 come housing property owned by the organization shall inure to any indi-
24 vidual or for-profit entity other than normal employee compensation.
25 (3) In order to qualify for the exemption provided in this section,
26 the low-income housing property shall meet the following qualifications:
27 provided in this subsection.
28 (a) Both legal and equitable title to the property is solely owned by
29 the nonprofit organization seeking the exemption and is managed by the
30 owner or a related nonprofit organization qualifying for the exemption
31 set forth in section 63-602C, Idaho Code; and The property shall be
32 owned and operated by, or shall be a wholly owned subsidiary of, a quali-
33 fied nonprofit organization pursuant to subsection (2) of this section,
34 or shall be owned by a limited partnership, limited liability limited
35 partnership, or limited liability company in which a general partner or
36 a managing member, as applicable, is a qualified nonprofit organization
37 pursuant to subsection (2) of this section or a single-purpose entity
38 that is wholly owned by one (1) or more qualified nonprofit organiza-
39 tions pursuant to subsection (2) of this section.
40 (b) Tenants shall not be evicted based upon on their inability to pay
41 for a period of three (3) months if such inability is due to a cata-
42 strophic event that is not under the tenant's control. For purposes

2
1 of this subsection, "catastrophic event" means a medical condition or
2 injury in which sudden, serious and unexpected symptoms of illness or
3 injury are sufficiently severe to render the tenant unable to partici-
4 pate in employment and such illness or injury has been certified by one
5 (1) or more licensed physicians and/or psychiatrists or psychologists.
6 The term "catastrophic event" does not apply to individuals who volun-
7 tarily remove themselves from the workforce; and.
8 (c) Except for a the manager's unit units, all of the housing units in
9 the low-income housing property are shall be dedicated to low-income
10 housing in the following manner: Fifty-five percent (55%) of the units
11 and shall be rented to those earning an average of sixty percent (60%) or
12 less of the area median gross income for the county in which the housing
13 is located; twenty percent (20%) of the units shall be rented to those
14 earning fifty percent (50%) or less of the median income of the county in
15 which the housing is located; and twenty-five percent (25%) of the units
16 shall be rented to those earning thirty percent (30%) or less of the me-
17 dian income for the county in which the housing is located as determined
18 in a manner consistent with 26 U.S.C. 42(g), and rents on such housing
19 units shall meet the requirements of 26 U.S.C. 42 (g)(2).
20 (d) In each year following a year in which the exemption was allowed un-
21 der this section, the owner of a low-income housing property shall sub-
22 mit to the county assessor of the county in which such property is lo-
23 cated a statement certifying that the low-income housing property is in
24 compliance with the provisions of paragraph (c) of this subsection. If
25 the owner of such low-income housing property fails to submit the state-
26 ment required by this paragraph, the low-income housing property shall
27 not be eligible for the exemption provided in this section for the imme-
28 diately succeeding tax year.
29 (4) An application for the low-income property tax exemption provided
30 for in this section shall be filed no later than April 15 pursuant to section
31 63-602, Idaho Code, and shall include, at a minimum, the following informa-
32 tion:
33 (a) A general description of the low-income housing property project,
34 including but not limited to the number of low-income housing units
35 available and the number of units, if any, offered at market rate;
36 (b) A description of the ownership interest of the applicants and the
37 nonprofit organization partnering with the applicants; and
38 (c) The duration of the affordability period. For the purposes of this
39 section "affordability period" means the period of time in which the
40 housing units in the low-income housing property are required to comply
41 with the restrictions on tenant eligibility and rent levels set forth
42 in a land use restrictive agreement executed with the Idaho housing
43 and finance association for the purpose of qualifying for the federal
44 low-income housing tax credit pursuant to 26 U.S.C. 42.
45 (5)(a) In addition to the application required pursuant to subsection
46 (4) of this section, the owner of a proposed low-income housing property
47 project that has not received all required local land use or zoning ap-
48 provals may request from the county a written preliminary determination
49 as to whether the project, if developed as proposed, will qualify for

3
1 the exemption authorized by this section. Such request may be submitted
2 prior to filing any land use or zoning application.
3 (b) A request for preliminary determination shall include sufficient
4 information to demonstrate compliance with the requirements of this
5 section, including the information described in subsection (4)(a)
6 through (c) of this section.
7 (c) A written preliminary determination issued pursuant to this sub-
8 section shall be binding on the county, provided the project is devel-
9 oped substantially in accordance with the materials submitted with the
10 request. Any material change to the project, other than modifications
11 required or approved through the applicable local land use approval
12 process, shall require a new request for preliminary determination.
13 (d) A preliminary determination issued pursuant to this subsection
14 shall not relieve the owner of the obligation to file a timely appli-
15 cation pursuant to subsection (4) of this section in order to receive
16 the exemption for any tax year after the low-income housing property
17 project is complete.
18 (6)(a) Nothing in this section shall be construed to require a board of
19 county commissioners to grant a property tax exemption pursuant to this
20 section for low-income housing property.
21 (b) A decision of the board of county commissioners to deny an applica-
22 tion for the exemption provided for in this section shall be final and
23 shall not be subject to judicial review.
24 (c) If at any time the use of the property no longer meets the criteria
25 of the exemption or the affordability period on the low-income housing
26 property changes, the board of county commissioners shall remove the
27 property tax exemption provided for in this section.

33 2026.
34 (a) If the project is financed after the effective date of this act and
35 applicable law permits the payment of property taxes with federal or

37 (b) If the property is receiving federal project-based assistance,
38 as provided by 42 U.S.C. sections 1437f(d)(2), 1437f(f)(6) and
39 1437f(o)(13); or
40 (c) To any property used by a taxpayer to qualify for tax credits under
41 the provisions of 26 U.S.C. chapter 42 or any successor programs until

44 credits.
