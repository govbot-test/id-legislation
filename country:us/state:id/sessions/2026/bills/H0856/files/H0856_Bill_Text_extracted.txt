LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 856
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO PROTECTION OF GRAVES; AMENDING SECTION 27-502, IDAHO CODE, TO
3 REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING PROHIBITED ACTS, TO PROHIBIT CERTAIN ACTS,
4 TO PROVIDE EXEMPTIONS, TO PROVIDE A PENALTY, TO PROVIDE FOR SEIZURE OF
5 EVIDENCE AND REMAINS, AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING
6 AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 27-502, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 27-502. PROHIBITED ACTS. (1) Except as provided in section 27-503,
11 Idaho Code, no person shall wilfully willfully remove, mutilate, deface,
12 injure or destroy any cairn or grave. Persons disturbing graves through in-
13 advertence, including by construction, mining, or logging, shall cause the
14 human remains to be reinterred. The expense for such reinterment shall be at
15 least partially borne by the state historical society with the exception of
16 acts prohibited by subsection (2) of this section that are not exempted by
17 subsection (3) of this section.
18 (2) No person or entity shall:
19 (a) Possess, sell, purchase, or lease any artifacts or any human re-
20 mains taken from a cairn, grave, or burial site or human remains taken
21 from a cairn or grave on or after January 1, 1984, in a manner other than
22 that authorized under section 27-503, Idaho Code., or subsection (3) of
23 this section;
24 (b) Publicly display or exhibit any human remains.; or
25 (c) Sell any human artifacts or human remains taken from a cairn or
26 grave.
27 (c) Sell or advertise the sale of any material advertised as real human
28 remains.
29 (3) The provisions of this section do shall not apply to:
30 (a) The possession or sale of artifacts discovered in or taken from lo-
31 cations other than cairns or graves or artifacts that were removed from
32 cairns or graves by other than human action; or
33 (b) (a) Actions taken in the performance of official law enforcement
34 duties., county coroner, or any other governmental duties and actions
35 taken in accordance with the instructions of government officials;
36 (b) Actions taken in the care of a decedent in compliance with Idaho law
37 by a licensed medical practice or care center, mortuary, cemetery, hu-
38 man remains transportation service, first responder service, or medi-
39 cal transportation service;
40 (c) Actions taken by state-licensed medical centers, medical research
41 facilities, or other research centers in good standing with the state

1 of Idaho and federally registered as a tax-exempt organization with the

5 registered in good standing with the state of Idaho and federally regis-

12 (f) Any work in compliance with the revised uniform anatomical gifts

15 (g) Any possession of a decedent by a loved one or good Samaritan await-
16 ing response by those described in paragraphs (a), (b), and (c) of this
17 subsection;
18 (h) Any action deemed exempt by the Idaho attorney general;
19 (i) Any action or human remains specimen as provided in section 27-503,
20 Idaho Code, or the federal native American graves protection and repa-
21 triation act;
22 (j) Any action taken in accordance with or by the representative hold-
23 ing right of disposition of cremated human remains;
24 (k) Possession of cremains, as defined in section 54-1102, Idaho Code;
25 (l) Possession of protected religious relics as provided in section
26 73-402, Idaho Code; and
27 (m) Actions taken by persons to turn in human remains, in good faith, to
28 law enforcement or the county coroner.
29 (4) A violation of the provisions of this section shall be a felony pun-
30 ishable as provided in section 18-112, Idaho Code.
31 (5) Seizure of evidence of a violation:
32 (a) Evidence of a violation of the provisions of this section shall be
33 subject to seizure by persons authorized to enforce the provisions of
34 this section and subject to forensic and DNA testing.
35 (b) Evidence seized pursuant to paragraph (a) of this subsection shall
36 be subject to forfeiture upon the order of a court if the court deter-
37 mines by a preponderance of the evidence that the evidence was used in,
38 or constitutes the fruit of, a violation of this section.
39 (c) Human remains forfeited pursuant to paragraph (b) of this subsec-
40 tion shall be sent to the county coroner at the conclusion of all pro-
41 ceedings related to the violation of the provisions of this section.
42 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
43 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
44 July 1, 2026.
