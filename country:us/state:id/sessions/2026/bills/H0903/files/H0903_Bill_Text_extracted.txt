LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 903
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO PUBLIC ASSISTANCE; AMENDING SECTION 56-275, IDAHO CODE, TO RE-
3 VISE THE DEFINITIONS OF CANDY AND SODA; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND
4 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 56-275, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 56-275. WAIVER TO ENHANCE NUTRITION IN THE SUPPLEMENTAL NUTRITION AS-
9 SISTANCE PROGRAM. (1) For the purposes of this section:
10 (a) "Candy" means a shelf-stable preparation of sugar, honey, or other
11 natural or artificial nutritive or nonnutritive sweeteners in combina-
12 tion with chocolate, fruits, nuts, or other ingredients or flavorings,
13 in the form of bars, drops, or pieces. "Candy" shall not include any
14 preparation containing flour and shall require no requiring refrigera-
15 tion. or any of the following:
16 (i) Baked goods such as cookies, cakes, muffins, and brownies;
17 (ii) Baking ingredients such as sprinkles, chocolate melting
18 wafers, toffee bits, chocolate chips, etc.;
19 (iii) Savory or nonsweet snacks such as chips, pretzels, and
20 crackers;
21 (iv) Fruit snacks made with real fruit, including fruit puree and
22 fruit juice concentrate, such as dried mango, apple chips, and
23 fruit leathers;
24 (v) Trail mix and products that list peanuts or tree nuts as the
25 first ingredient;
26 (vi) Granola bars, breakfast bars, snack bars, protein bars, meal
27 replacement bars, and weight loss bars; or
28 (vii) Items used as ingredients in baking such as sugar, honey, and
29 baking chocolate.
30 (b) "Soda" means any nonalcoholic beverage that contains natural or ar-
31 tificial sweeteners. "Soda" does not include any beverages that:
32 (i) contain Contain milk or milk substitutes,;
33 (ii) that are Are greater than fifty percent (50%) vegetable juice
34 or fruit juice by volume,;
35 (iii) or that require Require preparation before consumption,
36 such as powders or concentrates.;
37 (iv) Are sports drinks used for hydration;
38 (v) Are used as an oral rehydration electrolyte solution formu-
39 lated to prevent or treat dehydration; or
40 (vi) Are manufactured as a source of necessary nutrition or di-
41 etary supplement.

2
1 (2) The director of the department of health and welfare shall request a
2 waiver from the United States department of agriculture to exclude candy and
3 soda from the definition of eligible foods under 7 CFR 271.2. If such waiver
4 is granted, then the director shall prohibit the purchase of candy and soda
5 with benefits provided through the supplemental nutrition assistance pro-
6 gram. If no such waiver is granted, then the director shall request such a
7 waiver annually until such waiver is granted.
8 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
9 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
10 July 1, 2026.
