LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 123
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 A SENATE RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE SENATE AND PROVIDING THAT ALL TEMPORARY AND PEND-
3 ING RULES OF THE IDAHO DEPARTMENT OF INSURANCE, THE DIVISION OF OCCUPA-
4 TIONAL AND PROFESSIONAL LICENSES, AND THE DEPARTMENT OF COMMERCE HAVE
5 BEEN REVIEWED AND APPROVED BY THE SENATE COMMERCE AND HUMAN RESOURCES
6 COMMITTEE, WITH AN EXCEPTION.
7 Be It Resolved by the Senate of the State of Idaho:
8 WHEREAS, pursuant to Section 67-5291, Idaho Code, standing committees
9 of the Legislature shall review any temporary or pending rule that is ger-
10 mane to its committee and has been published in the Idaho Administrative Bul-
11 letin; and
12 WHEREAS, the Senate Commerce and Human Resources Committee reviewed
13 temporary and pending rules adopted by the Idaho Department of Insurance,
14 the Division of Occupational and Professional Licenses, and the Department
15 of Commerce.
16 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Senate, assem-
17 bled in the Second Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature,
18 that all temporary and pending rules adopted by the Idaho Department of
19 Insurance, the Division of Occupational and Professional Licenses, and the
20 Department of Commerce pursuant to the Administrative Procedure Act and
21 submitted through the Office of the Administrative Rules Coordinator to
22 the Legislature for review during the 2026 legislative session have been
23 reviewed and approved by the Senate Commerce and Human Resources Committee
24 with the exception of the following temporary rule that was not heard by the
25 committee and thus not approved: IDAPA 24.39.50, Division of Occupational
26 and Professional Licenses, Rules of the Public Works Contractors License
27 Board, Docket No. 24-3950-2501.
28 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Senate Commerce and Human Resources Com-
29 mittee held that the following pending rule dockets become effective upon
30 sine die of the Second Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legisla-
31 ture:
32 (1) IDAPA 18.04.15, Idaho Department of Insurance, Rules Governing
33 Short-Term Health Insurance Coverage, Docket No. 18-0415-2501; and
34 (2) IDAPA 24.39.50, Division of Occupational and Professional Li-
35 censes, Rules of the Public Works Contractors License Board, Docket No.
36 24-3950-2501.
