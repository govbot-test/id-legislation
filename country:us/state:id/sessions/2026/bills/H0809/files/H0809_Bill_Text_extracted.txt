8 16-1615. SHELTER CARE HEARING. (1) Notwithstanding any other provi-

11 should be released shall be held according to the provisions of this section.
12 (2) Each of the parents or custodian from whom the child was removed

14 the time, place, and purpose of the hearing; and, that such person is enti-
15 tled to be represented by legal counsel. Notice as required by this subsec-
16 tion shall be given at least twenty-four (24) hours before the shelter care

18 (3) Notice of the shelter care hearing shall be given to the parents or
19 custodian from whom the child was removed by personal service and the return
20 of service shall be filed with the court and to any person having joint legal
21 or physical custody of the subject child. Provided, however, that such ser-
22 vice need not be made where the undelivered notice is returned to the court
23 along with an affidavit stating that such parents or custodian could not be
24 located or were out of the state.
25 (4) The shelter care hearing may be continued for a reasonable time upon
26 request by the parent, custodian or counsel for the child.
27 (5) If, upon the completion of the shelter care hearing, it is shown
28 that:
29 (a) A petition has been filed; and
30 (b) There is reasonable cause to believe the child comes within the ju-
31 risdiction of the court under this chapter and either:
32 (i) The department made reasonable efforts to eliminate the need
33 for shelter care but the efforts were unsuccessful; or
34 (ii) The department made reasonable efforts to eliminate the need
35 for shelter care but was not able to safely provide preventive ser-
36 vices; and
37 (c) The child could not be placed in the temporary sole custody of a par-
38 ent having joint legal or physical custody; and
39 (d) It is contrary to the welfare of the child to remain in the home; and
40 (e) It is in the best interests of the child to remain in temporary shel-
41 ter care pending the conclusion of the adjudicatory hearing.

43 ter care order placing the child in the temporary legal custody of the de-
44 partment or other authorized agency. Any evidence may be considered by the
45 court which is of the type which reasonable people may rely upon.
46 (6) Upon finding reasonable cause pursuant to subsection (5)(b) of this
47 section, the court shall order an adjudicatory hearing to be held as soon as
48 possible, but in no event later than thirty (30) days from the date the peti-

5 SECTION 7. That Chapter 16, Title 16, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
6 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
7 ignated as Section 16-1625A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
8 16-1625A. SCHOOL OF ORIGIN HEARINGS. (1) Whenever a child is removed
9 from the child's home by the department pursuant to this chapter, the de-
10 partment shall immediately determine if it is in the best interests of the
11 child to remain in the school of origin. There shall be a presumption that
12 the school of origin is in the child's best interests. Parents have a right
13 to choose the form of education for their children.
14 (a) The department shall provide formal written notice of its deter-
15 mination to the parties, including the child, parent, guardian, and
16 guardian ad litem, no later than three (3) business days after the date
17 on which the determination is made. The written notice shall include
18 the factors that form the basis of the department's decision. Proof of
19 service of such notice shall be filed with the court.
20 (b) Any party may object to the department's determination no later
21 than fourteen (14) days after receipt of such notice by filing the ob-
22 jection with the court.
23 (c) The child shall remain in the school of origin until the time for ob-
24 jection has passed and until any dispute is resolved, except as provided
25 in subsection (5) of this section.
26 (d) The department shall bear the burden of proof that the school place-
27 ment decision is in the child's best interests and, if the child is moved
28 from the school of origin, why the department cannot make reasonable ac-
29 commodations to keep the child in the school of origin.
30 (2) The court, on its own accord or upon a formal objection from a party,
31 shall hold an evidentiary hearing, at which:
32 (a) The parent or guardian or guardian ad litem of the child may present
33 evidence regarding how the child remaining in the school of origin is in
34 the best interests of the child;
35 (b) The child may provide testimony regarding whether the child be-
36 lieves remaining in the school of origin is in the best interests of the
37 child; and
38 (c) The department may present evidence regarding how placement in a
39 school other than the school of origin is in the best interests of the
40 child.
41 (3) At the conclusion of a hearing held pursuant to subsection (2) of
42 this section, the court shall make a best interests determination regarding
43 the school placement of the child and may order that the child remain in the
44 child's school of origin or be moved from the child's school of origin.
45 (4) A hearing requested pursuant to subsection (2) of this section
46 shall be held within fourteen (14) days of the date of such request.
47 (5) The provisions of this section shall not apply to a child who has
48 been found to be without proper education due to a failure to comply with sec-
49 tion 33-202, Idaho Code.
