17 SECTION 1. That Section 33-5502, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
18 amended to read as follows:
19 33-5502. CREATION -- LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS -- GOAL. (1) There is hereby
20 created the Idaho digital learning academy, a public school-choice learn-
21 ing environment which that joins the best technology with the best instruc-
22 tional practices. The Idaho digital learning academy as provided for in this
23 chapter, is not a single department of state government unto itself, nor is
24 it a part of any of the twenty (20) departments of state government autho-
25 rized by section 20, article IV, of the constitution of the state of Idaho,
26 or of the departments prescribed in section 67-2402, Idaho Code. It is leg-
27 islative intent that the Idaho digital learning academy operate and be rec-
28 ognized not as a state or local agency or department, but as a governmental an
29 entity whose creation has been authorized by the state, much in the manner as
30 other single purpose districts.
31 (2) The legislature finds that it is in the best public interest to cre-
32 ate the Idaho digital learning academy based on findings that indicate:
33 (a) Technology continues to impact all facets of life, including the
34 education of students of school age and adult learners the education of
35 students in this state;
36 (b) Systems for delivery of education are as diverse as the learners;
37 and
38 (c) Public school systems Idahoans are seeking high quality educa-
39 tional choices within the public system, and schools and other edu-
40 cation providers are aligning curriculum and assessment with state
41 achievement standards; and.

2
1 (d) The development of a comprehensive digital learning environment is
2 cost prohibitive for individual school districts.
3 (3) The goal of the digital learning academy is to provide choice, ac-
4 cessibility, flexibility, quality, and equity in curricular offerings for
5 students in this state. The digital learning academy shall function as a re-
6 source to fill instructional gaps and is not intended to supplant regularly
7 scheduled courses or full instructional programs offered by a student's lo-
8 cal school district or public charter school. Courses offered shall include
9 credit recovery, dual credit, courses not offered in small or rural school
10 districts, courses required by the state of Idaho for graduation, and over-
11 load courses.
