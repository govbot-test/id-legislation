LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 675
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO BROADBAND; AMENDING SECTION 40-517, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A DEF-
3 INITION AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
4 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 40-517, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 40-517. DEFINITIONS. As used in sections 40-515 through 40-520, Idaho
9 Code:
10 (1) "Broadband" means wide bandwidth communication transmissions
11 allowing capable of providing high-speed services, such as internet access
12 with an ability to simultaneously transport multiple signals and traffic
13 types at a minimum transmission speed of one hundred (100) megabits per sec-
14 ond for downloads and twenty (20) megabits per second for uploads.
15 (2) "Broadband infrastructure" means networks of deployed telecommu-
16 nications equipment, conduit, and technologies necessary to provide broad-
17 band and other advanced telecommunications services to wholesalers or end
18 users, including but not limited to private homes, businesses, commercial
19 establishments, schools, or and public institutions.
20 (3) "Broadband provider" means any entity that:
21 (a) Provides broadband services, including but not limited to a
22 telecommunications provider, cable service provider, broadband
23 provider, cellular provider, political subdivision that provides
24 broadband services, electric cooperative that provides broadband
25 services, electric utility that provides broadband services, state
26 government entity that provides broadband services, tribal government
27 that provides broadband services, or internet service provider; or
28 (b) Builds broadband infrastructure, including but not limited to a
29 port, nonprofit organization, or private-public partnership estab-
30 lished for the purpose of expanding broadband in the state.
31 (4) "Department" means the Idaho transportation department.
32 (5) "Dig Once Policy once policy" means a policy or practice that mini-
33 mizes the number and scale of excavations or construction and costs when in-
34 stalling broadband infrastructure in highway rights-of-way.
35 (6) "Highway" means a road that is part of the state highway system.
36 (7) "Longitudinal access" means access to or use of any part of a right-
37 of-way of a highway that extends generally parallel to the right-of-way for a
38 total of one hundred (100) or more linear feet.
39 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
40 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
41 July 1, 2026.
