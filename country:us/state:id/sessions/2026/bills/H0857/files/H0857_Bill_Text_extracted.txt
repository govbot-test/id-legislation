LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 857
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO CHILD CUSTODY; REPEALING SECTION 32-717A, IDAHO CODE, RELATING
3 TO PARENTS' ACCESS TO RECORDS AND INFORMATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 7, TI-
4 TLE 32, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 32-717A, IDAHO CODE,
5 TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING PARENTS' ACCESS TO RECORDS AND INFOR-
6 MATION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 32-717A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 repealed.
10 SECTION 2. That Chapter 7, Title 32, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
11 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
12 ignated as Section 32-717A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
13 32-717A. PARENTS' ACCESS TO RECORDS AND INFORMATION. (1) Consistent
14 with section 32-1015, Idaho Code, and notwithstanding any other provisions
15 of law, each parent shall have equal access to all records relating to the
16 parent's minor child, including medical, educational, childcare, law en-
17 forcement, and court records, regardless of custodial status. Upon written
18 request, a records custodian shall provide copies within fourteen (14) cal-
19 endar days unless a shorter period is required by law.
20 (2) Access may be restricted only by court order with written findings
21 that the restriction is necessary to prevent substantial harm to the child.
22 Nothing in this section shall require disclosure in violation of federal
23 law.
24 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
25 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
26 July 1, 2026.
