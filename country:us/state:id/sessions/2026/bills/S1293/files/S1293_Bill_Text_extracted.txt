LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1293
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO PUBLIC BENEFITS; AMENDING SECTION 67-7903, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE
3 PROVISIONS REGARDING EXCEPTIONS TO VERIFICATION OF LAWFUL PRESENCE FOR
4 PUBLIC BENEFITS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE
5 DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 67-7903, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 67-7903. VERIFICATION OF LAWFUL PRESENCE -- EXCEPTIONS -- REPORT-
10 ING. (1) Except as otherwise provided in subsection (3) of this section, each
11 agency or political subdivision of this state shall verify the lawful pres-
12 ence in the United States of each natural person eighteen (18) years of age
13 or older who applies for state or local public benefits or for federal public
14 benefits for the applicant.
15 (2) This section shall be enforced without regard to race, religion,
16 gender, ethnicity or national origin.
17 (3) Verification of lawful presence in the United States shall not be
18 required:
19 (a) For obtaining health care items and services that are necessary for
20 the treatment of an emergency medical condition of the person involved
21 and that are not related to an organ transplant procedure;
22 (b) For short-term, noncash, in-kind emergency disaster relief; or
23 (c) For programs, services, or assistance at short-term shelters that:
24 (i) Deliver in-kind services at the community level, including
25 services through public or private nonprofit agencies;
26 (ii) Do not condition the provision of assistance, the amount of
27 assistance provided, or the cost of assistance provided on the in-
28 dividual recipient's income or resources; and
29 (iii) Are necessary for the protection of life or public safety.;
30 or
31 (d) To apply for or receive crime victims compensation pursuant to
32 chapter 10, title 72, Idaho Code.
33 (4) An agency or a political subdivision shall verify the lawful pres-
34 ence in the United States of each applicant eighteen (18) years of age or
35 older for federal public benefits or state or local public benefits by:
36 (a) Employing electronic means to verify an applicant is legally
37 present in the United States; or
38 (b) Requiring the applicant to provide:
39 (i) An Idaho driver's license or an Idaho identification card
40 issued pursuant to section 49-2444, Idaho Code;
41 (ii) A valid driver's license or similar document issued for the
42 purpose of identification by another state or territory of the

2
1 United States, if such license or document contains a photograph
2 of the individual or such other personal identifying information
3 relating to the individual that the director of the department of
4 health and welfare or, with regard to unemployment compensation
5 benefits, the director of the department of labor finds, by rule,
6 sufficient for purposes of this section;
7 (iii) A United States military card or a military dependent's
8 identification card;
9 (iv) A United States coast guard merchant mariner card;
10 (v) A native American tribal document;
11 (vi) A copy of an executive office of immigration review, immi-
12 gration judge or board of immigration appeals decision, granting
13 asylee status;
14 (vii) A copy of an executive office of immigration review, immi-
15 gration judge or board of immigration appeals decision, indicat-
16 ing that the individual may lawfully remain in the United States;
17 (viii) Any United States citizenship and immigration service-is-
18 sued document showing refugee or asylee status or that the indi-
19 vidual may lawfully remain in the United States;
20 (ix) Any department of state or customs and border protection-is-
21 sued document showing the individual has been permitted entry into
22 the United States on the basis of refugee or asylee status or on any
23 other basis that permits the individual to lawfully enter and re-
24 main in the United States; or
25 (x) A valid United States passport; and
26 (c) Requiring the applicant to provide a valid social security number
27 that has been assigned to the applicant; and
28 (d) Requiring the applicant to attest, under penalty of perjury and on
29 a form designated or established by the agency or the political subdivi-
30 sion, that:
31 (i) The applicant is a United States citizen or legal permanent
32 resident; or
33 (ii) The applicant is otherwise lawfully present in the United
34 States pursuant to federal law.
35 (5) Notwithstanding the requirements of subsection (4)(b) of this sec-
36 tion, the agency or political subdivision may establish by appropriate legal
37 procedure such rules or regulations to ensure that certain individuals law-
38 fully present in the United States receive authorized benefits including,
39 but not limited to, homeless state citizens.
40 (6) For an applicant who has attested pursuant to subsection (4)(d) of
41 this section stating that the applicant is an alien lawfully present in the
42 United States, verification of lawful presence for federal public benefits
43 or state or local public benefits shall be made through the federal system-
44 atic alien verification of entitlement program, which may be referred to as
45 the "SAVE" program, operated by the United States department of homeland se-
46 curity or a successor program designated by the United States department of
47 homeland security. Until such verification of lawful presence is made, the
48 attestation may be presumed to be proof of lawful presence for purposes of
49 this section.

1 (a) Errors and significant delays by the SAVE program shall be reported
2 to the United States department of homeland security to ensure that the
3 application of the SAVE program is not wrongfully denying benefits to
4 legal residents of this state.
5 (b) Agencies or political subdivisions may adopt variations of the re-
6 quirements of subsection (4)(d) of this section to improve efficiency
7 or reduce delay in the verification process or to provide for adjudica-
8 tion of unique individual circumstances in which the verification pro-
9 cedures in this section would impose unusual hardship on a legal resi-
10 dent of this state; except that the variations shall be no less strin-
11 gent than the requirements of subsection (4)(d) of this section.
12 (c) A person who knowingly makes a false, fictitious or fraudulent
13 statement or representation in an attestation executed pursuant to
14 subsection (4)(d) or (6)(b) of this section or who knowingly provides
15 a social security number that has not been assigned to him pursuant to
16 subsection (4)(c) of this section shall be:
17 (i) Guilty of a misdemeanor for the first and second offense; and
18 (ii) Guilty of a felony for each subsequent offense.
19 (7) An agency or political subdivision may accept as prima facie evi-
20 dence of an applicant's lawful presence in the United States the information
21 required in subsection (4) of this section, as may be modified by subsection
22 (5) of this section, when issuing a professional license or a commercial li-
23 cense.
24 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
25 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
26 July 1, 2026.
