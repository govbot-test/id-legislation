LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 500
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO MOTOR VEHICLES; AMENDING SECTION 49-615, IDAHO CODE, TO RE-
3 VISE PROVISIONS REGARDING THE EXERCISE OF DUE CARE; AMENDING SECTION
4 49-714, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR ELECTRIC-ASSISTED BICYCLES; AMEND-
5 ING SECTION 49-715, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR HUMAN-POWERED VEHICLES,
6 BICYCLES, AND ELECTRIC-ASSISTED BICYCLES AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL COR-
7 RECTION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Section 49-615, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
10 amended to read as follows:
11 49-615. DRIVERS TO EXERCISE DUE CARE. Notwithstanding other provi-
12 sions of this title or the provisions of any local ordinance, every driver of
13 a motor vehicle shall exercise due care to avoid colliding with any pedes-
14 trian or any person propelling operator or rider of a human-powered vehicle,
15 bicycle, or electric-assisted bicycle and shall give an audible signal when
16 necessary. Every driver of a motor vehicle shall exercise proper precaution
17 upon observing any child or any obviously confused, incapacitated, or intox-
18 icated person.
19 SECTION 2. That Section 49-714, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
20 amended to read as follows:
21 49-714. TRAFFIC LAWS APPLY TO PERSONS ON BICYCLES AND OTHER HUMAN-POW-
22 ERED VEHICLES OPERATORS OR RIDERS OF HUMAN-POWERED VEHICLES, BICYCLES, OR
23 ELECTRIC-ASSISTED BICYCLES -- DUE CARE. (1) Every person operating a vehi-
24 cle propelled by human power or riding a bicycle Every operator or rider of a
25 human-powered vehicle, bicycle, or electric-assisted bicycle shall have all
26 of the rights and all of the duties applicable to the driver of any other ve-
27 hicle under the provisions of chapters 6 and 8 of this title, except as oth-
28 erwise provided in this chapter and except as to those provisions which by
29 their nature can have no application.
30 (2) Every operator or rider of a bicycle or human-powered vehicle
31 human-powered vehicle, bicycle, or electric-assisted bicycle shall exer-
32 cise due care.
33 SECTION 3. That Section 49-715, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
34 amended to read as follows:
35 49-715. RIDING ON BICYCLES HUMAN-POWERED VEHICLES, BICYCLES, OR ELEC-
36 TRIC-ASSISTED BICYCLES. (1) A person propelling a bicycle shall not No oper-
37 ator or rider of a human-powered vehicle, bicycle, or electric-assisted bi-
38 cycle shall ride other than upon on or astride an attached permanent and reg-
39 ular seat.
2
1 (2) No bicycle or human-propelled vehicle human-powered vehicle, bicy-
2 cle, or electric-assisted bicycle shall be used to carry more persons at one
3 (1) time than the number for which it is designed and equipped.
4 (3) An adult rider may carry a child securely attached to his person in a
5 backpack or sling or in a child carrier attached to the bicycle a human-pow-
6 ered vehicle, bicycle, or electric-assisted bicycle.
7 SECTION 4. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
8 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
9 July 1, 2026.
