6 SECTION 1. That Section 49-106, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 49-106. DEFINITIONS -- E.
9 (1)(a) "Electric-assisted bicycle" means a bicycle equipped with fully
10 operable pedals and an electric motor of less than seven hundred fifty
11 (750) watts and that meets one (1) of the following requirements:
12 (a) (i) "Class 1 electric-assisted bicycle" means an electric-
13 assisted bicycle equipped with a motor that provides assistance
14 only when the rider is pedaling and ceases when the rider stops
15 pedaling or when the bicycle reaches the speed of twenty (20) miles
16 per hour.
17 (b) (ii) "Class 2 electric-assisted bicycle" means an elec-
18 tric-assisted bicycle equipped with a motor that may be used
19 exclusively to propel the bicycle and that is not capable of pro-
20 viding assistance when the bicycle reaches the speed of twenty
21 (20) miles per hour.
22 (c) (iii) "Class 3 electric-assisted bicycle" means an elec-
23 tric-assisted bicycle equipped with a motor that provides as-
24 sistance only when the rider is pedaling and ceases when the
25 rider stops pedaling or when the bicycle reaches the speed of
26 twenty-eight (28) miles per hour.
27 (b)(i) "Electric-assisted bicycle" shall not include a vehicle,
28 which may have the general appearance of a bicycle, on which any
29 person may ride, that has two (2) tandem wheels, has an electric
30 motor, and is not an E-scooter or similar device, if it meets one
31 (1) or more of the following criteria:
32 1. It is equipped with an electric motor of seven hundred
33 fifty (750) watts or more;
34 2. It is equipped with an electric motor that does not cease
35 to provide assistance when the vehicle reaches speeds above
36 thirty (30) miles per hour; or
37 3. It is not equipped with operable pedals that can propel
38 the device.
39 (ii) Electric vehicles described in paragraph (b)(i) of this sub-
40 section shall not be included in any reference to an electric-as-
41 sisted bicycle within Idaho law, rules, or regulations and shall

4 ing two (2) nontandem wheeled device designed to transport only one (1) per-
5 son, with an electric propulsion system that limits the maximum speed of the

26 feet from a stationary motor or engine.
27 (9) "Excessive speed" means any speed of fifteen (15) miles per hour or
28 more above the posted speed limit, and is only for purposes of determining
29 disqualification of commercial driving privileges.
30 (10) "Executive head," as used in chapter 20, title 49, Idaho Code,
31 means the governor of the state of Idaho.
