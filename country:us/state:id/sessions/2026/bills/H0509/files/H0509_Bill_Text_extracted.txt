LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 509
BY TRANSPORTATION AND DEFENSE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE IDAHO TRANSPORTATION DEPARTMENT; AMENDING SECTION 40-511,
3 IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE THAT CERTAIN VEHICLES SHALL NOT BE SUBJECT TO
4 MORE THAN ONE INSPECTION WITHIN A SEVENTY-TWO-HOUR PERIOD; AND DECLAR-
5 ING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 40-511, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 40-511. STOPPING AND INSPECTION. (1) Wherever by the laws of the state
10 of Idaho any vehicle with a maximum gross weight or registered gross weight,
11 or operated at a gross weight, of twenty-six thousand one (26,001) pounds
12 or more, excepting those transporting placardable quantities of hazardous
13 materials, is used to transport any merchandise, product, commodity, or
14 livestock within the state, within the state to without the state, or from
15 without the state to within the state, the owner or operator of either the
16 motor vehicle or trailer, as defined in chapter 1, title 49, Idaho Code, used
17 to transport such merchandise, product, commodity, or livestock is hereby
18 required to stop at such ports of entry or checking stations established
19 by the Idaho transportation department and submit to inspection, grading
20 or weighing for compliance with the laws of the state of Idaho. A vehicle
21 that is required to stop for inspection pursuant to this section shall not
22 be subject to another inspection pursuant to this section for seventy-two
23 (72) hours after an initial inspection pursuant to this section. The de-
24 partment shall implement technology and procedures to allow vehicles that
25 have been inspected pursuant to this section to lawfully bypass additional
26 inspections pursuant to this section for seventy-two (72) hours following
27 an initial inspection pursuant to this section. Noncommercial vehicles, as
28 defined in section 49-123(2)(k), Idaho Code, shall not be required to stop
29 for inspection pursuant to this section.
30 (2) Vehicles or combinations of vehicles with a maximum gross weight of
31 ten thousand (10,000) pounds or more transporting placardable quantities of
32 hazardous materials are required to stop at all ports of entry or checking
33 stations established by the Idaho transportation department.
34 (3) It shall be the duty of such owner or operator of every motor vehicle
35 or trailer to drive the motor vehicle or trailer upon any state-owned inspec-
36 tion station, stationary or portable scale, or private scale, certified by
37 the state of Idaho, when requested to do so by any peace officer, excepting
38 fish and game officers, or authorized employees of the Idaho transportation
39 department.
40 (4) Authorized employees of the transportation department may stop any
41 vehicle with a maximum gross weight of eighteen thousand (18,000) pounds or
42 more by displaying a flashing red light if the authorized employee has prob-

2
1 able cause to believe the vehicle bypassed a weighing or inspection station
2 or proceeded through the station without regard for the directional signals.
3 Authorized employees may direct a vehicle that has bypassed a weighing or in-
4 spection station, or has proceeded through the station without regard for
5 the directional signals, to return to the bypassed inspection or weighing
6 station and may issue a citation for failure to stop as required in this sec-
7 tion. The operator of a vehicle shall bring the vehicle to a stop, pulling
8 off the traveled portion of the highway, when directed to do so by an autho-
9 rized employee of the transportation department by use of emergency lights
10 or siren.
11 (5) The department is authorized to implement technology and proce-
12 dures, where practicable, to allow vehicles that meet established criteria
13 to lawfully bypass a port of entry. The department may promulgate rules to
14 implement the provisions of this section.
15 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
16 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
17 July 1, 2026.
