LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1420
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO MEDICAL EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTION 33-3731, IDAHO CODE, TO PRO-
3 VIDE FOR THE MEDICAL EDUCATION FUND AND TO PROVIDE FOR A SOURCE OF FUND-
4 ING; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 33-3731, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 33-3731. CONTRACT REQUIREMENTS FOR STATE-SUPPORTED MEDICAL STUDENTS
9 -- MEDICAL EDUCATION REIMBURSEMENT FUND. (1) An individual who qualifies as
10 an Idaho resident pursuant to section 33-3717B, Idaho Code, and who is ac-
11 cepted into a medical education program enrolling Idaho students supported
12 by Idaho state funds in slots reserved for Idaho students shall, before
13 confirming enrollment in such program or school, enter into a contract with
14 the state board of education or the board's designee, which contract shall
15 specify that the individual will commit to entering active full-time profes-
16 sional practice in Idaho for a period of four (4) years within one (1) year
17 of:
18 (a) Obtaining a license to practice medicine;
19 (b) Finishing a residency or subspecialty residency; or
20 (c) Finishing a medical education fellowship as defined by the state
21 board of education or the board's designee.
22 (2) Each year of a residency served in Idaho, up to four (4) years, will
23 be credited as one-half (1/2) year of the practice requirement provided in
24 subsection (1) of this section. After the fourth year, no credit shall be
25 given for any additional residency years.
26 (3) An individual who fails to abide by the contract described in sub-
27 section (1) of this section shall reimburse the state for the state's finan-
28 cial obligation in supporting the individual's medical education, as deter-
29 mined by the state board of education or the board's designee. Reimbursement
30 must:
31 (a) Begin within one (1) year of:
32 (i) Obtaining a license to practice medicine;
33 (ii) Finishing a residency or subspecialty residency; or
34 (iii) Finishing a medical education fellowship as defined by the
35 state board of education or the board's designee;
36 (b) Be paid according to an amortized schedule established by the state
37 board of education or the board's designee; and
38 (c) Be completed within eight (8) years of the date the reimbursement
39 requirement began.
40 (4) The reimbursement obligation described in subsection (3) of this
41 section:
42 (a) Shall not accrue any interest; and

2
1 (b) May, by act of the state board of education or the board's designee,
2 be:
3 (i) Suspended if reimbursement is temporarily impossible or
4 would create extreme hardship for a temporary period, including
5 but not limited to suspension for medical reasons, personal rea-
6 sons, parental leave, or call to active duty in the armed forces;
7 or
8 (ii) Waived if:
9 1. Reimbursement is permanently impossible or would create
10 extreme hardship, including but not limited to death, in-
11 ability to complete the program, or inability to obtain a li-
12 cense to practice medicine; or
13 2. The individual owing reimbursement is participating in
14 a program of the federal government or United States armed
15 forces that has a service requirement.
16 (5) The provisions of this section shall apply to individuals matric-
17 ulating in a medical education program enrolling Idaho students supported
18 by Idaho state funds in slots reserved for Idaho students in fall 2023 and
19 thereafter.
20 (6) There is hereby established in the state treasury a medical educa-
21 tion reimbursement fund, which shall be administered by the state board of
22 education or the board's designee. Moneys in the fund shall:
23 (a) Consist of:
24 (i) Reimbursement paid pursuant to subsection (3) of this sec-
25 tion;
26 (ii) On and after July 1, 2028, one percent (1%) of the gross re-
27 ceipts by the state of the tax on health insurance premiums, as
28 provided by section 41-402, Idaho Code, hereby perpetually appro-
29 priated to the medical education fund;
30 (ii) (iii) Legislative appropriations;
31 (iii) (iv) Donations; and
32 (iv) (v) Interest earned on idle moneys in the fund; and
33 (b) Be used for, subject to availability:
34 (i) The costs of administering and enforcing the provisions of
35 this section; and
36 (ii) Incentive grants in an amount and according to a program
37 established under state board of education policy. Such grants
38 shall be awarded to licensed physicians who practice medicine in
39 Idaho and who meet other criteria provided in board policy.
40 (7) The state board of education is authorized to take such actions as
41 are necessary to effectuate the provisions of this section.
42 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
43 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
44 July 1, 2026.
