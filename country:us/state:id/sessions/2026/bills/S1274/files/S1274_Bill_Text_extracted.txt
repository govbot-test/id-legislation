9 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
10 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
11 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
12 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
13 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
14 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.

14 SECTION 3. That Section 59-1607, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
15 amended to read as follows:
16 59-1607. HOURS OF WORK AND OVERTIME. (1) It is the policy of the legis-
17 lature of the state of Idaho that all classified and nonclassified officers
18 and employees of the executive branch of state government shall be treated
19 substantially similar with reference to hours of employment, holidays and
20 vacation leave, except as provided in this chapter. For wage, hour and
21 working conditions, the supreme court and the legislative council shall
22 prescribe rules for employees of the judicial branch and the legislative
23 branch, respectively. The policy of this state shall not restrict the ex-
24 tension of regular work hour schedules on an overtime basis, which shall be
25 the same for classified and nonclassified employees, in those activities and
26 duties where such extension is necessary and authorized by the appointing
27 authority.
28 (2) The appointing authority of any department shall determine the ne-
29 cessity for overtime work and shall provide for cash compensation or compen-
30 satory time off for such overtime work for eligible classified and nonclas-
31 sified officers and employees.
32 (3) Classified and nonclassified officers and employees who fall
33 within one (1) or more of the following categories are ineligible for cash
34 compensation or compensatory time for overtime work:
35 (a) Elected officials; or
36 (b) Those included in the definition of section 67-5303(j), Idaho Code.
37 (4) Classified and nonclassified employees who are designated as exec-
38 utive, as provided in section 67-5302, Idaho Code, and who are not included
39 in the definition of subsection (3) of this section, shall be ineligible for
40 compensatory time or cash compensation for overtime work. Such salaried
41 employees shall report absences in excess of one-half (1/2) day. Employees
42 who become executives within their current agency as set forth in section
43 67-5302(12), Idaho Code, shall have twelve (12) months from the date of
44 this act or of appointment, whichever is later, to use any compensatory time
45 balance. After twelve (12) months, any remaining compensatory time will be
46 forfeited. Compensatory time is not transferable and shall be forfeited at
47 the time of transfer to another appointing authority or upon separation from
48 state service.

33 state service shall be liquidated at the time of such transfer or separation
34 by payment in cash.

38 SECTION 4. That Section 67-5315, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
39 amended to read as follows:
40 67-5315. ESTABLISHMENT AND ADOPTION OF EMPLOYEE PROBLEM SOLVING AND
41 DUE PROCESS PROCEDURES. (1) Each participating department shall, on or be-
42 fore July 1, 1999, establish and adopt an employee problem solving proce-
43 dure within such department, which shall be reduced to writing and shall be
44 in full compliance with the provisions of the uniform problem solving pro-
45 cedure as adopted by rule by the administrator pursuant to subsection (4) of
46 this section. The department problem solving procedure shall be approved by
47 the administrator of the division of human resources prior to implementation
48 or amendment. A copy of the approved problem solving procedure plan shall
49 be furnished and explained to each employee of the department concerned. No

4
1 employee shall be disciplined or otherwise prejudiced in his employment for
2 exercising his rights under the plan, and department heads shall encourage
3 the use of the plan in the resolution of grievances arising in the course of
4 public employment. A classified employee may file under the problem solving
5 procedure any matter, except that compensation shall not be deemed a proper
6 subject for consideration under the problem solving procedure except as it
7 applies to alleged inequities within a particular agency or department, and
8 except for termination during the entrance probationary period, and except
9 for those matters set forth in subsection (2) of this section.
10 (2) No action of a participating department relating to a disciplinary
11 dismissal, suspension or demotion, or an involuntary transfer of a classi-
12 fied state employee shall be effective until the affected employee shall
13 have received notice and an opportunity to be heard. The classified employee
14 may then appeal to the Idaho personnel commission those disciplinary matters
15 set forth in section 67-5316(1)(a), Idaho Code.
16 (3) If the filing concerns a matter which is reviewable pursuant to
17 section 67-5316, Idaho Code, the time for appeal to the commission shall not
18 commence to run until the employee has completed the problem solving proce-
19 dure provided by the department in accordance with the terms thereof or, in
20 the case of disciplinary actions set forth in subsection (2) of this section,
21 until the disciplinary action becomes effective; provided, however, the
22 failure of an employee to pursue the problem solving procedures established
23 within the department shall constitute a waiver of the employee's right of
24 review by the commission.
25 (4) The division of human resources shall adopt a rule defining uni-
26 form problem solving and due process procedures for use by all participating
27 departments with classified employees. With respect to the problem solv-
28 ing procedure, the rule shall provide a complete procedure for all stages of
29 the process, including problem solving meetings with department represen-
30 tatives in the employee's chain of command. With respect to the due process
31 procedure, the rule shall provide that the employee receive notice and an op-
32 portunity to be heard before the department decides in favor of disciplinary
33 action. The rule shall also provide for time periods for each step of the
34 procedures. The rule shall provide for the use of an impartial mediator upon
35 agreement between the agency and the employee. The employee shall be enti-
36 tled to be represented by a person of the employee's own choosing at each step
37 of the procedures, except the initial informal discussion with the immediate
38 supervisor prior to filing under the problem solving procedure.
