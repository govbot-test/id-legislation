7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. The Legislature finds
9 that providing oversight of Medicaid rates paid to residential habilitation
10 providers and increasing transparency for taxpayers is one of the most im-
11 portant functions the Legislature provides. Currently, the state pays more
12 than $176.5 million from the general fund through Medicaid to help people
13 with disabilities live independently with residential habilitation and
14 other home and community-based services. In 2022, the Legislature approved
15 a line item for $70,393,100 to increase provider rates for adult develop-
16 mental disabilities in residential habilitation settings in Chapter 252,
17 Laws of 2022. Those provider rate increases were related to a line item of
18 $66,663,200 providing service enhancements and a new budget tool stemming
19 from the KW v. Armstrong lawsuit. A new court order halted the implemen-
20 tation of that budget tool, and as a result, the service enhancements have
21 not gone into effect. The state general fund match for the rate increases
22 related to the service enhancement is approximately $21,800,000 and can be
23 reduced from the current budget. To support accountability and transparency
24 for taxpayers and improve legislative oversight of provider payments, this
25 statute change is necessary.
26 SECTION 2. That Section 56-265, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
27 amended to read as follows:
28 56-265. PROVIDER PAYMENT. (1) Where there is an equivalent, the pay-
29 ment to medicaid providers:
30 (a) May be up to but shall not exceed one hundred percent (100%) of the
31 current medicare rate for primary care procedure codes as defined by the

33 (b) Shall be ninety percent (90%) of the current medicare rate for all
34 other procedure codes.

39 velopmental disability agency services, community-supported employment,
