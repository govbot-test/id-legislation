LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 24
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE, HONORING BATTALION CHIEFS FRANK HAR-
3 WOOD AND JOHN MORRISON JR., FALLEN FIREFIGHTERS OF KOOTENAI COUNTY FIRE
4 AND RESCUE AND THE COEUR D'ALENE FIRE DEPARTMENT, FOR THEIR HEROIC SER-
5 VICE AND ULTIMATE SACRIFICE IN THE LINE OF DUTY, HONORING CRITICALLY IN-
6 JURED ENGINEER DAVID TYSDAL OF THE COEUR D'ALENE FIRE DEPARTMENT, AND
7 EXPRESSING SUPPORT FOR ALL FIRST RESPONDERS IN IDAHO.
8 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 WHEREAS, on June 29, 2025, Battalion Chief Frank Harwood of Kootenai
10 County Fire and Rescue (Local 2856) and Battalion Chief John Morrison Jr. of
11 the Coeur d'Alene Fire Department (Local 710) responded to a reported brush
12 fire on Canfield Mountain near Coeur d'Alene, Idaho, only to be ambushed and
13 tragically killed in the line of duty by a premeditated attack; and
14 WHEREAS, a third firefighter, Engineer David Tysdal of the Coeur
15 d'Alene Fire Department, was critically wounded in the same incident but
16 demonstrated remarkable resilience in his recovery; and
17 WHEREAS, Battalion Chief Harwood dedicated years of selfless service to
18 protecting the lives and property of Idahoans, exemplifying courage, lead-
19 ership, and commitment to his community; and
20 WHEREAS, Battalion Chief Morrison served with distinction, embodying
21 the highest ideals of the fire service through his bravery, expertise, and
22 unwavering dedication to public safety; and
23 WHEREAS, these fallen heroes made the ultimate sacrifice while protect-
24 ing their fellow citizens, highlighting the daily risks faced by firefight-
25 ers, law enforcement officers, emergency medical personnel, and other first
26 responders across Idaho; and
27 WHEREAS, the families of fallen first responders endure profound loss
28 and hardship, yet their strength and resilience inspire gratitude and sup-
29 port from the people of Idaho; and
30 WHEREAS, it is fitting and proper for the State of Idaho to recognize and
31 honor the sacrifices of all first responders who risk their lives to safe-
32 guard our communities and to affirm that their contributions and difficul-
33 ties should not be forgotten.
34 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
35 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives
36 and the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislature honors the memory
37 of Battalion Chiefs Frank Harwood and John Morrison Jr. for their heroic
38 actions and ultimate sacrifice in the line of duty and extends its deepest
39 condolences to their families, colleagues, and the communities they served.
40 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature recognizes the bravery of
41 Engineer David Tysdal of the Coeur d'Alene Fire Department and extends its
42 support and gratitude to him, his family, his colleagues, and the community
43 that he has served.

2
1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature recognizes the bravery of
2 all first responders and commits to supporting measures that enhance their
3 safety.
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that an enrolled copy of this resolution be pre-
5 sented to the families of Battalion Chiefs Frank Harwood and John Morrison
6 Jr. and Engineer David Tysdal, the International Association of Fire Fight-
7 ers, Kootenai County Fire and Rescue, and the Coeur d'Alene Fire Department
8 as an expression of our gratitude and respect.
