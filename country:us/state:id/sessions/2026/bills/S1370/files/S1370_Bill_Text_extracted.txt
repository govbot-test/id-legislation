LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1370
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO ABORTION TRAFFICKING; AMENDING SECTION 18-623, IDAHO CODE, TO
3 REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING ABORTION TRAFFICKING; AND DECLARING AN
4 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 18-623, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 18-623. ABORTION TRAFFICKING. (1) An adult who, with the intent to
9 conceal an abortion from the parents or guardian of a pregnant, unemanci-
10 pated minor, either procures an abortion, as described in section 18-604,
11 Idaho Code, or obtains an abortion-inducing drug for the pregnant minor to
12 use for an abortion by recruiting, harboring, or transporting the pregnant
13 minor within this state commits the crime of abortion trafficking. As used
14 in this subsection, the terms "procure" and "obtain" shall not include the
15 providing of information regarding a health benefit plan.
16 (2) It shall be an affirmative defense to a prosecution under subsec-
17 tion (1) of this section that a parent or guardian of the pregnant minor con-
18 sented to trafficking of the minor.
19 (3) It shall not be an affirmative defense to a prosecution under sub-
20 section (1) of this section that the abortion provider or the abortion-in-
21 ducing drug provider is located in another state.
22 (4) The Idaho attorney general has the authority, at the attorney gen-
23 eral's sole discretion, to prosecute a person for a criminal violation of
24 this section if the prosecuting attorney authorized to prosecute criminal
25 violations of this section refuses to prosecute violations of any of the pro-
26 visions of this section by any person without regard to the facts or circum-
27 stances.
28 (5) Any person who commits the crime of abortion trafficking, as pro-
29 vided in subsection (1) of this section, shall be punished by imprisonment
30 in the state prison for no less than two (2) years and no more than five (5)
31 years.
32 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
33 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
34 July 1, 2026.
