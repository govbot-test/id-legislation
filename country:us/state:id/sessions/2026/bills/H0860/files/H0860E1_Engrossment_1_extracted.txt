17 SECTION 1. That Section 32-1010, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
18 amended to read as follows:
19 32-1010. IDAHO PARENTAL RIGHTS ACT. (1) This section through section
20 32-1014 32-1015, Idaho Code, shall be known and may be cited as the "Idaho
21 Parental Rights Act."
22 (2) The interests and role of parents in the care, custody and control
23 of their children are both implicit in the concept of ordered liberty and
24 deeply rooted in our nation's history and tradition. They are also among the
25 unalienable rights retained by the people under the ninth amendment to the
26 constitution of the United States.
27 (3) The interests of the parents include the high duty and right to nur-
28 ture and direct their children's destiny, including their upbringing and ed-
29 ucation.
30 (4) The state of Idaho has independent authority to protect its par-
31 ents' fundamental right to nurture and direct their children's destiny, up-
32 bringing and education.
33 (5) The protections and rights recognized in sections 32-1011 through
34 32-1014 32-1015, Idaho Code, are rooted in the due process of law guaranteed
35 pursuant to section 13, article I, of the constitution of the state of Idaho.
36 (6) Governmental efforts that restrict or interfere with these funda-
37 mental rights are only permitted if that restriction or interference satis-
38 fies the strict scrutiny standard provided in section 32-1013, Idaho Code.
39 (7) Nothing in this act shall be construed as altering the established
40 presumption in favor of the constitutionality of statutes and regulations.
41 (8) The provisions of the Idaho parental rights act are hereby declared
42 to be severable, and if any provision of the act or the application of such

2
1 provision to any person or circumstance is declared invalid for any reason,
2 such declaration shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of
3 the act.
4 SECTION 2. That Section 32-1013, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
5 amended to read as follows:
6 32-1013. INTERFERENCE WITH FUNDAMENTAL PARENTAL RIGHTS RE-
7 STRICTED. (1) Neither the state of Idaho, nor any political subdivision
8 thereof, may violate a parent's fundamental and established rights pro-
9 tected by this act, and any restriction of or interference with such rights
10 shall not be upheld unless it demonstrates by clear and convincing evidence
11 that the restriction or interference is both:
12 (a) Essential to further a compelling governmental interest; and
13 (b) The least restrictive means available for the furthering of that
14 compelling governmental interest.
15 (2) The foregoing principles apply to any interference whether now ex-
16 isting or hereafter enacted.
17 (3) Nothing in this act shall be construed as invalidating the provi-
18 sions of the child protective act in chapter 16, title 16, Idaho Code, or
19 modify modifying the burden of proof at any stage of proceedings under the
20 child protective act.
21 (4) When a parent's fundamental rights protected by this act are vio-
22 lated, a parent may assert that violation as a claim or defense in a judicial
23 proceeding and may obtain appropriate relief against the governmental en-
24 tity.
25 (5) If a parent prevails in a civil action against the state, or a polit-
26 ical subdivision thereof, as provided in subsection (4) of this section, the
27 parent is entitled to reasonable attorney's fees and costs.
28 (6) All civil actions brought pursuant to sections 32-1010 through
29 32-1015, Idaho Code, shall be initiated within two (2) years after the harm
30 occurred or two (2) years after the discovery, by the parent, of the facts
31 constituting the claim, whichever is later.

6 SECTION 8. That Section 66-320, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 66-320. RIGHT TO RELEASE ON APPLICATION -- EXCEPTIONS. (1) A volun-
9 tary patient admitted in accordance with the procedure outlined in section
10 66-318, Idaho Code, who requests his release or whose release is requested,
11 in writing, by his legal guardian, parent, spouse, or adult next of kin shall
12 be released except that:
13 (a) If the patient was admitted on his own application and the request
14 for release is made by a person other than the patient, release may be
15 conditioned upon the agreement of the patient thereto;
16 (b) If the patient, by reason of his age, was admitted on the applica-
17 tion of another person, his release prior to becoming sixteen (16) years
18 of age may be conditioned upon the consent of his parent or guardian; or
19 (b) Any patient under eighteen (18) years of age must be released with
20 the consent of the patient's parent or guardian; or
21 (c) If the director of the facility determines that the patient should
22 be hospitalized under the provisions of this chapter, the patient may be
23 detained up to three (3) days, excluding Saturdays, Sundays and legal
24 holidays, for the purpose of examination by a designated examiner and
25 the filing of an application for continued care and treatment.
26 (2) Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, judicial pro-
27 ceedings authorized by this chapter shall not be commenced with respect to
28 a voluntary patient unless release of the patient has been requested by him-
29 self or the individual who applied for his admission.
30 (3) The date and time of any request for release under this section
31 shall be entered in the patient's clinical record. If the request for
32 release is denied, the reasons for denial also shall be entered in the pa-
33 tient's clinical record.
34 (4) A patient admitted for examination pursuant to section 18-211,
35 Idaho Code, may not be released except for purposes of transportation back to
36 the court ordering, or party authorizing, the examination.
