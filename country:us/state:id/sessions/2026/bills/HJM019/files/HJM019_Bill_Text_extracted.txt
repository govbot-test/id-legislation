LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 19
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CON-
3 GRESS ASSEMBLED AND TO THE CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE
4 STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES.
5 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
6 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
7 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
8 WHEREAS, the Tenth Amendment to the United States Constitution reserves
9 to the states powers not delegated to the federal government, and education
10 is not enumerated as a responsibility of the federal government; and
11 WHEREAS, the U.S. Department of Education's creation in 1980 has led to
12 significant federal overreach in education through burdensome regulations
13 and one-size-fits-all mandates, many left unfunded despite the department's
14 annual budget of $60 billion; and
15 WHEREAS, as the least regulated state in the nation, Idaho would welcome
16 the opportunity to extend its red tape reduction efforts to the field of pub-
17 lic education where it is desperately needed; and
18 WHEREAS, states and local communities possess unique knowledge of their
19 students' educational needs and are best positioned to develop the curricu-
20 lum and policies that reflect their values and priorities; and
21 WHEREAS, President Trump has publicly expressed a desire to eliminate
22 the U.S. Department of Education and rightfully put the states back in charge
23 of education; and
24 WHEREAS, the elimination of the U.S. Department of Education is not only
25 a constitutional necessity, but will also cause taxpayer dollars to be spent
26 more efficiently and lead to better student outcomes.
27 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
28 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives
29 and the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislature supports federal
30 efforts to eliminate the U.S. Department of Education and urges the United
31 States Congress to fully cooperate with these efforts.
32 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Representa-
33 tives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this
34 Memorial to the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Repre-
35 sentatives of Congress and to the congressional delegation representing the
36 State of Idaho in the Congress of the United States.
