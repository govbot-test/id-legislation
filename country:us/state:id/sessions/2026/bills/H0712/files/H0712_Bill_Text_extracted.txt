LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 712
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 5, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDI-
3 TION OF A NEW SECTION 33-528, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR THE IDAHO STATE
4 SEAL OF EXCELLENCE IN CIVICS PROGRAM; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND
5 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Chapter 5, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
8 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
9 ignated as Section 33-528, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 33-528. IDAHO STATE SEAL OF EXCELLENCE IN CIVICS PROGRAM. (1) The
11 legislature finds that a strong constitutional republic relies on citizens
12 who possess civic knowledge, civic skills, civic values, and a commitment to
13 constructive civic participation. It is the intent of the legislature to es-
14 tablish a voluntary diploma endorsement program recognizing Idaho students
15 who have achieved excellence in civics education and civic engagement.
16 (2) There is hereby established the Idaho state seal of excellence in
17 civics to recognize graduating high school students who have attained a high
18 level of civics proficiency and civic engagement.
19 (3) Participation in the Idaho state seal of excellence in civics pro-
20 gram is voluntary for school districts and public charter schools. A school
21 district or public charter school shall notify the superintendent of public
22 instruction of its intent to participate. Each participating school dis-
23 trict or public charter school shall:
24 (a) Maintain records of students who meet program requirements;
25 (b) Identify each eligible student prior to graduation;
26 (c) Affix the state seal to each eligible student's diploma and note the
27 award on the student's transcript; and
28 (d) Allow eligible students to wear a red, white, and blue honor cord
29 during graduation ceremonies to recognize the achievement.
30 (4) To earn the Idaho state seal of excellence in civics, a student
31 shall meet the following requirements:
32 (a) Demonstrate civic knowledge by:
33 (i) Successful completion of all state-required social studies
34 coursework for graduation with an overall GPA of 3.25 or higher in
35 such courses;
36 (ii) Demonstrating mastery, which shall be ninety percent (90%)
37 or higher, on the Idaho civics assessment required by section
38 33-1602, Idaho Code; and
39 (iii) Completion of a civics inquiry or research project, which
40 may include:
41 1. A civics readiness research project;
42 2. A capstone paper or presentation;

2
1 3. An advanced placement, international baccalaureate, or
2 dual credit civics or government project; or
3 4. A standards-aligned civics inquiry investigation;
4 (b) Demonstrate civic participation by completing at least three (3) of
5 the following:
6 (i) Service learning or community service, requiring completion
7 of at least fifty (50) hours of voluntary service with a public
8 agency, school, nonprofit organization, or charitable organiza-
9 tion that contributes to the public good. Students who enlist in
10 the armed forces of the United States prior to graduation shall be
11 deemed to have met this requirement;
12 (ii) Civic engagement activities, requiring participation in at
13 least fifteen (15) hours of civic engagement activities, such as
14 public meetings, mock trial, model united nations, American le-
15 gion boys state or American legion auxiliary girls state, youth
16 legislature, local government internship, or student government;
17 (iii) Completion of at least two (2) approved civic learning pro-
18 grams, including:
19 1. Junior reserve officers' training corps, with a minimum
20 two (2) year participation;
21 2. Summer civics institutes;
22 3. School-sponsored extracurricular civics programs; or
23 4. Projects aligned to Idaho and United States government
24 standards; or
25 (iv) Civics-related work experience, including internships,
26 volunteering, and other work-based learning opportunities; and
27 (c) Demonstrate good attendance and not incur any major disciplinary
28 infractions, as defined by local school board policy.
29 (5) The state seal shall be affixed to the diploma of a student who sat-
30 isfies the requirements of this section and shall be noted on the student's
31 official transcript. The state superintendent of public instruction shall
32 design and produce the insignia and deliver appropriate quantities to par-
33 ticipating school districts and public charter schools at no cost to stu-
34 dents.
35 (6) The state department of education shall provide participating
36 school districts and public charter schools with implementation guidance
37 surrounding participation in the state seal of excellence in civics program,
38 including:
39 (a) Rubrics, examples of eligible activities, and reporting templates;
40 (b) Professional development to support instruction aligned to civics
41 proficiency; and
42 (c) Annually, a list of approved civic learning programs. Schools may
43 design local projects, assessments, or activities that align with state
44 civics standards.
45 (7) Participating school districts and public charter schools shall
46 report to the state department of education by August 1 each year:
47 (a) The number of students awarded the Idaho state seal of excellence in
48 civics; and
49 (b) Aggregate data on student participation in civic learning path-
50 ways.
