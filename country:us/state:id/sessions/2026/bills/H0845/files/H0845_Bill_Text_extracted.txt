Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 845
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO MEDICAID; AMENDING CHAPTER 2, TITLE 56, IDAHO CODE, BY THE AD-
3 DITION OF A NEW SECTION 56-276, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS RE-
4 GARDING MEDICAID PRESUMPTIVE ELIGIBILITY DETERMINATIONS BY QUALIFIED
5 HOSPITALS, TO PROVIDE PERFORMANCE STANDARDS, AND TO PROVIDE FOR VIOLA-
6 TIONS, NOTICE, AND RIGHTS OF APPEAL; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PRO-
7 VIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Chapter 2, Title 56, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
10 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
11 ignated as Section 56-276, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 56-276. MEDICAID PRESUMPTIVE ELIGIBILITY -- QUALIFIED HOSPITAL PER-
13 FORMANCE STANDARDS -- VIOLATIONS. (1) The director shall limit presumptive
14 eligibility determination to qualified hospitals.
15 (2) A hospital making a presumptive eligibility determination shall:
16 (a) Notify the department of each presumptive eligibility determina-
17 tion no later than five (5) business days after the date of the determi-
18 nation;
19 (b) Assist individuals who the hospital determines are presumptively
20 eligible with completing and submitting a full medicaid application;
21 (c) Notify the applicant in writing, on all relevant forms, with plain
22 language and in large print, that:
23 (i) The applicant's presumptive eligibility will end on the last
24 day of the following month if the applicant does not file a full
25 medicaid application with the department before the last day of
26 the following month; and
27 (ii) The applicant's presumptive eligibility will continue until
28 an eligibility determination is made by the department concerning
29 the application if the applicant files a full medicaid application
30 with the department before the last day of the following month; and
31 (d) Comply with all other regulations and standards required pursuant
32 to subsection (3) of this section.
33 (3) The department shall:
34 (a) Establish performance standards for hospitals to use in making
35 presumptive eligibility determinations in compliance with the require-
36 ments of subsection (2) of this section;
37 (b) Establish an appeals process for a hospital that disputes a deter-
38 mination that a presumptive eligibility standard was violated; and
39 (c) Use the following performance standards to establish and ensure ac-
40 curate presumptive eligibility determinations by a qualified hospital:

2
1 (i) Determine whether each presumptive eligibility determina-
2 tion received from the hospital complied with the time requirement
3 set forth in subsection (2) of this section;
4 (ii) Determine whether the department received the full applica-
5 tion from the individual determined by the hospital to be presump-
6 tively eligible before the expiration of each presumptive eligi-
7 bility period; and
8 (iii) Determine whether each applicant who was determined by the
9 hospital to be presumptively eligible was determined to be eligi-
10 ble for medicaid after the full application was received.
11 (4) Each violation by a hospital of any of the performance standards set
12 forth in subsection (3) of this section counts as one (1) violation for the
13 presumptive eligibility determination. Each subsequent violation of a per-
14 formance standard is an additional violation for purposes of this section.
15 (5) For the first violation of a presumptive eligibility standard un-
16 der this section that a hospital receives in a calendar year, the depart-
17 ment shall notify the hospital of the violation in writing no later than five
18 (5) days after the determination of a violation is made. The notice must in-
19 clude:
20 (a) A description of the standard that was not met and an explanation of
21 why the hospital did not meet the standard;
22 (b) Notice that a second violation by the hospital of a presumptive el-
23 igibility standard will result in a requirement that the hospital's ap-
24 plicable staff participate in mandatory training on hospital presump-
25 tive eligibility rules and standards that is performed by the depart-
26 ment; and
27 (c) A description of the appeal procedures available to the hospital
28 to dispute the finding of a violation of presumptive eligibility stan-
29 dards.
30 (6) If the department determines that a hospital has failed to meet any
31 of the presumptive eligibility standards under this section in any presump-
32 tive eligibility determination by the hospital for a second time within a
33 twelve (12) month period of a first violation, the department shall notify
34 the hospital of the violation in writing no later than five (5) days after
35 the determination that a second violation has occurred. The notice must in-
36 clude:
37 (a) A description of the standard that was not met and an explanation of
38 why the hospital did not meet the standard;
39 (b) Notice that the hospital's applicable staff must participate in
40 mandatory training on hospital presumptive eligibility rules and stan-
41 dards that is performed by the department, and information concerning
42 the date, time, and location of the training;
43 (c) A description of the appeal procedures available to the hospital
44 to dispute the finding of a violation of presumptive eligibility stan-
45 dards; and
46 (d) Notice that a third violation by the hospital of a presumptive eli-
47 gibility standard within a twelve (12) month period from the second vi-
48 olation will result in the hospital no longer being qualified to make
49 presumptive eligibility determinations.
