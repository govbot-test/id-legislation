Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026

IN THE SENATE
SENATE AMENDMENT TO S.B. NO. 1271

2 On page 1 of the printed bill, delete lines 7 through 40; and on page 2,
3 delete lines 1 through 37, and insert:
4 "SECTION 1. That Title 52, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
5 amended by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated
6 as Chapter 5, Title 52, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
7 CHAPTER 5
8 PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY NUISANCES
9 52-501. RODENTS OF UNUSUAL SIZE -- NUISANCES. (1) The legislature
10 finds that the state of Idaho, in particular its more metropolitan areas,
11 has seen a dramatic increase in the presence of rats, which is especially
12 concerning given their lack of historic presence in Idaho and their ability
13 to easily transport diseases that are transmittable to humans and to cause
14 economic damage to agriculture and property.
15 (2) The Norway rat and the roof rat (rats) are hereby declared to be:
16 (a) A public health and safety nuisance, pursuant to the provisions of
17 this section;
18 (b) An invasive species for the purposes of chapter 19, title 22, Idaho
19 Code;
20 (c) An agricultural pest for the purposes of chapter 26, title 25, Idaho
21 Code; and
22 (d) A vermin and public health and welfare pest for the purposes of
23 chapter 28, title 39, Idaho Code.
24 (3) The director of the department of agriculture shall:
25 (a) Coordinate efforts, to the maximum extent possible, with:
26 (i) The boards of county commissioners within Idaho;
27 (ii) Control districts, the boards thereof, and the boards of
28 county commissioners operating pursuant to chapter 26, title 25,
29 Idaho Code;
30 (iii) Abatement districts, the boards thereof, and the boards of
31 county commissioners operating pursuant to chapter 28, title 39,
32 Idaho Code;
33 (iv) Public health districts and the boards thereof, pursuant to
34 chapter 4, title 39, Idaho Code;
35 (v) Any other state agency, department, or political subdivision
36 that the director determines necessary to enact the provisions of
37 this section; and
38 (vi) Any other entity, public or private, that the director deter-
39 mines necessary to coordinate or contract with to enact the provi-

2
1 sions of this section, including to promote public education mate-
2 rials;
3 (b) Develop and implement a coordinated plan to engage the public, po-
4 litical subdivisions, and other relevant stakeholders to determine the
5 extent and scope of rats in Idaho; and
6 (c) Develop and distribute public education material that promotes on-
7 going reporting and best practices to prevent and eradicate rats.
8 (4) The finding of a rat in Idaho is encouraged to be promptly reported
9 to the department of agriculture.
10 (a) The department shall collect and compile all such reports regarding
11 the presence of rats in order to determine:
12 (i) The movement and location of the rats;
13 (ii) Where abatement is necessary;
14 (iii) The type of abatement that is necessary; and
15 (iv) Where the department may need assistance to abate the rats.
16 (b) Such reports and educational materials shall be made publicly
17 available on the department's website.
18 (5) The department shall use such reports to create an abatement plan
19 that includes ongoing public education material distribution.
20 (a) The abatement plan shall:
21 (i) Summarize the extent and scope of rats in Idaho;
22 (ii) Describe the actions already taken and that are currently in
23 progress to address the rats;
24 (iii) Describe any planned future actions to address the rats;
25 (iv) Provide information on how to report the presence of rats to
26 the department; and
27 (v) Provide accessible ways to prevent or eliminate rats within
28 the community and household.
29 (b) The abatement plan shall be made publicly available on the depart-
30 ment's website.
31 (c) The department shall review and update, if necessary, the abatement
32 plan at least every three (3) years.
33 (d) The director shall present and provide the abatement plan to the
34 following legislative bodies for review during the 2027 legislative
35 session and any legislative session following a revision of the plan:
36 (i) The house and senate agricultural affairs committees;
37 (ii) The joint finance-appropriations committee; and
38 (iii) The interim committee on natural resources, if so requested.
39 (e) If the director determines that abatement actions should be under-
40 taken, the anticipated costs to abate the rats shall be included in the
41 director's report. Such moneys may be requested by the director and ap-
42 propriated by the legislature.
43 (6) State agencies and political subdivisions shall make themselves
44 available, subject to available personnel, equipment, and funding, to as-
45 sist the department in enacting the provisions of this section. Nothing in
46 this section shall require mandatory participation or the expenditure of
47 local funds.
48 (7) This section shall be known and may be cited as "The Idaho Rodents of
49 Unusual Size Act."".
