LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1271, As Amended
BY AGRICULTURAL AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO ABATEMENT OF RATS; AMENDING TITLE 52, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDI-
3 TION OF A NEW CHAPTER 5, TITLE 52, IDAHO CODE, TO DECLARE CERTAIN RATS
4 A PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY NUISANCE AND TO REQUIRE THE DIRECTOR OF THE
5 DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE TO DETERMINE THE EXTENT AND SCOPE OF SUCH RATS
6 IN IDAHO AND WHETHER ABATEMENT IS NECESSARY; AND DECLARING AN EMER-
7 GENCY.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Title 52, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
10 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
11 ter 5, Title 52, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 CHAPTER 5
13 PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY NUISANCES
14 52-501. RODENTS OF UNUSUAL SIZE -- NUISANCES. (1) The legislature
15 finds that the state of Idaho, in particular its more metropolitan areas,
16 has seen a dramatic increase in the presence of rats, which is especially
17 concerning given their lack of historic presence in Idaho and their ability
18 to easily transport diseases that are transmittable to humans and to cause
19 economic damage to agriculture and property.
20 (2) The Norway rat and the roof rat (rats) are hereby declared to be:
21 (a) A public health and safety nuisance, pursuant to the provisions of
22 this section;
23 (b) An invasive species for the purposes of chapter 19, title 22, Idaho
24 Code;
25 (c) An agricultural pest for the purposes of chapter 26, title 25, Idaho
26 Code; and
27 (d) A vermin and public health and welfare pest for the purposes of
28 chapter 28, title 39, Idaho Code.
29 (3) The director of the department of agriculture shall:
30 (a) Coordinate efforts, to the maximum extent possible, with:
31 (i) The boards of county commissioners within Idaho;
32 (ii) Control districts, the boards thereof, and the boards of
33 county commissioners operating pursuant to chapter 26, title 25,
34 Idaho Code;
35 (iii) Abatement districts, the boards thereof, and the boards of
36 county commissioners operating pursuant to chapter 28, title 39,
37 Idaho Code;
38 (iv) Public health districts and the boards thereof, pursuant to
39 chapter 4, title 39, Idaho Code;

2
1 (v) Any other state agency, department, or political subdivision
2 that the director determines necessary to enact the provisions of
3 this section; and
4 (vi) Any other entity, public or private, that the director deter-
5 mines necessary to coordinate or contract with to enact the provi-
6 sions of this section, including to promote public education mate-
7 rials;
8 (b) Develop and implement a coordinated plan to engage the public, po-
9 litical subdivisions, and other relevant stakeholders to determine the
10 extent and scope of rats in Idaho; and
11 (c) Develop and distribute public education material that promotes on-
12 going reporting and best practices to prevent and eradicate rats.
13 (4) The finding of a rat in Idaho is encouraged to be promptly reported
14 to the department of agriculture.
15 (a) The department shall collect and compile all such reports regarding
16 the presence of rats in order to determine:
17 (i) The movement and location of the rats;
18 (ii) Where abatement is necessary;
19 (iii) The type of abatement that is necessary; and
20 (iv) Where the department may need assistance to abate the rats.
21 (b) Such reports and educational materials shall be made publicly
22 available on the department's website.
23 (5) The department shall use such reports to create an abatement plan
24 that includes ongoing public education material distribution.
25 (a) The abatement plan shall:
26 (i) Summarize the extent and scope of rats in Idaho;
27 (ii) Describe the actions already taken and that are currently in
28 progress to address the rats;
29 (iii) Describe any planned future actions to address the rats;
30 (iv) Provide information on how to report the presence of rats to
31 the department; and
32 (v) Provide accessible ways to prevent or eliminate rats within
33 the community and household.
34 (b) The abatement plan shall be made publicly available on the depart-
35 ment's website.
36 (c) The department shall review and update, if necessary, the abatement
37 plan at least every three (3) years.
38 (d) The director shall present and provide the abatement plan to the
39 following legislative bodies for review during the 2027 legislative
40 session and any legislative session following a revision of the plan:
41 (i) The house and senate agricultural affairs committees;
42 (ii) The joint finance-appropriations committee; and
43 (iii) The interim committee on natural resources, if so requested.
44 (e) If the director determines that abatement actions should be under-
45 taken, the anticipated costs to abate the rats shall be included in the
46 director's report. Such moneys may be requested by the director and ap-
47 propriated by the legislature.
48 (6) State agencies and political subdivisions shall make themselves
49 available, subject to available personnel, equipment, and funding, to as-
50 sist the department in enacting the provisions of this section. Nothing in

3
1 this section shall require mandatory participation or the expenditure of
2 local funds.
3 (7) This section shall be known and may be cited as "The Idaho Rodents of
4 Unusual Size Act."
5 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
6 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its
7 passage and approval.
