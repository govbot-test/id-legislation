LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1271
BY AGRICULTURAL AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO ABATEMENT OF RATS; AMENDING TITLE 52, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDI-
3 TION OF A NEW CHAPTER 5, TITLE 52, IDAHO CODE, TO DECLARE CERTAIN RATS A
4 PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY NUISANCE AND TO REQUIRE PROMPT ABATEMENT; AND
5 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Title 52, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
8 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
9 ter 5, Title 52, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 CHAPTER 5
11 PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY NUISANCES
12 52-501. RODENTS OF UNUSUAL SIZE -- NUISANCES. (1) The legislature
13 finds that the state of Idaho, in particular its more metropolitan areas,
14 has seen a dramatic increase in the presence of rats, which is especially
15 concerning given their lack of historic presence in Idaho and their ability
16 to easily transport diseases that are transmittable to humans and to cause
17 economic damage to agriculture and property.
18 (2)(a) The Norway rat and the roof rat (rats), are hereby declared to
19 be:
20 (i) A public health and safety nuisance, pursuant to the provi-
21 sions of this section;
22 (ii) An invasive species for the purposes of chapter 19, title 22,
23 Idaho Code;
24 (iii) An agricultural pest for the purposes of chapter 26, title
25 25, Idaho Code; and
26 (iv) A vermin and public health and welfare pest for the purposes
27 of chapter 28, title 39, Idaho Code.
28 (b) The public health and safety nuisance created by the presence of
29 such rats in Idaho shall be summarily abated consistent with the pro-
30 visions of this section, existing statutory authority, and due process
31 requirements.
32 (3) The director of the department of agriculture shall:
33 (a) Execute the provisions of this section as expeditiously as possible
34 and to the fullest extent permitted by law;
35 (b) Coordinate efforts, to the maximum extent possible, with:
36 (i) The boards of county commissioners wherein the rats are, are
37 suspected to be, or may soon be present;
38 (ii) Control districts, the boards thereof, and the boards of
39 county commissioners operating pursuant to chapter 26, title 25,
40 Idaho Code;

2
1 (iii) Abatement districts, the boards thereof, and the boards of
2 county commissioners operating pursuant to chapter 28, title 39,
3 Idaho Code;
4 (iv) Public health districts and the boards thereof, pursuant to
5 chapter 4, title 39, Idaho Code;
6 (v) Any other state agency, department, or political subdivision
7 that the director determines necessary to summarily abate rats in
8 Idaho; and
9 (vi) Any other entity, public or private, that the director de-
10 termines necessary to summarily abate rats in Idaho, including
11 through the promotion of public education materials;
12 (c) Develop and implement a coordinated plan of action by November 1,
13 2026, to summarily abate the rats; and
14 (d) Develop and distribute the public educational material that will
15 assist in eradication of the rats and prevention of their return.
16 (4)(a) The public is encouraged to report, and political subdivisions
17 of the state shall report, the presence of rats to the Idaho invasive
18 species hotline.
19 (b) The director shall collect and compile all reports regarding the
20 presence of rats in order to:
21 (i) Determine the movement and location of the rats;
22 (ii) Determine where abatement is necessary; and
23 (iii) Determine where the department needs assistance to abate the
24 rats.
25 (c) Such reports along with the department's abatement plan, the ac-
26 tions already taken and currently in progress to abate the rats, and
27 educational materials shall be made publicly available on the depart-
28 ment's website.
29 (5) If the director requests assistance from any political subdivision
30 of the state to execute the provisions of this section, the political subdi-
31 vision shall make itself available, to the maximum extent possible.
32 (6) The director is authorized to expend funds pursuant to section
33 22-1912, Idaho Code, to execute the provisions of this section as quickly
34 as possible. Cooperating entities with available funding mechanisms shall
35 share in the cost of abating the rats, to the extent that funds are available.
36 (7) This section may be known and shall be cited as "The Idaho Rodents of
37 Unusual Size Act."
38 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
39 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its
40 passage and approval.
