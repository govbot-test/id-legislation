LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 613
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO POLLING PLACES; AMENDING SECTION 6-1605, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
3 A LIMITATION ON LIABILITY FOR WORKERS OR VOLUNTEERS AT CERTAIN POLLING
4 PLACES; AMENDING SECTION 34-302, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR STATE FUND-
5 ING OF COMMERCIAL LIABILITY INSURANCE FOR CERTAIN PRIVATE FACILITIES
6 USED AS POLLING PLACES; AMENDING SECTION 63-602, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTAB-
7 LISH A PROVISION REGARDING PROPERTY EXEMPT FROM TAXATION; AMENDING SEC-
8 TION 67-5773, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE THAT THE DIRECTOR OF THE DEPARTMENT
9 OF ADMINISTRATION SHALL PURCHASE CERTAIN COMMERCIAL LIABILITY INSUR-
10 ANCE FOR CERTAIN PRIVATE FACILITIES DESIGNATED AS POLLING PLACES; AND
11 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.
12 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
13 SECTION 1. That Section 6-1605, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
14 amended to read as follows:
15 6-1605. LIMITATION ON LIABILITY OF VOLUNTEERS, OFFICERS AND DIREC-
16 TORS OF NONPROFIT CORPORATIONS AND ORGANIZATIONS AND TRUSTEES OF CHARITABLE
17 TRUSTS. (1) In any nonprofit corporation or organization or charitable
18 trust as defined in section 6-1601(6), Idaho Code, officers, directors,
19 and volunteers who serve the nonprofit corporation or organization without
20 compensation and trustees of the charitable trust who serve without compen-
21 sation shall be personally immune from civil liability arising out of their
22 conduct as an officer, director, trustee or volunteer, if such conduct is
23 within the course and scope of the duties and functions of the individual
24 officer, director, trustee or volunteer and at the direction of the corpo-
25 ration or organization or charitable trust. The provisions of this section
26 shall not eliminate or limit, and no immunity is hereby granted for the lia-
27 bility of an officer, director, trustee or volunteer:
28 (a) For conduct which is willful, wanton, or which involves fraud or
29 knowing violation of the law;
30 (b) To the extent of coverage for such conduct under a policy of li-
31 ability insurance, whether the policy is purchased by the corporation
32 or organization or charitable trust, the individual officer, director,
33 trustee, volunteer or some third party;
34 (c) For any intentional breach of a fiduciary duty or duty of loyalty
35 owed by the officer, director or volunteer to the corporation, organi-
36 zation or the members thereof, or owed by the trustee to the charitable
37 trust or the members thereof;
38 (d) For acts or omissions not in good faith or which involve intentional
39 misconduct, fraud or a knowing violation of law;
40 (e) For any transaction from which the officer, director, trustee or
41 volunteer derived an improper personal benefit;

2
1 (f) For any violation of the provisions of section 30-3-82, Idaho Code;
2 or
3 (g) For damages which result from the operation of a motor vehicle.
4 (2) Reimbursement of an officer, director or volunteer of a nonprofit
5 corporation or organization or of a trustee of a charitable trust for costs
6 and expenses actually incurred shall not be considered compensation.
7 (3) Nothing in this section shall be construed to supersede, abrogate,
8 or limit any immunities or limitation of liability otherwise provided by
9 law.
10 (4) The provisions of this section shall apply to any election worker or
11 volunteer conducting an election pursuant to the provisions of chapter 11,
12 title 34, Idaho Code, at a private, nonprofit polling place, to any entity
13 providing such location as a polling place, and to any election-related ac-
14 tivity during the duration of the workers or volunteers on the premises.
15 SECTION 2. That Section 34-302, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
16 amended to read as follows:
17 34-302. DESIGNATION OF PRECINCT POLLING PLACES -- POLLING PLACE EX-
18 PENSES. (1) The board shall, by the fifth Friday before any election, des-
19 ignate a suitable polling place for each election precinct. Insofar as pos-
20 sible, the board shall designate the same polling place for the general elec-
21 tion that it designated for the primary election. The physical arrangements
22 of the polling place shall be sufficient to guarantee all voters the right
23 to cast a secret ballot. Public school facilities shall be made available
24 to the board as precinct polling places. All polling places designated as
25 provided herein shall conform to the accessibility standards adopted by the
26 secretary of state pursuant to the voting accessibility for the elderly and
27 handicapped act, 52 U.S.C. 20101 et seq.
28 (2)(a) The expense of providing such polling places shall be a public
29 charge and paid out of the county treasury, except as provided in para-
30 graph (b) of this subsection.
31 (b) For private facilities designated as polling places under this sec-
32 tion, the secretary of state shall establish in coordination with the
33 department of administration a program to provide proof of self-funded
34 liability coverage for such private facilities used as polling places
35 on election day. Each county shall submit a list of private facilities
36 designated as polling places that require proof of liability insurance
37 at least sixty (60) days prior to the election. The secretary of state
38 shall verify the eligibility of the private facilities as a qualified
39 polling place under this section and forward the verified list of pri-
40 vate facilities to the director of the department of administration,
41 requesting proof of self-funded liability coverage for those locations
42 at least forty-five (45) days prior to the election.
