7 SECTION 1. That Section 18-6605, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 18-6605. VIDEO VOYEURISM. (1) As used in this section:
10 (a) "Broadcast" means the electronic transmittal of a visual image with
11 the intent that it be viewed by a person or persons.
12 (b) "Disseminate" means to make available by any means to any person.
13 (c) "Imaging device" means any instrument capable of recording, stor-
14 ing, viewing or transmitting visual images.
15 (d) "Intimate areas" means the nude genitals, nude pubic area, nude
16 buttocks or nude female nipple.
17 (e) "Person" means any natural person, corporation, partnership, firm,
18 association, joint venture or any other recognized legal entity or any
19 agent or servant thereof.
20 (f) "Place where a person has a reasonable expectation of privacy"
21 means:
22 (i) A place where a reasonable person would believe that he could
23 undress, be undressed or engage in sexual activity in privacy,
24 without concern that he is being viewed, photographed, filmed or
25 otherwise recorded by an imaging device; or
26 (ii) A place where a person might reasonably expect to be safe from
27 casual or hostile surveillance by an imaging device; or
28 (iii) Any public place where a person, by taking reasonable
29 steps to conceal intimate areas, should be free from the viewing,
30 recording, storing or transmitting of images obtained by imaging
31 devices designed to overcome the barriers created by a person's
32 covering of intimate areas.
33 (g) "Publish" means to:
34 (i) Disseminate with the intent that such image or images be made
35 available by any means to any person; or
36 (ii) Disseminate with the intent that such images be sold by an-
37 other person; or
38 (iii) Post, present, display, exhibit, circulate, advertise or
39 allow access by any means so as to make an image or images available
40 to the public; or
41 (iv) Disseminate with the intent that an image or images be
42 posted, presented, displayed, exhibited, circulated, advertised

1 or made accessible by any means and to make such image or images
2 available to the public.
3 (h) "Sell" means to disseminate to another person, or to publish, in ex-
4 change for something of value.
5 (i) "Sexual act" includes, but is not limited to, masturbation; gen-
6 ital, anal or oral sex; sexual penetration with an object; or the
7 transfer or transmission of semen upon any part of the depicted person's
8 body.
9 (2) A person is guilty of video voyeurism when, with the intent of
10 arousing, appealing to or gratifying the lust or passions or sexual desires
11 of such person or another person, or for his own or another person's lasciv-
12 ious entertainment or satisfaction of prurient interest, or for the purpose
13 of sexually degrading or abusing any other person, he uses, installs or per-
14 mits the use or installation of an imaging device at a place where a person
15 would have a reasonable expectation of privacy, without the knowledge or
16 consent of the person using such place.
17 (3) A person is guilty of video voyeurism when:
18 (a) With the intent to annoy, terrify, threaten, intimidate, harass,
19 offend, humiliate or degrade, he intentionally disseminates, publishes
20 or sells, or threatens or conspires to disseminate, publish or sell, any
21 image of another person who is identifiable from the image itself or in-
22 formation displayed in connection with the image and whose intimate ar-
23 eas are exposed, in whole or in part, or who is engaged in a sexual act;
24 (b) He knew or reasonably should have known that the person depicted in
25 the image understood that the image should remain private; and
26 (c) He knew or reasonably should have known that the person depicted in
27 the image did not consent to the dissemination, publication or sale of
28 the image.
29 (4) A violation of this section is a felony.
30 (5) This section does not apply to:
31 (a) An interactive computer service, as defined in 47 U.S.C. 230(f)(2),
32 an information service, as defined in 47 U.S.C. 153 or a telecommuni-
33 cation service, as defined in section 61-121(2) or 62-603(13), Idaho
34 Code, for content provided by another person, unless the provider in-
35 tentionally aids or abets video voyeurism;
36 (b) Images involving voluntary exposure in public or commercial set-
37 tings; or
38 (c) Disclosures made in the public interest including, but not limited
39 to, the reporting of unlawful conduct or the lawful and common practices
40 of law enforcement, criminal reporting, legal proceedings or medical
41 treatment.
42 SECTION 2. That Section 18-6606, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
43 amended to read as follows:
44 18-6606. DISCLOSING EXPLICIT SYNTHETIC MEDIA. (1) A person is guilty
45 of disclosing explicit synthetic media when the person knowingly:
46 (a) Discloses explicit synthetic media and knows or reasonably should
47 know that:
48 (i) An identifiable person portrayed in whole or in part in the
49 explicit synthetic media did not consent to such disclosure; and

3
1 (ii) Disclosure of the explicit synthetic media would cause the
2 identifiable person substantial emotional distress;
3 (b) Discloses explicit synthetic media with the intent to annoy, ter-
4 rify, threaten, intimidate, harass, offend, humiliate, or degrade an
5 identifiable person portrayed in whole or in part in the explicit syn-
6 thetic media; or
7 (c) Possesses and threatens to disclose explicit synthetic media with
8 the intent to obtain money or other valuable consideration from an iden-
9 tifiable person portrayed in whole or in part in the explicit synthetic
10 media.
11 (2) A person who violates subsection (1) of this section is guilty of a
12 misdemeanor unless such person is guilty of a felony as provided in subsec-
13 tion (3) of this section.
14 (3) A person who violates subsection (1) of this section when the iden-
15 tifiable person is under eighteen (18) years of age or when the person was
16 previously found guilty of a violation of this section or a similar statute
17 in another state or any local jurisdiction within the past five (5) years,
18 notwithstanding the form of the judgment or withheld judgment, is guilty of
19 a felony punishable by imprisonment for a period no more than ten (10) years,
20 or by a fine of no more than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000), or by
21 both such fine and imprisonment.
22 (4) As used in this section:
23 (a) "Disclose" means to make available by any means to any person.
24 (b) "Explicit synthetic media" means synthetic media that depicts or
25 appears to depict:
26 (i) An identifiable individual engaged in sexual conduct;
27 (ii) The intimate parts of another individual or artificially
28 generated intimate parts presented as the intimate parts of an
29 identifiable individual; or
30 (iii) The display or transfer of sexual bodily fluids onto any part
31 of the body of an identifiable individual or from the body of an
32 identifiable individual.
33 (c) "Identifiable individual" means a person who is portrayed in whole
34 or in part in synthetic media and who is recognizable by the person's
35 face, likeness, or other distinguishing characteristic, such as a
36 unique birthmark or other recognizable feature.
37 (d) "Intimate parts" means the nude genitals, pubic area, anus, or
38 postpubescent female nipple.
39 (e) "Realistically" means that the synthetic media is sufficiently
40 convincing such that an ordinary person viewing the synthetic media
41 would conclude that it is an actual image or video of the identifiable
42 person but does not include drawings, cartoons, or paintings.
43 (f) "Sexual conduct" means actual or simulated sexual intercourse,
44 oral-genital contact, manual genital contact, genital-anal contact,
45 oral-anal contact, or other physical-genital contact.
46 (g) "Synthetic media" means any image or video created or altered us-
47 ing technical means, such as artificial intelligence, to realistically
48 misrepresent an identifiable individual as engaging in conduct in which
49 the identifiable individual did not engage.
