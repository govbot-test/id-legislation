LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 695
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO FORCIBLE ENTRY AND UNLAWFUL DETAINER; AMENDING SECTION 6-310A,
3 IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING A FEE FOR SERVICE; AND
4 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 6-310A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 6-310A. LIMITED ALTERNATIVE REMEDY TO REMOVE UNAUTHORIZED PERSONS
9 FROM RESIDENTIAL REAL PROPERTY. (1) The legislature finds that the right
10 to exclude others from entering, and the right to direct others to immedi-
11 ately vacate, residential real property are the most important real property
12 rights. The legislature further finds that existing remedies regarding
13 unauthorized persons who unlawfully remain on residential real property
14 fail to adequately protect the rights of the property owner and fail to ad-
15 equately discourage theft and vandalism. The intent of this section is to
16 quickly restore possession of residential real property to the lawful owner
17 of the property when the property is being unlawfully occupied and to thereby
18 preserve property rights while limiting the opportunity for criminal activ-
19 ity.
20 (2) A property owner or the owner's authorized agent may request from
21 the sheriff of the county in which the property is located the immediate
22 removal of a person or persons unlawfully occupying a residential dwelling
23 pursuant to this section if all of the following conditions are met:
24 (a) The requesting person is the property owner or authorized agent of
25 the property owner;
26 (b) The real property that is being occupied includes a residential
27 dwelling;
28 (c) An unauthorized person or persons have unlawfully entered and re-
29 main or continue to reside on the property owner's property;
30 (d) The real property was not open to members of the public at the time
31 the unauthorized person or persons entered;
32 (e) The property owner has directed the unauthorized person to leave
33 the property;
34 (f) The unauthorized person or persons are not current or former ten-
35 ants pursuant to a written or oral rental agreement authorized by the
36 property owner;
37 (g) The unauthorized person or persons are not immediate family members
38 of the property owner; and
39 (h) There is no pending litigation related to the real property between
40 the property owner and any known unauthorized person.
41 (3) To request the immediate removal of an unlawful occupant of a res-
42 idential dwelling, the property owner or the owner's authorized agent must

47 (4) Upon receipt of the complaint, the sheriff shall verify that the
48 person submitting the complaint is the record owner of the real property or
49 the authorized agent of the owner and appears otherwise entitled to relief
50 pursuant to the provisions of this section. If verified, the sheriff shall,

3
1 without delay, serve a notice to immediately vacate on all the unlawful occu-
2 pants and shall put the owner in possession of the real property. Service may
3 be accomplished by hand delivery of the notice to an occupant or by posting
4 the notice on the front door or entrance of the dwelling. The sheriff shall
5 also attempt to verify the identities of all persons occupying the dwelling
6 and note the identities on the return of service. If appropriate, the sher-
7 iff may arrest any person found in the dwelling for trespass, outstanding
8 warrants, or any other legal cause.
9 (5) The sheriff is entitled to the same fee for service of the notice
10 to immediately vacate as if the sheriff were serving a writ of possession
11 restitution. After the sheriff serves the notice to immediately vacate, the
12 property owner or authorized agent may request that the sheriff stand by to
13 keep the peace while the property owner or agent of the owner changes the
14 locks and removes the personal property of the unlawful occupants from the
15 premises to or near the property line. When such a request is made, the sher-
16 iff may charge a reasonable hourly rate, and the person requesting the sher-
17 iff to stand by and keep the peace is responsible for paying the reasonable
18 hourly rate set by the sheriff. The sheriff is not liable to the unlawful oc-
19 cupant or any other party for loss, destruction, or damage of property. The
20 property owner or the owner's authorized agent is not liable to an unlawful
21 occupant or any other party for the loss, destruction, or damage to the per-
22 sonal property unless the removal was wrongful.
23 (6) A person may bring a civil cause of action for wrongful removal un-
24 der this section. A person harmed by a wrongful removal under this section
25 may be restored to possession of the real property and may recover actual
26 costs and damages incurred, statutory damages equal to triple the fair mar-
27 ket rent of the dwelling, court costs, and reasonable attorney's fees. The
28 court shall advance the cause on the calendar.
29 (7) The provisions of this section shall not limit the rights of a prop-
30 erty owner or limit the authority of a law enforcement officer to arrest an
31 unlawful occupant for trespassing, vandalism, theft, or other crimes.
32 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
33 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
34 July 1, 2026.
