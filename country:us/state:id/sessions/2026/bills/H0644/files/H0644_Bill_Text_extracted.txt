28 41-4501. SHORT TITLE. This chapter shall be known and may be cited as
29 the "Idaho Travel Insurance Act."
30 41-4502. PURPOSE. (1) The purpose of this chapter is to promote the
31 public welfare by creating a comprehensive legal framework within which
32 travel insurance may be sold in this state.
33 (2) The requirements of this chapter apply to travel insurance that
34 covers any resident of this state and that is sold, solicited, negotiated,
35 or offered in this state and to certificates that are delivered or issued
36 for delivery in this state. The provisions of this chapter do not apply to
37 cancellation fee waivers or travel assistance services, except as expressly
38 provided in this chapter.
39 (3) All other applicable provisions of the insurance laws in this state
40 shall continue to apply to travel insurance, except that the specific pro-

2
1 visions of this chapter shall supersede any general provisions of law that
2 would otherwise be applicable to travel insurance.

33 group of students, teachers, employees, or volunteers;
34 (c) Any employer covering any group of employees, volunteers, contrac-
35 tors, board of directors, dependents, or guests;
36 (d) Any sports team, camp, or sponsor thereof, covering any group of

43 parent holding company, trustee, or agent or designee of one (1) or more

47 (g) Any incorporated or unincorporated association, including labor

26 of a travel protection plan confirming the purchase and providing the travel
27 protection plan's coverage and assistance details.
28 (7) "Group travel insurance" means travel insurance issued to any eli-

29 41-4504. PREMIUM TAX. (1) A travel insurer shall pay a premium tax, as
30 provided in section 41-402, Idaho Code, on travel insurance premiums paid
31 by:
32 (a) An individual primary policyholder who is a resident of this state;
33 (b) A primary certificate holder who is a resident of this state and who
34 elects coverage under a group travel insurance policy; or
35 (c) A blanket travel insurance policyholder that is a resident of this
36 state or that has its principal place of business in this state or whose
37 affiliate or subsidiary has its principal place of business in this
38 state, which policyholder has purchased blanket travel insurance in
39 this state for eligible blanket group members.
40 (2) A travel insurer shall:
41 (a) Document the state of residence or principal place of business of
42 the policyholder or certificate holder, as required in subsection (1)
43 of this section; and
44 (b) Report as premiums only the amount allocable to travel insurance
45 and not any amounts received for travel assistance services or cancel-
46 lation fee waivers.

1 41-4505. TRAVEL PROTECTION PLANS. Travel protection plans may be of-
2 fered for one (1) price for the combined features that the travel protection
3 plan offers in this state if:
4 (1) The travel protection plan clearly discloses to the consumer, at
5 or prior to the time of purchase, that it includes travel insurance, travel
6 assistance services, and cancellation fee waivers, as applicable, and pro-
7 vides information and an opportunity, at or prior to the time of purchase,
8 for the consumer to obtain additional information regarding the features and
9 pricing of each; and
10 (2) The fulfillment materials:
11 (a) Describe and delineate the travel insurance, travel assistance
12 services, and cancellation fee waivers in the travel protection plan;
13 and
14 (b) Include the travel insurance disclosures and the contact informa-
15 tion for persons providing travel assistance services and cancellation
16 fee waivers, as applicable.
17 41-4506. SALES PRACTICES. (1) All persons offering travel insurance
18 to residents of this state are subject to the provisions of chapter 13, title
19 41, Idaho Code, except as otherwise provided in this chapter. In the event of
20 a conflict between the provisions of this chapter and other provisions of ti-
21 tle 41, Idaho Code, regarding the sale and marketing of travel insurance and
22 travel protection plans, the provisions of this chapter shall control.
23 (2) Offering or selling a travel insurance policy that could never re-
24 sult in payment of any claims for any insured under the policy is consid-
25 ered illusory travel insurance and constitutes an unfair or deceptive act
26 or practice prohibited pursuant to the provisions of chapter 13, title 41,
27 Idaho Code.
28 (3) With regard to marketing practices:
29 (a) All documents provided to consumers prior to the purchase of travel
30 insurance, including but not limited to sales materials, advertising
31 materials, and marketing materials, shall be consistent with the travel
32 insurance policy itself, including but not limited to forms, endorse-
33 ments, policies, rate filings, and certificates of insurance.
34 (b) For travel insurance policies or certificates that contain preex-
35 isting condition exclusions, information and an opportunity to learn
36 more about the preexisting condition exclusions shall be provided any
37 time prior to the time of purchase and in the coverage's fulfillment ma-
38 terials.
39 (c) The fulfillment materials and the information described in sec-
40 tion 41-1092(2)(a), Idaho Code, shall be provided to a policyholder
41 or certificate holder as soon as practicable following the purchase
42 of a travel protection plan. Unless the insured has either started a
43 covered trip or filed a claim under the travel insurance coverage, a
44 policyholder or certificate holder may cancel a policy or certificate
45 for a full refund of the travel protection plan price from the date of
46 purchase of a travel protection plan until at least:
47 (i) Fifteen (15) days following the date the fulfillment mate-
48 rials are sent by postal mail to the policyholder or certificate
49 holder; or

6
1 (ii) Ten (10) days following the date the fulfillment materials
2 are sent by electronic means or personally handed to the policy
3 holder or certificate holder.
4 (d) The company shall disclose in the policy documentation and fulfill-
5 ment materials whether the travel insurance is primary or secondary to
6 other applicable coverage.
7 (e) Where travel insurance is marketed directly to a consumer through
8 an insurer's website or by others through an aggregator site, it shall
9 not be an unfair or deceptive act or practice or other violation of law
10 for the website to provide an accurate summary or short description of
11 coverage, as long as the consumer has access to the full provisions of
12 the policy through electronic means.
13 (4) No person offering, soliciting, or negotiating travel insurance or
14 travel protection plans on an individual or group basis may do so by using
15 negative option or opt out, which would require a consumer to take an affir-
16 mative action to deselect coverage, such as unchecking a box on an electronic
17 form, at the time the consumer purchases a trip.
18 (5) It shall be an unfair or deceptive act or practice to market blanket
19 travel insurance coverage as free.
20 (6) Where a consumer's destination jurisdiction requires insurance
21 coverage, it shall not be an unfair or deceptive act or practice to require
22 that the consumer choose between the following options as a condition of
23 purchasing a trip or travel package:
24 (a) Purchasing the coverage required by the destination jurisdiction
25 through the travel retailer or limited lines travel insurance producer
26 supplying the trip or travel package; or
27 (b) Agreeing to obtain and provide proof of coverage that meets the des-
28 tination jurisdiction's requirements prior to departure.
29 41-4507. TRAVEL ADMINISTRATORS. (1) Notwithstanding any other provi-
30 sions of this title, no person shall act or represent itself as a travel ad-
31 ministrator for travel insurance in this state unless that person:
32 (a) Is a licensed property and casualty insurance producer in this
33 state for activities permitted under that producer license; or
34 (b) Holds a valid managing general agent license in this state.
35 (2) A travel administrator and its employees are exempt from the li-
36 censing requirement set forth in section 41-1103, Idaho Code, for travel in-
37 surance it administers.
38 (3) An insurer is responsible for the acts of a travel administrator ad-
39 ministering travel insurance underwritten by the insurer and is responsible
40 for ensuring that the travel administrator maintains all books and records
41 relevant to the insurer, which shall be made available by the travel adminis-
42 trator to the director upon request.
43 41-4508. POLICY. (1) Notwithstanding any other provision of this ti-
44 tle, and except as provided in subsection (2) of this section, travel insur-
45 ance shall be classified and filed for purposes of rates and forms under an
46 inland marine line of insurance.
47 (2) Travel insurance may be filed under either an accident and health
48 line of insurance or an inland marine line of insurance if it provides cov-

7
1 erage for sickness, accident, disability, or death occurring during travel,
2 whether exclusively or in conjunction with related coverages of emergency
3 evacuation or repatriation of remains.
4 (3) Travel insurance may be in the form of an individual, group, or
5 blanket policy.
6 (4) Eligibility and underwriting standards for travel insurance may be
7 developed and provided based on travel protection plans designed for indi-
8 vidual or identified marketing or distribution channels, if those standards
9 also meet the state's underwriting standards for inland marine lines of in-
10 surance.
