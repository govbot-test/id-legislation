34 SECTION 1. IDAHO CODE CLEANUP -- LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent
35 of the Legislature to ensure that the state laws provided in Idaho Code are
36 streamlined, up-to-date, and essential for the citizens of Idaho, while best
37 serving the public health, safety, and welfare. In accordance with the Idaho
38 Code Cleanup Act, following comprehensive review, the Legislature has iden-
39 tified the provisions of this act as obsolete, outdated, or unnecessary.
40 SECTION 2. That Section 26-2128, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
41 amended to read as follows:

2
1 26-2128. LIQUIDITY REQUIREMENTS. (a) Every credit union shall have
2 on hand as a liquidity reserve an amount equal to four percent (4%) of its
3 outstanding shares, certificates of deposit, and certificates of indebted-
4 ness. Share or deposit accounts from which a member may withdraw funds by
5 the use of a negotiable instrument shall be subject to the liquidity reserve
6 requirements of subsection (b) of this section and not to the liquidity re-
7 serve requirements of this subsection. Said liquidity reserves, except as
8 hereinafter otherwise provided, shall be kept in cash on hand or on deposit
9 subject to check or draft, with any bank or banks or corporate credit union
10 located in the state of Idaho, which shall have been approved by the director
11 as liquidity reserve depositories and shall be computed monthly as follows:
12 on the basis of average daily bank deposits and average daily cash on hand.
13 (b) Every credit union which provides for its member's share or deposit
14 accounts from which the member may withdraw funds by the use of negotiable
15 instrument shall have on hand as a liquidity reserve in addition to the liq-
16 uidity reserve required by subsection (a) of this section an amount equal
17 to ten percent (10%) of its share and deposit accounts which are subject to
18 withdrawal by the use of negotiable instrument. Said liquidity reserves
19 shall be kept in cash on hand or on deposit subject to check or draft, with any
20 bank or banks or corporate credit union located in the state of Idaho which
21 shall have been approved by the director as liquidity reserve depositories
22 and shall be computed monthly as follows: on the basis of average daily bank
23 or corporate credit union deposits, and average daily cash on hand.
24 (c) Certificates of deposit issued by the Idaho Corporate Credit Union
25 may be included in meeting the requirements of this section. To the extent a
26 credit union is required to maintain reserves pursuant to the monetary con-
27 trol act of 1980 and the implementing regulations of the board of governors
28 of the federal reserve system, as the same is presently enacted and as it may
29 be amended in the future, the reserves required to be so maintained shall be
30 considered as a part of, and not in addition to, the liquidity reserves re-
31 quired by this section.
