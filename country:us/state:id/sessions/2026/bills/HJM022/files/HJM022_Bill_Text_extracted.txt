LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 22
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES AND THE
3 SENATE OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, THE CONGRESSIONAL
4 DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED
5 STATES, AND THE DIRECTOR OF THE UNITED STATES FISH AND WILDLIFE SERVICE.
6 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
7 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
8 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
9 WHEREAS, the Migratory Bird Treaty Act of 1918 (MBTA) was implemented
10 to prevent the extinction of birds due to unsustainable feather-trading and
11 hunting practices and makes it illegal to "take," which includes harassing,
12 pursuing, capturing, killing, selling, trading, buying, and transporting,
13 among other activities, a protected migratory bird species, of which there
14 are over 1,000, without prior authorization by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife
15 Service (USFWS); and
16 WHEREAS, one such protected migratory bird species is the pelican,
17 which is also classified as a predator species as they feast on fish; and
18 WHEREAS, a once-endangered species, the pelican has made a remarkable
19 return and has established a stronghold in Idaho's abundant wetland refuge
20 ecosystems such that its population is beginning to impact the overall use
21 and health of the ecosystem; and
22 WHEREAS, Idaho's streams and rivers are renowned by fishermen across
23 the nation, particularly the Henry's Fork River that meanders for eight
24 miles through Harriman State Park, attracting fly-fishers from all four
25 corners of the state and nation thereby generating revenues for the local
26 community and business owners; and
27 WHEREAS, predatory birds, such as pelicans, have been documented to
28 harvest more fish in Idaho than fisherman; and
29 WHEREAS, pelicans have been documented to consume over three pounds of
30 fish per day; and
31 WHEREAS, over 30% of the electronic fish tags that originated in Idaho's
32 renowned Silver Creek were documented by the Idaho Department of Fish and
33 Game (IDFG) in pelican feces at the Minidoka National Wildlife Refuge pel-
34 ican rookeries; and
35 WHEREAS, millions of sportsmen's dollars are spent by IDFG to create,
36 promote, and enhance Idaho's fishing opportunities; and
37 WHEREAS, the pelican, a protected migratory bird under the MBTA, is ac-
38 tively managed and bred by the USFWS and cannot be taken, harassed, killed,
39 or otherwise disturbed without prior authorization by the USFWS; and
40 WHEREAS, the rigorous protections of the MBTA and other environmental
41 acts have successfully ensured this predatory bird's survival but are now
42 hindering the survival of other species and conflicting with areas' tradi-
43 tional use due to the birds' overpopulation.

2
1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
2 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
3 the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislature supports and urges the
4 U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service to curtail, or allow curtailment of, the num-
5 ber of pelicans within Idaho.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature supports and urges the fed-
7 eral government to compensate the State of Idaho for loss of its resources
8 due to the pelican's overpopulation.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Represen-
10 tatives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of
11 this Memorial to the President of the United States, the President of the
12 Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of Congress, the con-
13 gressional delegation representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the
14 United States, and the Director of the United States Fish and Wildlife Ser-
15 vice.
