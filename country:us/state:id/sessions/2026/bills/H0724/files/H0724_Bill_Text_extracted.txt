LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 724
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO FOSTER CARE; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; AMENDING CHAPTER
3 12, TITLE 39, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 39-1212,
4 IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING FOSTER CHILD SAFETY; AND
5 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. The Legislature finds that
8 children in foster care are among the most vulnerable individuals under the
9 care and supervision of the Idaho Department of Health and Welfare. It is
10 the intent of the Legislature to ensure their safety, health, and welfare by
11 articulating certain protections guaranteed to such children. It is further
12 the intent of the Legislature that this act govern the treatment and safety
13 of children while in the care or custody of the state and not be construed to
14 expand general government authority or diminish parental rights beyond what
15 is necessary to ensure the safety, health, and welfare of the child.
16 SECTION 2. That Chapter 12, Title 39, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
17 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
18 ignated as Section 39-1212, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
19 39-1212. FOSTER CHILD SAFETY. (1) All children placed in foster care
20 shall have the following safety-related rights:
21 (a) The right to reside in a safe and healthy environment;
22 (b) The freedom from physical, sexual, emotional, or other abuse. This
23 includes the freedom to be placed away from other children, siblings, or
24 individuals known to pose a credible threat of physical or sexual abuse
25 to the foster child, either because of the foster child's own risk fac-
26 tors or those of the other children, siblings, or individuals;
27 (c) The right to timely and appropriate medical assessments and foren-
28 sic testing when a child discloses sexual abuse to a mandatory reporter,
29 consistent with applicable Idaho law and mandatory reporting require-
30 ments;
31 (d) The right to adequate and appropriate basic essentials, including
32 food, shelter, clothing, and personal necessities;
33 (e) The right to limit, restrict, suspend, or deny visitation by court
34 order because visitation would be contrary to the safety and well-being
35 of the child. Substantiated safety concerns shall be considered by the
36 court to limit, restrict, suspend, or deny visitation. The court may
37 consider any relevant factors, including:
38 (i) Safety and risk assessments;
39 (ii) Placement and supervision decisions;
40 (iii) Protective plans;

2
1 (iv) Medical, behavioral health, and developmental evaluations;
2 and
3 (v) Reunification plans and case plans; and
4 (f) The right to reasonable consideration of the child's safety during
5 transitions between foster care placements when practicable and con-
6 sistent with the safety of the child. Nothing in this paragraph shall
7 be construed to delay an emergency placement or supersede a court order
8 or immediate safety determination. In evaluating safety, the court may
9 consider factors relevant to the child's circumstances, including age,
10 developmental needs, emotional or behavioral health, prior trauma, and
11 the reasons for the placement change.
12 (2) The department shall develop a written notice of, at a minimum, the
13 rights enumerated in this section. The department shall provide each child
14 with such written notice at the time of initial placement in foster care,
15 at each placement change, and at least annually. Foster homes licensed by
16 the department shall post the notice in a place where children have access.
17 Failure to provide or post such notice shall not, by itself, create a cause of
18 action or constitute a violation of law.
19 (3) The rights in this section are a broad expression of the rights of
20 children in foster care, are not exhaustive, and shall be interpreted and ap-
21 plied consistent with the best interests of the child.
22 (4) Nothing in this section shall be construed to expand the author-
23 ity of the state or the department beyond that which is otherwise provided by
24 law or be construed to create a private cause of action or a cause of action
25 against the state, the judiciary, the department, or any employee, agent, or
26 contractor thereof. Implementation of this section shall occur through ex-
27 isting court proceedings and department processes and shall not require new
28 enforcement mechanisms, agencies, reporting structures, or appropriations.
29 (5) Alleged violations of the rights enumerated in this section may be
30 raised within the child's ongoing juvenile proceeding pursuant to chapter
31 16, title 16, Idaho Code, by the child's attorney, guardian ad litem, court-
32 appointed special advocate, the department, or another party to the proceed-
33 ing already granted standing under existing Idaho law or by a foster par-
34 ent through any of the aforementioned. Reports or information submitted by
35 a mandatory reporter pursuant to Idaho law may be brought to the attention
36 of the court through any such party and considered by the court in determin-
37 ing whether a violation of these rights has occurred. Upon such notice, the
38 court shall consider the allegation and may, within its discretion, order
39 appropriate remedies within the juvenile proceeding to promote the safety
40 and welfare of the child. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to
41 require the initiation of a separate action or to expand the jurisdiction of
42 the court beyond existing law. Relief under this subsection shall be limited
43 to corrective orders within the juvenile proceeding pursuant to chapter 16,
44 title 16, Idaho Code.
45 (6) The department shall make reasonable efforts to ensure that all
46 reports received pursuant to mandatory reporting laws related to a child
47 subject to a juvenile proceeding pursuant to chapter 16, title 16, Idaho
48 Code, are documented and made available for consideration by the court
49 within that proceeding in a manner consistent with existing confidential-
50 ity, evidentiary, and due process requirements.
