9 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
10 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. (1) The Legislature finds
11 that:
12 (a) Parenthood is a natural and fundamental relationship from which
13 inherent parental rights and responsibilities arise, protected by the
14 United States Constitution and the Constitution of the State of Idaho;
15 (b) A parent has the right, obligation, responsibility, and authority
16 to raise, manage, train, educate, provide for, and reasonably disci-
17 pline the parent's child;
18 (c) The role of the state is secondary and supportive to the primary
19 role of a parent;
20 (d) A parent's fundamental rights may be restricted only upon a judi-
21 cial finding of unfitness or necessity, supported by clear and convinc-
22 ing evidence; and
23 (e) The interests of the state favor preservation and not severance of
24 natural familial bonds in circumstances where a positive, nurturing
25 parent-child relationship can exist, including extended family associ-
26 ation and support. The procedural safeguards contained in this act are
27 essential to the best interests of the child.
28 (2) The Legislature declares that family court proceedings affecting
29 the custody, care, or visitation of a child implicate fundamental liberty
30 interests.
31 (3) It is the intent of the Legislature to:
32 (a) Protect children by preserving stable relationships with fit par-
33 ents;
34 (b) Ensure that restrictions on parental rights are narrowly tailored,
35 based on clear and convincing evidence, and are the least restrictive
36 means available;
37 (c) Promote transparency, accountability, and timely judicial action
38 in all custody matters; and
39 (d) Promote stability, reduce unnecessary litigation, and support
40 the child's meaningful relationship with fit parents by establishing
41 clear, uniform standards for custody determinations.

21 SECTION 4. That Chapter 7, Title 32, Idaho Code, be, and the same is

24 32-721. DEFINITIONS. For the purpose of this chapter:
25 (1) "Adaptive equipment" means equipment used to enhance the parenting
26 capabilities of a parent with a disability.
27 (2) "Custodial parent" means a parent awarded more than substantially
28 equal physical custody of the parent's child, or, if no order exists, the
29 parent providing the child's principal residence more than fifty-five per-
30 cent (55%) of the time.
31 (3) "Custodian" means a parent or other adult recognized by the court
32 as having standing to exercise care, supervision, or custody of a child, in-
33 cluding grandparents in stable, ongoing caregiving arrangements.
34 (4) "Custody" means responsibility for a child, including legal, phys-
35 ical, joint, or sole custody.
36 (5) "Disability" means a mental or physical impairment substantially
37 limiting a major life activity. "Disability" does not include sexual orien-
38 tation, sexual behavior disorders, substance use disorders, compulsive gam-
39 bling, kleptomania, or pyromania.
40 (6) "Joint legal custody" means equally shared decision-making rights,
41 responsibilities, and authority relating to the health, education, and gen-
42 eral welfare of a child, unless otherwise specified in a court order.
43 (7) "Joint physical custody" means equal periods of residence or su-
44 pervision, except for temporary deviations for events such as documented
45 travel, military deployment, temporary relocation, or medical conditions.
46 (8) "Legal custody" means the right and responsibility to make major
47 decisions regarding the child's upbringing, including health, education,
48 and welfare.
