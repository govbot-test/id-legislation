LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 700
BY BUSINESS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE UNLAWFUL EMPLOYMENT OF UNAUTHORIZED ALIENS; AMENDING TITLE
3 44, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW CHAPTER 28, TITLE 44, IDAHO
4 CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS PROHIBITING THE UNLAWFUL EMPLOYMENT OF
5 UNAUTHORIZED ALIENS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFEC-
6 TIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Title 44, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
9 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
10 ter 28, Title 44, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 CHAPTER 28
12 UNLAWFUL EMPLOYMENT OF UNAUTHORIZED ALIENS
13 44-2801. UNLAWFUL EMPLOYMENT OF UNAUTHORIZED ALIENS. (1) It shall be
14 unlawful for any employer; any person acting as an agent for an employer; any
15 person who, for a fee, refers an alien for employment; or any officer, agent,
16 or representative of a labor organization to knowingly employ, continue to
17 employ, rehire, recruit, or refer for employment in this state any alien who
18 is not lawfully present in the United States or whose immigration status does
19 not authorize employment under federal law. A violation of the provisions of
20 this subsection shall be a misdemeanor.
21 (2) For purposes of this section, an alien shall be deemed unauthorized
22 for employment if the alien:
23 (a) Is not lawfully present in the United States;
24 (b) Lacks a valid, unexpired federal immigration status that indepen-
25 dently authorizes employment; or
26 (c) Previously held employment authorization that was based on an un-
27 derlying immigration status that has expired, been terminated, or been
28 revoked.
29 (3) An employer who, after hiring an alien, knows or reasonably should
30 know that the alien's lawful immigration status or employment authorization
31 has expired, been revoked, or otherwise ceased shall not continue to employ
32 such alien.
33 (4)(a) An employer, or agent thereof, shall not be subject to criminal
34 liability pursuant to the provisions of this section with respect to a
35 specific employee if, prior to the commencement of employment:
36 (i) The employer verified the employee's identity and employment
37 eligibility through the federal e-verify program or any successor
38 program designated by the United States department of homeland se-
39 curity; and
40 (ii) The verification process resulted in a confirmation of em-
41 ployment authorization.

2
1 (b) Compliance with e-verify in good faith shall constitute an affirma-
2 tive defense to prosecution pursuant to this section.
3 (5) As used in this section, "employer," "employment," "knowingly,"
4 and "unauthorized alien" shall be construed with 8 U.S.C. 1324a.
5 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
6 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
7 July 1, 2026.
