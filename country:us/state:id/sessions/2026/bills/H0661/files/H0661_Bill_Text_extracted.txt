LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 661
BY LOCAL GOVERNMENT COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO SOLID WASTE DISPOSAL; AMENDING SECTION 31-4403, IDAHO CODE,
3 TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING SOLID WASTE DISPOSAL SITE CONTRACTS IN
4 COUNTIES AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
5 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 31-4403, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 31-4403. OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE. (1) It shall be the duty of the
10 board of county commissioners in each of the several counties to acquire
11 sites or facilities, and maintain and operate solid waste disposal systems.
12 Such maintenance and operation may, by exclusive or nonexclusive means, be
13 performed through or by:
14 (1) (a) Employees, facilities, equipment, and supplies hired by or ac-
15 quired by the board of county commissioners;
16 (2) (b) Contracts, franchises, or otherwise, entered into by the board
17 to have the maintenance and operation performed by private persons;
18 (3) (c) Contracts entered into by the board to have the maintenance and
19 operation performed by another unit of government;
20 (4) (d) Contracts, franchises, or otherwise, granted pursuant to law by
21 the board, for all or any part or parts of the county; or
22 (5) (e) Any combination of subsections (1), (2), (3) and (4) of this
23 section; paragraphs (a), (b), (c), and (d) of this subsection.
24 (6) (2) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, in
25 order to provide for the public health, safety, and well-being, the board
26 of county commissioners and/or or another unit of state government, may de-
27 termine whether solid waste disposal systems services are to be provided by
28 means of a contract, franchise, or otherwise, provided for under subsection
29 (2) pursuant to subsection (1)(b) of this section, or any contract, fran-
30 chise, or otherwise, awarded under subsection (4) pursuant to subsection
31 (1)(d) of this section, with or without compulsory competitive bidding;.
32 (3) Notwithstanding any county contract or franchise for solid waste
33 collection to the contrary, any person may contract with a solid waste col-
34 lection provider of the person's choosing for the use of solid waste col-
35 lection containers that are temporary and project-specific, as defined by
36 county ordinance.
37 (7) (4) The board of county commissioners, before entering into such
38 contracts, franchises, or otherwise, may require such security for the per-
39 formance thereof as it deems appropriate or may waive such undertaking.

2
1 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
2 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
3 July 1, 2026.
