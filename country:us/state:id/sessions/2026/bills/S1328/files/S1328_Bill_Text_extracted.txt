34 SECTION 1. That Section 38-107, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
35 amended to read as follows:
36 38-107. UNCONTROLLED FIRES A NUISANCE -- ABATEMENT -- CIVIL LIABIL-
37 ITY. (1) Any forest or range fire on any land within the state of Idaho, re-
38 gardless of ownership status, that is burning out of control or without ad-
39 equate and proper precautions having been taken to prevent its spread, is
40 hereby declared a public nuisance, by reason of its menace to life and/or
41 property. Any person responsible through his conduct, acts and/or control
42 of property or operations for either the starting or the existence of such

2
1 fire is hereby required to make a reasonable effort to control or extinguish
2 it immediately, without awaiting instructions from the director of the de-
3 partment of lands or a fire warden. The director of the department of lands
4 or any fire warden may summarily abate the nuisance thus constituted by con-
5 trolling or extinguishing such fire and the person willfully or negligently
6 responsible for the starting or existence of such fire shall be liable for
7 the costs incurred by the state or its authorized agencies in controlling
8 or extinguishing the same. The amount of such costs shall be recovered by
9 a civil action prosecuted in the name of the state of Idaho pursuant to sec-
10 tion 58-120, Idaho Code, and any amounts recovered shall be paid to the state
11 treasurer for deposit to the forest protection fund. Civil liability pro-
12 vided for herein shall be exclusive of and in addition to any criminal penal-
13 ties otherwise provided.
14 (2)(a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, in a civil action
15 against any person, legal entity, state, or political subdivision for
16 forest or range fire caused by a negligent or unintentional act, which
17 act was not willful or intentional under section 6-202, Idaho Code, the
18 real and personal property damage is limited to:
19 (a) (i) The reasonable costs for controlling or extinguishing the
20 forest or range fire;
21 (b) (ii) Economic damages; and
22 (c) (iii) Either (i) the diminution of fair market value of the
23 real and personal property resulting from the fire, or (ii) the ac-
24 tual and tangible restoration costs associated with bringing the
25 damaged real and personal property back to its pre-injured state
26 to the extent that such actual and tangible restoration costs are
27 reasonable and practical.
28 (b) As used in this subsection, "economic damages" means objectively
29 verifiable monetary loss including, but not limited to, out-of-pocket
30 expenses, loss of earnings, loss of use of property, or loss of business
31 or employment opportunities.
32 (c) As further used in this subsection, "fair market value" means the
33 amount a willing buyer would pay a willing seller in an arms-length
34 transaction when both parties are fully informed about all of the advan-
35 tages and disadvantages of the property and neither is acting under any
36 compulsion to buy or sell, as determined by a state certified appraiser,
37 who is qualified to appraise the property.
38 (d) Claims against the state or a political subdivision shall remain
39 subject to the requirements of chapter 9, title 6, Idaho Code, and dam-
40 ages against the state or a political subdivision shall be the amount
41 set forth in chapter 9, title 6, Idaho Code, as limited in this subsec-
42 tion.
43 (3) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, civil actions brought
44 pursuant to this section shall be barred by a statute of limitations after
45 ten (10) years.
