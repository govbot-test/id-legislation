6 SECTION 2. That Section 22-2804, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 22-2804. COMMISSION -- MEMBERS -- QUALIFICATIONS -- APPOINTMENT --
9 COMPENSATION. (1) There is hereby created and established in the department
10 of agriculture the Idaho honey commission to be known and designated as such,
11 and shall be composed of the director of the department and three (3) members
12 who shall be honey producers or beekeepers.
13 (2) Each member shall be a resident citizen of the state of Idaho for a
14 period of five (5) years prior to his appointment, shall be a commercial bee-
15 keeper as defined in section 22-2502, Idaho Code, actively engaged in honey
16 production, registered as a beekeeper with the Idaho department of agricul-
17 ture, and deriving a substantial portion of his income from honey produc-
18 tion, or be the directing or managing head of a corporation, firm, partner-
19 ship or other business unit that derives a substantial portion of its income
20 from honey production. To continue as a member of the commission each member
21 must remain qualified pursuant to the provisions of this section.
22 (3) The executive committee of the Idaho honey industry association may
23 request the removal of a commissioner, with or without cause, by a majority
24 vote. Upon receipt of such a request, the governor may immediately withdraw
25 the commissioner's appointment.
26 (4) The Idaho honey industry association shall meet for the purpose of
27 nominating members of the commission. The board of directors shall review
28 the names of active beekeepers in Idaho that meet the qualifications as pro-
29 vided in this section. By June 1 of each year, the names of two (2) honey pro-
30 ducers or beekeepers nominated by the association for each vacancy occurring
31 on the commission shall be submitted to the governor for his consideration.
32 From such list of nominees, the governor shall designate and appoint one (1)
33 member for each vacancy on the commission.
34 (5) Members shall serve for a term of three (3) years. Terms shall
35 expire on the last day of June of the year in which the term for which the
36 members member was appointed terminates. Provided however, each member
37 shall serve until his respective successor is appointed and qualified. Ap-
38 pointments to fill vacancies shall be for the balance of the unexpired term.
39 On and after the effective date of this act, terms that are currently vacant
40 or held by the commission members shall expire and be filled on the following
41 schedule: one (1) member term shall expire on June 30, 2015; one (1) member
42 term shall expire on June 30, 2016; and one (1) member term shall expire on
43 June 30, 2017.
44 (6) Commission members shall elect a chairman. The chairman may del-
45 egate the function of the Idaho honey commission to an administrator whose
46 function will be subject to the approval of the Idaho honey commission.
47 (7) A majority of the members of the commission shall constitute a quo-
48 rum for the transaction of all business and the carrying out of the duties of
49 the commission. Before entering on the discharge of their duties as members

19 22-2810. SAMPLING AND ANALYSIS. (1) For the purpose of enforcement of
20 this chapter, and in order to determine whether its provisions have been com-
21 plied with, including whether or not any operations may be subject to such
22 provisions, officers or employees duly designated by the director or com-
23 mission upon presenting appropriate credentials, to the owner, operator, or
24 agent in charge, are authorized upon written complaint:
25 (a) To enter, during normal business hours, any factory, warehouse, or
26 establishment within the state in which honey is processed, packed, or
27 held for distribution for retail sales, or to enter any vehicle being
28 used to transport or hold such honey, and sample such honey that is pack-
29 aged and labeled for retail sale.
30 (b) To inspect at reasonable times and within reasonable limits and in
31 a reasonable manner, such factory, warehouse, establishment or vehicle
32 and all pertinent equipment, finished and unfinished materials, con-
33 tainers, and labeling therein, related to retail sales. The inspection
34 may include the verification of only such records, and production and
35 control procedures as may be necessary to determine compliance with the
36 good manufacturing practice rules established under the provisions of
37 this chapter United States standards for grades of extracted honey.
38 Each inspection shall be commenced and completed with reasonable
39 promptness. Upon completion of the inspection, the person in charge of
40 the facility or vehicle shall be so notified.
41 (2) Sampling and analysis shall be conducted in accordance with methods
42 prescribed in rules, or in accordance with other generally recognized meth-
43 ods the United States standards for grades of extracted honey.
44 (3) The director or commission, in determining for administrative pur-
45 poses whether a honey is adulterated, shall be guided by the official sample
46 as defined in subsection (7) of section 22-2803, Idaho Code, and obtained and
47 analyzed as provided for in this section.
48 (4) If the owner of any factory, warehouse, or establishment described
49 in subsection (1) of this section, or authorized agent, refuses to admit the

1 director, commission or an authorized agent to inspect in accordance with
2 subsections (1) and (5) of this section, the director or commission is autho-
3 rized to obtain from any state court of competent jurisdiction a warrant di-
4 recting such owner or agent to submit the premises described in such warrant
5 to inspection.
6 (5) For the enforcement of this chapter, the director, commission or
7 a duly authorized agent is authorized to enter upon any public or private
8 premises including any vehicle of transport during regular business hours

21 SECTION 8. That Chapter 28, Title 22, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
22 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
23 ignated as Section 22-2811, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
24 22-2811. PENALTIES FOR VIOLATIONS. (1) Any person that violates or
25 fails to comply with any of the provisions of this chapter may be assessed
26 a civil penalty by the department or its duly authorized agent of no more
27 than ten thousand dollars ($10,000) for each offense and shall be liable for
28 reasonable attorney's fees.
29 (2) Assessment of a civil penalty may be made in conjunction with any
30 other department administrative action.
31 (3) No civil penalty may be assessed unless the person charged was given
32 notice and opportunity for a hearing pursuant to the Idaho administrative
33 procedure act.
34 (4) If the department is unable to collect such penalty or if any person
35 fails to pay all or a set portion of the civil penalty as determined by the
36 department, it may recover such amount by action in the appropriate district
37 court.
38 (5) Any person against which the department has assessed a civil
39 penalty under the provisions of this section may, within thirty (30) days of
40 the final action by the agency making the assessment, appeal the assessment
41 to the district court of the county in which the violation is alleged by the
42 department to have occurred.
43 (6) All civil penalties collected pursuant to this section shall be re-
44 mitted to the department.
