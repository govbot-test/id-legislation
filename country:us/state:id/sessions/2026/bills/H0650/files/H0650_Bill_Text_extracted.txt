LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 650
BY RESOURCES AND CONSERVATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO STATE GOVERNMENT AND STATE AFFAIRS; AMENDING CHAPTER 23, TITLE
3 67, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 67-2364, IDAHO CODE,
4 TO PROVIDE FOR STATE SOVEREIGNTY AND A JURISDICTIONAL PRESUMPTION; PRO-
5 VIDING SEVERABILITY; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFEC-
6 TIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Chapter 23, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
9 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
10 ignated as Section 67-2364, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 67-2364. STATE SOVEREIGNTY AND JURISDICTIONAL PRESUMPTION -- LIMITA-
12 TIONS ON FEDERAL POWER. (1) The legislature finds and declares that:
13 (a) Under the ninth and tenth amendments to the constitution of the
14 United States and section 1, article I of the constitution of the state
15 of Idaho, all powers not delegated to the federal government are re-
16 served to the states respectively or to the people;
17 (b) The state of Idaho asserts its sovereignty and reserves all powers
18 not expressly granted to the federal government by the constitution of
19 the United States; and
20 (c) The proper balance of authority between the state of Idaho and the
21 federal government is essential to protecting the liberties of the peo-
22 ple of Idaho.
23 (2) It is the policy of the state of Idaho that jurisdiction over all
24 matters arising within the state shall be presumed to reside with the state
25 unless the federal government demonstrates that the constitution of the
26 United States expressly and specifically delegates such jurisdiction to the
27 federal government. Such presumption may be rebutted only by a clear showing
28 that the federal government's exercise of jurisdiction is authorized pur-
29 suant to the text of the constitution of the United States.
30 (3) Areas in which the state has primary and inherent police power ju-
31 risdiction shall include but are not limited to:
32 (a) Public health and safety;
33 (b) Education;
34 (c) Water and natural resources, excluding federal reserve water
35 rights;
36 (d) Agriculture;
37 (e) Energy;
38 (f) Land use and zoning; and
39 (g) Local economic regulation.
40 (4) The federal government is a government of limited and enumerated
41 powers. Any assertion of authority based on implied, incidental, or ancil-

2
1 lary powers shall be narrowly construed and shall not overcome the presump-
2 tion of state jurisdiction established in subsection (2) of this section.
3 (5) In any conflict between the state and federal government regard-
4 ing jurisdiction, the state of Idaho shall assert its rights in coordina-
5 tion with the office of the attorney general, the governor, and the legisla-
6 ture. The burden shall be on the federal government to establish the consti-
7 tutional basis for any asserted jurisdiction.
8 SECTION 2. SEVERABILITY. The provisions of this act are hereby declared
9 to be severable and if any provision of this act or the application of such
10 provision to any person or circumstance is declared invalid for any reason,
11 such declaration shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of
12 this act.
13 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
14 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
15 July 1, 2026.
