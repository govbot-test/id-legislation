8 SECTION 1. That Section 41-1842, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 41-1842. COMMERCIAL INSURANCE -- CANCELLATION -- NONRENEWAL. (1) Ap-
11 plicability. The provisions of this section apply only to:
12 (a) Commercial property insurance policies;
13 (b) Commercial liability insurance policies other than aviation and
14 employer's liability insurance policies;
15 (c) Commercial multiperil insurance policies.
16 The provisions of this section do not apply to: block cancellations or
17 block nonrenewals as provided in section 41-1841, Idaho Code, reinsurance,
18 excess and surplus lines insurance, residual market risks, worker's compen-
19 sation insurance, multistate location risks, policies subject to retrospec-
20 tive rating plans, excess or umbrella policies and such other policies that
21 are exempted by the director of the department of insurance.
22 (2) Definitions. For the purposes of this section:
23 (a) "Cancellation" means termination of a policy at a date other than
24 its expiration date.
25 (b) "Expiration date" means the date upon which coverage under a policy
26 ends. It also means, for a policy written for a term longer than one (1)
27 year or with no fixed expiration date, each annual anniversary date of
28 such policy.
29 (c) "Nonpayment of premium" means the failure or inability of the named
30 insured to discharge any obligation in connection with the payment of
31 premiums on a policy of insurance subject to this section, whether such
32 payments are payable directly to the insurer or its agent or indirectly
33 payable under a premium finance plan or extension of credit.
34 (d) "Nonrenewal" or "not to renew" means termination of a policy at its
35 expiration date.
36 (e) "Renewal" or "to renew" means the issuance, or the offer so to is-
37 sue, by an insurer of a policy succeeding a policy previously issued and
38 delivered by the same insurer or an insurer within the same group of in-
39 surers, or the issuance of a certificate or notice extending the term of
40 an existing policy for a specified period beyond its expiration date.
41 (3) Notice of cancellation.

1 (a) Permissible cancellations. If coverage under a policy has not been
2 in effect for sixty (60) days and the policy is not a renewal, cancella-
3 tion of such policy shall be effected by mailing or delivering a written
4 notice to the first-named insured at the last known mailing address
5 shown on the policy at least thirty (30) days before the effective date
6 of the cancellation, provided however, if such cancellation is for the
7 reason stated in subsection (3)(a)(i) of this section, the time such
8 cancellation may be effective following notice shall be as provided in
9 subsection (3)(b)(i) of this section. A cancellation requested by the
10 insured shall be effective on the later of the date requested by the in-
11 sured or the date it is received by the insurer. After coverage has been
12 in effect for more than sixty (60) days or after the effective date of a
13 renewal policy, whichever is earlier, no insurer shall cancel a policy
14 unless the cancellation is based on at least one (1) of the following
15 reasons:
16 (i) Nonpayment of premium.
17 (ii) Fraud or material misrepresentation made by or with the
18 knowledge of the named insured in obtaining the policy, continuing
19 the policy, or in presenting a claim under the policy.
20 (iii) Activities or omissions on the part of the named insured
21 which increase any hazard insured against, including a failure to
22 comply with loss control recommendations.
23 (iv) Change in the risk which materially increases the risk of
24 loss after insurance coverage has been issued or renewed includ-
25 ing, but not limited to, an increase in exposure to regulation,
26 legislation or court decision.
27 (v) Loss or decrease of the insurer's reinsurance covering all or
28 part of the risk or exposure by the policy.
29 (vi) Determination by the director that the continuation of the
30 policy would jeopardize an insurer's solvency or would place the
31 insurer in violation of the insurance laws of this state or any
32 other state.
33 (vii) Violation or breach by the insured of any policy terms or
34 conditions other than nonpayment of premium.
35 (b) Notification of cancellation.
36 (i) A notice of cancellation of insurance coverage by an in-
37 surer shall be in writing and shall be mailed or delivered to the
38 first-named insured at the last known mailing address as shown
39 on the policy. Notices of cancellation based on subsections
40 (3)(a)(ii) through (a)(vii) of this section shall be mailed or
41 delivered at least thirty (30) sixty (60) days prior to the ef-
42 fective date of the cancellation. Notices of cancellation for
43 the reason stated in subsection (3)(a)(i) of this section without
44 regard to when such cancellation shall be effected shall be mailed
45 or delivered at least ten (10) days prior to the effective date of
46 cancellation. If delivered via United States mail, the ten (10)
47 day notification period shall begin to run five (5) days following
48 the date of postmark. The notice shall state the effective date of
49 the cancellation.

1 (ii) The insurer shall provide the first-named insured with a
2 written statement setting forth the reason(s) for the cancella-
3 tion if: (1) the insured requests such a statement in writing;
4 and (2) the named insured agrees in writing to hold the insurer
5 harmless from liability for any communication giving notice of
6 or specifying the reasons for a cancellation or for any statement
7 made in connection with an attempt to discover or verify the ex-
8 istence of conditions which would be a reason for a cancellation

10 (4) Notice of nonrenewal.
11 (a) An insurer may decline to renew a policy if the insurer delivers or
12 mails to the first-named insured, at the last known mailing address,
13 written notice that the insurer will not renew the policy. Such no-
14 tice shall be mailed or delivered at least forty-five (45) sixty (60)
15 days before the expiration date. If the notice is mailed less than
16 forty-five (45) sixty (60) days before expiration, coverage shall re-
17 main in effect until forty-five (45) sixty (60) days after notice is
18 mailed or delivered. Earned premium for any period of coverage that
19 extends beyond the expiration date shall be considered pro rata based
20 upon the previous year's rate. For purposes of this section, the trans-
21 fer of a policyholder between companies within the same insurance group
22 is not a nonrenewal or a refusal to renew. In addition, changes in de-
23 ductibles, changes in premium, and changes in the amount of insurance or
24 reductions in policy limits or coverage shall not be deemed to be nonre-
25 newals or refusals to renew. Notice of nonrenewal is not required if:
26 (i) The insurer or a company within the same insurance group has
27 offered to issue a renewal policy; or
28 (ii) Where the named insured has obtained replacement coverage or
29 has agreed in writing to obtain replacement coverage.
30 (b) If an insurer provides the notice described in subsection (4) of
31 this section and thereafter the insurer extends the policy for ninety
32 (90) days or less, an additional notice of nonrenewal is not required
33 with respect to the extension.
34 (5) Notice of premium or coverage changes. An insurer shall mail or de-
35 liver to the named insured, at the last known mailing address, written no-
36 tice of a total premium increase greater than ten percent (10%) which is the
37 result of a comparable increase in premium rates, changes in deductibles,
38 reductions in limits, or reductions in coverages at least thirty (30) days
39 prior to the expiration date of the policy. If the insurer fails to provide
40 such thirty (30) day notice, the coverage provided to the named insured shall
41 remain in effect until thirty (30) days after such notice is given or until
42 the effective date of replacement coverage obtained by the named insured,
43 whichever first occurs. For the purposes of this section, notice is consid-
44 ered given thirty (30) days following date of mailing or delivery of the no-
45 tice to the named insured. If the insured elects not to renew, any earned
46 premium for the period of extension of the terminated policy shall be calcu-
47 lated pro rata at the lower of the current or previous year's rate. If the
48 insured accepts the renewal, the premium increase, if any, and other changes
49 shall be effective on and after the first day of the renewal term.

15 SECTION 2. That Section 41-2401, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
16 amended to read as follows:
17 41-2401. STANDARD FIRE POLICY. (1) No fire insurer shall issue any
18 fire insurance policy covering on property or interest therein in this
19 state, other than on the form known as the New York standard as revised in
20 1943, except as follows:
21 (a) An insurer may print on or in its policy its name, location, date
22 of incorporation, plan of operation, whether stock, mutual, reciprocal
23 or organized under special charter provisions, and if mutual or recip-
24 rocal whether on cash premium or assessment plan; and if it be a stock
25 company, the amount of its paid up capital stock, the names of its offi-
26 cers and agents, the number and date of the policy, and, if it is issued
27 by an agent, the words, "this policy shall not be valid until counter-
28 signed by the duly authorized agent of the company at ...."; and, if a
29 mutual or reciprocal insurer, the policy must state the contingent lia-
30 bility, if any, of its policyholders, members, or subscribers for pay-
31 ment of losses and expenses not provided for by its cash funds.
32 (b) An insurer may print or use in its policies printed forms of de-
33 scription and specifications of the property insured.
34 (c) An insurer insuring against damage by lightning may print in the
35 clause enumerating the perils insured against the additional words,
36 "also any damage by lightning whether fire ensues or not," and in the
37 clause providing for an apportionment of loss in case of other insurance
38 the words, "whether by fire, lightning or both."
39 (d) A domestic insurer may print in its policies any provisions which it
40 is authorized or required by the law to insert therein, and any foreign
41 insurer may, with the approval of the director, so print any provision
42 required by its charter or deed of settlement, or by the laws of its own
43 state or country, not contrary to the laws of this state; but the direc-
44 tor shall require any provision which, in his opinion modifies the con-
45 tract of insurance in such a way as to affect the question of loss, to
46 be appended to the policy by an endorsement or rider as hereinafter pro-
47 vided.
48 (e) The blanks in the standard form may be completed in print or in writ-
49 ing.

32 (k) Every fire policy shall contain language that provides for a sixty

37 of mailing of notice of intention not to renew to the named insured at
38 his address shall be sufficient proof of notice.
39 (k) (l) Every fire policy shall provide that it becomes effective at
40 12:01 a.m. of the standard time of the place where the property covered
41 by the insurance is located, on the effective date of the policy.
42 (2) An insurer issuing the standard fire policy is authorized to affix
43 thereto or include therein a written statement that the policy does not cover

45 contamination, all whether directly or indirectly resulting from an insured
