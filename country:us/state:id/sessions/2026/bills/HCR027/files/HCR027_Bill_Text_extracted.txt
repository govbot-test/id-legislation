LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 27
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND HONORING THE SACRIFICE OF IDAHO
3 CIVILIANS WHO SERVED ON WAKE ISLAND DURING WORLD WAR II.
4 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
5 WHEREAS, the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, on December 7,
6 1941, which propelled the United States into World War II, was followed five
7 hours later and 2,000 miles to the west by an attack on Wake Island; and
8 WHEREAS, on Wake Island, civilian contractors were building a vital
9 United States Navy base and refueling facility that the Japanese also needed
10 in order to expand their operations in the Pacific Theater; and
11 WHEREAS, Morrison-Knudsen Company, a member of the Contractors Pacific
12 Naval Air Bases consortium, was employing more than 1,100 American civilians
13 who were skilled in heavy construction to build the naval base on Wake Is-
14 land, including 225 civilians with Idaho connections; and
15 WHEREAS, many of the civilian contractors on Wake Island abandoned
16 their tools and heavy construction equipment to aid the United States Marine
17 Corps detachment during the 16-day siege and final battle against Japan-
18 ese forces, during which 49 U.S. military personnel and 34 civilians were
19 killed, including five Idahoans; and
20 WHEREAS, a United States Navy task force was dispatched from Pearl Har-
21 bor to rescue and reinforce the embattled civilian and military personnel on
22 Wake Island, but it was ordered to abort shortly after Japanese forces landed
23 on the island, leaving the Americans on their own and without hope of rescue;
24 and
25 WHEREAS, Japanese forces eventually captured Wake Island and took all
26 surviving Americans as prisoners of war (POWs), transporting more than 1,000
27 civilian and military POWs to Japan and China and forcing 98 POWs to remain
28 on Wake Island and perform construction work for Japan, now the enemy of the
29 United States; and
30 WHEREAS, 10 Idahoans were among the 98 civilian contractor POWs who were
31 brutally executed on Wake Island by the Japanese on October 7, 1943, nearly
32 two years after their capture and forced labor; and
33 WHEREAS, 17 Idaho civilian contractors died as slave prisoners of war in
34 Japanese POW camps in China and Japan during the war; and
35 WHEREAS, the Japanese forces on Wake Island surrendered to U.S. forces
36 on September 4, 1945; and
37 WHEREAS, in 1981 the United States Department of Defense recognized the
38 civilians who contributed to the defense of Wake Island during World War II
39 as eligible for military discharge, medals, and veterans' benefits; and
40 WHEREAS, Idaho became home to annual reunions of Wake Island survivors
41 and their families until 2017; and

2
1 WHEREAS, Veterans Memorial Park in Boise honors those who served on Wake
2 Island with a monument that notes: "The workers signed up for a job; they
3 ended up fighting in a war."
4 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
5 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
6 the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislature recognizes and honors
7 the sacrifice and faithful service to our country on Wake Island during World
8 War II of Idaho's 225 civilians, 32 of whom lost their lives as a result of
9 their service.
