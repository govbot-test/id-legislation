23 39-4801. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
24 (1) "Daycare" means a daycare center or daycare facility subject to the
25 provisions of chapter 11, title 39, Idaho Code, or any other daycare center
26 or daycare facility that is subject to regulation by any political subdivi-
27 sion of this state.
28 (2) "Medical intervention" means a medical procedure, treatment, de-
29 vice, drug, injection, vaccine, immunization, medication, or medical action
30 taken to diagnose, prevent, or cure a disease or alter the health or biologi-
31 cal function of a person.
32 (3) "Political subdivision" means any county, city, district, depart-
33 ment, agency, board, or any other political subdivision of this state.
34 (4) "School" means any public, private, or parochial preschool; any
35 kindergarten, elementary, or secondary school; any postsecondary institute
36 of education, including trade schools, colleges, and universities; or any
37 other institute of primary, secondary, or higher learning operating in this
38 state. The term "school" includes any governing body or administrator pro-
39 viding oversight or acting on behalf of a school.

1 SECTION 4. That Chapter 48, Title 39, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
2 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
3 ignated as Section 39-4802, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
4 39-4802. PREEMPTION OF MEDICAL INTERVENTION MANDATES. (1) The legis-
5 lature finds that uniform laws prohibiting medical intervention mandates
6 are necessary to preserve medical freedom for individuals and for parents
7 and guardians of minor children, consistent with section 1, article I of the
8 constitution of the state of Idaho. It is the intent of the legislature to
9 wholly occupy the field of medical intervention laws within this state.
10 (2) Except as expressly authorized by state statute, no political sub-
11 division of this state or school may adopt or enforce any law, rule, regula-
12 tion, ordinance, or policy that requires, mandates, advises, recommends, or
13 encourages any medical intervention or that limits or restricts any person's
14 access to or use of any facility or service, including a school or daycare, on
15 the basis of the person having declined or not received a medical interven-
16 tion.
17 (3) Any law, rule, regulation, ordinance, or policy that is in viola-
18 tion of this chapter is hereby declared to be null, void, and of no force and
19 effect.
20 (4) The provisions of this chapter may be enforced by and injunctive re-
21 lief may be pursued by either the attorney general or the prosecuting attor-
22 ney for the county where a violation occurs.
23 (5) If a political subdivision of this state is found to have violated
24 the provisions of this chapter, the attorney general or prosecuting attor-
25 ney, as applicable, shall be awarded attorney's fees and costs incurred in
26 pursuing the enforcement action.

28 amended to read as follows:
29 39-4803. IMMUNIZATION REGISTRY. (1) The department of health and wel-
30 fare shall provide for the establishment of a voluntary registry of the immu-
31 nization status of Idaho children against childhood diseases. The registry
32 shall be maintained and its data disclosed as set out herein to further the
33 following purposes:
34 (a) To make immunizations readily available to every Idaho citizen that
35 desires to have their child immunized;
36 (b) To increase the voluntary immunization rate in Idaho to the maximum
37 extent possible without mandating such immunizations;
38 (c) (a) To recognize and respect the rights of parents and guardians to
39 make health care decisions for their children; and
40 (d) (b) To provide for timely reminders records to parents of children
41 in the registry.
42 (2) The name of a child and information relating to the immunization
43 status of that child shall be collected and included in the registry unless
44 if a parent, guardian or other person legally responsible for the care of the
45 child chooses not to have the child included in the registry upon a specified
46 written statement. Such statement may not be part of a general authorization
47 or release. The registry shall contain the following information for each
48 child:

1 (a) The child's name, address and birth date;
2 (b) The name and address of each parent of the child;
3 (c) The month, day, year and type of each immunization that has been ad-
4 ministered to the child;
5 (d) The name, address and phone number of each provider that has admin-
6 istered an immunization to the child;
7 (e) If requested by a parent or guardian, any statement made pursuant to
8 subsection (4) of this section; and
9 (f) Other information as authorized or requested by a parent or
10 guardian.
11 (3) The department of health and welfare shall only disclose informa-
12 tion relating to an individual child in the registry to the following upon a
13 specific request:
14 (a) Employees of the health district in which the child resides or seeks
15 medical services;
16 (b) Health records staff of the school or school district in which the
17 child is enrolled;
18 (c) The operator of a licensed daycare facility in which the child is
19 enrolled;
20 (d) (b) Persons who are legally responsible for the long-term care of
21 the child, including operators of licensed ICF/ID's and residential or
22 assisted living facilities, adoptive and foster parents and a guardian

24 (e) (c) Any health care provider rendering treatment to the child, and
25 the provider's agents;
26 (f) (d) Any person possessing a lawful release, properly executed by
27 the child's parent or guardian;
28 (g) (e) A parent of the child;
29 (h) (f) Any hospital where the child is receiving care; or
30 (i) (g) The Idaho health data exchange.
31 (4) A parent or guardian of the child shall have free and open access to
32 all information in the registry that relates to their child or themselves.
33 Upon the written request of a parent or guardian, the department of health
34 and welfare shall:
35 (a) Cause all information relating to the child to be removed from the
36 registry;
37 (b) Include in the registry the a written statement of a physician
38 or parent pursuant to section 39-4801(5) or 39-1118(2), Idaho Code.
39 parent or guardian declining or refusing immunizations for religious or
40 any other reasons.
41 (5) All information contained in the registry or disclosed from it is
42 confidential and may not be sold and may only be disclosed as specifically
43 authorized in this section. A person or entity to whom information is dis-
44 closed from the registry may not thereafter disclose it to others except in
45 accordance with state and federal laws applicable to the use of protected
46 health information. Any person who discloses or authorizes disclosure of
47 any information contained in the registry, except as authorized in this sec-
48 tion, is guilty of a misdemeanor and is liable for civil damages in the amount
49 of one hundred dollars ($100) for each violation.

40 SECTION 7. That Section 73-503, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
41 amended to read as follows:
42 73-503. MEDICAL MANDATES PROHIBITED. (1) A business entity doing
43 business in the state of Idaho shall not refuse to provide any service,
44 product, admission to a venue, or transportation to a person or otherwise pe-
45 nalize a person because that person has or has not received or used a medical
46 intervention.
47 (2) A business entity doing business in the state of Idaho shall not re-
48 quire a medical intervention as a term of employment or otherwise penalize an

5
1 employee because that employee has or has not received or used a medical in-
2 tervention unless required by federal law or in such cases where the terms of
3 employment include travel to foreign jurisdictions requiring a medical in-
4 tervention as the only means of entry or where the terms of employment re-
5 quire entry into a place of business or facility in a foreign jurisdiction
6 and such place of business or facility requires a medical intervention as the
7 only means of entry. In any such instance where an employee is required to
8 obtain or use a medical intervention due to travel to a foreign jurisdiction
9 or entry into a place of business or facility in a foreign jurisdiction, said
10 requirement shall either be included in a valid written employment contract
11 between the employer and the employee or, when a written employment contract
12 does not exist, advance written notice shall be provided to an impacted em-
13 ployee no less than fourteen (14) days prior to such employee being required
14 to receive or use a medical intervention. Business entities that receive
15 medicare or medicaid funding shall be exempt from the requirements of this
16 subsection.
17 (3) A ticket issuer shall not penalize, discriminate against, or deny
18 access to an entertainment event to a ticket holder because the ticket holder
19 has or has not received or used a medical intervention.
20 (4)(a) A school operating in the state or a business subject to chapter
21 11, title 39, Idaho Code, operating in the state shall not mandate a med-
22 ical intervention for any person to attend, enter campus or buildings,
23 or be employed, subject to the requirements of the Idaho parental rights
24 act, sections 32-1010 through 32-1015, Idaho Code; sections 39-4801
25 through 39-4804, Idaho Code;, and sections 33-205 and 33-512(7), Idaho
26 Code. For the purposes of this subsection, private and parochial
27 schools shall be considered as having the same authority as school dis-
28 tricts under section 33-512(7), Idaho Code.
29 (b) A school operating in the state or businesses subject to chapter 11,
30 title 39, Idaho Code, operating in the state shall not require medical
31 documents, medical records, proof of immunization, or proof of immunity
32 from any disease as conditions of attendance, enrollment, entry, or em-
33 ployment.
34 (5) Unless required by federal law, no No state, county, or local gov-
35 ernmental entity or official in Idaho shall require any person to receive or
36 use a medical intervention.
37 (6) Unless required by federal law, no No state, county, or local gov-
38 ernmental entity or official in Idaho shall require any person to receive or
39 use a medical intervention as a condition for:
40 (a) Receipt of any government benefit;
41 (b) Receipt of any government services;
42 (c) Receipt of any government-issued license or permit;
43 (d) Entrance into any public building;
44 (e) Use of public transportation; or
45 (f) A term of employment, provided that such entities that receive
46 medicare or medicaid funding shall be exempt from the requirements of
47 this paragraph.
48 (7) No state, county, or local governmental or business entity in Idaho
49 shall provide or offer any different salary, hourly wage, or other ongoing

6
1 compensation or benefits to an employee based on whether the employee has or
2 has not received or used a medical intervention.
3 (8) The ability to require a medical intervention under this chapter is
4 subject to other statutory or constitutional provisions regarding requests
5 for medical interventions and requirements to provide reasonable accommoda-
6 tion.
7 (9) The prohibition on medical interventions shall not apply to any
8 situation where personal protective equipment, items, or clothing are
9 required by a business entity in the public or private sectors based on ex-
10 isting traditional and accepted industry standards or federal law. These
11 exemptions to the prohibition on medical interventions shall not apply to
12 or include any vaccines, mask requirements, or other medical interventions
13 introduced during the COVID-19 pandemic.
14 (10) The provisions of this chapter may be enforced and injunctive re-
15 lief may be pursued by either the attorney general or the prosecuting attor-
16 ney for the county where a violation occurs. If a business entity or state,
17 county, city, or local governmental entity in Idaho is found to have violated
18 the provisions of this chapter, the attorney general or prosecuting attor-
19 ney, as applicable, shall be awarded attorney's fees and costs incurred in
20 pursuing the enforcement action.
21 (11) A state agency shall not adopt any policy, administrative rule, or
22 regulation that conflicts with the provisions of this chapter.
23 (11) Any person aggrieved by a violation of this chapter may bring a
24 civil action in a court of competent jurisdiction for injunctive relief,
25 damages, or both. If the court finds that a person violated any of the pro-
26 visions of this chapter, it shall award actual damages plus an amount equal
27 to treble the amount of actual damages, or one thousand dollars ($1,000) per
28 violation, whichever is greater. The court shall also award a prevailing
29 plaintiff reasonable attorney's fees and costs.
30 (12) The provisions of this chapter shall apply at all times and shall
31 not be suspended, nullified, or otherwise disregarded during any declared
32 emergency, public health crisis, or state of emergency issued by any local,
33 state, or federal authority.
34 (12) (13) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary,
35 under no circumstance may a healthy person be excluded in a disease outbreak
36 due to such person's vaccination status.
