LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 545
BY BUSINESS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO COUNSELORS AND THERAPISTS; AMENDING SECTION 54-3405, IDAHO
3 CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR THE GRANTING OF LICENSURE AS A LICENSED PROFES-
4 SIONAL COUNSELOR TO CERTAIN INDIVIDUALS WHO HAVE SERVED AS A CHAPLAIN IN
5 THE UNITED STATES MILITARY AND TO MAKE A TECHNICAL CORRECTION; AMENDING
6 SECTION 54-3405A, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR THE GRANTING OF LICENSURE
7 AS A LICENSED CLINICAL PROFESSIONAL COUNSELOR TO CERTAIN INDIVIDUALS
8 WHO HAVE SERVED AS A CHAPLAIN IN THE UNITED STATES MILITARY; AND DECLAR-
9 ING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
10 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
11 SECTION 1. That Section 54-3405, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
12 amended to read as follows:
13 54-3405. QUALIFICATIONS FOR LICENSURE. (1) Licensure as a "licensed
14 professional counselor" shall be restricted to persons who:
15 (a) Have not been convicted, found guilty, or received a withheld judg-
16 ment or a suspended sentence in this state or in any other state of a
17 crime that is deemed relevant in accordance with section 67-9411(1),
18 Idaho Code;
19 (b) Have submitted to a fingerprint-based criminal history background
20 check in accordance with section 67-9411A, Idaho Code; and
21 (c) Have successfully completed each of the following requirements:
22 (i) A master's degree or higher in counseling or a related field
23 of study, as approved by the board, that includes completion of a
24 practicum of supervised clinical contact as defined by rule;
25 (ii) An examination, if required by the board's rules; and
26 (iii) One thousand (1,000) hours of supervised experience in coun-
27 seling acceptable to the board.
28 (2) In accordance with the provisions of section 67-9406, Idaho Code,
29 the provisions of subsection (1)(c) of this section shall also be deemed sat-
30 isfied with respect to a person who:
31 (a) Is a resident of the state of Idaho;
32 (b) Has served as a chaplain in any branch of the United States military
33 as evidenced by a DD form 214 certificate of release or discharge;
34 (c) Possesses a DD form 214 certificate of release or discharge for each
35 period of active duty indicating time served in the United States mili-
36 tary with honorable discharge for all periods of active duty;
37 (d) Has completed four (4) units of clinical pastoral education; and
38 (e) Has completed and possesses documentation of one thousand (1,000)
39 hours of direct client contact under the direction of a supervisory
40 chaplain or a supervisory counselor in any branch of the United States
41 military.

2
1 (2) (3) A license will not be issued to an individual whose license,
2 certification, or registration has been revoked or suspended in this or any
3 other state and in this or any other related field. Such an individual may
4 not be licensed under this chapter unless the period of revocation or sus-
5 pension has been completed and the board has conducted a competency review
6 and determined that an acceptable degree of rehabilitation has been accom-
7 plished.
8 (4) In accordance with the provisions of section 67-9406(4), Idaho
9 Code, a person who qualifies for licensure pursuant to subsection (2) of this
10 section shall not be required by the board to take an examination or pay an
11 application fee.
12 SECTION 2. That Section 54-3405A, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
13 amended to read as follows:
14 54-3405A. QUALIFICATIONS FOR LICENSURE. (1) Licensure as a "licensed
15 clinical professional counselor" shall be restricted to persons who:
16 (1) (a) Hold a valid licensed professional counselor license in good
17 standing;
18 (2) (b) Document two thousand (2,000) hours of direct client contact
19 experience under supervision in no less than a two (2) year period as set
20 forth in this chapter and the rules of the board;
21 (3) (c) Successfully complete a written examination as approved by the
22 board and defined in rules of the board; and
23 (4) (d) Submit to a fingerprint-based criminal history background
24 check in accordance with section 67-9411A, Idaho Code.
25 (2)(a) In accordance with the provisions of section 67-9406, Idaho
26 Code, the provisions of subsection (1)(a), (b), and (c) of this section
27 shall be deemed satisfied with respect to a person who:
28 (i) Is a resident of the state of Idaho;
29 (ii) Has served as a chaplain in any branch of the United States
30 military as evidenced by a DD form 214 certificate of release or
31 discharge;
32 (iii) Possesses a DD form 214 certificate of release or discharge
33 for each period of active duty indicating time served in the United
34 States military with honorable discharge for all periods of active
35 duty;
36 (iv) Has completed four (4) units of clinical pastoral education;
37 and
38 (v) Has completed and possesses documentation of two thousand
39 (2,000) hours of direct client contact under the direction of a
40 supervisory chaplain or a supervisory counselor in any branch of
41 the United States military.
42 (b) In accordance with the provisions of section 67-9406(4), Idaho
43 Code, a person who qualifies for licensure pursuant to this subsection
44 shall not be required by the board to take an examination or pay an ap-
45 plication fee.
46 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
47 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
48 July 1, 2026.
