7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND INTENT. As a result of the hor-
9 rific, violent, and targeted attack on firefighters in North Idaho on June
10 29, 2025, that resulted in the murder of two Idaho public safety officers and
11 significant injuries to others, it was identified that catastrophic bene-
12 fits available to surviving spouses or dependent children of public safety
13 officers who died in the line of duty were less than the catastrophic injury
14 benefit available to public safety officers who were catastrophically in-
15 jured but survived.
16 This legislation creates consistency between benefits for a cata-
17 strophic injury or death that occurs in the line of duty, ensuring that
18 one benefit is not greater than the other. Specifically, this legislation
19 increases the lump-sum death benefit available to surviving spouses or de-
20 pendent children to match the catastrophic injury benefit. It also provides
21 a pension benefit to surviving spouses that is consistent with the benefit
22 currently available when a public safety officer dies following qualifica-
23 tion for the catastrophic injury benefit.
24 The increase in benefits as a result of this legislation will be funded
25 solely through public safety officer pension contribution rates.

36 or the result of intoxication; provided, however, that no psychological
37 injury, disorder, or condition shall be considered a catastrophic in-
38 jury under this definition. The injury, if it does not result in death,
39 must be of such severity that it causes the loss of ability to maintain
40 certifications required by the state of Idaho, the member's department,

30 (d) "Dependent child" means a surviving natural or legally adopted
31 child who is under twenty-one (21) years of age at the time of a public
32 safety officer's catastrophic injury that results in death.
33 (c) (e) "Public safety officer" means a police officer member as set
34 forth in section 59-1303, Idaho Code, or a firefighter member as set
35 forth in section 59-1302(16), Idaho Code.
36 (2) A public safety officer who sustains a catastrophic injury or the
37 surviving spouse of a public safety officer who sustained a catastrophic in-
38 jury that results in death as set forth in this section is eligible for:
39 (a) A onetime permanent catastrophic injury benefit in the amount of
40 five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000); and
41 (b) An ongoing annual benefit in an amount not less than seventy-five
42 thousand dollars ($75,000) per year, to be adjusted every four (4) years
43 pursuant to an actuarial study to determine the change in average public
44 safety officer benefits over the previous four (4) years.
45 (3) In the event of a public safety officer's catastrophic injury that
46 results in death, if there is no surviving spouse, the public safety offi-
