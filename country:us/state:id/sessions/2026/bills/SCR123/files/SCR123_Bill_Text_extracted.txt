LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 123
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND SUPPORTING THE 2026 INTERNATIONAL
3 YEAR OF RANGELANDS AND PASTORALISTS.
4 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
5 WHEREAS, rangelands are natural ecosystems in which the native vegeta-
6 tion is predominantly grasses, grass-like plants, forbs, or shrubs that are
7 typically grazed by livestock and wildlife; and
8 WHEREAS, such ecosystems cover up to 19.8 billion acres of land glob-
9 ally, roughly 54% of Earth's terrestrial surface, which is proportionally
10 mirrored in Idaho; and
11 WHEREAS, rangelands are economically, socially, and culturally vital
12 to communities across the world as they provide an array of ecological ser-
13 vices; and
14 WHEREAS, the productivity and environmental health of rangelands are
15 critical for the livelihoods and cultures of more than 500 million pastoral-
16 ists globally; and
17 WHEREAS, billions more non-pastoralists across the world benefit from
18 these areas through animal products, tourism, wildlife, biodiversity con-
19 servation, renewable energy, and other uses and services; and
20 WHEREAS, pastoralists are stewards of rangelands who raise livestock
21 or semidomesticated animals on rangelands and who may be otherwise known as
22 ranchers, sheepherders, or nomads; and
23 WHEREAS, pastoralists played a foundational role in Idaho's formation
24 and development and maintain their cornerstone enterprise in Idaho; and
25 WHEREAS, pastoralism is a dynamic and transformative livelihood linked
26 to the diverse ecosystems, cultures, identities, traditional knowledge, and
27 historical experience of coexisting with nature; and
28 WHEREAS, through deep knowledge and connection with the land, pastoral-
29 ists have coupled the health of landscapes with economic prosperity; and
30 WHEREAS, healthy rangelands are vital for contributing to economic
31 growth, resilient livelihoods, and the sustainable development of pastoral-
32 ism. However, rangelands and pastoralism are currently facing urgent and
33 different challenges around the world; and
34 WHEREAS, Idaho's pastoralists contribute directly and indirectly to a
35 variety of experiences in Idaho's rural and urban economies, thereby sup-
36 porting local families and businesses, reinforcing an Idaho tradition, and
37 sustaining overall vitality in Idaho's communities; and
38 WHEREAS, recognizing 2026 as the International Year of Rangelands and
39 Pastoralists provides a timely opportunity to reaffirm Idaho's support for
40 sustainable livestock grazing, balanced public use, and collaboration among
41 pastoralists, recreationalists, conservation partners, and land management
42 agencies to ensure healthy working landscapes throughout the state.

2
1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
2 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of Rep-
3 resentatives concurring therein, that the Legislature recognizes and sup-
4 ports Governor Little's proclamation recognizing 2026 as the International
5 Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of the State of Idaho sup-
7 ports the 2026 International Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists and the ef-
8 forts made by the associated global and regional alliances and groups.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature recognizes the importance
10 of national policies on conservation and sustainable use of natural re-
11 sources in order to manage grazing areas, wildlife, water sources, livestock
12 movement, risk, and resilience and to enable land use planning and ecosystem
13 management by pastoralists and relevant public entities.
