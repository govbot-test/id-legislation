24 SECTION 2. That Section 42-202B, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
25 amended to read as follows:
26 42-202B. DEFINITIONS. Whenever used in this title, the term:
27 (1) "Consumptive use" means that portion of the annual volume of water
28 diverted under a water right that is transpired by growing vegetation, evap-
29 orated from soils, converted to nonrecoverable water vapor, incorporated
30 into products, or otherwise does not return to the waters of the state. Con-
31 sumptive use is not an element of a water right. Consumptive use does not
32 include any water that falls as precipitation directly on the place of use.
33 Precipitation shall not be considered to reduce the consumptive use of a
34 water right. "Authorized consumptive use" means the maximum consumptive use
35 that may be made of a water right. If the use of a water right is for irriga-
36 tion, for example, the authorized consumptive use reflects irrigation of the
37 most consumptive vegetation that may be grown at the place of use. Changes in
38 consumptive use do not require a transfer pursuant to section 42-222, Idaho
39 Code.
40 (2) "Digital boundary" means the boundary encompassing and defining an
41 area consisting of or incorporating the place of use or permissible place of
42 use for a water right prepared and maintained by the department of water re-
43 sources using a geographic information system in conformance with the na-
44 tional standard for spatial data accuracy or succeeding standard.
45 (3) "Local public interest" is defined as the interests that the people
46 in the area directly affected by a proposed water use have in the effects of
47 such use on the public water resource.

4
1 (4) "Municipality" means a city incorporated under section 50-102,
2 Idaho Code, a county, or the state of Idaho acting through a department or
3 institution.
4 (5) "Municipal provider" means:
5 (a) A municipality that provides water for municipal purposes to its
6 residents and other users within its service area;
7 (b) Any corporation or association holding a franchise to supply water
8 for municipal purposes, or a political subdivision of the state of Idaho
9 authorized to supply water for municipal purposes, and which does sup-
10 ply water, for municipal purposes to users within its service area; or
11 (c) A corporation or association which supplies water for municipal
12 purposes through a water system regulated by the state of Idaho as a
13 "public water supply" as described in section 39-103(12), Idaho Code.
14 (6)(a) "Municipal purposes" refers to water for residential, com-
15 mercial, industrial, irrigation of parks and open space, and related
16 purposes, excluding use of water from geothermal sources for heating,
17 which that a municipal provider is entitled or obligated to supply to
18 all those users within a service area, including those located outside
19 the boundaries of a municipality served by a municipal provider, for
20 residential use, commercial use, industrial use, irrigation of parks
21 and open spaces, geothermal heating and cooling, and related purposes,
22 or a combination thereof.
23 (b)(i) Geothermal heating and cooling are considered "municipal
24 purposes" if:
25 1. It is provided by a municipality from a low temperature
26 geothermal water source, as that term is defined in section
27 42-230(a)(1), Idaho Code;
28 2. The water use is non-consumptive under normal operating
29 conditions; and
30 3. The use otherwise complies with section 42-233, Idaho
31 Code.
32 (ii) A municipality is obligated to supply geothermal heating and
33 cooling only to those users approved for service in accordance
34 with its local codes and policies.
35 (7) "Planning horizon" refers to the length of time that the department
36 determines is reasonable for a municipal provider to hold water rights to
37 meet reasonably anticipated future needs. The length of the planning hori-
38 zon may vary according to the needs of the particular municipal provider.
39 (8) "Reasonably anticipated future needs" refers to future uses of
40 water by a municipal provider for municipal purposes within a service area
41 which, on the basis of population and other planning data, are reasonably
42 expected to be required within the planning horizon of each municipality
43 within the service area not inconsistent with comprehensive land use plans
44 approved by each municipality. Reasonably anticipated future needs shall
45 not include uses of water within areas overlapped by conflicting comprehen-
46 sive land use plans. Reasonably anticipated future needs for geothermal
47 heating and cooling require injection of diverted low temperature geother-
48 mal water back into the same aquifer from which it was diverted and shall be
49 considered when determining what needs will be expected within the planning
50 horizon.
