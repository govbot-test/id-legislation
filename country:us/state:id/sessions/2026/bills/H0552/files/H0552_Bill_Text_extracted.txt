18 (7) Administrative hearing on suspension. A person who has been served
19 with a notice of suspension and notice of the requirement to install the ig-
20 nition interlock system after submitting to an evidentiary test may request
21 an administrative hearing on the suspension before a hearing officer desig-
22 nated by the department. The hearing may be held only on the suspension and
23 not on the requirement to install an ignition interlock system. The request
24 for hearing shall be in writing and must be received by the department within
25 seven (7) calendar business days of the date of service upon the person of
26 the notice of suspension and notice of the requirement to install the igni-
27 tion interlock system and shall include what issue or issues shall be raised
28 at the hearing. The date on which the hearing request was received shall be
29 noted on the face of the request. Failure to request a hearing or appear at a
30 requested hearing shall be considered a default judgment against the defen-
31 dant.
32 If a hearing is requested, the hearing shall be held within twenty (20)
33 days of the date the hearing request was received by the department unless
34 this period is, for good cause shown, extended by the hearing officer for a
35 ten (10) day period. Such extension shall not operate as a stay of the sus-
36 pension, notwithstanding an extension of the hearing date beyond such thirty
37 (30) day period. Written notice of the date and time of the hearing shall
38 be sent to the party requesting the hearing at least seven (7) business days
39 prior to the scheduled hearing date. The department may conduct all hearings
40 by telephone if each participant in the hearing has an opportunity to partic-
41 ipate in the entire proceeding while it is taking place.
