16 SECTION 2. That Section 39-5704, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
17 amended to read as follows:
18 39-5704. PERMITTING OF TOBACCO PRODUCTS OR ELECTRONIC SMOKING DEVICES
19 RETAILERS. (1) It shall be unlawful to sell or distribute or offer tobacco
20 products or electronic smoking devices for sale or distribution at retail
21 or to possess tobacco products or electronic smoking devices with the in-
22 tention of selling at retail without having first obtained a tobacco product
23 or electronic smoking device permit from the department, which shall be the
24 only retail tobacco product or electronic smoking device permit or license
25 required. Provided however, this section shall not be deemed to require a
26 wholesaler or manufacturer's representative or employees who, in the course
27 of their employment, stock shelves and replenish tobacco products or elec-
28 tronic smoking devices at a permittee's place of business to obtain a permit.
29 (2) The department shall administer the permitting of tobacco product
30 or electronic smoking device retailers and shall be authorized to ensure
31 compliance with this chapter.
32 (3)(a) Permits shall be issued annually for each permanent business lo-
33 cation to ensure compliance with the requirements of this chapter. A
34 copy of signage required by this chapter, and any other materials deemed
35 necessary shall be provided with each permit issued.
36 (b) Permits for each mobile cigar retailer shall be issued annually to
37 a specific mobile unit identified by a vehicle identification number
38 or other unique serial number. A single permit shall authorize the re-
39 tailer to operate at various temporary locations throughout the state.
40 (4) A separate permit must be obtained for each place of business or mo-
41 bile unit and is nontransferable to another person, business, or location.
42 (5) The applicant shall select one (1) or more permit endorsements and
43 make the permit available upon request.
44 (6) A permittee may display a sign in each location within a place of
45 business where tobacco products or electronic smoking devices are sold or
46 distributed. A sign may be clearly visible to the customer and the seller and
47 shall state: "STATE LAW PROHIBITS THE SALE OF TOBACCO PRODUCTS OR ELECTRONIC
48 SMOKING DEVICES TO PERSONS UNDER THE AGE OF TWENTY-ONE (21) YEARS. PROOF OF
49 AGE REQUIRED. ANYONE WHO SELLS OR DISTRIBUTES TOBACCO PRODUCTS OR ELECTRONIC

4
1 SMOKING DEVICES TO A PERSON UNDER THE AGE OF TWENTY-ONE (21) YEARS IS SUBJECT
2 TO STRICT FINES AND PENALTIES. PERSONS UNDER THE AGE OF TWENTY-ONE (21) YEARS
3 ARE SUBJECT TO FINES AND PENALTIES."
4 (a) For a mobile cigar retailer, the sign described in this subsection
5 and required permit shall be permanently affixed to the interior or ex-
6 terior of the mobile unit in a location readily visible to patrons.
7 (b) When performing on-site cigar rolling services or operating a
8 designated cigar garden at various temporary locations, the permittee
9 shall display temporary signage at the specific point of distribution
10 or entry. Such signage shall be clearly visible to both the customer and
11 the seller and include the language provided in this subsection.
12 (7) Permittees are responsible to educate employees as to the require-
13 ments of this chapter and retain forms signed by each employee stating that
14 the employee understands such requirements.
15 (8) It shall be unlawful for the permittee to allow employees who are
16 minors to sell or distribute tobacco products or electronic smoking devices,
17 except as provided in this subsection. Employees who are minors may possess
18 but not sell or distribute tobacco products or electronic smoking devices
19 in the course of employment, for such duties as stocking shelves or carrying
20 purchases to customers' vehicles, and employees who are eighteen (18) years
21 to twenty (20) years of age may sell or distribute tobacco products or elec-
22 tronic smoking devices in the course and scope of their employment.
23 (9) A permit shall be closed when the permittee closes the business, no
24 longer sells tobacco or electronic smoking device products, moves to a dif-
25 ferent physical location, or sells the business.
26 (10) A permit shall be revoked when it is determined that a permit was
27 fraudulently obtained to avoid penalties on an existing permit.
28 (11) All permits expire annually at midnight on December 31 unless re-
29 newed. The department shall mail notice of renewal for permits at least
30 ninety (90) days prior to expiration.
31 SECTION 3. That Section 39-5706, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
32 amended to read as follows:
33 39-5706. VENDOR-ASSISTED SALES. (1) It shall be unlawful to sell or
34 distribute tobacco products or electronic smoking devices by any means other
35 than vendor-assisted sales where the customer has no access to the product
36 except through the assistance of the seller.
37 (2) It shall be unlawful to sell or distribute tobacco products or elec-
38 tronic smoking devices from vending machines or self-service displays.
39 (3) Stores with tobacco products or electronic smoking devices com-
40 prising at least seventy-five percent (75%) of total merchandise are exempt
41 from requiring vendor-assisted sales if minors are not allowed in the store
42 and such prohibition is posted clearly on all entrance doors.
43 (4) This section shall not apply to a permitted mobile cigar retailer
44 or designated cigar garden provided that the mobile unit or designated cigar
45 garden is strictly restricted to persons twenty-one (21) years of age or
46 older and is under the continuous supervision of the permittee or the permit-
47 tee's employee.
