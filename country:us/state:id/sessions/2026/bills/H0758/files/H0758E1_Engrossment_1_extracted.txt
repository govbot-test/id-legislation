7 SECTION 1. That Section 39-1102, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 39-1102. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
10 (1) "Board" means the Idaho board of health and welfare.
11 (2) "Child" means a person less than thirteen (13) years of age.
12 (3) "Daycare" means care and supervision provided for compensation
13 during part of a twenty-four (24) hour day, for a child or children not
14 related by blood, marriage or legal guardianship to the person or persons
15 providing the care, in a place other than the child's or children's own home
16 or homes.
17 (4) "Daycare center" means a place or facility providing daycare for
18 compensation for thirteen (13) or more children.
19 (5) "Daycare facility" means a place or facility providing daycare
20 services for compensation to seven (7) or more children not related to the
21 provider.
22 (6) "Department" means the Idaho department of health and welfare.
23 (7) "Employee" means any person working for compensation in a facility
24 that provides daycare.
25 (8) "Family daycare home" means a home, place, or facility providing
26 daycare for six (6) or fewer children.
27 (9) "Group daycare facility" means a home, place, or facility providing
28 daycare for seven (7) to twelve (12) children.
29 (10) "Group size" means the maximum number of children in one (1) group
30 or classroom.
31 (11) "Mixed age group" means a care group that includes children of mul-
32 tiple ages.
33 (12) "Operator" means a person who operates or maintains a licensed day-
34 care facility.
35 (13) "Owner" means a person, group of individuals, or entity that has
36 legal control and authority over a daycare business.
37 (14) "Provider" means a person who provides care and supervision at a
38 daycare facility and is included in the child:staff ratio.
39 (15) "Ratio" means the number of staff required to supervise a certain
40 number of children.
41 (16) "Single age group" means a care group that includes children of
42 similar age.

21 SECTION 2. That Section 39-1109, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
22 amended to read as follows:
23 39-1109. SAFETY STANDARDS. (1) Daycare facilities, owners and opera-
24 tors shall comply with the following safety standards in the area of the day-
25 care facility in which daycare is provided:
26 (a) Adequate fire and smoke alarms;
27 (b) A functional telephone located on the daycare premises during the
28 hours of operation;
29 (c) Adequate fire extinguishers;
30 (d) Adequate exits;
31 (e) Firearms or other weapons that are stored on the premises of a day-
32 care facility must be kept in a locked container that is inaccessible to
33 children while daycare attendees are present;
34 (f) Pools, hot tubs, ponds and other bodies of water that are on the day-
35 care facility premises must provide the following safeguards:
36 (i) The area surrounding the body of water must be fenced and
37 locked in a manner that prevents access by children and meets the
38 following requirements:
39 1. The fence must be at least four (4) feet high with no ver-
40 tical opening more than four (4) inches wide, be designed so
41 that a young child cannot climb or squeeze under or through
42 the fence, surround all sides of the pool and have a gate that
43 is self-closing and that has a self-latching mechanism in
44 proper working order out of the reach of young children;
45 2. If the house forms one (1) side of the barrier for the
46 pool, all doors that provide unrestricted access to the pool
47 must have alarms that produce an audible sound when the door
48 is opened;

3
1 3. Furniture or other large objects must not be left near the
2 fence in a manner that would enable a child to climb on the
3 furniture or other large object and gain access to the pool;
4 and
5 (ii) If the area surrounding a pool, hot tub, pond or other body of
6 water is not fenced and locked, there must be a secured protective
7 covering that will not allow access by a child;
8 (iii) Wading pools must be empty when not in use;
9 (iv) Children must be under direct supervision of at least one (1)
10 adult employee while using a pool, hot tub, pond or other body of
11 water; and
12 (v) A minimum of a four (4) foot high fence must be present that
13 prevents access from the daycare facility premises if the daycare
14 premises are adjacent to a body of water; and
15 (g) The owner or operator of a daycare facility shall ensure that at
16 all times when a child or children are present, at least one (1) adult
17 employee on the premises has current certification in pediatric rescue
18 breathing and first aid treatment from a certified instructor.
19 (2) No fire standards developed pursuant to this chapter shall be more
20 stringent than the standards contained in the International Fire Code, as
21 adopted by Idaho.
22 (3) At least one (1) adult employee must be present at all times when a
23 child or children are in attendance.
24 (4)(a) The maximum allowable child:staff ratio shall be a maximum of
25 twelve (12) points per staff member using the following point system:
26 (i) Each child in attendance under the age of twenty-four (24)
27 months shall equal two (2) points.
28 (ii) Each child in attendance from twenty-four (24) months to un-
29 der thirty-six (36) months of age shall equal one and thirty-three
30 hundredths (1.33) points.
31 (iii) Each child in attendance from thirty-six (36) months to un-
32 der five (5) years of age shall equal nine hundred twenty-three
33 thousandths (0.923) of a point.
34 (iv) Each child in attendance from five (5) years to under thirteen
35 (13) years of age shall equal forty-eight hundredths (0.48) of a
36 point.
37 (b) Each child in attendance shall be counted by the department for pur-
38 poses of calculating maximum allowable points, counting the number of
39 children in attendance and for determining compliance with child:staff
40 ratios; however, in a family daycare home or a group daycare facility,
41 the provider's own children who are five (5) years of age or older shall
42 not be counted if the provider is operating out of the provider's home.
43 (c) Daycare facilities may operate with child:staff ratios more strin-
44 gent than those specified in paragraph (a) of this subsection.
45 (5) Sleeping children must be within sight and normal hearing range of a
46 provider supervised as described in section 39-1102(18), Idaho Code.
47 (6) For daycare facilities providing overnight care of children:
48 (a) A sleeping child shall sleep on the same level as a staff member;
49 (b) At least one (1) staff member shall be awake and on duty;
