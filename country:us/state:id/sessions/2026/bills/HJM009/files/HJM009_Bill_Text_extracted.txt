LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 9
BY RESOURCES AND CONSERVATION COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESEN-
3 TATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, THE CONGRESSIONAL
4 DELEGATIONS REPRESENTING THE STATES OF IDAHO, UTAH, NEVADA, AND WYOMING
5 IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES, THE UNITED STATES ATTORNEY GEN-
6 ERAL, AND THE SECRETARY OF THE INTERIOR.
7 We, your Memorialists, the House of Representatives and the Sen-
8 ate of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
9 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
10 WHEREAS, the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) administers 245 million
11 acres of public lands, which equates to roughly one tenth of all land in the
12 United States; and
13 WHEREAS, Idaho, like most other western states, has large portions of
14 land managed by BLM within its borders; and
15 WHEREAS, BLM land, and other federally owned land, is often intermin-
16 gled with state and private lands, so exchanges and other disposal options
17 are vital tools for administering such lands and achieving important recre-
18 ation, community development, wildlife conservation, and resource develop-
19 ment objectives, such as timber harvesting, mining, and energy projects; and
20 WHEREAS, given BLM's large ownership interest of lands within the
21 United States, particularly within Idaho, and the intermingled nature of
22 these lands, it is critical to Idaho, as it should be to the nation, that
23 these lands are predictably managed in accordance with laws that were
24 adopted to ensure orderly and consistent administration of such lands; and
25 WHEREAS, the federal government once managed these public lands under a
26 patchwork of some 3,000 public land laws; and
27 WHEREAS, in 1964, Congress charged a commission to bring order to the
28 process and propose ways to rationalize and systematize the management of
29 public lands; and
30 WHEREAS, in 1976, Congress acted on these recommendations by enacting
31 the Federal Land Policy and Management Act (FLPMA). FLPMA is the comprehen-
32 sive system for predictably managing public lands for multiple uses. The
33 statute broadly applies to any land and interest in land owned by the United
34 States, without regard to how the United States acquired ownership, that is
35 administered by the Secretary of the Department of the Interior, wherein BLM
36 resides; and
37 WHEREAS, FLPMA provided the tools vital to administering intermingled
38 lands by establishing uniform procedures for the disposal of federally owned
39 public land, including the authority and procedures to exchange public lands
40 for non-federal lands, when it is in the public interest; and
41 WHEREAS, for the past 50 years, FLPMA's broad authority for conducting
42 land exchanges has been universally recognized and acknowledged; however,

2
1 a legal decision originating in Idaho threatens FLPMA's comprehensive
2 process; and
3 WHEREAS, Congress needs to immediately reaffirm FLPMA's primary in-
4 tent. Failure to act may have far-reaching consequences in Idaho, including
5 encumbering the management of millions of acres across the West, along with
6 threatening the certainty of other completed FLPMA land exchanges and dis-
7 posals.
8 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
9 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
10 the Senate concurring therein, that the Idaho Legislature strongly urges the
11 Congress of the United States to, as expeditiously as possible, enact legis-
12 lation reaffirming that FLPMA is the proper and primary avenue to facilitate
13 effective federal land management and disposal, including by land exchange.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that as intended by Congress and provided for in
15 FLPMA, previous land disposal laws should not be interpreted to conflict
16 with or override FLPMA's disposal authority. Such expeditious action by
17 Congress will prevent the West from being pushed back into the management
18 chaos that Congress fixed in 1976 by enacting FLPMA.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the House of Represen-
20 tatives be, and she is hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of
21 this Memorial to the President of the United States, the President of the
22 Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of Congress, the con-
23 gressional delegations representing the states of Idaho, Utah, Nevada, and
24 Wyoming in the Congress of the United States, the United States Attorney Gen-
25 eral, and the Secretary of the Interior.
