LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 32
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND SUPPORTING ADVANCEMENT OF STATE EN-
3 ERGY SOVEREIGNTY.
4 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
5 WHEREAS, Idaho participates in interstate energy markets and benefits
6 at times from cost-effective imported power, particularly during seasonal
7 fluctuations, and at other times from being able to sell excess power to
8 neighboring states, with financial benefits directly benefiting Idaho cus-
9 tomers; and
10 WHEREAS, Idaho is a net energy importer, and as such, long-term energy
11 security, ratepayer protection, and state sovereignty require that imported
12 energy supplement, but not replace, secure in-state base load generation
13 that is capable of sustaining Idaho's residential, agricultural, commer-
14 cial, industrial, and mining-related energy demand under all seasonal and
15 water conditions; and
16 WHEREAS, Idaho's economy and workforce, including its national labo-
17 ratory, agricultural producers, mining operations, advanced manufacturing
18 facilities, and rural communities, depend on affordable and reliable energy
19 that is responsibly developed and selected by electric utilities to meet the
20 needs of Idahoans in a least-cost and least-risk manner; and
21 WHEREAS, the Eastern Snake River Plain Aquifer is a critical ground wa-
22 ter resource supporting Idaho agriculture, rural communities, and economic
23 stability, and its protection is a matter of statewide importance; and
24 WHEREAS, the Idaho Legislature has formally expressed opposition to the
25 Lava Ridge project, citing documented concerns regarding impacts to agri-
26 culture, the Eastern Snake River Plain Aquifer, aviation safety, wildlife
27 habitat, and Idaho's natural landscapes; and
28 WHEREAS, the Legislature finds that transmission infrastructure con-
29 structed that does not benefit Idaho utility customers and does materially
30 harm agriculture, critical water resources, or rural communities is incon-
31 sistent with the expressed policy position of the State of Idaho; and
32 WHEREAS, Idaho's long-term reliability and grid stability require se-
33 cure, dispatchable base load generation capable of continuous output inde-
34 pendent of weather variability, including advanced and conventional nuclear
35 energy technologies, hydroelectric plants, and natural gas generation; and
36 WHEREAS, Idahoans rely on affordable, dependable energy from respon-
37 sibly developed natural gas generation that provides flexible, on-demand
38 power and supports energy security through locally owned and operated re-
39 sources; and
40 WHEREAS, all major transmission infrastructure projects that mate-
41 rially affect Idaho ratepayers must demonstrate a clear, measurable, and
42 durable net benefit to Idaho ratepayers, measured by reduced long-term cost
43 exposure, strengthened in-state energy security, and tangible benefits to

2
1 Idaho's in-state energy and economic interests, before approval or cost al-
2 location is permitted at the state level.
3 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
4 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
5 the Senate concurring therein, that the Legislature of the State of Idaho
6 formally acknowledges that transmission projects built in Idaho must demon-
7 strate a clear, measurable, and durable net benefit to Idaho ratepayers as
8 demonstrated by utility-integrated resource plans.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the standards expressed in this Resolution
10 are intended by the Legislature to guide state-level permitting, siting re-
11 view, and any cost recovery proceedings before the Idaho Public Utilities
12 Commission, which ensures that Idaho ratepayers are not required to subsi-
13 dize infrastructure that does not benefit Idaho utility customers or is in-
14 consistent with Idaho's base load reliability priorities.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature affirms that private prop-
16 erty rights, local land use authority, and the protection of agricultural
17 and rural communities are paramount considerations in the siting and review
18 of any major transmission infrastructure within the State of Idaho, and that
19 the use of eminent domain should be strictly limited to the purposes allow-
20 able under Idaho law.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature supports the advancement
22 of responsibly developed conventional and advanced nuclear technologies,
23 hydroelectric generation, geothermal, and natural gas generation as the
24 proper foundation for Idaho's future energy system and as the preferred
25 means of providing secure base load energy for Idaho citizens and indus-
26 tries, including the necessary major transmission resources to move energy
27 from its generation source to Idaho citizens.
28 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature encourages Idaho-regulated
29 public utilities to continue to review and analyze utility participation in
30 any interstate energy market to ensure participation in such markets does
31 not conflict with Idaho's commitment to reliable, dispatchable base load
32 generation, ratepayer protection, private property rights, and state energy
33 sovereignty.
34 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
35 the Governor of the State of Idaho, members of the Idaho Public Utilities
36 Commission, Idaho-regulated electric utilities, and Idaho's congressional
37 delegation.
