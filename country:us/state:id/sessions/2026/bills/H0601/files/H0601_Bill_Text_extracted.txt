1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO COLLECTIVE BARGAINING; AMENDING TITLE 44, IDAHO CODE, BY THE

16 SECTION 1. That Title 44, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended

19 CHAPTER 28
20 GOVERNMENT UNIONS
21 44-2801. DECLARATION OF POLICY. The legislature of the state of Idaho
22 finds and declares that:
23 (1) Public employees are free to form and join membership organiza-
24 tions, professional associations, labor unions, or similar organizations of
25 their own choosing to advance their mutual interests.
26 (2) Unlike private sector labor relations, collective bargaining be-
27 tween government entities and labor unions representing public employees is
28 an inherently political process with significant implications for the size,
29 cost, operations, transparency, and accountability of government.
30 (3) The operation of state and local government in Idaho is to be deter-
31 mined by the people through their elected representatives rather than by un-
32 elected private interests, and therefore, no public employer should autho-
33 rize or engage in collective bargaining with labor organizations represent-
34 ing government employees unless the legislature finds collective bargaining
35 in that instance to be in the public interest and expressly authorizes it by
36 statute.
37 (4) To advance the existing policy of the state of Idaho that special
38 interests be prevented from unduly influencing governmental action and that
39 governmental functions and policies reflect, to the maximum extent possi-
40 ble, the public interest, public funds and taxpayer-funded facilities, sys-

2
1 tems, and resources should not be used to preferentially aid or subsidize,
2 directly or indirectly, the operations or advocacy of government unions.
3 44-2802. DEFINITIONS. As used in this chapter:
4 (1) "Affiliate" means any membership organization affiliated with a
5 government union or with which a government union is affiliated.
6 (2) "Collective bargaining agreement" means:
7 (a) A written agreement negotiated between a public employer and gov-
8 ernment union that addresses public employee compensation, benefits,
9 or other terms or conditions of employment; or
10 (b) A negotiated agreement governed by sections 33-1271 through
11 33-1276, Idaho Code.
12 (3) "Government union" means:
13 (a) An organization of any kind, agency, or employee representation
14 committee or union that exists for the purpose, in whole or in part, of
15 dealing with public employers concerning public employee compensation,
16 benefits, or other terms or conditions of employment; or
17 (b) A local education organization as defined in section 33-1272, Idaho
18 Code.
19 (4) "Government union activities" means activities undertaken at the
20 direction of, on behalf of, or to advance the purposes of a government union
21 or its affiliate by:
22 (a) Supporting or opposing any candidate for federal, state, or local
23 public office;
24 (b) Influencing the passage or defeat of any federal or state legisla-
25 tion, federal or state regulation, local ordinance, or any ballot mea-
26 sure;
27 (c) Promoting or soliciting membership or participation in, or finan-
28 cial support of, a government union or its affiliate;
29 (d) Seeking to become the exclusive representative of a unit of public
30 employees for the purposes of collective bargaining;
31 (e) Participating in the administration, business, or internal gover-
32 nance of a government union or its affiliate;
33 (f) Preparing, conducting, or attending government union or affili-
34 ate events, conferences, conventions, meetings, or trainings, unless
35 such training is directly related to the performance of public employ-
36 ees' job duties;
37 (g) Distributing government union or affiliate communications;
38 (h) Representing or speaking on behalf of a government union or its af-
39 filiate in any setting, venue, or procedure in which the public employer
40 is not a participant;
41 (i) Preparing, filing, or pursuing grievances;
42 (j) Representing public employees in investigatory interviews, disci-
43 plinary proceedings, or appeals, up to and including termination, or
44 other administrative or legal proceedings;
45 (k) Engaging in collective bargaining and any related mediation or ar-
46 bitration;
47 (l) Administering a collective bargaining agreement; or
48 (m) Participating in labor-management committees.
49 (5) "Public employee" means:

3
1 (a) An employee of a public employer; or
2 (b) A professional employee as defined in section 33-1272, Idaho Code.
3 (6)(a) "Public employer" means:
4 (i) The state of Idaho or an agency, commission, or other govern-
5 mental body of the state; or
6 (ii) A county, municipality, school district, including a spe-
7 cially chartered district, highway district, planning and zoning
8 commission, or any other political subdivision or local govern-
9 mental body of the state of Idaho.
10 (b) "Public employer" does not include:
11 (i) A corporate authority as defined in section 44-1801, Idaho
12 Code; or
13 (ii) A city that is governed by title 50, Idaho Code.
14 (7) "Representational activities" means those activities specified in
15 subsection (4)(i) through (m) of this section.
16 44-2803. TAXPAYER FUNDING OF GOVERNMENT UNIONS PROHIBITED. (1) No
17 public employer, its agents, or representatives may use or authorize the use
18 of taxpayer funds to promote government unions by:
19 (a) Deducting dues, fees, assessments, fines, or contributions from
20 the pay of a public employee on behalf of a government union or its af-
21 filiate;
22 (b) Increasing a public employee's compensation with the intent that
23 the additional compensation, or a part of it, be used to pay government
24 union or affiliate dues, fees, assessments, fines, or contributions;
25 (c) Providing more personal or contact information of a public employee
26 to a government union or its affiliate than the public employer is re-
27 quired to disclose pursuant to chapter 1, title 74, Idaho Code, unless
28 the public employer receives written authorization from the public em-
29 ployee to disclose additional information;
30 (d) Requiring or coercing a public employee to meet, communicate, lis-
31 ten to, or otherwise interact with a government union or its affiliate;
32 (e) Distributing communications or membership solicitations on behalf
33 of a government union or its affiliate;
34 (f) Contributing public funds to, or expending public funds on behalf
35 of, a government union or its affiliate; or
36 (g) Except as provided by sections 33-1216 and 33-1279, Idaho Code, and
37 except as otherwise provided by this section, providing any form of com-
38 pensation or paid leave to a public employee, directly or indirectly,
39 for the purpose of engaging in government union activities.
40 (2) If provided by a collective bargaining agreement between a public
41 employer and a government union:
42 (a) A public employee may be granted time off without pay or benefits to
43 engage in government union activities, and a government union may com-
44 pensate a public employee for engaging in government union activities.
45 (b) A public employee may use compensated personal leave, whether the
46 public employee's own leave or leave voluntarily donated by other pub-
47 lic employees, to engage in government union activities if:

4
1 (i) The leave is accrued at the same rate by similarly situated
2 public employees of the public employer without regard to member-
3 ship or participation in a government union; and
4 (ii) The public employee may freely choose how to use the leave.
5 (c) A public employee may engage in representational activities while
6 in a duty status without loss of pay or benefits if:
7 (i) The government union reports to the public employer no less
8 than twice per calendar year the amount of time, in increments
9 rounded to the nearest quarter of an hour, spent on representa-
10 tional activities each day by each public employee engaged in
11 representational activities;
12 (ii) The public employer calculates the pro rata value of compen-
13 sation, including wages and benefits, paid or accruing to a pub-
14 lic employee for time spent engaged in representational activi-
15 ties and provides an invoice to the government union no less than
16 twice per calendar year for the amounts so calculated; and
17 (iii) Upon receipt of the invoice from the public employer, the
18 government union remits full payment to the public employer within
19 thirty (30) days.
20 44-2804. ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES.
21 (1)(a) A person who violates the provisions of section 44-2803, Idaho
22 Code, shall be subject to a civil penalty not to exceed two hundred and
23 fifty dollars ($250).
24 (b) A person who knowingly violates the provisions of section 44-2803,
25 Idaho Code, shall be subject to a civil penalty not to exceed one thou-
26 sand five hundred dollars ($1,500).
27 (c) A person who knowingly violates the provisions of section 44-2803,
28 Idaho Code, and who has previously admitted to committing or has been
29 previously determined to have committed a violation pursuant to para-
30 graph (b) of this subsection shall be subject to a civil penalty not to
31 exceed two thousand five hundred dollars ($2,500).
32 (2) The attorney general or appropriate county prosecutor shall inves-
33 tigate complaints regarding the violation of a provision of section 44-2803,
34 Idaho Code, and shall ensure the effective enforcement of this section.
35 44-2805. APPLICATION. Nothing in section 44-2803, Idaho Code, shall
36 be construed to:
37 (1) Authorize a public employer, who does not independently possess
38 such authority, to engage in collective bargaining with a government union.
39 (2) Limit the right of firefighters to collectively bargain as provided
40 by chapter 18, title 44, Idaho Code.
41 (3) Limit any right a city may possess to authorize and engage in col-
42 lective bargaining pursuant to the provisions of title 50, Idaho Code.
43 (4) Require a public employer to take actions that would violate the
44 provisions of 49 U.S.C. 5333(b) or jeopardize its continued eligibility to
45 receive federal transit administration funding.

12 SECTION 4. That Section 33-1275, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby

14 33-1275. TERMS OF AGREEMENTS. (1) All agreements, by any name or ti-
15 tle, entered into pursuant to the provisions of this act, shall have a one
16 (1) year duration of July 1 through June 30 of the ensuing fiscal year. The
17 parties shall not have the authority to enter into any agreement negotiated
18 under the provisions of this act that has any term that allows for such agree-
19 ment or any provision of such agreement to be in any force or effect for mul-
20 tiple years or indefinitely, or otherwise does not expire on its own terms on
21 or before June 30 of the ensuing fiscal year.
22 (2) Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (1) of this section,
23 upon mutual ratification, any item other than compensation and benefits as
24 defined in subsection (3) of this section of any agreement entered into pur-
25 suant to this act may have a nonrolling two (2) year duration with a desig-
26 nated start date and end date. A second year term for any item not defined in
27 subsection (3) of this section cannot be added, automatically or by mutual
28 consent, back into the agreement after the expiration of the first year but
29 rather may be addressed by the parties at the expiration of the end date of
30 the two (2) year term.
31 (3) For purposes of this section, "compensation" means salary and ben-
32 efits for professional employees. "Benefits" means employee insurance,
33 leave time and sick leave benefits.
34 (4) No agreement by any name or title that is entered into pursuant to
35 the provisions of this section or otherwise may require or permit a school
36 district, including specially chartered districts, to use taxpayer funding
37 to promote a local education organization or its affiliate in a manner pro-
38 hibited by section 44-2803, Idaho Code. Any provision of an agreement in vi-
39 olation of this subsection shall be null and void, contrary to public policy,
40 and of no legal force or effect.
41 SECTION 5. SEVERABILITY. The provisions of this act are hereby declared
42 to be severable and if any provision of this act or the application of such
43 provision to any person or circumstance is declared invalid for any reason,
44 such declaration shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of
45 this act.
