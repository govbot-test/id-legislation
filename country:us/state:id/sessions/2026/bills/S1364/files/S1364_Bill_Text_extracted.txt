LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1364
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO ADMINISTRATIVE RULES; AMENDING SECTION 67-5226, IDAHO CODE, TO
3 REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING THE EXPIRATION OF TEMPORARY RULES; AMEND-
4 ING SECTION 67-5291, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING THE EX-
5 PIRATION OF TEMPORARY RULES; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING
6 AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 67-5226, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 67-5226. TEMPORARY RULES. (1) If the governor finds that:
11 (a) Protection of the public health, safety, or welfare; or
12 (b) Compliance with deadlines in amendments to governing law or federal
13 programs; or
14 (c) Reducing a regulatory burden that would otherwise impact individu-
15 als or businesses;
16 requires a rule to become effective before it has been submitted for review,
17 the agency may proceed with such notice as is practicable and adopt a tem-
18 porary rule, except as otherwise provided in section 67-5229(1)(d), Idaho
19 Code. The agency may make the temporary rule immediately effective. The
20 agency shall incorporate the required finding and a concise statement of its
21 supporting reasons in each rule adopted in reliance upon the provisions of
22 this subsection.
23 (2) A pending fee rule adopted pursuant to subsection (1) of this sec-
24 tion may become effective under this section before it has been approved by
25 concurrent resolution only if the governor finds that the fee or charge is
26 necessary to avoid immediate danger.
27 (3) Temporary rules shall be published in the first available issue of
28 the bulletin.
29 (4) Temporary rules are not subject to the requirements of section
30 67-5223, Idaho Code, provided that the coordinator sends a copy of the tempo-
31 rary rules to the director of the legislative services office.
32 (5) Concurrently with the promulgation of a rule under this section, or
33 as soon as reasonably possible thereafter, an agency shall commence the pro-
34 mulgation of a proposed rule in accordance with the rulemaking requirements
35 of this chapter, unless the temporary rule adopted by the agency will expire
36 by its own terms or by operation of law pursuant to section 67-5291, Idaho
37 Code, before the proposed rule could become final.
38 SECTION 2. That Section 67-5291, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
39 amended to read as follows:

2
1 67-5291. LEGISLATIVE REVIEW OF RULES. (1) A standing committee of the
2 legislature shall review any temporary, pending, or final rule that is ger-
3 mane to its committee and has been published in the bulletin or in the admin-
4 istrative code to determine if the rule is consistent with the legislative
5 intent of the statute that the rule was written to interpret, prescribe, im-
6 plement, or enforce. If a reviewed rule is approved, the standing committee
7 that approves the rule shall report its findings and recommendations to the
8 body. If ordered by the presiding officer, the committee's report shall be
9 printed in the journal.
10 (2)(a) All temporary, pending, and final rules of any nature may be ap-
11 proved or rejected by a concurrent resolution of the legislature. The
12 concurrent resolution shall state the effective date of the approval or
13 rejection.
14 (b) The legislature may reject a rule, in whole or in part, where the
15 legislature determines that the rule, or part of the rule, is not con-
16 sistent with the legislative intent of the statute that the rule was
17 written to interpret, prescribe, implement, or enforce. When rejecting
18 a rule, the legislature shall make a finding of facts as to why the rule
19 does not meet the legislative intent of the enabling statute by identi-
20 fying how the rule is inconsistent with the authority granted by or the
21 requirements of the corresponding section of Idaho Code. For purposes
22 of this section, "part of the rule" means a provision in a rule that is
23 designated either numerically or alphabetically or the entirety of any
24 new or amended language contained therein. The rejection of a rule in
25 whole or in part shall terminate the rule, in whole or in part, as of the
26 effective date of the rejection. An agency shall not, subsequent to the
27 rejection, issue a proposed rule that is substantially similar to the
28 rejected rule unless it is consistent with the legislative intent of the
29 statute as expressed in the concurrent resolution.
30 (c) The secretary of state shall immediately notify the affected agency
31 of the filing and effective date of any concurrent resolution enacted to
32 approve or reject, in whole or in part, an agency rule. When an agency
33 rule has been partially rejected, the secretary of state shall trans-
34 mit a copy of the concurrent resolution to the director of the agency for
35 promulgation of the rule as amended.
36 (d) The agency shall be responsible for implementing legislative in-
37 tent as expressed in the concurrent resolution, including, as appropri-
38 ate, the reinstatement of the prior rule in whole or in part, if any, in
39 the case of a resolution rejecting a rule in whole or in part. The agency
40 shall publish notice of rejection of a rule in whole or in part in the
41 bulletin.
42 (3) A temporary rule that is not approved by a concurrent resolution
43 shall expire by its own terms or at adjournment sine die of the next succeed-
44 ing regular session of the legislature on June 30 of the year following the
45 temporary rule's adoption, whichever date is earlier.
46 (4) Except as set forth in sections 67-5226 and 67-5228, Idaho Code, a
47 pending fee rule that is not approved by a concurrent resolution shall expire
48 upon adjournment sine die of the legislative session during which the agency
49 submits the pending fee rule to the legislature for review.

1 (5) Except as set forth in sections 67-5226 and 67-5228, Idaho Code, a
