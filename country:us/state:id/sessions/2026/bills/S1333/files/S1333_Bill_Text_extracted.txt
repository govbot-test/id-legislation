16 CHAPTER 54
17 VIRTUAL CURRENCY KIOSK FRAUD PREVENTION ACT
18 28-5401. SHORT TITLE. This act shall be known and may be cited as the
19 "Virtual Currency Kiosk Fraud Prevention Act."
20 28-5402. DEFINITIONS. For the purposes of this chapter:
21 (1) "Blockchain analytics" means the analysis of data from blockchains
22 or public distributed ledgers, including associated transaction informa-
23 tion.
24 (2) "Blockchain analytics and tracing software" means a software ser-
25 vice that uses blockchain analytics data to provide risk-specific informa-
26 tion and tracing of virtual currency wallet addresses.
27 (3) "Department" means the Idaho department of finance.
28 (4) "Fraud" or "fraudulent" means an intentional deception or misrep-
29 resentation made by a person with the knowledge that the deception could re-
30 sult in some unauthorized benefit to himself or some other person.
31 (5) "New customer" means a user who is transacting with an operator:
32 (a) For the first time; or
33 (b) Within seventy-two (72) hours after such user's first transaction
34 with the operator.
35 (6) "Operator" means a person who owns, operates, or manages a virtual
36 currency kiosk located in the United States or in any United States terri-
37 tory.
38 (7) "Potentially vulnerable older adult" means a person sixty-five
39 (65) years of age or older who is unable to protect himself from maltreatment
40 because of:

2
1 (a) A mental, physical, or developmental disability;
2 (b) A degenerative brain disease;
3 (c) An inability to communicate or implement decisions regarding his
4 person; or
5 (d) Other infirmities of aging in an older adult.
6 (8) "Transaction fees" means fees or expenses paid by a user in order
7 to conduct a virtual currency kiosk transaction. Transaction fees equal the
8 difference between the market price of the virtual currency and the price of
9 the virtual currency charged to the user.
10 (9) "Transaction hash" means the unique alphanumeric identifier as-
11 signed to a virtual currency kiosk transaction.
12 (10) "User" means any individual or entity that initiates, authorizes,
13 or completes a transaction involving virtual currency through a virtual cur-
14 rency kiosk, whether for the purpose of purchasing, selling, transferring,
15 or otherwise exchanging virtual currency.
16 (11) "Virtual currency" means a type of digital asset, as defined in
17 section 28-5303, Idaho Code, that confers economic, proprietary, or access
18 rights or powers and is recorded using cryptographically secured dis-
19 tributed ledger technology or any similar analogue.
20 (12) "Virtual currency address" means an alphanumeric identifier asso-
21 ciated with a virtual currency wallet identifying the location to which a
22 virtual currency transaction can be sent.
23 (13) "Virtual currency kiosk" means a stand-alone machine that is ca-
24 pable of accepting or dispensing legal tender in exchange for virtual cur-
25 rency.
26 (14) "Virtual currency kiosk transaction" means a transaction con-
27 ducted or performed, in whole or in part, by electronic means via a virtual
28 currency kiosk. Virtual currency kiosk transaction also means a transaction
29 made at a virtual currency kiosk to purchase virtual currency with or to sell
30 virtual currency for United States dollars.
31 (15) "Virtual currency wallet" means a software application or other
32 mechanism providing a means to hold the keys necessary to access and transfer
33 virtual currency.
34 28-5403. OPERATOR LICENSING REQUIREMENT. In order for an operator to
35 engage in or hold itself out as being able to engage in a virtual currency
36 kiosk transaction, such operator must be a licensed money transmitter pur-
37 suant to the provisions of chapter 29, title 26, Idaho Code.
38 28-5404. OPERATOR REPORTING REQUIREMENTS. (1) An operator shall com-
39 plete, collect, and maintain quarterly reports and any supporting records
40 for each virtual currency kiosk that it operates in Idaho. Such reports and
41 records shall be made available to the department, upon request, in a format
42 specified by the department, within three (3) business days of such request.
43 Reports pursuant to this subsection shall include, at a minimum, the follow-
44 ing information:
45 (a) The operator's legal name;
46 (b) Any fictitious or trade name used by the operator, if applicable;
47 (c) The operator's physical address;

1 (d) The date the operator commenced operation of the virtual currency
2 kiosk at the location;
3 (e) The date the operator ceased operation of the virtual currency
4 kiosk at the location, if applicable;
5 (f) Each address used by the operator to provide customer service in
6 Idaho; and
7 (g) The number of transactions that the operator declined due to suspi-
8 cion of fraud or other illegal activity.
9 (2) An operator shall complete, collect, and maintain annual reports
10 and any supporting records for each virtual currency kiosk it operates in
11 Idaho, to be completed on or before March 31, relating to the operator's
12 business conducted in the prior year. Such reports and records shall be made
13 available to the department, upon request, in a format specified by the de-

15 to this subsection shall be on a form prescribed by the department and shall
16 include, at a minimum:
17 (a) Gross revenue attributable to virtual currency transactions con-
18 ducted via virtual currency kiosks in the state;
19 (b) A copy of any complaint filed against the operator with the bet-

23 (c) The total number and value of virtual currency transactions con-

28 (e) Contact details of the operator's compliance officer;
29 (f) A dedicated United States phone number and email address for commu-
30 nications with government fraud investigators and law enforcement; and
31 (g) The total number of virtual currency corresponding locations.
32 (3) The phone number and email address required pursuant to subsection
33 (2)(f) of this section shall be frequently monitored. Upon request from law
34 enforcement or the department, a virtual currency kiosk operator shall pro-
35 vide trace findings and grant assistance with blockchain analytics for in-

38 department, the virtual currency kiosk operator shall provide any transac-

41 (5) Transaction data collected by the department pursuant to this sec-
42 tion shall be confidential and may be released only in composite form, unless

44 (6) Failure to complete, collect, or maintain a report or record as re-
45 quired pursuant to this section, or to timely provide such reports or records
46 to the department, shall constitute a violation of this chapter.

48 relevant terms and conditions generally associated with the products, ser-
49 vices, and activities of the operator and virtual currency, including but

22 currency transactions shall be disclosed to each user of a virtual currency

25 (a) The name, address, and telephone number of the operator;
26 (b) The days and times the operator is available and the methods that a

29 shall be displayed on or at the location of the virtual currency kiosk or
30 on the first screen of such kiosk.
31 28-5406. CUSTOMER SERVICE REQUIREMENTS. (1) An operator shall provide
32 live customer service at a toll-free number seven (7) days a week. Daily
33 business hours shall be from, at a minimum, 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. local time
34 for all Idaho locations where the operator's virtual currency kiosks are lo-
35 cated.
36 (2) Trained representatives of an operator shall be available to re-
37 spond to customer telephone inquiries during the business hours required
38 pursuant to this section.
39 (3) Inquiries received after normal business hours shall be responded
40 to by a trained representative on the next business day.
41 28-5407. TRANSACTION RECEIPTS. (1) An operator shall provide each
42 user with paper and electronic receipts at the conclusion of each virtual
43 currency kiosk transaction.

12 28-5408. PREVENTION OF FRAUDULENT ACTIVITY AND MONEY LAUNDERING. (1)
13 An operator shall take reasonable steps to detect and prevent fraud and money
14 laundering. This shall include:
15 (a) Establishing and maintaining a written anti-fraud policy;
16 (b) Using blockchain analytics and tracing software to assist in the
17 prevention of sending virtual currency to a virtual currency wallet
18 known or likely to be affiliated with fraudulent activity at the time of
19 a transaction and to detect transaction patterns indicative of fraud or
20 other illicit activities;
21 (c) Blocking transactions to virtual currency wallets associated
22 with overseas exchanges non-accessible for users located in the United
23 States;
24 (d) Not accepting from or dispensing to a new customer an amount that
25 is more than two thousand dollars ($2,000) in United States currency,
26 or its equivalent in virtual currency, during any twenty-four (24) hour
27 period;
28 (e) Ensuring that during the thirty (30) calendar days following a new
29 customer's first transaction, the cumulative total of all transactions
30 with an operator shall not exceed ten thousand dollars ($10,000) in U.S.
31 currency or its equivalent in virtual currency; and
32 (f) Implementing the following protections for new customers:
33 (i) Delaying settlement of a new customer's first virtual cur-
34 rency kiosk transaction for seventy-two (72) hours; and
35 (ii) Allowing, upon request, for the full refund of any fraudulent
36 virtual currency kiosk transaction experienced by a new customer
37 within seventy-two (72) hours after such transaction, if such
38 user:
39 1. Contacts the operator in writing to provide notice of the
40 fraudulent nature of the virtual currency kiosk transac-
41 tion; and
42 2. Files a report with a government or law enforcement
43 agency memorializing the fraudulent nature of the virtual
44 currency kiosk transaction.
45 (2) A refund pursuant to subsection (1)(f) this section shall include
46 all transaction fees paid by a new customer at the time of the transaction
47 and shall occur within twenty-one (21) days after such new customer provides
48 notice of a fraudulent virtual currency transaction. A refund pursuant to
49 this section shall be paid to a user in the user's originating currency.

13 28-5409. TRANSACTION FEE REFUNDS. (1) Subject to the requirements of
14 this section, an operator shall issue a refund of all transaction fees paid
15 by a user at the time of a transaction if such user experiences fraud with re-
16 spect to such transaction. Such refund shall be issued notwithstanding any
17 acknowledgment that a user may have made prior to finalizing such transac-
18 tion.
19 (2) A user who seeks a refund pursuant to the provisions of this section
20 shall:
21 (a) Have engaged in a transaction or transactions involving the virtual
22 currency kiosk that were affected by fraud, whether authorized or unau-
23 thorized;
24 (b) Contact the virtual currency kiosk operator to inform the opera-
25 tor of the fraudulent nature of the transaction or transactions at issue
26 within thirty (30) days after the last transaction to occur or becoming
27 aware of the theft; and
28 (c) Within thirty (30) days after contacting the virtual kiosk opera-
29 tor, submit a police report, government agency report, or a sworn state-
30 ment detailing the fraudulent nature of the transaction or transactions
31 and provide a copy to the virtual currency kiosk operator.
32 (3) If conditions for refund issuance pursuant to this section are met,
33 an operator shall issue a full refund of transaction fees within seventy-two
34 (72) hours after receiving notice from a user and receiving a copy of the po-
35 lice report, government agency report, or sworn statement.
36 (4) A refund pursuant to this section shall be paid to a user in the
37 user's originating currency.
38 (5) The refund available pursuant to this section is distinct from the
39 refund available to protect new customers pursuant to section 28-5408, Idaho
40 Code, and such refunds shall be exclusive of each other.
41 28-5410. PENALTIES. (1) A violation of this chapter shall be deemed an
42 unfair and deceptive practice in violation of the Idaho consumer protection
43 act, chapter 6, title 48, Idaho Code.
44 (2) The attorney general may undertake any and all actions authorized
45 by the Idaho consumer protection act to investigate and enforce a violation
46 of this chapter.
47 (3) A person harmed by a violation of this chapter may initiate a civil
48 action and may seek remedies pursuant to section 48-608, Idaho Code.

7
1 (4) In addition to any other penalties:
2 (a) An operator who violates this chapter shall be subject to revoca-
3 tion of such operator's money transmitter licensure pursuant to the
4 provisions of chapter 29, title 26, Idaho Code; and
5 (b) An operator who willfully and knowingly violates this chapter shall
6 be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, may be sentenced to pay
7 a fine not exceeding one thousand dollars ($1,000).
8 28-5411. INFORMATION-SHARING RELATED TO VIRTUAL CURRENCY KIOSK
9 FRAUD. (1) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, upon rea-
10 sonable suspicion of fraud or financial exploitation involving a virtual
11 currency kiosk transaction, the department, the attorney general, any state
12 or local law enforcement agency, the Idaho commission on aging, or an adult
13 protective services provider may share relevant information relating to
14 such suspicion for the purposes of investigation, enforcement, or consumer
15 protection.
16 (2) If the department is conducting an investigation or becomes aware
17 of an ongoing investigation involving suspected fraud or financial ex-
18 ploitation related to a virtual currency kiosk transaction, the department
19 shall share relevant information with the attorney general and any state or
20 local law enforcement agency with jurisdiction and with the Idaho commission
21 on aging or an adult protective services provider when the affected individ-
22 ual is a potentially vulnerable older adult.
23 (3) Information shared pursuant to this section that is confidential or
24 exempt from disclosure under chapter 1, title 74, Idaho Code, shall retain
25 its confidential status and shall not be subject to public disclosure except
26 as otherwise authorized by law.
27 (4) Any agency, political subdivision, or employee thereof who acts in
28 good faith when sharing information pursuant to this section shall be immune
29 from any civil or criminal liability arising from the sharing of such infor-
30 mation.
