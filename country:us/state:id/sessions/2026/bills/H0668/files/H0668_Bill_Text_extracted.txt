LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 668
BY JUDICIARY, RULES AND ADMINISTRATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO CHILD CUSTODY INTERFERENCE; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT;
3 AMENDING SECTION 18-4506, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PROVISIONS REGARD-
4 ING CHILD CUSTODY INTERFERENCE AND TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING
5 PENALTIES AND ENFORCEMENT; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN
6 EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent of the Legislature to
9 strengthen Idaho's child custody interference law by clarifying enforcement
10 duties, providing uniform guidance, establishing objective timelines for
11 response and compliance, and implementing a graduated penalty structure
12 that ensures proportional accountability. The Legislature affirms that
13 child custody interference is a criminal offense under Title 18, Idaho Code,
14 and is distinct from kidnapping. The Legislature further intends to ensure
15 that children subject to custodial interference receive prompt welfare
16 checks.
17 SECTION 2. That Section 18-4506, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
18 amended to read as follows:
19 18-4506. CHILD CUSTODY INTERFERENCE DEFINED -- DEFENSES -- PUNISH-
20 MENT. 1. (1) A person commits child custody interference if the person,
21 whether a parent or other, or agent of that person, intentionally and without
22 lawful authority:
23 (a) Takes, entices away, keeps or withholds any minor child from a
24 parent or another person or institution having custody, joint cus-
25 tody, visitation or other parental rights, whether such rights arise
26 from temporary or permanent custody order, or from the equal custodial
27 rights of each parent in the absence of a custody order; or
28 (b) Takes, entices away, keeps or withholds a minor child from a parent
29 after commencement of an action relating to child visitation or custody
30 but prior to the issuance of an order determining custody or visitation
31 rights.
32 2. (2) It shall be an affirmative defense to a violation of the provi-
33 sions of subsection 1. (1) of this section and the charged party has the bur-
34 den to establish that:
35 (a) The action is taken to protect the child from imminent physical
36 harm;
37 (b) The action is taken by a parent fleeing from imminent physical harm
38 to himself;
39 (c) The action is consented to by the lawful custodian of the child; or
40 (d) The child is returned within twenty-four (24) hours after expira-
41 tion of an authorized visitation privilege.

37 (5)(a) A person guilty of child custody interference shall be punished
38 as follows:
39 (i) For a first offense, such person shall be subject to an in-
40 fraction punishable by a fine not to exceed one hundred dollars

42 (ii) For a second offense, such person shall be subject to an in-

45 (iii) For a third and fourth offense, such person shall be guilty

48 such fine and imprisonment.
49 (iv) For a fifth offense and subsequent offenses, such person
