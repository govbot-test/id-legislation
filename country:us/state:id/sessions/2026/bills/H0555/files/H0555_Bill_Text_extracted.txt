16 SECTION 3. That Section 39-7401, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
17 amended to read as follows:
18 39-7401. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND PURPOSES. (1) The legislature
19 finds:
20 (a) That adverse public health and environmental impacts can result
21 from the improper land disposal of solid waste and that the need for es-
22 tablishing safe sites with adequate capacity for the disposal of solid
23 waste is a matter of statewide concern and necessity; and
24 (b) That the resource conservation and recovery act, (42 U.S.C. sec.
25 6901, et seq.), as amended, and regulations adopted pursuant thereto,
26 establish complex, detailed, and costly provisions for the location,
27 design, operation, and monitoring of solid waste disposal sites, in-
28 cluding such sites as may be operated pursuant to the responsibility es-
29 tablished in chapter 44, title 31, Idaho Code; and
30 (c) That a state program to implement flexible standards provided in
31 40 CFR 258, if approved by the U.S. environmental protection agency,
32 enables a state to take advantage of site specific factors in the de-
33 sign and, operation, closure, post-closure, and financial assurance of
34 solid waste facilities and flexibility in meeting federal criteria set
35 forth in that regulation; and
36 (d) That 40 CFR 258 provides that such a program of flexible standards
37 requires approvals by a designated state agency; and
38 (e) That chapter 1, title 39, Idaho Code, vests the Idaho department of
39 environmental quality with the responsibility to issue a certificate of
40 suitability concerning prospective solid waste landfill sites, to ap-
41 prove solid waste facility design and ground water monitoring programs,
42 and to provide approvals pursuant to 40 CFR 258; and
43 (f) That chapter 44, title 31, Idaho Code, imposes on the counties the
44 primary responsibility for the development and operation of a solid
45 waste management system; and
46 (g) That chapter 4 74, title 39, Idaho Code, vests the health districts
47 Idaho department of environmental quality with the primary responsi-
48 bility for the review and approval of solid waste facility operations

8
1 plans operations, closure, post-closure, and corrective action plans,
2 and the enforcement of solid waste management operations; and
3 (h) That the coordination and timeliness of response to federal law on
4 the part of all public officials within the state is critical to compli-
5 ance with federal regulations, the ability of each affected agency to
6 carry out their statutory responsibilities, and the avoidance of exces-
7 sive construction and public expenditures.
8 (2) Therefore, it is the intent of the legislature to establish a
9 program of solid waste management which complies with 40 CFR 258 federal
10 regulations and facilitates the incorporation of flexible standards in
11 facility design and, operations , closure, post-closure, and corrective ac-
12 tion. The legislature hereby establishes the solid waste disposal standards
13 and procedures outlined herein and a facility approval process for the state
14 of Idaho, the political subdivisions thereof, and any private solid waste
15 disposal site owner in order to facilitate the development and operation of
16 solid waste disposal sites, to effect timely and responsible completion of
17 statutory duties, and to ensure protection of human health and the environ-
18 ment, to and protect the air, land, and waters of the state of Idaho.
19 SECTION 4. That Section 39-7402, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
20 amended to read as follows:
21 39-7402. APPLICABILITY. (1) The standards and procedures set forth in
22 this chapter apply to owners and operators of new municipal solid waste land-
23 fill (MSWLF) units, existing MSWLF units, and lateral expansions of existing
24 MSWLF units, except as otherwise specifically provided.
25 (2) The requirements of this chapter do not apply to MSWLF units that
26 ceased to accept waste on or prior to October 9, 1991.
27 (3) MSWLF units that receive waste after October 9, 1991, but stop
28 receiving waste in conformance with the provisions of 40 CFR 258.1(d), are
29 exempt from the requirements of this chapter, except as expressly provided
30 herein.
31 (4) All MSWLF units that receive waste on or after October 9, 1993, must
32 comply with all of the requirements of this chapter, unless otherwise al-
33 lowed in 40 CFR 258.1(d), (e), or (f).
34 (5) MSWLF units failing to satisfy these standards shall cease oper-
35 ation and shall not accept municipal solid waste for disposal by order of
36 the department of environmental quality and/or the district health depart-
37 ment until provisions of this chapter are complied with, unless a compliance
38 schedule or corrective action plan has been approved by the director of the
39 department of environmental quality and/or the district health department.
40 (6) MSWLF units failing to satisfy the requirements set forth in this
41 chapter are considered open dumps for purposes of state solid waste manage-
42 ment planning and are prohibited under section 4005 of RCRA.
43 (7) MSWLF units containing sewage sludge and which fail to satisfy the
44 criteria set forth in 40 CFR 258 violate sections 309 and 405(e) of the clean
45 water act.
