LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 114
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND PROVIDING FOR THE ADDITION OF A NEW
3 JOINT RULE 24 OF THE JOINT RULES OF THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESEN-
4 TATIVES OF THE STATE OF IDAHO.
5 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 WHEREAS, the Idaho Legislature values the principles of limited and ef-
7 ficient government; and
8 WHEREAS, the consideration of a large volume of legislation is not nec-
9 essarily compatible with Idaho's part-time, citizen legislature; and
10 WHEREAS, at least 24 state legislative chambers have undertaken mea-
11 sures to reduce the amount of legislation entering the legislative process
12 in order to allow legislators to more efficiently consider and debate the
13 most important issues facing their states; and
14 WHEREAS, the Senate and the House of Representatives deem it necessary
15 and desirable to add a new Joint Rule 24 to the Joint Rules of the Senate and
16 the House of Representatives.
17 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular Ses-
18 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of Rep-
19 resentatives concurring therein, that the Joint Rules of the Senate and the
20 House of Representatives shall be amended by the addition thereto of a new
21 Joint Rule 24 to read as follows:
22 JOINT RULE 24
23 LIMITATIONS ON DRAFTING REQUESTS AND ROUTING SLIPS -- Except as other-
24 wise provided for in this rule, each member of the Legislature may request
25 from the Legislative Services Office the drafting of no more than 25 pieces
26 of draft legislation and no more than 10 completed routing slips (RSs) during
27 the period from July 1 through June 30 of each year.
28 For the purposes of this rule, variations of the same draft legislation
29 revised through the drafting process shall count as a single piece of draft
30 legislation, and whenever a completed RS is revised as a change RS or as a new
31 RS, it shall count as a single RS. Additionally, the limitations provided for
32 in this rule shall not apply:
33 (a) If an exception is expressly granted in writing by the Senate Pro
34 Tempore, for members of the Senate, or the Speaker of the House of Represen-
35 tatives, for members of the House of Representatives;
36 (b) To legislation pertaining to appropriations drafted by the Budget
37 and Policy Division of the Legislative Services Office;
38 (c) To drafts or RSs of amendments or trailer bills; or
39 (d) To legislation sponsored by legislative interim committees.
