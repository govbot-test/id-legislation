17 SECTION 3. That Section 41-5603, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
18 amended to read as follows:
19 41-5603. INTEREST PAYMENTS.
20 (1)(a) An insurer that fails to pay, request additional information
21 or documentation, or deny a claim from a beneficiary, practitioner or
22 facility within the time periods established in this chapter shall pay
23 interest at the contract statutory rate pursuant to section 28-22-104,
24 Idaho Code, on the unpaid amount of a claim that is determined to be due
25 and owing. The interest shall accrue from the date payment was due,
26 pursuant to the provisions of this chapter, until the claim is paid.
27 Payment of any interest amount of less than four dollars ($4.00) shall
28 not be required. Insurers may add any interest due to a future payment
29 to the beneficiary, practitioner or facility shall be required to pay
30 interest upon payment of the claim if the director, after receiving a
31 complaint regarding noncompliance with the provisions of this chapter,
32 determines after investigating and reviewing the complaint that:
33 (i) The provider has acted in good faith and attempted to provide
34 all information requested by the insurer in a timely manner; and
35 (ii) The insurer has not acted in good faith and has not been
36 forthcoming with all documentation necessary to pay or deny the
37 claim.
38 (b) Interest shall begin accruing from the date a complaint is filed
39 with the department.
40 (2) Interest shall accrue at the following compounding annual rates:
41 (a) Ten percent (10%) per annum for the first one hundred eighty (180)
42 days after the payment deadline; and
43 (b) Fifteen percent (15%) per annum for any period after the initial one
44 hundred eighty (180) days following the payment deadline.
45 (3) Interest owed shall be in addition to the claim amount and may not be
46 offset against future payments.

1 41-5605. EXCEPTIONS. (1) The time periods set forth in section

21 in section 41-5602, Idaho Code, if the fee or premium entitling a beneficiary

9 SECTION 6. That Chapter 56, Title 41, Idaho Code, be, and the same is

11 ignated as Section 41-5607, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 41-5607. CIVIL ACTIONS -- PROHIBITED RETALIATORY CONDUCT BY INSUR-
13 ERS. (1) A practitioner or facility may commence a civil action in a court
14 of competent jurisdiction against an insurer for violations of the provi-
15 sions of this chapter. If a practitioner or facility proves that an insurer
16 violated the provisions of this chapter, such practitioner or facility is
17 entitled to recover:
18 (a) Injunctive relief;
19 (b) Actual damages;
20 (c) Accrued interest; and
21 (d) Reasonable costs and attorney's fees.
22 (2)(a) An insurer shall not retaliate against a practitioner or facil-
23 ity for the exercise of rights pursuant to this chapter.
24 (b) Prohibited retaliatory actions include reducing reimbursement,
25 altering network status, increasing administrative burdens, delaying
26 credentialing, modifying utilization criteria, or any other conduct
27 reasonably interpretable as dissuading a practitioner or facility from
28 exercising statutory rights.
29 (c) Retaliation shall constitute an unfair claim-settlement practice.

1 (1)(a) An insurer that uses automated decision tools, algorithms, ar-
2 tificial intelligence, or similar technologies to assist with claims
3 intake, review, adjudication, payment determination, or denial, par-
4 tial denial, or other adverse payment determination shall disclose, in
5 a standardized manner, whether such tools materially assisted in the
6 determination of a claim submitted under this chapter.
7 (b) Disclosure pursuant to this subsection shall be limited to the fact
8 of use of such tools and the general function for which they were used
9 and shall not require disclosure of proprietary systems, algorithms,
10 prompts, models, workflows, thresholds, scoring criteria, or trade se-
11 crets.
12 (c) The use of automated decision tools, algorithms, artificial in-
13 telligence, or similar technologies shall not relieve an insurer of re-
14 sponsibility for the accuracy, timeliness, and statutory compliance of
15 any claim determination made under this chapter or excuse noncompliance
16 with any provision of this chapter.
17 (d) A disclosure made pursuant to this subsection shall not, by itself:
18 (i) Constitute evidence of an improper claims practice;
19 (ii) Create a presumption that a claim determination was incor-
20 rect, arbitrary, or unlawful;
21 (iii) Extend or toll any statutory time frames applicable under
22 this chapter;
23 (iv) Create a right to appeal; or
24 (v) Require additional review, reconsideration, or appeal beyond
25 those otherwise provided by law.
26 (2)(a) A practitioner or facility that uses automated documentation
27 tools, coding assistance tools, artificial intelligence, or similar
28 technologies to assist with clinical dictation, medical record genera-
29 tion, or claim coding shall disclose, in a standardized manner, whether
30 such tools materially assisted in the preparation of a claim submitted
31 under this chapter.
32 (b) Disclosure pursuant to this subsection shall be limited to the fact
33 of use of such tools and shall not require disclosure of proprietary
34 systems, algorithms, prompts, models, workflows, or clinical deci-
35 sion-making processes.
36 (c) Use of automated documentation tools, coding assistance tools,
37 artificial intelligence, or similar technologies shall not relieve a
38 practitioner or facility of responsibility for the accuracy, complete-
39 ness, and truthfulness of information submitted in a claim.
40 (d) An insurer shall not impose additional claim submission require-
41 ments, prepayment review, audits, or utilization management controls
42 solely on the basis that a practitioner or facility disclosed use of au-
43 tomated documentation tools, coding assistance tools, artificial in-
44 telligence, or similar technologies.
45 (e) A disclosure made pursuant to this subsection shall not, by itself:
46 (i) Constitute a material defect or impropriety in a claim;
47 (ii) Create a presumption of fraud, misrepresentation, or im-
48 proper billing;
49 (iii) Justify denial of a claim, delay of payment, or extension of
50 statutory time frames under this chapter; or
