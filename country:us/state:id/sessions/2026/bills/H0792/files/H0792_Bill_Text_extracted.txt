11 63-3622K. OCCASIONAL SALES. (a) There are exempted from the taxes im-
12 posed by this chapter occasional sales of tangible personal property.
13 (b) As used in this section, the term "occasional sales" means:
14 (1) A sale of property not held or used by a person in the course of an
15 activity for which he is required to hold a seller's permit, provided
16 such sale is not one (1) of a series of sales sufficient in number or
17 of such a nature as to constitute the seller a "retailer" under section
18 63-3610(c), Idaho Code. The definition of "occasional sales" provided
19 in this subsection does not apply to use tax in regard to tangible per-
20 sonal property used to improve real property when such property is ob-
21 tained, directly or indirectly, from a person in the business of making
22 like or similar improvements to real property.
23 (2) Any transfer of all or substantially all of the property held or
24 used by a person in a business requiring a seller's permit when, after
25 such transfer, the real or ultimate ownership of such property is sub-
26 stantially similar to that which existed before such transfer. For the
27 purpose of this section, stockholders, bondholders, partners, or other
28 persons holding an interest in a corporation or other entity are re-
29 garded as having a "real or ultimate ownership" of the property of such
30 corporation or other entity.
31 (3) A transfer of capital assets to or by a business when the transfer

35 to:

16 SECTION 2. That Section 63-3622XX, Idaho Code, as enacted by Section 1,
17 Chapter 86, Laws of 2025, be, and the same is hereby amended to read as fol-
18 lows:
19 63-3622XX 63-3622YY. SMALL SELLER. (1) There is hereby exempted from
20 the taxes imposed by this chapter sales of tangible personal property made by
21 an individual making small sales. A small seller shall not collect or remit
22 any state sales or use tax imposed by this chapter.
23 (2) For purposes of this section, "small seller" means an Idaho resi-
24 dent making sales that do not exceed five thousand dollars ($5,000) in cumu-
25 lative gross receipts in the current or previous calendar year. It shall not
26 include partnerships, corporations, or limited liability corporations.
27 (3) The exemption provided in this section shall not apply to:
28 (a) Sales of motor vehicles, trailers, all-terrain vehicles, utility
29 type vehicles, specialty off-highway vehicles, motorcycles intended
30 for off-road use, snowmobiles, aircraft, vessels as defined in section
31 67-7003, Idaho Code, alcohol, or tobacco; or
32 (b) Items purchased to be sold or incorporated into items to be sold.
33 (4) A small seller with gross receipts exceeding five thousand dollars
34 ($5,000) in a calendar year shall within thirty (30) days file with the state
35 tax commission an application for a temporary seller's permit and begin col-
36 lecting and remitting sales and use tax on all sales exceeding five thousand
37 dollars ($5,000) in such calendar year. No temporary seller's permit shall
38 be issued for a period of time greater than the remaining calendar year in
39 which sales exceeded five thousand dollars ($5,000) for a small seller. The
40 following year, If such seller reasonably anticipates to exceed five thou-
41 sand dollars ($5,000) in sales for the next calendar year, such seller shall
42 apply for a seller's permit under the provisions of section 63-3620(a),
43 Idaho Code. A small seller with gross receipts exceeding seven thousand five
44 hundred dollars ($7,500) in a calendar year shall collect and remit sales and
45 use tax on all sales made during such calendar year.
46 (5) A small seller shall indicate on such seller's invoice, if re-
47 quested by the purchaser, that the sale was exempt from sales and use tax
48 pursuant to the provisions of this section.
