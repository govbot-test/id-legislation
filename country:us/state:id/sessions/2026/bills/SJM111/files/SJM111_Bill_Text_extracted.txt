LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 111
BY RESOURCES AND ENVIRONMENT COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CON-
3 GRESS ASSEMBLED AND TO THE CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE
4 STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES.
5 We, your Memorialists, the Senate and the House of Representatives
6 of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
7 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
8 WHEREAS, Idaho's public lands contain unique landscapes, including the
9 timberlands of north Idaho, the canyon rivers of central Idaho, the range-
10 lands that provide livestock forage across southern Idaho, and the habitat
11 that provides hunting and angling opportunities across the state for all
12 Idahoans; and
13 WHEREAS, public lands, and access to those lands, are a cornerstone of
14 Idaho's identity, and once sold these lands will be lost forever; and
15 WHEREAS, these lands are more than empty spaces, they offer a level of
16 freedom that defines what it means to be an Idahoan and our way of life; and
17 WHEREAS, the Second Amendment to the U.S. Constitution preserves and
18 protects U.S. citizens' right to bear arms, and Idahoans frequently exercise
19 this right on public lands within the state as those lands provide the free-
20 dom and space to do so; and
21 WHEREAS, section 23, article I of the Idaho Constitution guarantees the
22 right to hunt, fish, and trap, including by the use of traditional methods,
23 thereby recognizing such right as a valued part of Idaho's heritage that
24 shall forever be preserved for the people and ensuring that the right is pro-
25 tected and preserved through any law, rule, or proclamation; and
26 WHEREAS, our public lands remain a shared resource, accessible to all
27 Idahoans, not just the wealthy or well-connected; and
28 WHEREAS, public lands serve as lifelines for Idaho's small businesses,
29 communities, and families; and
30 WHEREAS, the vital contributions of the grazing, timber, mining, and
31 outdoor recreation industries, which collectively generate more than $15
32 billion annually for Idaho's economy, must be safeguarded; and
33 WHEREAS, selling or transferring these lands would not only jeopardize
34 access and tradition, but also place unsustainable and unpredictable finan-
35 cial burdens on states, local governments, and Idaho's taxpayers; and
36 WHEREAS, shared stewardship and collaborative land management prac-
37 tices that include local, state, federal, and tribal governments keep public
38 lands in public hands while giving the State of Idaho and local stakeholders
39 more input and decision-making power for critical public land management
40 decisions; and
41 WHEREAS, removing the threat of mass disposal of public lands will allow
42 decision makers and stakeholders to focus on solutions for improved manage-

2
1 ment, increased funding for counties with public lands, wildfire response,
2 shared stewardship agreements, and mutually beneficial land exchanges; and
3 WHEREAS, the commitment to keeping public lands public reflects a prin-
4 cipled, conservative approach to stewardship and fiscal responsibility.
5 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
6 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of
7 Representatives concurring therein, that the Legislature commends the Idaho
8 congressional delegation for opposing efforts to sell or transfer Idaho's
9 cherished public lands.
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature encourages the Idaho con-
11 gressional delegation to continue fighting for Idahoans by, among other re-
12 lated activities, introducing or cosponsoring legislation such as the Pub-
13 lic Lands in Public Hands Act.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate be, and she is
15 hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this Memorial to the
16 President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of
17 Congress and to the congressional delegation representing the State of Idaho
18 in the Congress of the United States.
