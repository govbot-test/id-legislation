LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 109
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CON-
3 GRESS ASSEMBLED AND TO THE CONGRESSIONAL DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE
4 STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES.
5 We, your Memorialists, the Senate and the House of Representatives
6 of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
7 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
8 WHEREAS, Section 2, Article I of the Constitution of the State of Idaho
9 declares that "All political power is inherent in the people. Government is
10 instituted for their equal protection and benefit, and they have the right to
11 alter, reform or abolish the same whenever they may deem it necessary"; and
12 WHEREAS, the Tenth Amendment to the Constitution of the United States
13 affirms that any powers not delegated to the federal government are reserved
14 to the states or the people, reinforcing the principle of federalism; and
15 WHEREAS, the states have long served as laboratories of a republic,
16 uniquely positioned to establish election laws and policies that reflect the
17 needs, values, and interests of their citizens; and
18 WHEREAS, a robust system of state-led election governance strengthens
19 self-government, promotes accountability, and preserves the integrity of
20 the electoral process by ensuring that the people of each state determine
21 how best to protect their representation, secure their ballots, and maintain
22 participation in democratic processes; and
23 WHEREAS, the framers of the Constitution of the United States rec-
24 ognized the primary role of the states in structuring election laws and
25 mechanisms, understanding that a one-size-fits-all federal approach is nei-
26 ther necessary nor desirable; and
27 WHEREAS, the people of Idaho have a vested interest in ensuring that
28 their electoral processes remain transparent, secure, responsive to Idaho
29 priorities, and free from disproportionate federal influence or mandates
30 imposed by entities or individuals that may not reflect the will or security
31 needs of Idaho voters; and
32 WHEREAS, the principle of self-governance requires that states retain
33 the ability to establish laws governing elections, including the influence
34 of money and outside forces, consistent with their unique circumstances and
35 policy considerations; and
36 WHEREAS, the constitutional amendment process provides an avenue for
37 reaffirming the role of the states in overseeing elections and ensuring that
38 their authority in this realm is preserved.
39 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
40 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House
41 of Representatives concurring therein, that the Legislature hereby calls
42 upon the members representing Idaho in the Congress of the United States to
43 actively uphold the principles of federalism and to be proponents of ap-

2
1 propriate action, including, if necessary, championing a constitutional
2 amendment that affirms the primary role and the authority of states to govern
3 their election processes and establishes the ability of the states to enact
4 policies regarding campaign finance, election security and transparency,
5 and outside influence in a manner that is consistent with established con-
6 stitutional principles and that prioritizes state sovereignty.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate be, and she is
8 hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this Memorial to the
9 President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives of
10 Congress and to the congressional delegation representing the State of Idaho
11 in the Congress of the United States.
