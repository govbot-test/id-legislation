LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1335
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING SECTION 33-3731, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE PRO-
3 VISIONS REGARDING STATE-SUPPORTED MEDICAL STUDENTS AND THE HUMAN MEDI-
4 CAL EDUCATION REIMBURSEMENT FUND; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 33-3731, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 33-3731. CONTRACT REQUIREMENTS FOR STATE-SUPPORTED MEDICAL STUDENTS
9 -- HUMAN MEDICAL EDUCATION REIMBURSEMENT FUND.
10 (1)(a) An individual who qualifies as an Idaho resident pursuant to
11 section 33-3717B, Idaho Code, and who is accepted into a human medical
12 or veterinary education program enrolling Idaho students supported by
13 Idaho state funds in slots reserved for Idaho students shall, before
14 confirming enrollment in such program or school, enter into a contract
15 with the state board of education or the board's designee, which.
16 (b) For human medical and veterinary students, such contract shall
17 specify that the individual will commit to entering active full-time
18 professional practice in Idaho for a period of four (4) years within one
19 (1) year of:
20 (a) (i) Obtaining a license to practice medicine;
21 (b) (ii) Finishing a residency or subspecialty residency; or
22 (c) (iii) Finishing a human medical or veterinary education fel-
23 lowship as defined by the state board of education or the board's
24 designee.
25 (c) For veterinary education students beginning the 2027 academic
26 year, the contract shall specify that the individual will commit to a
27 course of study and subsequent service that includes mixed practice
28 agricultural animal veterinary medicine. The contract shall also re-
29 quire that no less than six hundred (600) hours of annual full-time
30 clinical practice shall be devoted to providing veterinary services to
31 agricultural animals. Compliance with this requirement shall be re-
32 viewed on an annual basis.
33 (d) For purposes of this section, the term "agricultural animal" shall
34 include cattle, sheep, goats, and swine.
35 (2) Each year of a human medical or veterinary medicine residency
36 served in Idaho, up to four (4) years, will be credited as one-half (1/2) year
37 of the practice requirement provided in subsection (1) of this section. For
38 veterinary education students, such residency training shall require that a
39 majority of time be devoted to mixed practice agricultural animal veterinary
40 medicine. After the fourth year, no credit shall be given for any additional
41 residency years.

1 (3) An individual who fails to abide by the contract described in sub-
2 section (1) of this section shall reimburse the state for the state's fi-
3 nancial obligation in supporting the individual's human medical or veteri-
4 nary medicine education, as determined by the state board of education or the
5 board's designee. Reimbursement must:
6 (a) Begin within one (1) year of:
7 (i) Obtaining a license to practice medicine;
8 (ii) Finishing a residency or subspecialty residency; or
9 (iii) Finishing a human medical or veterinary education fellow-
10 ship as defined by the state board of education or the board's de-
11 signee;
12 (b) Be paid according to an amortized schedule established by the state
13 board of education or the board's designee; and
14 (c) Be completed within eight (8) years of the date the reimbursement
15 requirement began.
16 (4) The reimbursement obligation described in subsection (3) of this
17 section:
18 (a) Shall not accrue any interest; and
19 (b) May, by act of the state board of education or the board's designee,
20 be:
21 (i) Suspended if reimbursement is temporarily impossible or
22 would create extreme hardship for a temporary period, including
23 but not limited to suspension for medical reasons, personal rea-
24 sons, parental leave, or call to active duty in the armed forces;
25 or
26 (ii) Waived if:
27 1. Reimbursement is permanently impossible or would create
28 extreme hardship, including but not limited to death, in-
29 ability to complete the program, or inability to obtain a li-
30 cense to practice medicine; or
31 2. The individual owing reimbursement is participating in
32 a program of the federal government or United States armed
33 forces that has a service requirement.
34 (5) The provisions of this section shall apply to individuals matricu-
35 lating in a medical education program enrolling Idaho students supported by
36 Idaho state funds in slots reserved for Idaho human medical students in fall
37 2023 and thereafter and to individuals matriculating in a veterinary edu-
38 cation program enrolling Idaho students supported by Idaho state funds in
39 slots reserved for Idaho veterinary students in fall 2027 and thereafter.
40 (6) There is hereby established in the state treasury a human medical
41 education reimbursement fund, which shall be administered by the state board
42 of education or the board's designee. Moneys in the fund shall:
43 (a) Consist of:
44 (i) Reimbursement paid pursuant to subsection (3) of this sec-
45 tion;
46 (ii) Legislative appropriations;
47 (iii) Donations; and
48 (iv) Interest earned on idle moneys in the fund; and
49 (b) Be used for, subject to availability:

1 (i) The costs of administering and enforcing the provisions of
2 this section; and
3 (ii) Incentive grants in an amount and according to a pro-

5 grants shall be awarded to licensed physicians who practice human

8 (7) The state board of education is authorized to take such actions as

10 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
11 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its
12 passage and approval.
