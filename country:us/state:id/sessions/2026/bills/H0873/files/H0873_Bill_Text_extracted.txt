9 SECTION 1. That Section 22-2721, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
10 amended to read as follows:
11 22-2721. ELECTION, APPOINTMENT, QUALIFICATIONS AND TENURE OF SUPER-
12 VISORS. (1) The governing body of the district shall consist of five (5)
13 supervisors, elected or appointed as provided in this chapter. Elections
14 shall be conducted pursuant to the provisions of this section and the uni-
15 form district election law, chapter 14, title 34, Idaho Code. If at any time
16 the supervisors of a district deem it necessary, upon majority vote of the
17 district board, they may increase the number of supervisors to seven (7) or
18 decrease the number of supervisors to five (5). If increased, the additional
19 supervisors shall then be appointed as outlined in this section until such
20 time as regular district elections for two (2) supervisors in each district.
21 At that time, those districts having seven (7) supervisors shall then elect
22 four (4) supervisors for four (4) year terms. The two (2) supervisors ap-
23 pointed by the district shall be persons who are by training and experience
24 qualified to perform the specialized services that will be required of them
25 in the performance of their duties. In the event the supervisors of a dis-
26 trict, upon majority vote of the district board, deem it necessary to reduce
27 the number of supervisors to five (5), there must either be two (2) vacant
28 positions on the board or two (2) of the supervisors must first volunteer to
29 give up their seats. Written notification of the reduction in supervisors,
30 signed by the district board chairman, shall then be sent to the state soil
31 and water conservation commission. Such notification shall also be sent
32 to the secretary of state to ensure only two (2) or three (3) supervisors
33 shall be on the ballot for the next election cycle. All supervisors shall be
34 landowners or farmers of the district where they are elected or appointed and
35 shall be registered to vote in the state of Idaho.
36 (2) Within thirty (30) days after the date of issuance by the secretary
37 of state of a certificate of organization of a soil conservation district,
38 nominating petitions may be filed with the state soil and water conservation
39 commission to nominate candidates for supervisors of each district. The
40 county clerk shall conduct the election for the district in compliance with
41 chapter 14, title 34, Idaho Code, and shall be the election official for the
42 district. The election official shall have authority to extend the time

13 supervisors for such district.
14 (3) All elections in districts shall be conducted by the county clerk.
15 Such election shall be held on the first Tuesday succeeding the first Monday
16 of November in each even-numbered odd-numbered year. Such elections shall
17 be in compliance with the provisions of chapter 14, title 34, Idaho Code,
18 and shall be supervised and conducted by the county clerk. The cost of con-
19 ducting such elections shall be borne by the county that conducted the elec-
20 tion. The county clerk shall certify to the soil and water conservation dis-
21 trict the names of the elected supervisors. The soil and water conservation
22 district shall issue certificates of election to each elected supervisor so

24 which the district is located shall conduct the election for the soil conser-
25 vation district, and the county clerk must provide a ballot for the district
26 election and must provide a process that allows only qualified electors of
27 the district to vote in that district's election.
28 (4) In any election for supervisor, if, after the deadline for filing
29 a declaration of intent as a write-in candidate, it appears that the num-
30 ber of qualified candidates who have been nominated is equal to the number
31 of supervisors to be elected, it shall not be necessary for the candidates to
32 stand for election. The board of supervisors shall declare such candidates
33 elected as supervisors, and the soil and water conservation district shall
34 immediately make and deliver to such persons certificates of election.
35 (5) The supervisors shall designate a chairman and may, from time to
36 time, change such designation. The term of office of each supervisor shall
37 be four (4) years commencing on the first day of January next following elec-
38 tion, except that the two (2) supervisors who are first appointed shall be
39 designated to serve for terms of two (2) years. Vacancies shall be filled for
40 the unexpired term. The selection of successors to fill an unexpired term
41 or for a full term shall be made by a vote of the majority of the supervi-
42 sors duly qualified and acting at the time the vacancy shall arise, and the
43 supervisors shall certify the name of the appointed supervisor to the state
44 soil and water conservation commission. In the event supervisors fail to
45 appoint a replacement within ninety (90) days following a vacancy, and the
46 vacancy results in the loss of a quorum of the district board, the governor
47 shall appoint a replacement sufficient to reestablish a quorum of the dis-
48 trict board. The soil conservation district shall issue a certificate of
49 such appointment.

43 term of four (4) years; and the election for supervisor scheduled for Novem-
44 ber 2028 shall instead be held on the Tuesday following the first Monday in
45 November 2029 and shall be for a term of four (4) years.

9 SECTION 3. That Section 34-1410, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
10 amended to read as follows:
11 34-1410. CANVASSING OF ELECTION RESULTS. The board of county commis-
12 sioners shall conduct the canvass of the election results within ten (10)
13 nine (9) days after the election, in the manner provided in chapter 12, ti-
14 tle 34, Idaho Code. The county clerk shall certify the election results to
15 the clerk of each political subdivision for which an election was held. Each
16 political subdivision shall issue the appropriate certificates of election.
