LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE SENATE
SENATE JOINT MEMORIAL NO. 108
BY RESOURCES AND ENVIRONMENT COMMITTEE
1 A JOINT MEMORIAL
2 TO THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES, THE SENATE AND THE HOUSE OF REPRESEN-
3 TATIVES OF THE UNITED STATES IN CONGRESS ASSEMBLED, THE CONGRESSIONAL
4 DELEGATION REPRESENTING THE STATE OF IDAHO IN THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED
5 STATES, THE UNITED STATES DEPARTMENT OF AGRICULTURE, AND THE UNITED
6 STATES DEPARTMENT OF THE INTERIOR.
7 We, your Memorialists, the Senate and the House of Representatives
8 of the State of Idaho assembled in the Second Regular Session of the
9 Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, do hereby respectfully represent that:
10 WHEREAS, the State of Idaho has abundant wildlife and open space and re-
11 lies on the agricultural and livestock industries, which, in turn, rely on
12 Idaho's open space. However, these industries are often negatively impacted
13 by depredating wildlife such as wolves and grizzly bears; and
14 WHEREAS, owners and operators of livestock operations within the State
15 of Idaho often have federal livestock grazing permits to graze livestock on
16 land managed by the United States Forest Service or the Bureau of Land Man-
17 agement; and
18 WHEREAS, Idaho residents and policymakers have been struggling for
19 years to address the negative impact depredating wildlife has on the agri-
20 cultural and livestock industries; and
21 WHEREAS, even though Idaho law supports hunters helping control wolf
22 populations, livestock is still being slaughtered by depredating wildlife,
23 primarily by wolves and grizzly bears; and
24 WHEREAS, livestock depredation directly and significantly harms the
25 owners' livelihoods, the local community's morale, and the regional econ-
26 omy; and
27 WHEREAS, to help offset the loss of the livestock, the state has created
28 the Idaho Wolf Depredation Control Board and the Idaho Depredating Wildlife
29 Appeals Board, both of which oversee claims of depredation and distribute
30 compensation; and
31 WHEREAS, it has come to the attention of the Idaho Legislature that own-
32 ers and operators of livestock operations are not reporting claims of depre-
33 dation to the state depredation boards for fear of federal retaliation in the
34 form of materially modifying or revoking their federal grazing permits; and
35 WHEREAS, the nation is reliant on the agricultural and livestock indus-
36 tries, and the livelihoods of the owners and operators of such industries are
37 persistently threatened by depredating wildlife, which state law attempts
38 to redress but cannot when our citizens fear federal retaliation.
39 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Second Regular
40 Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the Senate and the House of
41 Representatives concurring therein, that the Legislature hereby calls on
42 the Trump administration and Congress to protect Idaho's agricultural and
43 livestock industries by mandating that claims of depredation are not a basis

2
1 for materially modifying or revoking federal grazing permits and directing
2 the United States Forest Service and Bureau of Land Management to promulgate
3 rules protecting grazing permittees from the federal government modifying
4 or revoking grazing privileges based on claims of wildlife depredation.
5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate be, and she is
6 hereby authorized and directed to forward a copy of this Memorial to the
7 President of the United States, the President of the Senate and the Speaker
8 of the House of Representatives of Congress, the congressional delegation
9 representing the State of Idaho in the Congress of the United States, the
10 United States Secretary of Agriculture, and the United States Secretary of
11 the Interior.
