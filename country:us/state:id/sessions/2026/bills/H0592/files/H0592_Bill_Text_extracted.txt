LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 592
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO PATIENT IMMIGRATION STATUS; AMENDING CHAPTER 13, TITLE 39, IDAHO
3 CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 39-1397, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTAB-
4 LISH PROVISIONS REGARDING PATIENT IMMIGRATION STATUS DATA COLLECTION
5 AND TO DEFINE A TERM; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFEC-
6 TIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Chapter 13, Title 39, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
9 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
10 ignated as Section 39-1397, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 39-1397. PATIENT IMMIGRATION STATUS DATA COLLECTION. (1) Every hospi-
12 tal that accepts medicaid shall include a provision on its patient admission
13 or registration forms for the patient or the patient's representative to
14 state or indicate the immigration status of the patient, including whether
15 the patient is a United States citizen or lawfully present in the United
16 States or is not lawfully present in the United States. The inquiry shall
17 be followed by a statement that the response will not affect patient care
18 or result in a report of the patient's immigration status to immigration
19 authorities.
20 (2) Every hospital shall submit a quarterly report to the Idaho depart-
21 ment of health and welfare within thirty (30) days after the end of each cal-
22 endar quarter. Such report shall include the number of hospital admissions
23 or emergency department visits within the previous quarter that were made by
24 a patient self-reporting as a citizen of the United States, as being lawfully
25 present in the United States, or as being not lawfully present in the United
26 States.
27 (3) By March 1 of each year, the department of health and welfare shall
28 submit a report to the governor, the president pro tempore of the senate, and
29 the speaker of the house of representatives. The report shall include the
30 total number of hospital admissions and emergency department visits for the
31 previous calendar year broken down by the three (3) categories described in
32 subsection (2) of this section. The report shall also describe information
33 relating to the costs of uncompensated care for aliens who are not lawfully
34 present in the United States, the impact of uncompensated care on the cost
35 or ability of hospitals to provide services to the public, hospital funding
36 needs, and other related information.
37 (4) The department of health and welfare may promulgate rules, subject
38 to legislative approval, relating to the format of and information to be con-
39 tained in quarterly reports and the acceptable formats for hospitals to use
40 in requesting information regarding a patient's immigration status on hos-
41 pital admission or registration forms. The rules may not require the disclo-

2
1 sure of patient names or any other personal identifying information to the
2 department of health and welfare.
3 (5) As used in this section, "immigration status" means a person's le-
4 gal status in the United States as governed by the immigration and national-
5 ity act. "Immigration status" includes the following groups:
6 (a) Individuals with citizenship who are born within the territorial
7 limits of the United States or who have successfully completed the natu-
8 ralization process after living as a permanent resident for a specified
9 period;
10 (b) Permanent residents, including lawful permanent residents who
11 are granted authorization to live and work in the United States in-
12 definitely and conditional permanent residents who have gained status
13 through marriage or certain investor programs, which status is contin-
14 gent on fulfilling specific conditions;
15 (c) Nonimmigrants who are authorized to enter the United States only
16 temporarily for certain purposes, such as students or temporary work-
17 ers;
18 (d) Refugees and asylees who have been admitted to the United States
19 as a refugee, have been granted asylum, or can provide documentary evi-
20 dence that they have applied for asylum; and
21 (e) Undocumented migrants or illegal aliens who have overstayed their
22 visa or entered the United States without authorization regardless of
23 any temporary status they may have been granted by the department of
24 homeland security allowing them to be present in the United States.
25 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
26 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
27 July 1, 2026.
