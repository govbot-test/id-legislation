LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
Sixty-eighth Legislature Second Regular Session - 2026
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 794
BY EDUCATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS; AMENDING CHAPTER
3 2, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 33-213, IDAHO
4 CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING PROHIBITIONS ON EXCUSED AB-
5 SENCES FOR CERTAIN ACTIVITIES IN IDAHO PUBLIC SCHOOLS; AND DECLARING AN
6 EMERGENCY.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS. The Legislature finds that the
9 purpose of civic education is to instruct students about the history, struc-
10 ture, and operations of their civic institutions. The Legislature finds
11 that respect for the views of a politically diverse citizenry and the tra-
12 dition of political neutrality in our public institutions of learning means
13 that political activism has no place in public education. The Legislature
14 finds that granting excused absences for student walkouts or lobbying for
15 political purposes invites political favoritism from school districts and
16 places inappropriate political pressures on students with diverse views,
17 neither of which have a place in the public education system of Idaho.
18 SECTION 2. That Chapter 2, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
19 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
20 ignated as Section 33-213, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
21 33-213. PROHIBITION ON EXCUSED ABSENCES FOR CERTAIN POLITICAL ACTIVI-
22 TIES. (1) As used in this section:
23 (a) "School" means any public school teaching K-12 students within an
24 Idaho school district.
25 (b) "School district" means an Idaho public school district and in-
26 cludes Idaho public charter schools.
27 (2) No school district, school, or teacher shall permit or grant ex-
28 cused absences to any student for:
29 (a) Participating in political protests;
30 (b) Working or volunteering for a group known to engage in political
31 protests; or
32 (c) Participating in student walkouts.
33 (3) Any absence based on the activities provided in subsection (2) of
34 this section shall be considered an unexcused absence for the day. Such ab-
35 sences shall count against the school district's average daily attendance.
36 (4) School districts shall report annually the total number of unex-
37 cused absences based on the activities provided in subsection (2) of this
38 section to the Idaho superintendent of public instruction. Beginning in
39 January 2027, the state superintendent of public instruction shall, by Jan-
40 uary 15 of each year, submit an annual report to the legislature including
41 such data.

2
1 (5) The state board of education shall promulgate rules, subject to
2 legislative approval, implementing the provisions of this section.
3 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
4 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its
5 passage and approval.
